# Microstructure effects on high temperature fatigue crack initiation and short crack growth in turbine disc nickel-base superalloy Udimet 720Li  

H.T. Pang, P.A.S. Reed   ∗  

Materials Research Group, School of Engineering Sciences, University of Southampton, Highﬁeld, Southampton SO17 1BJ, UK Received 7 July 2006; received in revised form 22 September 2006; accepted 6 November 2006  

# Abstract  

An assessment of the effects of microstructure on fatigue crack initiation and short crack growth in a turbine disc nickel-base superalloy at 650   ◦ C in air is presented. U720Li and microstructural variants of U720Li, i.e. U720Li-LG (large grain variant) and U720Li-LP (large intragranular coherent   ′   precipitate variant) have been assessed by uninterrupted and replicated short crack tests in polished U-notch specimens using a 1-1-1-1 trapezoidal loading cycle at nominal stress levels ranging between 700 and 850 MPa (calculated in the uncracked ligament). Crack initiation was primarily due to porosity on or near the surface but also due to grain boundary oxidation. Initial transgranular crack growth across four to six grains in air was noted at short crack lengths before oxidation-assisted intergranular crack growth modes were established at larger crack lengths. At a nominal applied stress of 840 MPa, U720Li and U720Li-LP show similar fatigue lifetimes while U720Li-LG demonstrates a signiﬁcantly improved fatigue lifetime, particularly when lifetimes are compared on a local strain range basis. A larger grain size gave the most signiﬁcant performance beneﬁts in terms of overall fatigue lifetime under these test conditions.

# 1. Introduction  

The turbine disc in a gas turbine aeroengine is subjected to severe operating conditions. Repeated start-up and shutdown cycles and prolonged application of stress at elevated tempera- tures, in addition to highly corrosive hot exhaust gases, results in fatigue, creep, oxidation and corrosion damage become critical factors in determining component life. Nickel-base superalloys are frequently chosen for turbine disc applications as gener- ally these alloys have good resistance against combinations of fatigue, creep, oxidation and corrosion damage. Nickel-base superalloys consist of an austenitic FCC    matrix strengthened mainly by coherent nickel-rich   ′   precipitates. Incoherent pri- mary   ′   precipitates may also be present, depending on heat treatment conditions, e.g. sub-solvus heat treatments of PM alloys. These reside mainly at grain boundaries as they are   ′ which are not dissolved during the solution heat treatment and act as grain boundary pinners. By altering heat treatment condi- tions, grain size and the amount and characteristics of primary (incoherent)   ′   and coherent   ′   may be varied. This in turn gives rise to a variety of mechanical/oxidation/corrosion properties that may be optimised to give improved overall component life.  

Fatigue crack initiation and short crack growth are impor- tant, as a majority of turbine disc fatigue life is spent in this regime during service  [1–4] . High component stresses result in limited fatigue crack propagation prior to fast fracture, hence a large proportion of fatigue life is spent in the short crack growth regime  [5,6] . Microstructure and environment play a dominant role in fatigue crack initiation and subsequent short crack growth, especially when crack lengths are comparable to the scale of characteristic microstructural features  [2,7] .  

At elevated temperatures, fatigue cracks have been observed to initiate from slip bands  [1,6,8–11] , grain boundaries  [12,13] , twin boundaries  [10,14] , at inclusions  [14–17]  or at defects  [18] . The small fatigue cracks that grow immediately after initiation tend to exhibit anomalously high, irregular growth rates when compared to larger cracks at similar   K  [2,9,19,20] . These small cracks also grow below the threshold   K  found for large cracks [9,21–23] . This anomaly is due to the inability of the stress intensity factor  K  to correctly describe the magnitude of the crack tip stress–strain ﬁeld for short cracks  [19] , and the driving force for short crack growth appears signiﬁcantly higher than

Application of con- ventional methods of life prediction, e.g. integrating the long crack Paris law relationship between an initial and critical ﬂaw size, may lead to non-conservative estimates of component life if short crack growth behaviour is not taken into account  [19] .

Tests were conducted in 3-point bend with a span of 25 mm under load control as shown in  Fig. 2 a. Loads were applied to give nominal maximum stresses between 750 and 850 MPa across the uncracked ligament at an applied  R -ratio of 0.1, with a 1-1-1-1 trapezoidal loading waveform ( Fig. 2 b). Lifetimes (to ﬁnal failure) were established for uninterrupted tests in U720Li over this range of applied stresses. Interrupted, replicated tests in all three materials were then carried out at a stress level of 840 MPa in light of the lifetimes achieved in the uninterrupted test results. Testing was interrupted every 500/1000 cycles with slow cooling under a low constant load to allow room tem- perature acetate replication of the polished notch root surface, followed by slow reheating (20   ◦ C/min, with 30 min to stabilise at test temperature) before resuming testing. Acetate replicas, rounded to ﬁt the U-notch, were used for replication. A series of replication tests at stress levels of 800, 840 and 850 MPa were also carried out on U720Li. The replication tests allowed fatigue crack initiation and short crack growth behaviour assessment in the materials. Due to the time-consuming nature of the replica- tion tests, relatively few samples could be tested in this detailed manner, but these replication tests are believed to reveal new and detailed information on crack initiation and early stages of crack growth at 650   ◦ C in these alloys. To conﬁrm the lifetime ﬁndings, where possible, the lifetime data were compared with relevant LCF lifetime to ﬁnal failure data obtained on smooth cylindrical gauge length specimens for the same materials. This work was carried out at QinetiQ Farnborough in a parallel testing programme  [27] .  

Acetate replicas were examined using an Olympus stereomi- croscope to identify crack initiation points and to characterise the distribution and number of cracks (crack ﬁeld) evolving in the notch root of the specimen during the test. Crack lengths of surface cracks were also measured from the replica records to calculate growth rates of surface cracks. Crack growth rates d a /d N  were plotted against   K . Stress intensity factor  K  was calculated from the half surface crack length using the empiri- cal formula of Scott and Thorpe  [28]  with stresses at the notch root computed using ﬁnite element analysis with the ANSYS ﬁnite element package. Cyclically stabilised stress–strain curves obtained at 650   ◦ C from the strain-controlled high tempera- ture LCF tests of the same materials at QinetiQ, Farnborough were used as the materials model in the ﬁnite element analy- sis. A non-linear analysis was carried out on a single monotonic loading step of the model with application of cyclically sta- bilised stress–strain data to take into account the elastic–plastic behaviour of the notched specimens. The symmetric model was meshed with four-noded quadrilateral elements and loaded with point contacts to simplify computation. Fractographic examina- tion of the test specimen notch root surface and fracture surface was carried out in a JEOL JSM-6500 FEG-SEM operating at an accelerating voltage of 10 kV.

Another feature observed in the notch root of the U720Li-LG replication test specimen is the occurrence of grain boundary oxidation ( Fig. 5  shows grain boundary delineation by dark oxide product) leading to formation of cracks along the grain boundaries as shown in  Fig. 5 a. Oxide debris is visible on the surface and was also noted in the wake of the primary crack on the surface ( Fig. 5 b). This indicates that primary crack ini- tiation is in part due to grain boundary oxidation in addition to the possible effect of the subsurface pore. The grain boundary oxide debris along the top surface of the primary crack in the U720Li-LG replication test was noted very early in the test and so was not due to subsequent oxidation of the material after the crack initiated. Further evidence supporting crack initiation from both the subsurface pore and grain boundary oxidation on the surface for the U720Li-LG replication test is the appearance of a ligament-like feature between the surface and the subsur- face pore as shown in  Fig. 4 c formed where the cracks due to both initiation mechanisms may have coalesced. The occurrence of grain boundary oxidation leading to crack initiation was not observed in the other materials.  

Immediately after initiation, crack growth was transgranu- lar, as evidenced from the ﬂat fracture surface around the crack initiation site, for all materials. The extent of the transgranular region is indicated by the dashed white line on each micrograph in  Fig. 4 a–c indicating the boundary between initial transgran- ular and subsequent intergranular crack growth. As reported earlier  [24]  for the same applied stress levels (840 MPa), the ﬂat transgranular region around the primary crack initiation site of U720Li-LG was distinctly larger compared to that of U720Li- LP, while U720Li tested at the same nominal stress level showed the smallest ﬂat transgranular region. It should be noted how- ever that the primary crack in the U720Li-LG replication test is thought to be due to both initiation from the subsurface pore and grain boundary oxidation on the surface. The porosity in these materials remains from the original PM production route, after HIPing and is expected to have no signiﬁcant gas content, hence subsurface growth from such a pore can be considered to grow under effective vacuum conditions until the crack tip is very close to the surface. Hence some degree of ﬂat transgran- ular crack growth would have occurred in vacuum for the crack which initiated from the subsurface pore before it coalesced with the crack which initiated on the surface by grain boundary oxida- tion. There is a very limited extent of likely transgranular crack growth in vacuum for both the U720Li and U720Li-LP repli- cation tests as the pores that had initiated the primary cracks in these tests are on or extremely close to the surface.

# 5. Discussion  

# 5.1. Fatigue crack initiation and short crack growth behaviour at 650   ◦ C  

Porosity was noted to be the dominant crack initiation mech- anism with no evidence of slip band crack initiation observed. At elevated temperatures, dislocation cross-slip and climb pro- cesses will lead to wavy slip, reducing the build-up of stresses within slip bands making slip band-induced cracking less likely to occur. The stress concentration offered by the pore is a more potent crack initiator, although local slip processes at the pore, e.g. protrusions of slip bands into the pore may give rise to fur- ther micro-notch effects and stress concentration. The initiation at surface or near-surface porosity is likely to be aided by oxida- tion mechanisms. The stress concentration introduced by these pores together with any grain boundaries that may be present on or near the pores would cause accelerated oxidation-assisted cracking compared to grain boundaries on the surface not sub- jected to any stress concentrations. The oxidation rate could be stress-enhanced by lowering the apparent activation energy for oxygen diffusion through an expansion of the lattice and/or by oxide cracking which exposes fresh material for rapid oxidation [12] . This may also partially explain the early crack initiation observed in the replication tests as oxidation is effectively in action as soon as the specimen is exposed to the test temperature and it is further accelerated by fatigue cycling and by possible stress concentrations due to the pores.  

Numbers of cracks initiating increase rapidly in all materials followed by a deceleration in the rate of crack initiation towards the middle of life and eventual acceleration in the rate of crack initiation towards the end of life. The rate of crack initiation is related to local strain effects at stress concentration features in addition to any oxidation effects that may be present. Notch root strains developed early in the life will rapidly increase due to cyclic softening which causes higher strains for a given applied stress. Cyclic softening at elevated temperatures is prevalent in these materials  [27] . Eventually, cyclic softening stabilises or saturates and the strains developed for a given cyclic load will follow a stabilised stress–strain relationship. In elevated temperature LCF tests at 650   ◦ C on U720Li and U720Li-LG conducted by QinetiQ Farnborough  [27] , cyclic softening was noted to stabilise at approximately 20–50% of the life of LCF specimens. The initial rapidly increasing strains are likely to give rise to a high rate of crack initiation. Eventually, the cyclic softening stabilises leading to a slowdown in the rate of crack initiation towards the middle of life (as observed). Accelera- tion in the rate of crack initiation in all samples nearing the end of life may be due to the inﬂuence of the now extensive crack tip stress–strain ﬁelds of the larger cracks creating signiﬁcant areas which will experience locally higher strains in the notch root.  

In U720Li-LG, oxide debris formation was frequently noted at grain boundaries. These oxidised grain boundaries eventu- ally led to initiation of many small cracks and signiﬁcantly more cracks were noted in U720Li-LG compared to other test materials in this study. Crack initiation from oxidised grain boundaries has also been noted in other elevated temperature short crack studies  [12] . On the surface of the material, grain boundaries constitute a discontinuity in the microstructure and higher stresses are experienced compared to regions within the grains. Grain boundaries are also less oxidation-resistant com- pared to other regions of the material and faster diffusion of oxygen occurs along these boundaries. Cyclic loading further weakens these boundaries and increases the degree of oxida- tion attack at these boundaries, and eventually initiates a fatigue crack. The initial high rate of crack initiation in U720Li-LG may be due to the higher strains experienced in the notch root at the same nominal applied stresses cf. the other materials, and may not reﬂect any increased propensity to grain boundary oxidation attack.  

Early crack growth at this temperature in these alloys is via a Stage II transgranular crack growth mechanism. This is followed by an apparent shift to inter granular crack growth modes at larger crack lengths. Transgranular crack growth immediately after crack initiation has also been observed in other elevated tem- perature short crack or LCF studies  [12,17,29,30] . If the crack initiates from features within a grain it will inevitably traverse the ﬁrst grain at short crack lengths, however the crack continues to grow by traversing grains until eventually at longer crack lengths intergranular crack growth becomes predominant. Possible fac- tors favouring the shift to intergranular crack growth at larger crack lengths are likely to be due to a grain boundary sampling- related criterion and/or due to changes in stress intensities at the crack tip. The grain boundary sampling-related criterion may be more relevant as the results shown in  Table 5  indicate that approximately four to six grains, rather than a constant crack length, have to be traversed before intergranular crack growth modes are fully established. Nevertheless, increased stress inten- si ties maybe required to provides uf cie nt crack tip driving force or crack tip process zone to cause a transition to an oxidation- assisted intergranular crack growth mode, where grain boundary embrittlement due to oxygen diffusion along these grain bound- aries occurs. An increased degree of intergranular crack growth at larger crack lengths/higher   K  levels is expected as such a transition was noted in corresponding elevated temperature long crack tests in air  [31] .  

# 5.2. Comparison of fatigue crack initiation and short crack growth behaviour at room temperature and at 650   ◦ C  

At room temperature, fatigue cracks were noted to initiate from both porosity and slip bands in these materials  [24] . At 650   ◦ C, porosity was noted to be the dominant crack initiation mechanism with no slip band crack initiation observed. This can be explained in terms of the increased dislocation cross-slip and climb processes at elevated temperatures as discussed earlier.  

In terms of short crack growth at room temperature, Stage I type crack growth was observed in all alloys at short crack lengths and Stage II type crack growth was observed at larger crack lengths  [24] . At 650   ◦ C, crack growth of the primary crack immediately after crack initiation was relatively smooth transgranular Stage II type crack growth followed by an appar- ent shift to intergranular crack growth modes at larger crack lengths. Distinct Stage I and Stage II crack growth was noted at room temperature due to the highly planar slip character of the materials at room temperature. At 650   ◦ C, the initial ﬂat trans- granular region is due to the crack propagating by a Stage II crack growth mechanism at short crack lengths and eventually shift- ing to intergranular crack growth modes at larger crack lengths due to the air environment and sufﬁcient sampling of grain boundaries occurring to set up the intergranular crack growth mode.  

Comparisons of room temperature and 650   ◦ C short crack growth rates are shown in  Fig. 9 a and b for U720Li and U720Li- LG. No comparison was made for U720Li-LP as the notch root stress analysis required to calculate   K  in the U720Li-LP 650   ◦ C short crack test was not available. Short crack growth rates of U720Li at room temperature are approximately one decade lower compared with those at 650   ◦ C. U720Li showed Stage I and/or Stage II crack growth at room temperature while at 650   ◦ C,approximately60%of the fatigue life( Table6 )wasspent in the oxidation-assisted intergranular crack growth regime. The difference in short crack growth rates of U720Li at room temperature and at 650   ◦ C may therefore be attributed to the oxi- dising environment of the 650   ◦ C short crack tests which gives highly accelerated oxidation-assisted intergranular crack growth modes. The slip mechanisms in the material are also expected to be altered at 650   ◦ C but this change is likely to have a less signiﬁcant role in altering crack growth rates when intergranular crack growth modes predominate.  

Short crack growth rates of U720Li-LG shown in  Fig. 9 b appear to indicate that short crack growth rates at room tem- perature and 650   ◦ C are relatively similar. At room temperature, U720Li-LG also showed Stage I and/or Stage II crack growth, and at 650   ◦ C approximately 90% of the fatigue life ( Table 6 ) was spent in the transgranular Stage II crack growth regime. The similarity in the short crack growth rates of U720Li-LG at room temperature and at 650   ◦ C may be reﬂecting the relatively similar crack growth modes observed in U720Li-LG at room temperature and at 650   ◦ C.

# 5.3. Comparison of short crack and long crack growth behaviour at 650   ◦ C  

Comparison of short crack and long crack behaviour at 650   ◦ C in air is made for U720Li and U720Li-LG by plotting short crack and long crack growth curves on the same graph as shown in  Fig. 9 c and d. Comparison was made on a   K  basis only for U720Li and U720Li-LG as the notch root stress analysis required to calculate   K  in the 650   ◦ C U720Li-LP short crack test was not available. The short growth curve of U720Li was noted to collapse relatively close to the corresponding long crack growth curve. For U720Li-LG, short crack growth rates obtained were at relatively low   K  which was below the lowest   K  of the long crack growth curve. However the short and long crack growth curves of U720Li-LG do appear to collocate when both curves are extrapolated to intermediate   K  levels between the two curves.  

In the short crack tests, the primary cracks initially prop- agate by a transgranular Stage II crack growth mechanism, changing to intergranular crack growth modes similar to those observed in long crack specimens only after approximately four to six grains have been traversed. This transition occurs at approximately 40 and 90% of the lifetime of short crack tests respectively for U720Li and U720Li-LG ( Table 6 ). The pre- dominantly oxidation-assisted intergranular crack growth mode in the short crack case was similar to the long crack growth mode of U720Li in air at 650   ◦ C as shown in  Fig. 10 a. Likewise short crack growth in U720Li-LG eventually adopted a mixed trans- granular/intergranular crack growth mode similar to long crack growth mode of U720Li-LG as shown in  Fig. 10 b. Although comparison of short crack and long crack growth behaviour was not possible for U720Li-LP, the similar short crack and long crack growth mechanism ( Fig. 10 c) would suggest similar trends should be expected as observed for U720Li and U720Li-LG.  

Although crack growth in the short crack tests adopts the crack growth modes observed in corresponding long tests only at longer crack lengths, limited differences are seen in d a /d N versus  K behaviourintheshortandlongcracktestsat650   ◦ Cin air. Flat transgranular crack growth may occur in long crack tests if the tests are extended to sufﬁciently low   K  levels. However at   K  levels where ﬂat transgranular crack growth occurs in the short crack tests, long cracks are likely to reach crack growth threshold. In short crack tests, the crack is still able to propagate at these short crack lengths presumably due to the relative lack of closure.  

The similarity in short crack and long crack growth curves also indicates some degree of applicability of the   K  parameter as a correlating parameter in the current series of elevated tem- peraturetests.This is surprising in view of the fact that at elevated temperatures the plastic zone size ahead of the crack tip will be signiﬁcantly larger and creep processes will be more signiﬁcant, both of which are likely to invalidate   K  assumptions at small crack lengths, nevertheless this ﬁnding is of interest in consider- ing appropriate liﬁng approaches for such materials in service.  

# 5.4. Overall materials comparison  

When tested at 840 MPa the fatigue lifetime of U720Li-LP is only marginally greater than the fatigue lifetime of U720Li. The approximately similar fatigue life in both cases reﬂects their similar crack initiation and growth characteristics. Crack initia- tion in both cases occurred due to porosity near the surface, and  

![](images/3a0adfeab5729e698842200d6ca2889637d2bfeefda89edd1cc7e29df243ab7d.jpg)  

relatively similar extents of transgranular crack growth were noted before transition to intergranular crack growth modes. Both U720Li and U720Li-LP tested at 840 MPa spent approx- imately 40% of their fatigue life in the ﬂat transgranular crack growth regime before adopting an intergranular crack growth mode. After transition to intergranular crack growth, the degree of inter granularity of crack growth was also noted to be very similar in both materials. Larger coherent   ′   precipitates are believed to promote homogeneous slip within the grain. This would minimise the intensity of slip band impingement on grain boundaries  [32]  and may lead to a reduced tendency of oxidation-assisted grain boundary cracking. Grain size increases from U720Li to U720Li-LP to U720Li-LG, which is also the trend observed for increasing fatigue lifetimes in the 840 MPa replication tests. Larger coherent   ′   precipitates are also found in U720Li-LG and U720Li-LP. However U720Li-LP which has twice as large coherent   ′   precipitates compared to U720Li only shows a marginal improvement in fatigue performance (which could also be attributed to scatter).  

The extended life of the U720Li-LG sample at 840 MPa is attributed to a few factors. Primary crack initiation in the U720Li-LG test appears to be due to combination of a surface-initiated crack from grain boundary oxidation and a subsurface-initiated crack from a sub-surface pore.  Fig. 7 appears to indicate that interaction between the two cracks has caused crack growth deceleration of the surface-initiated crack, which may be linked to interactions between the plastic zones of the two cracks. Initiation from a sub-surface pore would also imply a certain degree of transgranular crack growth in vacuum until the subsurface-initiated crack coalesces with the surface- initiated crack. Transgranular crack growth at 650   ◦ C in vacuum is much slower compared to corresponding crack growth in air [25] . The subsequent transgranular crack growth region imme- diately after crack initiation is also signiﬁcantly larger than that of U720Li and U720Li-LP. This is linked to the larger grain sizes of U720Li-LG, and the observed d a /d N  imply that transgranular crack growth is much slower than intergranular crack growth. Beyond the transgranular crack growth region, crack growth in U720Li-LG appears to be mixed transgranular/intergranular compared to predominantly intergranular crack growth beyond the transgranular region in U720Li and U720Li-LP. A higher degree of oxidation resistance of U720Li-LG may be inferred from the reduced propensity to oxidation-assisted intergranular crack growth modes in U720Li-LG.  

After transition from the ﬂat transgranular crack growth regime,U720Li-LG was noted to spend only approximately 10% of fatigue life in the mixed transgranular/intergranular crack growth regime before catastrophic failure occurred compared to approximately 60% of fatigue lifetime for U720Li and U720Li- LP ( Table 6 ). This result should not be interpreted as indicative of poor crack growth resistance of U720Li-LG, since the higher local strains in the notch root of U720Li-LG may induce catas- trophic failure at shorter crack lengths. The increased fatigue crack growth resistance of U720Li-LG compared to U720Li is highlighted when the elevated temperature short crack growth rates at 650   ◦ C in air of U720Li and U720Li-LG are compared on a   K  basis in  Fig. 7 .

The extended life of U720Li-LG tested at 840 MPa compared to U720Li and U720Li-LP should be considered to be solely due to the improved fatigue resistance of the U720Li-LG microstruc- ture. Although primary crack initiation in the U720Li-LG test was due to a complex combination of two cracks, implying some degree of deceleration in crack growth and some degree of trans- granular crack growth in vacuum for the crack initiating from subsurface pore, the absence of other large cracks in U720Li- LG which could give catastrophic failure despite the rapid crack initiation and high number of cracks in U720Li-LG compared to U720Li and U720Li-LP does indicate better fatigue resistance of the U720Li-LG microstructure. The higher fatigue resistance of U720Li-LG compared to U720Li is also highlighted in  Fig. 8 a–c when the fatigue lifetimes are compared on a stress-range and strain-range basis. While U720Li-LG appears to fall on the same stress-range against lifetime curve as U720Li, the strain-range against lifetime plots indicates that U720Li-LG has a much longer life for a given strain-range compared to U720Li. # 6. Summary and conclusions  

At 650   ◦ C, early crack initiation has been observed, lead- ing to fatigue lifetimes being essentially propagation controlled.  

Porosity has caused initiation of the fatal crack in all tests, indi- cating the importance of porosity control for these PM materials. For cracks which initiate from subsurface pores, as in the case of U720Li-LG, initial transgranular crack growth will occur in vacuum at much slower rates than corresponding crack growth in air  [25] , until the crack breaks the surface. In U720Li-LG, very slow crack propagation behaviour was also observed with a transgranular crack growth mode in air at small crack lengths. Larger grains have been associated with increased resistance to transgranular crack growth in both vacuum and air due to reduced fatigue damage accumulation associated with increased reversibility of slip with larger grains  [33] . This is likely to con- tribute to the signiﬁcantly longer fatigue lifetime observed for U720Li-LG.  

Initial crack growth (in air) after initiation was ﬂat transgran- ular Stage II type crack growth in the ﬁrst few grains before the onset of oxidation-assisted intergranular crack growth at larger crack lengths. Transition to intergranular crack growth requires a number of grain boundaries to be traversed by the growing crack. The results of this study indicate four to six grains to be sufﬁcient. The larger the grain size, the more extended the slow transgranular crack growth region.  

The mixed transgranular/intergranular crack growth in U720Li-LG and fully intergranular crack growth in U720Li and U720Li-LP shown in  Fig. 4  have also been observed in corre- sponding long crack tests carried out on these materials under similar conditions as shown in  Fig. 10 [25] . Preferential grain boundary oxidation causes crack growth along grain bound- aries. Larger grain size results in less embrittled grain boundary area available for intergranular crack growth and a more tortu- ous crack path (hence greater shielding). The fracture surface of U720Li-LG also shows mixed transgranular/intergranular crack growth compared to predominantly intergranular crack growth in U720Li and U720Li-LP, indicating a higher resis- tance of larger grains towards oxidation-assisted intergranular crack growth modes. Thus large grain sizes not only prolong initial slow transgranular growth but also improve high tem- perature intergranular fatigue crack growth resistance, leading to signiﬁcantly better fatigue performance of U720Li-LG com- pared to U720Li and U720Li-LP. In this study the inﬂuence of grain size on high temperature fatigue crack initiation and short crack growth resistance does indeed appear very marked.  

The elevated temperature short crack tests thus indicate larger grain size to be beneﬁcial to crack growth resistance as it not only prolongs the initial slow transgranular crack growth after crack initiation but also improves high temperature oxidation-assisted intergranular crack growth resistance. In terms of coherent   ′ precipitate sizes, the improvement in fatigue resistance expected for larger precipitate sizes is not really marked. It is believed that the overall fatigue performance is governed far more by grain size than by coherent   ′   precipitate sizes in U720Li and its variants.
