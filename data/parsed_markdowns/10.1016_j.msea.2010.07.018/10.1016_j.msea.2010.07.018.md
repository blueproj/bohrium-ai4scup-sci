The dynamic recrystallization (DRX) of the hot isostatically pressed P/M superalloy FGH4096 in isothermal working process was characterized and investigated by using optical microscope (OM) and transmission electron microscope (TEM) techniques. The P/M superalloy was ﬁrst prepared by hot iso- statically pressing. The isothermal compression tests of the P/M superalloy were then conducted in a Gleeble-1500D simulator at the temperature ranging from 1080 to 1140   ◦ C and the strain rate from 0.02 to 1.0 s − 1 . The microstructure observation of the deformed samples showed that the necklace microstruc- ture was obtained under the strain of 0.51 or below. The necklace microstructure is an intermediate microstructure for the complete recrystallized microstructure, which could be ﬁnally generated when the strain was about 0.69. In addition, it was found that the complete recrystallized grain quantity was increased with the increase of temperature and the decrease of strain rate. The DRX nucleation sites are classiﬁed into three regions, viz., (i) previous particle boundaries (PPBs), (ii) recrystallized grain bound- aries, and (iii) twin sources. As the repeated DRX took place, the grain size was eventually reﬁned from 30.0 to 4.0   m after the multiaxially forging and the homogeneous bulk billet with ﬁne DRX grains was ﬁnally obtained.

The DRX phenomena have been explored and studied for more than four decades since 1965 when the DRX was conﬁrmed to occur in deformation process  [9] . To summarize prior peer researches, McQueen  [10]  detailedly reviewed the DRX researches and the development of DRX theory. In addition, the geometric DRX, con- tinuous DRX and grain reﬁning dynamic recovery (DRV) have been extensively investigated  [11–18] . Huge efforts have also been pro- vided to characterize the DRX in hot working process for the superalloys including nimonic 75, nimonic 85A, nimonic 90, P/M IN-100, and IN718 by using processing map technology  [19–23] . Furthermore, Huang and Xu  [24]  investigated the evolution mech- anism of grain reﬁnement in DRX process and it was found that the austenite grains of Fe–32%Ni alloy were reﬁned from initially 200 to about 10   m after 3 passes of multiaxially forging at the tem- perature of 550   ◦ C and the strain rate of 2  ×  10 − 2   s − 1 . All of these provide a basis and insight for the further research on the DRX of the P/M superalloy FGH4096.  

# 1. Introduction  

Turbine disc is one of the most important components in advanced gas engines  [1,2] . This component, however, requires excellent material properties such as high ultimate tensile strength and fatigue resistance as it works in high temperature and severe stress working conditions  [3–5] . Turbine disc thus needs to employ excellent structural materials. FGH4096 with medium grain size is a new and preferred P/M superalloy for fabrication of turbine disc [6–8] , owing to its high temperature properties and microstructural stability, which can be maintained up to 750   ◦ C. To meet these strict requirements in terms of working condition and material property, the P/M superalloy should have ideal microstructure. In hot work- ing of the P/M superalloy FGH4096, recrystallization phenomenon in the process needs to be extensively explored and investigated as it reﬁnes the grains of the superalloy, which in turn signiﬁ- cantly affects the mechanical properties of the alloy. Therefore, the dynamic recrystallization (DRX) of the P/M superalloy in hot working process needs to be systematically studied.  

In this paper, the microstructure evolution in hot working pro- cess of the P/M superalloy FGH4096 was investigated. The DRX nucleation and development was characterized by using the trans- mission electron microscope (TEM) techniques. The DRX behavior and mechanisms in hot working process were systematically stud- ied in such a way to provide an in-depth understanding and knowledge to support the design and optimization of the hot work- ing processes of the superalloy FGH4096.

# 2. Experiment  

The chemical composition of the P/M superalloy FGH4096 is listed in  Table 1 . The detailed material preparation processes, including plasma-rotation-electronic-pole (PREP) atomization and hot-isostatic-press (HIP), are articulated in the previous paper [1] , in which the deformation behavior based on the process- ing maps technique was systematically studied. In this paper, the investigation on the microstructure characterization and dynamic recrystallization of the P/M superalloy in hot working process is the focus. The prepared HIPed P/M FGH4096 superalloy is a bar with a diameter of 30 mm. The cylindrical specimens with the dimen- sion of  ∅ 8 mm  ×  12 mm were machined from the HIPed bars. The samples were annealed at the temperature of 1150   ◦ C for 2 h and then air-cooled to room temperature before the isothermal com- pression.  

The isothermal compression tests were conducted under a constant strain rate by using a Gleeble-1500D simulator at the tem- perature from 1080 to 1140   ◦ C and the strain rate from 0.02 to 1.0 s − 1 . The specimens were heated with a heating rate of 10   ◦ C/s and soaked for 2.0 min at the deformation temperature. The defor- mation temperature was controlled within the accuracy of  ± 2   ◦ C. The specimens were deformed to the height reduction of 15, 35 and 50%, respectively, and then were cooled down to the room temper- ature via water spraying. The deformed specimens were sectioned parallel to the compression direction and prepared for microstruc- ture examination by using OLYMPUS-PM3 optical microscope (OM) technique. The grain size of the specimens was measured from the central region of the compressed specimen based on the mean- linear intercept method and the error measurement is within  ± 5% [25] . The DRX nucleation and development was characterized by HITACHI-H800 transmission electron microscopy (TEM).  

# 3. Results and discussion  

# 3.1. Microstructure evolution  

The typical microstructure of the prepared P/M FGH4096 super- alloy consisted of grains with the size of 30   m, is shown in Fig. 1 a, which obviously reveals the previous particle boundaries (PPBs, “ → ”), a little recrystallized grain boundaries (“ → ”) and twin boundaries (“ → ”). For the low stacking fault energy alloys such as FGH4096, the important process to control microstructural evolu- tion in hot working process is discontinuous DRX  [26,27] . The main mechanism to control dynamic nucleation is the serration of the initial grain boundaries associated with DRV  [28] . New grains were eventually generated along the prior grain boundaries. Since the new grains were ﬁne and led to grain reﬁnement, a typically incom- plete recrystallized microstructure, called necklace microstructure, was generated and observed, as shown in  Fig. 1 (b) and (c). The new grains were ﬁrst formed via the bulging of PPBs, resulted from the strain-inducing boundaries shape ﬂuctuation with the increasing strain. The new boundaries have orientation similar to those of the PPBs. With an increase of the strain in deformation process, mis- orientation was developed and the area fraction ( f DRX ) of the ﬁne new grains in necklace microstructure became greater and greater ( f DRX ). It should be pointed that the “necklace” is an incomplete DRX microstructure and the complete DRX microstructure would be obtained when a critical strain is reached.

The nucle- ation sites of the superalloy in DRX process are classiﬁed into three regions. They are: (i) previous particle boundaries (PPBs) accumulated critical dislocation, (ii) recrystallized grain boundaries containing dislocations for next DRX nucleation, (iii) twin sources containing growing substructure. The typical TEM micrographs of the DRX nucleation in three different regions are presented in  Fig. 6 .  

The initiation of the DRX of the P/M superalloy FGH4096 is characterized by extensively irregular PPBs, as shown in  Fig. 6 (a). The occurrence of such boundariy morphology implies that the recrystallization mechanism of the P/M superalloy is the discon- tinuous DRX (DDRX). The GB shearing during the early stage of hot deformation would result in the development of inhomogeneous deformation and the formation of orientation gradients near the GBs, which contributes to the operation of grain boundary bulging. The grain boundary migration is also coupled with twining. The twinning acts as an active nucleation mechanism of DRX for the superalloy in hot working process. It further accelerates the bulging as well as the separation of new grains from the original ones, resulting in the development of DRX grains.  

# 3.4. Repeated DRX and grain reﬁnement  

The typical microstructures of the P/M superalloy FGH4096 after isothermal compression conducted at 1080   ◦ C/1 s − 1   and the strain of 0.69 are shown in  Fig. 7 . The microstructures clearly reveal the different characterizations from region to region, including PPBs (3), necklace microstructure (2, 4, 5, 7, and 8) and the complete DRX microstructure (1 and 6). The microstructures of the com- pressed specimens show that the isothermal compression in only one deformation direction cannot produce the bulk billets with homogeneously ﬁne grains.  

The schematic grain size of the P/M superalloy FGH4096 pro- cessed with different deformation processes is shown in  Fig. 8 . Obviously, the grain size is greatly reﬁned to approximately 4.0   m after 3 passes of multiaxially forging at the deformation condition of 1110   ◦ C/1 s − 1 . The ultraﬁne microstructure of the P/M FGH4096 superalloy is presented in  Fig. 9 . It is found that the repeated DRX took place successfully in multiaxially forging process. For the ﬁrst pass of forging, the deformation energy was ﬁrstly accu- mulated at PPBs and then resulted in the ﬁrst DRX. The grain size was reﬁned for the ﬁrst time. Furthermore, the fresh recrystallized grains occurred and the fresh GBs also accumulated energy for the next DRX in the second pass of forging. When the next DRX took place, the grain size was further reﬁned.

# 4. Conclusions  

The dynamic recrystallization of the P/M superalloy FGH4096 deformed at the temperature from 1080 to 1140   ◦ C and the strain rate of 0.02–1.0 s − 1   has been extensively studied. Through the char- acterization of the microstructure evolution of the samples after hot working process, the following concluding remarks can be drawn:  

1. The irregular grain boundary morphology implied that the recrystallization mechanism of the P/M superalloy FGH4096 is discontinuous DRX.

 2. The necklace microstructure is an intermediate microstructure for the formation of the complete recrystallized microstructure, which is formed when the strain reaches 0.69. Meanwhile, the complete recrystallized grain is increased with the deformation temperature and the decrease of strain rate.

 3. The nucleation site of the superalloy in DRX process could be classiﬁed into three regions, namely, (i) the previous parti- cle boundaries (PPBs), accumulating critical dislocation, (ii) the recrystallized grain boundaries, containing dislocations for next DRX nucleation, (iii) and the twin sources, containing growing substructure.  

4. As the repeated DRX took place, the grains were reﬁned from 30.0 to 4.0   m after multiaxially forging and the homogeneous bulk billet with ﬁne DRX grains was obtained.
