# 1. Introduction  

Powder metallurgy nickel base superalloys have been widely used in gas turbine disk  of aero-engine owing to an exceptional combination of strength, fatigue/creep  properties and resistance to corrosion under elevated temperature [1]. These superior  mechanical properties primarily originate from the grain size and precipitation phases,  of 1.0 s ACCEPTED MANUSCRIPT -1   and0.001 s occur during the single-pass compression. In this article, we employ a two-pass  approach through introducing dwell treatment to investigate the difference of flow  stress and grain size evolution vis-à-vis the counterparts in the single-pass  compression. The experimental results show that the multi-pass compression by  imposing different strain rate is versatile to control the grain size than the single-pass  compression. A high strain rate, i.e., 0.1 s -1 , in the first-pass can promote the DRX  nucleation and produce a uniform and finer grain size. Decreasing the strain rate to  0.01 s -1  can trigger grain growth during compression. The multi-pass compression  tests easily distinguish the roles of DRX and SRX in controlling the flow stress and  grain size. Our results shed new light on understanding the DRX mechanism in tuning  grain size and provide new avenues for optimizing the forging process of nickel base  superalloy.
