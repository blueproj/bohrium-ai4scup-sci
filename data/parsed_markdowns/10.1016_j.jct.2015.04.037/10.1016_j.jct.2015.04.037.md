# Determination and correlation of the solubility of tricin in  water and ethanol mixtures  

Yan Cheng\*, Zhenhua Wang, Shangzhi Ding, Jingchao Li  Shandong Analysis and Test Centre, Shandong Academy of Sciences, 250014 Jinan, China  

ABSTRACT:  The solubility of tricin in water and ethanol mixtures was measured over the  temperature range of (288.15 to 328.15) K. The concentrations of tricin in the aqueous mixtures  were assayed by the ultraviolet spec tro photometric method. The experimental solubility data  indicated that the solubility of tricin increases with an increase in temperature and an enrichment  in ethanol content. The two models, including the modified Apelblat equation and  λ h equation  were used to correlate the experimental solubility data. The calculated solubility of tricin shows  good agreement with the experimental results. Additionally, the estimation of thermodynamic  properties including the activity coefficients, dissolution enthalpy, and entropy were obtained  from the experimental data. Within the studied temperature range the dissolution process of tricin  is endothermic, and the driving force is the entropy.  

Keywords: Tricin; Solubility; Determination; Correlation  

# 1. Introduction  

The flavonoids are naturally occurring phenolic compounds and are widely studied in food and  pharmacological industries. These compounds have anti-allergic, antimicrobial, antioxidant and  anticancer effects [1-3]. Tricin, with the formula C H O (CAS 520-32-1), is a type of flavonoid  17 14 7  and the chemical structure is shown in figure 1. The compound is one of the most important  active ingredients in the human diet [4-6]. The solubility of chemicals in different solvents is  important physicochemical parameters in the separation and purification processes [7,8]. The  systematic study of the solubility in liquid solvents has been growing [9-13], however, there is  very little data on the measurement of solubility of tricin. To determine the proper solvent and  design an optimized production process, it is necessary to investigate the solubility of tricin in  water and ethanol.

In this work, the solubility of tricin in water and ethanol mixtures was measured from  (288.15 to 328.15) K using a UV spec tro photometric method. The experimental solubility data  were correlated with the modified Apelblat equation [14,15] and  λ h equation [16]. Meanwhile,  the thermodynamic properties of the system were calculated based on the experimental data. The  experimental results can be used in the crystallization and purification processes of tricin.

# 2.2. Experimental  

The solubility experiments were carried out using the analytical isothermal shake-flask  method. The water temperature was controlled by a thermostat within (± 0.1 K) that was  provided from a constant-temperature water bath. For each measurement, an excess of tricin was  added to a known volume of solvent. Then the sample was stirred for 6 h. The preliminary  experiments proved that 6 h are enough to saturate the system. When the system came to saturate,  the stirrer was turned off. And later, 5.0 mL samples of the saturated liquid phase were collected  using plastic syringes coupled with polypropylene filters (0.22 µm) which were previously  heated in order to avoid any precipitation. A known volume of filtrate was appropriately diluted  with ethanol for ultraviolet analysis.  

The calibration curve was prepared in ethanol and used for quantification of tricin. The  maximum absorption wavelength was set at 352 nm. The solubility was expressed as mole  fraction and calculated as moles of solute to the sum of moles of solute and solvents. The  solubility of tricin in water and ethanol was very low. # 3.2.  Thermodynamic functions of solution  

In this work, the van’t Hoff equation for real solutions was used to calculate the molar  enthalpy and the molar entropy of fusion of the solute. In fact, most real solutions show  non-ideal behaviour. Thus, the solvent effect should be thought, and the enthalpy and entropy of  the mixtures should be taken into consideration through replacing enthalpy of fusion with  enthalpy of dissolution and entropy of fusion with entropy of dissolution. A description of the  model and equations can be found elsewhere [11].

# 4. Conclusions  

The solubility of tricin in (water + ethanol) mixtures was measured at temperatures from (288.15  to 318.15) K. Raising the temperature increased the solubility of tricin; at the same time, at  constant temperature, the solubility of tricin increased with increasing mole fraction of ethanol.  The measured values are well-correlated by the modified Apelblat equation and the  λ h equation,  and the calculated solubility shows good agreement with the experimental values. On the basis of  the above results, the dissolution enthalpy and entropy of tricin was calculated and the  dissolution of tricin is endothermic and is an entropy-driving course. Overall, our study offered  some information for the purification or extraction of tricin in the industry and could be used for  industrial design.
