Summary.  P2X purinoreceptors are a family of ligand-gated ion channels that are activated by ATP. To date, seven members of this family have been cloned, comprising P2X1 − 7. These channels can exist as homomers and heteromers. Among the P2X family members, in particular the P2X3 receptor has been recognized as an important mediator of nociception. It is mainly expressed in dorsal root ganglia in a subset of nociceptive sensory neurons. During inflammation the expression of the P2X3 receptor is increased, and activation of P2X3 receptor has been described to sensitize peripheral nerves. P2X3 receptor antagonists have been shown to act as anti-nociceptives in different models of pain and inflammatory pain.  

Besides its prominent role in nociception and in pain-related diseases involving both chronic and acute pain, the P2X3 receptor has been shown to be involved in genitourinary, gastrointestinal, and respiratory conditions including overactive bladder and chronic cough. Compounds blocking both the exclusively P2X3 subunit containing ion channel (P2X3 homomer) as well as the ion channel composed of P2X2 and P2X3 subunits (P2X2/3 heterotrimer) are called P2X3 and P2X2/3 nonselective receptor antagonists.  

The present application describes a series of novel P2X3 inhibitors for the treatment of neurogenic disorders, such as gynecological disorders, urinary tract disorders, respiratory disorders, and pain-associated disorders. Further, the applica- tion discloses compounds, their preparation, use, and pharmaceutical composition, and treatment.
