# Fluorine substituted benzothiazole-based low bandgap polymers for photovoltaic applications  

Lu Xiao,   Bo Liu,   Xuewen Chen,   Yongfang Li,   Wanjun Tang a   and Yingping Zou\* ad  

Two new D–A alternating copolymers, PBDFFBT and PBDTFBT, featuring a fluorinated benzothiazole (BT) electron-acceptor unit and the electron-donor segments of benzo[1,2- b :4,5- b 9 ]difuran (BDF) or benzo[1,2- b :4,5- b 9 ]dithiophene (BDT), were prepared by a standard Stille cross-coupling reaction. The resulting copolymers were characterized by TGA, GPC, UV-vis absorption spectroscopy and cyclic voltammetry measurements. PBDFFBT and PBDTFBT show good film forming properties. Bulk heterojunction polymer photovoltaic devices based on an active layer of the electron donor copolymers (PBDFFBT and PBDTFBT), blended with an electron acceptor [6,6]-phenyl-C 71  butyric acid methyl ester (PC 71 BM) in three weight ratios of 1 : 1, 1 : 2 and 1 : 3 were investigated. The devices with the configuration of ITO/PEDOT:PSS/ polymer:PC 71 BM/Ca/Al demonstrated promising power conversion efficiencies of 3.3% for PBDFFBT:PC 71 BM (1 : 3,  w / w ) and 4.0% for PBDTFBT:PC 71 BM (1 : 2,  w / w ) under the illumination of AM 1.5, 100 mW cm 2 2 .

# Introduction  

Bulk heterojunction polymer solar cells (PSCs),   which are composed of a photoactive layer of a p-type conjugated polymer donor (such as P3HT) and an n-type soluble fullerene derivative acceptor (such as PCBM),   have been a vigorous research topic from both the industry and academic commu- nities for photovoltaics over the past decade because of their outstanding features such as low-cost, light-weight, flexibility, easy processability over a large-area size  via  printing and their great potential application for the realization of flexible devices over a large area.   Inspiring success has been achieved over the past few years in polymer solar cells. Recently, Wu and his co-workers have reported a PCE over 9% for PTB7 (thieno[3,4- b ]thiophene/benz odi thi oph ene) using an inverted device structure. However, it is still low for applications when compared with inorganic solar cells which have been commercialized widely. When it comes to realize such applications, there is still a long way to develop excellent materials and suitable device designs.  

To obtain high performance PSCs, it is very critical to develop low bandgap polymers with broad absorption, deep- lying HOMO energy levels and high hole mobility.   The most

# Experimental  

# Materials  

Pd(PPh 3 ) 4  and 3,4-diamino-1-fluorobenzene ( 1 ) were obtained from Alfa Asia Chemical Co, and they were used without further purification. Toluene was dried over molecular sieves and freshly distilled prior to use. Other reagents and solvents were purchased commercially as analytical-grade quality and used without further purification. Column chromatography was carried out on silica gel (size: 200–300 mesh). Tributyl(4- dodecylthiophen-2-yl)stannane ( 4 ),   5-fluorobenzo[ c ][1,2,5]thiadiazole ( 2 ), 4,7dibromo-5-fluorobenzo[ c ][1,2,5]thiadiazole ( 3 ) and 2,6-bis(tri- methyltin)-4,8-di(2-ethylhexyloxyl)benzo[1,2- b :3,4- b 9 ]dithiophene M2 were synthesized according to the reported literatures.   2,6-Bis- (trimethyltin)-4,8-bis(2-ethylhexyloxy)benzo[1,2- b :3,4- b 9 ]difuran (M1) was synthesized according to our previous report.   All of the other compounds were synthesized following the procedures described herein.  

# Characterization  

1 H NMR spectra were recorded using a Bruker AV-400 spectrometer in deuterated chloroform solution at 298 K, unless specified otherwise. Chemical shifts were reported as  d values (ppm) with tetra methyl si lane (TMS) as the internal reference. Molecular weight and polydispersity of the polymers were determined by gel permeation chromatography (GPC) analysis with polystyrene as standard (Waters 515 HPLC pump, a Waters 2414 differential refractometer, and three Waters Styragel columns (HT2, HT3, and HT4)) using THF (HPLC grade) as eluent at a flow rate of 1.0 mL min 2 1   at 35  u C. Thermo grav i metric analysis (TGA) was conducted on a Perkin- Elmer TGA-7 with a heating rate of 20 K min 2 1   under a nitrogen atmosphere. UV-Vis absorption spectra were recorded on a Shimadzu UV-2450 spectrophotometer. For the solid state measurements, a polymer solution in chloroform was spin- coated onto quartz plates. The cyclic voltammetry was recorded with a computer controlled Zahner IM6e electro- chemical workstation using polymer films on a carbon electrode (1.0 cm 2 ) as the working electrode, a platinum wire as the counter electrode and Ag/AgCl (0.1 M) as the reference electrode in an anhydrous and argon-saturated solution of 0.1 M tetra but yl ammonium hex a flu oro phosphate (Bu 4 NPF 6 ) in acetonitrile at a scanning rate of 50 mV s 2 1 . Electrochemical onsets were determined at the position where the current starts to differ from the baseline. The morphology of the polymer/PC 71 BM blend films was investigated by a SPI 3800N atomic force microscope (AFM) in contacting mode with a 5 m m scanner.  

# Fabrication and characterization of polymer solar cells  

The PSCs were fabricated in the configuration of the traditional sandwich structure with an indium tin oxide (ITO) glass positive electrode and a Ca/Al negative electrode. Patterned ITO glass with a sheet resistance of 15–20  V / %  was purchased from CSG Holding Co. Ltd. (China). The ITO glass was cleaned by sequential ultrasonic treatment in detergent, deionized water, acetone, and isopropanol, and then treated in an ultraviolet-ozone chamber (Ultraviolet Ozone Cleaner, Jelight Company, USA) for 25 min. Then PEDOT:PSS

 (poly(3,4-ethylene dioxythiophene):poly(styrene sulfonate))

 (Baytron PVP Al 4083, Germany) was filtered through a 0.45 m m poly(tetra flu oro ethylene) (PTFE) filter and spin coated at 2000 rpm for 40 s onto the ITO substrate. Subsequently, the PEDOT:PSS film was baked at 150  u C for 15 min in the air, and the thickness of the PEDOT:PSS layer is about 40 nm. The polymers and PC 71 BM (10 mg mL 2 1   for polymers) were dissolved in  ortho -dichlorobenzene (ODCB) overnight, and spin-cast at 2000 rpm for 45 s onto the PEDOT:PSS layer. The thickness of the photoactive layer is about 150 nm measured by a Ambios Technology XP-2 profilometer. A bilayer cathode consisting of Ca ( y 20 nm) capped with Al ( y 80 nm) was thermally evaporated under a shadow mask with a base pressure of  ca.  10 2 5   Pa. The active area of the PSCs is 4 mm 2 . Device characterization was carried out under AM 1.5G irradiation with the intensity of 100 mW cm 2 2   (Oriel 67 005, 500 W), calibrating by a standard silicon cell.  J – V  curves were recorded with a Keithley 236 digital source meter. A xenon lamp with AM 1.5 filter was used as the white light source and the optical power was 100 mW cm 2 2 . The  EQE  measurements of the PSCs were performed using a Stanford Systems model SR830 DSP lock-in amplifier coupled with a WDG3 mono- chromator and 500 W xenon lamp. A calibrated silicon detector was used to determine the absolute photo sensitivity at different wavelengths. All of these fabrications and characterizations after the cleaning of the ITO substrates were conducted in a glove box.

# 5-Fluorobenzo[ c ][1,2,5]thiadiazole (2)  

3,4-Diamino-1-fluorobenzene ( 1 ) (2.52 g, 20 mmol), chloro- form (250 mL) and triethylamine (10.1 g, 14 mL) were put into a three-necked round-bottom flask. Under an argon atmo- sphere, thionyl chloride (3.2 mL, 44 mmol) was added dropwise  via  a syringe. The mixed solution was stirred at 80 u C overnight and then cooled to room temperature and to the reaction mixture was added 150 mL deionized water. The solution was washed several times with water. The organic phase was collected and dried over magnesium sulfate, then filtered. The solvents were removed in vacuum and the product was used in the next step without further purification.

# Synthesis of PBDFFBT  

4,7-Bis(5-bromo-4-dodecylthiophen-2-yl)-5-fluorobenzo[ c ][1,2,5] thiadiazole ( 6 ) (162.6 mg, 0.2 mmol) and 2,6-bis(trimethyltin)- 4,8-bis(2-ethylhexyloxy)benzo[1,2- b :3,4- b 9 ]difuran (M1) (148.0 mg, 0.2 mmol) were dissolved in 10 mL of dry toluene. The solution was flushed with Ar for 20 min and then Pd(PPh 3 ) 4 (20 mg, 0.016 mmol) was added into the flask. The flask was purged three times with successive vacuum and argon filling cycles. Then the oil bath was heated to 110  u C carefully, and the mixture was stirred for two days at this temperature under argon atmosphere. Then the reaction mixture was cooled to room temperature and then poured into methanol (100 mL) slowly. The resulting precipitate was filtered through a Soxhlet thimble, which was then subjected to Soxhlet extractions with methanol, hexane and chloroform. Finally the polymer was recovered as a solid from the chloroform fraction by rotary evaporation. The brown solid was dried under vacuum at 40  u C overnight (156 mg, yield: 51%).  

# Synthesis of PBDTFBT  

PBDTFBT was synthesized as a tan solid with a yield of 23% according to the method of PBDFFBT described above.

# Photovoltaic properties  

In order to investigate and compare the photovoltaic proper- ties of the two copolymers, PSCs with a sandwich structure of ITO/PEDOT:PSS/polymer:PC 71 BM/Ca/Al were fabricated. The polymer active layers were spin-coated from an  ortho -dichlor- obenzene ( o -DCB) solution with different concentrations (polymer : PC 71 BM = 1 : 1, 1 : 2 and 1 : 3;  w / w ). Fig. 5a shows the typical current density–voltage ( J – V ) curves of the PSCs based on polymers:PC 71 BM blend under the illumination of AM 1.5G, 100 mW cm 2 2 . The corresponding results of the devices are listed in Table 3.  J sc ,  V oc  and FF show little difference between PBDFFBT and PBDTFBT-based solar cells; a high  J sc  comes from strong absorbance and good charge transport in the active layer. The relatively higher  V oc  is from the deep-lying HOMO level compared to that of P3HT. Although having very similar HOMO levels, the polymers exhibit 0.1 V difference of  V oc , which may be from their blend morphology or interchain stacking because  V oc  has been affected by many factors besides the HOMO level of the polymers.   A high FF shows a balanced transport in the PSCs. The photovoltaic properties are affected by many parameters especially by the donor–acceptor (D–A) ratio. # Morphology  

The morphology of the photoactive layer is also one important parameter for PSCs, in some cases the performance of the devices strongly depends on its morphological features. Therefore, we used atomic force microscopy (AFM) to investigate the surface morphology of the active layer by spin-coating blends onto an ITO glass similar to the prepara- tion of the active layer in the photovoltaic devices. AFM topographic and phase images are shown in Fig. 6. The surface rms (root mean-square) roughness of the polymer:PC 71 BM films (1 : 2,  w / w ) are 7.86 nm and 3.08 nm for PBDFFBT and PBDTFBT, respectively. From Fig. 6, we can see that PBDTFBT shows a better phase separation. For the PBDFFBT:PC 71 BM blend, Fig. 6A shows that the domains are not distributed very well throughout the surface, revealing a little aggregation of one component (PBDFFBT or PC 71 BM), which maybe influ- ences the formation of the inter penetrating bicontinuous network between the polymer donor and the PC 71 BM which then affects the charge generation and transportation.   For the PBDTFBT:PC 71 BM blend, the blend films are smoother than those of PBDFFBT:PC 71 BM. The rough surface and coarse phase separation of PBDFFBT:PC 71 BM, at least in part, explain the relatively lower PCE and hole mobility.

# Conclusions  

In summary, we have synthesized two new D–A conjugated polymers containing a fluorinated benzodithiazole (BT) electron acceptor unit and the electron donor segments of benzo[1,2- b :4,5- b 9 ]difuran (BDF) or benzo[1,2- b :4,5- b 9 ]dithio- phene (BDT), by Stille cross-coupling polymerization reactions. The synthesized copolymers demonstrated good absorption in the visible region and high hole mobility. Preliminary photovoltaic results using the blend of PBDTFBT and PC 71 BM (1 : 2,  w / w ) as the active layer afforded a PCE up to 4% with a  V oc  of 0.72 V,  J sc  of 9.31 mA cm 2 2   and a FF of 0.6. For PBDFFBT blended with PC 71 BM (1 : 3,  w / w ) as the active layer, the PCE reaches up to 3.3%. It is believed that the photovoltaic properties can be further improved by modifications to the polymer backbone and different device processing conditions such as a different blend ratios, additives and thermal annealing  etc .
