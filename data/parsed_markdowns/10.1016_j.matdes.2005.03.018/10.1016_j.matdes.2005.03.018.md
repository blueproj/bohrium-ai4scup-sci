# Microstructure of Al 2 O 3  nanocrystalline/cobalt-based alloy composite coatings by laser deposition  

Mingxi Li   \* , Yizhu He, Xiaomin Yuan, Shihong Zhang  

Research Center for Laser Processing, School of Materials Engineering, Anhui University of Technology, 59 Hudong Road, Maanshan 243002, China  

Received 25 November 2004; accepted 23 March 2005 Available online 17 June 2005  

# Abstract  

Composite coatings, made of nano-Al 2 O 3 /cobalt-based alloy, produced by a 5-kW CO 2  laser on Ni-based superalloy were inves- tigated. The coatings were examined to reveal their microstructure using optical microscope, scanning electron microscope, trans- mission electron microscope and X-ray diﬀraction instrument. The results showed that some equilibrium or non-equilibrium phases, such as  c -Co, Cr 23 C 6 , CoAl 2 O 4 , Al 2 O 3  and  e -Co existed in the coatings. Fine and short dendritic microstructure and columnar to equiaxed transition occurred by adding nano-Al 2 O 3  particle. With the increase of nano-materials (1%, mass fraction), fully equiaxed crystallization appeared. These were contributed to that nano-Al 2 O 3  particles acted as new nucleation site and rapid solidiﬁcation of the melted pool. The results also showed inhomogeneous dispersion of nano-Al 2 O 3  that resulted in the formation of  e -Co phase in the coatings. The sub-microstructure of the clad was stacking fault. The mechanism of formation of equiaxed grains was also analyzed.

# 1. Introduction  

Nanocrystalline materials have strong characteristics of acoustic, optic, electric, magnetic and thermodynam- ics because of their quantum size eﬀect, surface eﬀect, etc. The properties of mechanic and environment service of materials can be obviously improved by nano surface engineering  [1] . At the present time, nano materials have been found many applications, such as mechanical alloying and bulk materials of oxide dispersion strength- ened (ODS) superalloy prepared by powder metallurgy [2–4] . On the surface engineering, there have been some primitive research reports on spraying  [5,6]  and laser surface alloying  [7,8] , etc. The technical research in laser cladding is gradually perfected, but the application of this technology is not widely available. The main reason is unavoidable cracks or other defects in cladding layer, especially in multi-tracks/layers coatings  [9] . By apply- ing nanocrystalline material into the alloy powder, changing the solidiﬁcation microstructure of the laser pool, a new trial can prepare composite coatings and avoid cracks.  

This paper investigates microstructure of Al 2 O 3 nanocrystalline/cobalt-based alloy composite coatings. Eﬀects of nano-Al 2 O 3  on microstructure, phase trans- formation and dispersion of nano particles in the coat- ings are analyzed.

dimension of 100  ·  100  ·  10 mm to form 0.8–1.0 mm thick powder bed. Then, put it into the furnace heating to 393 K for 2 h. Chemical compositions of the substrate and the powder are shown in  Table 1 .  

The equipment used for cladding is a 5 kW TJ-HL- T5000 type continuous wave CO 2  laser system. The laser beam was directed onto the surface of the substrate and a melt pool was created. Argon was used for shrouding the melting pool region to prevent oxidation during the process. By the relative movement between the laser beam and the substrate, a pool of molten coating metal covered the substrate to form a solidiﬁed metallic layer, which fused to the substrate. In each track, laser power was turned oﬀat the end of each pass to the new starting position, so that there was an overlap rate of approxi- mately 30% between tracks. Cladding parameters were kept constant for all tracks and given as follows: 2.5 kW laser power, 250-mm min  1   scanning speed and 5 mm spot diameter.  

Cross section of the coatings were observed to reveal their microstructure using OLYMPUS PME-3 type optical microscope, PHILIP-XL30 type scanning elec- tron microscope (SEM), XD-3A type X-ray diﬀractom- eter (XRD) and H 800 type transmission electron microscope (TEM).  

# 3. Results and analysis  

Fig. 1  shows the XRD patterns of the nano-Al 2 O 3 powder ( Fig. 1 (a)) and the clad ( Fig. 1 (b)).  Fig. 1 (a) sug- gests that the phase of the nano particles is  c -Al 2 O 3 . The primary phases of the composite coatings are  c -Co and e -Co, which appear strong intensity. Other phases, such as Cr 23 C 6 , Al 2 O 3  and CoAl 2 O 4  show weaker intensity because their amount is much less in the coatings. The reasons are less amount of additives, loss of nano- Al 2 O 3  attaching in the container by grinding and splash- ing during laser cladding process.  

Usually,  c -Co and Cr 23 C 6  are the main phases in Co- based alloy laser coatings  [10] . Equilibrium phase  e -Co with hcp structure appears in the composite coatings. This may be lower thermal conductivity of nano-Al 2 O 3 particles that results in lower cooling rate at least locally and urges the formation of  e -Co phase.

# 4. Conclusions  

By adding nano-Al 2 O 3  particles, there existed some equilibrium or non-equilibrium phases, such as  c -Co, Cr 23 C 6 , CoAl 2 O 4 , Al 2 O 3  and  e -Co in the composite coatings. Fine and short dendritic microstructure and columnar to equiaxed transition (CET) occurred and fully equiaxed crystallization appeared when nano- Al 2 O 3  particles amounted to 1% (mass fraction). Inho- mogeneous dispersion of nano-Al 2 O 3  resulted in the for- mation of  e -Co. The sub-microstructure of the clad was stacking fault. Crack free coatings were obtained.
