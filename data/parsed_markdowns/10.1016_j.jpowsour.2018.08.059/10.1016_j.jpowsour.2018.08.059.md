# H I G H L I G H T S  

•   A dual-salt electrolyte composed of LiPO 2 F 2  and LiFSI is proposed for LMBs.

 •   The electrolyte shows high ionic conductivity and wide electrochemical windows.

 •   Improved cyclic stability and high coulombic e ﬃ ciency have been achieved.

 •   Low voltage polarization and dendrite-free SEI layer have been observed.  

# A R T I C L E I N F O  

# A B S T R A C T  

Metallic lithium, with super high theoretical speci ﬁ c capacity, enjoys great potential as an anode for re- chargeable batteries. However, safety issues caused by lithium dendrite growth as well as limited cycling life restrict its practical application. Herein we validate that the application of a new dual-salt electrolyte composed of Lithium di ﬂ uorophosphate (LiPO 2 F 2 ) and lithium bis( ﬂ uorosulfony)imide (LiFSI) in the same proportion enables the improved long-term cycling performance along with high coulombic e ﬃ ciency for lithium metal batteries. With 1 M LiPO 2 F 2  and 1 M LiFSI in 1,2-dimethoxyethane as the electrolyte, a Li/Cu cell can be cycled at 1 mA for more than 300 cycles with a deposited capacity of 1.0 mAh. It also displays that a homogeneous and dendrite free solid electrolyte interphase layer is generated on the Cu electrode, which can be ascribed to the favorable stability of LiPO 2 F 2  and high ionic conductivity of LiFSI. These results deliver perceptions for the advancement of high-performance lithium metal anode.

# 1. Introduction  

Lithium-based batteries have been indispensable parts as energy supply from portable electronic equipment to hybrid electric vehicles for decades. Unfortunately, despite the energy density of currently used graphite anodes in lithium ion batteries has been optimized near to the theoretical value, it is still unable to satisfy the needs of high energy storage systems [ 1 – 3 ]. To cope with this challenge, lithium metal has been reinvestigated in recent years as a noteworthy anode material owing to its ultrahigh theoretical speci ﬁ c capacity (3860 mAh g − 1 ), low density (0.59 g cm − 3 ) and the most negative electrochemical potential ( − 3.040 V vs standard hydrogen electrode) [ 4 , 5 ]. However, the ex- tensive applications of lithium metal anodes have been blocked by two major obstacles. One is the undesirable safety threats result from the  

Over the last four decades, numerous researchers have carefully analyzed the failure mechanisms of lithium metal batteries and con- sidered various strategies in view of these issues, such as structured anode [ 10 , 11 ], electrolyte additive [ 12 – 14 ], solid electrolytes [ 15 , 16 ], arti ﬁ cial solid-electrolyte interphase (SEI) [ 17 , 18 ] and super con- centrated electrolyte [ 19 , 20 ] etc. Among these approaches, the role of the electrolyte is a critical factor for stabilizing the lithium metal sur- face. Aurbach et al. [ 21 ] once validated that lithium was highly ther- modynamic instable with most organic solvents and thus lead to con- tinuous electrolyte decomposition, forming a SEI  ﬁ lm on the electrode. During the repeated Li deposition/extraction processes, the large volume change of lithium metal will induce heterogeneity and cracks on the SEI layer, which exposes fresh lithium metal to the electrolyte again. The SEI layer becomes thicker as the constant breakage and re- paration processes, causing capacity degradation and uncontrollable lithium dendrites. In general, the component of the SEI layer are gen- erated by the decomposition of the solvent and Li salt of electrolyte. The SEI layers formed in electrolytes with solvents like ethylene (EC), 1,2- dimethoxyethane (DME), polycarbonate (PC), diethyl carbonate (DEC), 1 – 3 dioxolane (DOL), tetrahydrofuran (THF), etc. [ 22 – 25 ], and lithium salts like lithium hexa ﬂ uorophosphate (LiPF 6 ), Lithium Tetra- ﬂ uoroborate (LiBF 4 ), lithium perchlorate (LiClO 4 ), lithium hexa- ﬂ uoroarsenate (LiAsF 6 ), etc. [ 26 – 29 ] have been extensively studied and tremendous impressive achievements have been made. There has re- cently been a demand to  ﬁ nd out new lithium salts, which can form a high-quality SEI layer through the growth of more  ‘‘ adaptable ’’  de- composition products for a safer and more e ﬃ cient lithium metal bat- tery.  

Most recently, our groups [ 30 ] found that the cyclic stability of li- thium-based batteries can be signi ﬁ cantly enhanced with Li x PO y F z contained in the SEI layer. We utilized the Lithium di ﬂ uorophosphate (LiPO 2 F 2 ) as electrolyte additive to optimize compositions in the SEI layer, which improved the cyclability of LIBs with both LiCoO2 and graphite electrodes. However, LiPO 2 F 2  is not appropriate for electrolyte salt alone due to its low ionic conductivity. In view of that another salt lithium bis( ﬂ uorosulfony)imide (LiFSI) shows high ionic conductivity and great compatibility with lithium metal [ 19 , 31 ], the combination of LiPO 2 F 2  and LiFSI as new electrolyte salts have a promising application for improved cyclic performance [ 31 – 35 ].  

Herein, we reported a dual-salt electrolyte consisting of LiPO 2 F 2 together with LiFSI in 1,2-dimethoxyethane solvent for the application of LMBs. We  ﬁ rstly characterized the electrochemical properties of Li/ Cu cells in the electrolytes with a single LiPO 2 F 2  and LiFSI salt re- spectively. The cell with the single LiPO 2 F 2  salt demonstrated a favor- able cyclic stability but large voltage polarization, while the battery with LiFSI salt displayed a poor cycle life but stable voltage pro ﬁ le. Based on the complementarity of these two electrolyte salts, binary-salt electrolytes with di ﬀ erent concentrations of LiPO 2 F 2  and LiFSI were designed to realize small polarization and long cycle stability. The optimized dual-salt electrolyte was constructive for a dendrite-free SEI layer, and the coulombic e ﬃ ciency of cells could attain > 99% for over 180 cycles at 0.5 mA. The possible function mechanism behind the impressive performance was explored and discussed.  

# 2. Experimental section  

# 2.1. Materials  

All the basic materials for experiments are in battery-grade purity. Lithium bis( ﬂ uorosulfonyl)imide (LiFSI) and 1,2-dime th oxy methane

 (DME) were purchased from Qianmin Chemical Reagents Co. Ltd

 (Jiangsu, China). Lithium di ﬂ uorophosphate (LiPO 2 F 2 ) was purchased from Sigma. The electrolytes were achieved by mixing the needed amount of salts (LiPO 2 F 2 /LiFSI) with the solvent (DME) in an inert- atmosphere glove box (MBRAUN LABmaster, Shanghai, China). The contents of oxygen and water were controlled under 1 ppm. The cell performance test was investigated using CR-2032 coin cells, which contain a Li foil and a Cu foil as counter electrode and cathode, re- spectively. The Cu foil needed to be soaked in 1 M HCl for 10 min to remove the impurities [ 30 ], and then be rinsed for three times with ethanol and deionized water. Finally, the washed Cu foil was moved to the glove box after drying in a vacuum.

# 3. Results and discussion  

# 3.1. Electrochemical performance of LiPO 2 F 2 /DME and LiFSI/DME electrolytes  

Li/Cu two-electrode coin cells with di ﬀ erent concentrated electro- lytes of LiPO 2 F 2 /DME from 1 M to 4 M were used to examine the cou- lombic e ﬃ ciency and cyclic stability.  Figure S1  shows the coulombic e ﬃ ciency-cycle number and voltage-time curves of Li/Cu cells con- taining electrolytes with di ﬀ erent concentrations of LiPO 2 F 2 /DME, all tested at a current of 0.5 mA and a lithium deposition capacity of 1 mAh. After the activated process, the coulombic e ﬃ ciency becomes stable, approximating 98.5%, much higher than those in conventional electrolytes system. The cells with di ﬀ erent concentrated electrolytes from 1 M to 4 M deliver cyclic stability of 150cylces (620 h), 158 cycles (628 h), 246 cycles (800 h) and 300 cycles (892 h) respectively, which shows that the cycling life of the cells with LiPO 2 F 2 /DME electrolytes increase with increasing concentration of the electrolytes. It can be also observed from  Figure S2  that the SEI layer formed on the Cu substrates is more stable and more uniform with increasing concentration of the electrolytes after 150 cycles. However, serious voltage polarization is observed from the voltage pro ﬁ les ( Figure S1e ), leading to large in- terfacial resistance and capacity degradation. In general, LiPO 2 F 2  in electrolyte can bring amelioration to the cyclic stability, but also large voltage polarization of the cells.  

The electrochemical properties of LiFSI/DME electrolyte were also characterized with the Li/Cu two-electrode coin cells ( Figure S3 ). The performance of low concentrated LiFSI/DME degrade very fast for only 50cylces (200 h) with 1 M and 100 cycles (400 h) with 2 M at a current of 0.5 mA and a constant lithium deposition capacity of 1 mAh. But the ionic conductivity of LiFSI outperforms LiPO 2 F 2  in DME based elec- trolyte at di ﬀ erent temperatures ( Table 1 ), which is favorable for the decrease of interfacial resistance.

Moreover, good high-rate per- formance and small voltage polarization can be achieved in the dual- salt electrolyte. When cycled at 1 mA and a constant lithium deposition capacity of 1.0 mAh, the cell with (1 M LiFSI+1 M LiPO 2 F 2 )/DME electrolyte can be stable for over 300 cycles, indicating great high-rate stability. # 3.3. Characterization of the SEI layer  

To explore the possible function principle behind the long cy- clability of the cell with dual-salt electrolyte, SEM and XPS were con- ducted to study the morphology and ingredient of SEI layer on the Cu substrate during the lithium deposition/stripping processes.  Fig. 4 a – d presents the surface morphologies of lithium metal plated on the Cu substrates with the electrolyte of (0.5 M LiFSI+0.5 M LiPO 2 F 2 )/DME at 0.5 mA and a constant lithium deposition capacity of 1.0 mAh after 1st, 50th, 100th and 150th cycles respectively. It can be  ﬁ gured out that the Cu foil is covered with a thin layer of white and grained material after the  ﬁ rst cycle, guessed to be irreversible lithium deposits and products formed in side reactions since the  ﬁ rst coulombic e ﬃ ciency is 85.3% < 100%. After 50 cycles, the layer is thickened as the con- tinuous deposition of lithium, which can be ascribed to the low cou- lombic e ﬃ ciency in the early cycles. About 100 cycles later, the layer on the Cu substrate becomes denser and the color turns grey, which indicates that a stable SEI layer is forming. When cycled for 150 cycles, a uniform and compact SEI  ﬁ lm has formed on the Cu substrate visible to the naked eye. A smooth surface of SEI  ﬁ lm without the dendrite formation can be further observed from  Fig. 4 e and f. The dense SEI layer can lower the growth of dead lithium and minimize side reaction, which contributes to high coulombic e ﬃ ciency and long-term cyclic stability of lithium metal battery with (0.5 M LiFSI+0.5 M LiPO 2 F 2 )/ DME binary-salt electrolyte.

# 4. Conclusions  

We have demonstrated that long term cycling stability with high coulombic e ﬃ ciency can be achieved with a LiPO 2 F 2 -LiFSI/DME dual- salt electrolyte in Li/Cu cell. The de ﬁ ciencies in serious voltage polar- ization and inferior coulombic e ﬃ ciency with individual LiPO 2 F 2  or LiFSI salt in low concentration can be ameliorated by combining two salts. Although the ionic conductivity of the LiPO 2 F 2 /DME is far below LiFSI/DME, the ionic conductivity of the dual-salt electrolyte is about 10 mS cm − 1   at 25 °C, which is adequate for the practical application of electrolyte. During cycling processes, the LiPO 2 F 2  in the electrolyte will deposit on the Cu substrate to form a uniform and compact SEI layer, which can inhibit the formation of lithium dendrite and increase the cycle life. In addition, the voltage pro ﬁ le of dual-salt electrolyte is highly stable for 150 cycles owing to the high ionic conductivity of LiFSI, which can supply enough mobile Li ion for the cycling processes and reduce the interfacial resistance of the cells. This work validates an optimized electrolyte system for safe and high-performance lithium metal anode, which provides new strategies for next-generation high energy systems.
