Creep    tests of a directionally solidiﬁed (DS)    Ni-base    superalloy specimens containing local recrystalliza- tion (RX) were   carried   out   at 980   ◦ C/235 MPa    and the inﬂuence of the    characteristic of    RX grain    boundary on the    formation of creep    cracks was    investigated. The RX grain    boundary morphology, misorientation and dislocation pattern at high, low angle and twin   boundaries during   creep   were   examined. It was shown that only    a few    high    angle    grain boundaries (GBs),    but not    all    of    them    cracked during    creep and even    after ruptured. This    is    associated with    the    dislocation mobility at GBs    or the    morphology of GBs. The    possibility of crack    formation was    discussed based    on    the    transmission behavior of    dislocations at different GBs.

# 1. Introduction  

Recrystallization (RX) in DS blades caused either by the residual stresses or by the plastic deformation generated during manufac- turing is a well-known issue in the investment casting industry. The effects of applied stress [1] ,    heat treatment parameters [2,3] , heat treatment atmosphere [4] ,    as well as the microstructural features such as residual eutectics [5] and carbides [6] on RX behaviors in several DS superalloys have been reported.  

The aim of our present paper is to study the effect of the char- acteristic of RX GBs on the formation mechanism of cracks during high temperature creep of a DS superalloy, and try to reveal the microstructural evolution during creep.  

It is generally accepted that RX may reduce the mechanical prop- erties of DS superalloys [7–10] . For example, less than half of the creep rupture life of DZ22 alloy can be achieved when there was a surface RX layer induced by shot-peening [7] .   Similar data were also reported by Khan et al. [8] .    Besides the RX layer induced by shot-peening, local RX showed similar detrimental effect on the creep rupture life of DS superalloys [9,10] .

# 4. Discussion  

The present study indicates that formation of grain boundary cracks is related to the characteristic of RX grain boundary. This can be explained by the behavior of dislocations at RX GBs.  

As is well known, dislocation slip occurs when the applied stress is in excess of the critical resolved shear stress, while the resolved shear stress mainly depends on Schmid factor. In order to under- stand the mechanism of crack formation, Schmid factors of several neighbor grains with cracked GBs are calculated. The results are shown in Table 2 . Only those slip systems which exhibit Schmid factors more than 0.4 are listed in Table 2 (marked as boldface). One can see that more slip systems may be generated in the origi- nal DS material. On the other hand, only one or two slip systems are with Schmid factor more than 0.4 in RX grains. Therefore, disloca- tion density in original DS material is higher than that in RX grains, as observed in Figs. 5 and 7 .  

If dislocations do not transmit across or be absorbed, they will pile-up at the grain boundary. The dislocation pile-up and diffusion of vacancy along RX grain boundary eventually led to the forma- tion of micro-voids and cracks [18,19] . As shown in Fig. 10 [20,21] , dislocation transmission across grain boundary generally requires climb and rotation from the incoming to the emitting slip plane [22] . Therefore, dislocation transmission is related to the angle ˛ between the line direction of the incoming and emitted disloca- tions, that is the intersection angle between intersections of slip

By comparing the active slip systems of RX grain with those of original DS material shown in Table 2 , one can ﬁnd that three slip systems, ( 1  1 1) [101], ( 1  1 1) [ 1 1 0], and ( 1  1 1) [011] possess the same slip plane. In this case, dislocation transmission at GB is not easy due to the large misorientation angle (large ˛ ). If slip systems of RX grain and original DS material do not possess the same slip plane, the superimposition of GB misorientation and angle between slip planes must be considered. In most cases, high angle GBs lead to large ˛ and are vulnerable sites for crack formation. However, ˛ angle could be very small even if the GB misorientation is large. This can explain why some GBs kept intact while others cracked during creep ( Figs. 5, 6 and 9 ). Furthermore, grain boundary type (CSL GB), RX grain size (the size of transverse grain boundary) and strengthening of GB second phase may be the reason for integrity of high angle GBs.  

For the low angle grain boundary, slip systems on each side of the grain boundary are nearly coplanar, that is, the angle ˛ is very small, which provides an easy path for dislocation transmission between grains [20] . This was demonstrated from our present observations ( Fig. 7 b and c).  

In present study, only a few dislocations can be observed at the twin boundary and no cracks occur ( Figs. 3, 8 and 9 ), which is in agreement to the results observed in Ni 3 Al and Ir [17] . The interfa- cial region in a twin boundary has a structure of almost ideal lattice with high coherence and can easily deform plastically [17] . Stress concentration is thereby relieved [22] .

# 5. Summary  

In this study, relationship between crack formation and the characteristic of RX grain boundary in a DS superalloy during creep was investigated. Crack was observed in the very beginning of creep at some of the high angle GBs which misorientation angle was more than about 20 ◦ . It propagated along some of the RX GBs during the second stage of creep. However, most of the RX GBs were intact even after ruptured. It was found that crack formation is related to the dislocation mobility at GB. Generally, dislocations are difﬁcult to travel across the high angle GB, which leads to the formation of cracks. However, superimposition of GB misorienta- tion and intersection angle of the two started slip planes in each grain may result in easy transmission of dislocation, which leads to GBs free of crack. It was observed that dislocation can travel across the low angle or twin boundary due to the high coherence at these boundaries. Therefore, it is difﬁcult for cracks to form at these RX GBs.
