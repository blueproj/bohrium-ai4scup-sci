# New alkylthienyl substituted benzo[1,2- b :4,5- b 0 ]dithiophene-based polymers for high performance solar cells †

Three new alkylthienyl substituted benz odi thi oph ene (BDT)-based polymers, poly{4,8-bis(2 0 - ethyl hex yl thi oph ene)benzo[1,2- b ;3,4- b 0 ]dithiophene- alt -5,5-(4 0 ,7 0 -di-2-thienyl-5 0 ,6 0 -dioctyloxy-benzo[ c ] [1,2,5]oxadiazole)}( PBDTTDTBO ), poly{4,8-bis(2 0 -ethyl hex yl thi oph ene)benzo[1,2- b ;3,4- b 0 ] dithiophene- alt -5,5-(4 0 ,7 0 -di-2-thienyl-5 0 ,6 0 -dioctyloxy-2 0 ,1 0 ,3 0 -b enzo thi a diaz ole)}( PBDTTDTBT ) and poly{4,8-bis(2 0 -ethyl hexylthiophene)benzo[1,2- b ;3,4- b 0 ]dithiophene- alt -5,5-(4 0 ,7 0 -di-2-thienyl-2-octyl- 2 0 ,1 0 ,3 0 -benzotriazole)} ( PBDTTDTBTz ), were synthesized by Stille coupling polymerization reactions. All of the polymers were found to be soluble in common organic solvents such as chloroform, tetrahydrofuran and chlorobenzene with excellent ﬁlm forming properties. Their structures were veriﬁed by elemental analysis and NMR spectroscopy, the molecular weights were determined by gel permeation chromatography (GPC) and the thermal properties were investigated by thermo grav i metric analysis (TGA). The polymers exhibited tunable absorptions and energy levels on incorporation of different electron accepting units. All the copolymers showed high ﬁeld hole mobility up to 10  2   order, and their blends with PCBM exhibited mobility as high as 10  1   order by the space-charge-limited current (SCLC) method. Preliminary photovoltaic cells based on the device structure of ITO/ PEDOT:PSS/ PBDTTDTBO :PC 71 BM (1 : 1.5, w/w)/Ca/Al showed a power conversion efﬁciency of

 5.9% with a high open-circuit voltage ( V oc ) of 0.84 V and a short circuit current density ( J sc ) of

 11.45 mA cm  2 . To the best of our knowledge, this is the highest efﬁciency for dithienyl benzooxadiazole (DTBO)-based polymer solar cells.  

# 1. Introduction  

In the past decade, polymer solar cells (PSCs) have intrigued much interest because they allow the fabrication of light-weight, large-area, ﬂexible devices by low-cost solution processing methods,  etc. 1–3   To achieve highly efﬁcient PSCs, conjugated polymers with relatively broad absorption, suitable energy levels, and good solubility in organic solvents are important prerequi-   sites. Power conversion efﬁciencies up to 8% have now been   achieved. The design and synthesis of new conjugated poly- mers play a crucial role during the development of PSCs. Conjugated polymers containing alternating donor (D) and acceptor (A) units in their main or side chains have been widely used in PSCs because of their easily tunable electronic proper-   ties. It is important to choose suitable D and A units while designing D–A copolymers with high photovoltaic performance.  

To improve the polymer solubility while maintaining the poly- mer’s coplanarity, we choose three typical acceptors with alkyl or alkoxyl groups: (4,7-di(5-bromothien-2-yl)-5,6-dioctyloxy benzo [ c ][1,2,5]oxadiazole (M1), 4,7-di(5-bromo-thiophen-2-yl)-5,6- dioctyloxybenzo[ c ][1,2,5]-thiadiazole (M2), and 2-octyl-4,7- di(5-bromo-thiophen-2-yl)-2 H -benzo[ d ][1,2,3]triazole (M3). Our research group has reported that the copolymers from copoly- merization of M1, M2 or M3 with alkoxyl substituted benzodi- furan (BDF) or benz odi thi oph ene (BDT) units exhibit good   photovoltaic properties with a PCE reaching 4.5%. From the studies on polymer photovoltaic materials, it is obvious that the change of donors can affect greatly device performance by well tuning of absorption, energy levels, morphologies,  etc.  

During the process of searching excellent donors, some recent reports attract us. Some groups have reported alkylthienyl substituted BDT (BDTT)-based copolymers showing great   potential in PSCs with PCEs over 6%. For example, Hou and coworkers synthesized a BDTT based polymer-PBDTTT-C-T, indicating a PCE of up to 7.6% with a high short circuit current density ( J sc ) of 17.48 mA cm  2 ,   furthermore, they synthesized another BDTT based polymer-PBDTDTTT-S-T with a PCE   over 7%. BDTT seems an excellent donor and BDTT based polymers show better photovoltaic properties than those of their corresponding BDT or BDF based copolymers. Conjugated alkylthienyl unit replacing alkoxy group as side chain has proven to be a good strategy to improve the absorbance and mobility of   BDT-based polymers. However, it is still necessary to do further research on the inﬂuence of the BDTT unit on photo- voltaic properties of different D–A polymer systems.  

To further explore BDTT based polymers towards highly efﬁcient photovoltaic applications, we designed and synthesized three new BDTT based copolymers with M1, M2 and M3 as electron accepting units, namely poly{4,8-bis(2 0 -ethylhexyl- thiophene)benzo[1,2- b ;3,4- b 0 ]dithiophene- alt -5,5-(4 0 ,7 0 -di-2-thienyl-5 0 , 6 0 -dioctyloxy-benzo[ c ][1,2,5]oxadiazole)} ( PBDTTDTBO ), poly- {4,8-bis(2 0 -ethyl hex yl thi oph ene)benzo[1,2- b ;3,4- b 0 ]dithiophene- alt - 5,5-(4 0 ,7 0 -di-2-thienyl-5 0 ,6 0 -dioctyloxy-2 0 ,1 0 ,3 0 -b enzo thi a diaz ole)}

 ( PBDTTDTBT ) and poly{4,8-bis(2 0 -ethyl hexylthiophene)benzo-

 [1,2- b ;3,4- b 0 ]dithiophene- alt -5,5-(4 0 ,7 0 -di-2-thienyl-2-octyl-2 0 ,1 0 , 3 0 -benzotriazole)} ( PBDTTDTBTz ). All the polymers were found to be soluble in common organic solvents with good ﬁlm forming properties. Their thermal, absorption, and electro- chemical properties and hole mobilities were well characterized. Preliminary photovoltaic cells based on the device structure of ITO/PEDOT:PSS/ PBDTTDTBO :PC 71 BM (1 : 1, w/w)/Ca/Al showed a power conversion efﬁciency (PCE) of 5.9% with an open-circuit voltage ( V oc ) of 0.84 V and a short circuit current density ( J sc ) of 11.45 mA cm  2 . These investigations indicate that the photovoltaic properties can be well tuned by combining with different electron accepting units. More importantly, this work also provides some guidance about the molecular design towards high performance optoelectronic materials.

# 2.3 Fabrication of OFET devices  

Thin-ﬁlm OFETs were fabricated with top-contact conﬁgura- ++   tion. Thermally oxidized (100) silicon wafers (n doped) with a SiO 2  thickness of 300 nm were sequentially cleaned with deter- gent, deionized water, acetone and ethanol in an ultrasonic bath. Then hydrophilic treatment of these silicon wafers was per- formed according to the standard procedure. Brieﬂy, the substrates were soaked in a mixture of deionized water, 25% ammonium hydroxide and 30% H 2 O 2  (5 : 1 : 1 by volumetric ratio) for 20 min at 80    C, then rinsed with deionized water and dried with a nitrogen ﬂow. Oct a dec yl tri meth oxys i lane (OTMOS) was self-assembled on the surface of hydrophilic-treated substrates according to the reported method. Thin polymer ﬁlms were prepared by spin coating of the copolymers in chloroben- zene onto the OTMOS modiﬁed SiO 2 /Si substrates at a speed of 3000 rpm (revolutions per minute) for 40 s at room temperature. After annealing at 150    C under N 2  for half an hour, the gold ﬁlm (50 nm) was deposited on the organic layer to form the drain and source electrodes, for a typical device, the drain–source channel length ( L ) and width ( W ) are 80  m m and 8800  m m, respectively. OFET measurements were performed at room temperature using a Keithley 4200 SCS semiconductor parameter analyzer under ambient conditions.  

# 2.4 Fabrication and characterization of polymer solar cells  

The PSCs were fabricated in the conﬁguration of the traditional sandwich structure with an indium tin oxide (ITO) glass positive electrode and a metal negative electrode. Patterned ITO glass with a sheet resistance of 10  U  ,  1   was purchased from CSG HOLDING Co. LTD (China). The ITO glass was cleaned by sequential ultrasonic treatment in detergent, deionized water, acetone and isopropanol, then treated in an ultraviolet-ozone chamber (Ultraviolet Ozone Cleaner, Jelight Company, USA) for 20 min. The PEDOT:PSS (poly(3,4-ethylene dioxythiophe- ne):poly(styrene sulfonate)) (Baytron P 4083, Germany) was ﬁltered through a 0.45  m m ﬁlter and spin coated at 2000 rpm for 60 s onto the ITO electrode. Subsequently, the PEDOT:PSS ﬁlm was baked at 150    C for 15 min in air to give a thin ﬁlm with a thickness of 40 nm. A blend of the polymer and PCBM (different weight ratios, 10 mg mL  1   for polymer) was dissolved in  ortho - dichlorobenzene (ODCB), and spin-cast at 3000 rpm for 45 s onto the PEDOT:PSS layer. The substrates were then dried at 70    C for 15 min. The thickness of the photoactive layer is in the range of 70–80 nm as measured using an Ambios Technology XP-2 proﬁlometer. A bilayer cathode consisting of Ca (  20 nm) capped with Al (  100 nm) was thermally evaporated under a shadow mask with a base pressure of  ca.  10  5   Pa. The active area 2 of the PSCs is 4 mm . The devices were fabricated in a glovebox. Device characterization was carried out under AM 1.5G irradi- ation with an intensity of 100 mW cm  2   (Oriel 67005, 500 W) calibrated by a standard silicon cell.  J – V  curves were recorded with a Keithley 236 digital source meter.

# 3.5 Hole mobility  

For polymer solar cells (PSCs) using PCBM as the acceptor, power conversion efﬁciency (PCE) is proportional to the open- circuit voltage ( V oc ), short circuit current density ( J sc ) and ﬁll factor (FF).  V oc  is closely related to the HOMO level of the polymers. More importantly,  J sc  and FF are directly limited by the hole mobility. Higher charge mobilities enable better charge transport within an active layer without large photocurrent loss due to recombination of opposite charges. Therefore, besides the absorption and energy levels, hole mobility is another important aspect for photovoltaic applications. # 3.7 Morphology  

Besides the above parameters such as absorption, energy levels and charge mobility, the morphology of the photoactive layer is very important for PSCs. To better explain different photovol- taic properties and determine if the devices were fully optimized, we investigated the morphologies of the three polymers and PC 71 BM blends spin-coated from their o -dichlorobenzene (ODCB) solution by using a tapping-mode atomic-force micro- scope (AFM). Fig. 6 shows the height and phase images of polymer:PC 71 BM (1 : 1.5, w/w) ﬁlms. From the height image (Fig. 6a–c), the root-mean-square (RMS) surface roughness values of the blend ﬁlms for  PBDTTDTBO ,  PBDTTDTBT  and PBDTTDTBTz  are 1.04 nm, 1.24 nm and 0.77 nm, respectively. All the blends form very smooth high quality ﬁlms when spin- cast from  o -dichlorobenzene. The ﬁlms are very homogenous with little apparent phase separation and low surface roughness. From the phase images of the copolymer and PCBM blends (Fig. 6d–f), the connectivity is sensitive to the structures of the polymers. The ﬁne and nanoscale phase separation can be seen more clearly in the  PBDTTDTBO /PC 71 BM blend and the PBDTTDTBO  blend nanostructure is most pronounced among the three polymer blends, implying that long and good connected pathways have been formed, which further explains the better performance of  PBDTTDTBO  based solar cells. For BHJ PSCs, the nanoscale phase separation is a practical way to form a   bicontinuous donor–acceptor network. AFM images indicate that all the BDTT based polymers possess good morphology for PSCs. It is believed that the photovoltaic performance of this type of copolymers can be further improved by the optimization of ﬁlm morphology and device fabrication conditions.  

# 4. Conclusions  

In conclusion, we have synthesized three new BDTT based copolymers. The copolymers have been well characterized by TGA, UV-vis absorption and cyclic voltammetry. The opto- electronic properties of the polymers can be ﬁnely tuned by differing the electron accepting units. The polymers exhibit surprisingly high hole mobilities (10  1   to 10  2   cm 2   V  1   s  1 ). The energy levels, absorption, morphology,  etc. , have been greatly affected by the introduction of different electron accepting units, resulting in different photovoltaic performances. The PCE of

 5.9% from  PBDTTDTBO  based polymer solar cells with  V oc  ¼

 0.84 V,  J sc  ¼  11.45 mA cm  2   and FF  ¼  61.3% was recorded without any device modiﬁcations or post-treatment. These results indicate that  PBDTTDTBO  is a promising candidate for high-efﬁciency polymer solar cells.
