# INTRODUCTION  

Developing both hole-conducting (p-type) and electron- conducting (n-type) organic semiconducting materials is of high importance for many organic electronic devices, because both types of material are necessary for building comple- mentary logic circuits based on  ﬁ eld-e ﬀ ect transistors ,   (FETs), which are basic components of more complicated electrical circuits. Replacing conventional n-type fullerene derivatives in organic solar cells (OSCs) provides an opportunity to extend the light absorption range and tune the energy levels of solar cells to improve the cell perform- − 7   ance. The vigorous search for the better combinations of p- type and n-type polymeric materials (typically naphthalene diimide (NDI)-based polymers) has recently led to the high power conversion e ﬃ ciency of 7.7%, which is quickly catching up with those of OSCs with the fullerene derivatives as n-type material.   So far, the choice of n-type polymeric materials has been limited compared with p-type materials because the electrons in organic semiconductors are easily trapped by extrinsic species, such as oxygen or water. In addition, the injection of electrons from metals to the organic compound is di ﬃ cult owing to the large energetic barriers. These points, however, do not mean that hole conduction is intrinsically dominant in organic materials. Recently, a number of n-type 8 ,   organic materials have emerged because of the better understanding of electron injection and stable electron − 13 transport in organic materials.  

On the other hand, the molecular properties alone do not determine the performance of the organic electronic devices. The structures in either the pristine or the mixed thin  ﬁ lms such as crystallinity, chain orientations, interface structures, and mixing morphology largely a ﬀ ect the device performance. Particularly, the chain orientation of  π -conjugated polymers in thin  ﬁ lms was proven to be important for both FETs and OSCs.   In general, edge-on orientation in which the  π -plane align normal to the substrate could be advantageous for FET application,   while face-on orientation with the  π -plane align ,   parallel to the substrate could be preferred for OSCs. The control of the orientations in the binary mixed ﬁ lms for both of the polymer chains would be very challenging but important to further improve the performance in the polymer-blend OSCs.  

Recently, we developed naphtho[2,3- b :6,7- b ′ ]dithiophene- 18 − 20   4,5,9,10-diimide (NDTI) as an analogue of NDI and found that NDTI is a promising building block for n-type, p- − 25   type, and ambipolar organic semiconductors. The combination of electron-rich nap h tho dith i oph ene with elec- tron-de ﬁ cient diimide leads to a narrower energy gap of NDTI compared to that of NDI. The planar, rigid structure of NDTI with the larger  π -system could produce strong intermolecular π − π  overlap in the solid state. In addition, the synthesis of NDTI is well established, and the vacant  α -positions of the thiophenes make them easy to connect to various aromatic rings through coupling reactions to expand its  π -conjugation. These features are quite important because we can tune the linearity of the main chain in NDTI polymers by utilizing di ﬀ erent aromatic rings, which could play an important role on 26 ,   the molecular orientations and the device performances.  

In this paper, we synthesized three new NDTI-based copolymers (PNDTIs) with thiophene (PNDTI-T), thieno- thiophene (PNDTI-TT), and dith ie not hio phe ne units (PNDTI-DTT) and used them in FETs and OSCs. The combination of the strong electron a ﬃ nity of NDTI with the electron-rich fused thiophenes induces strong intramolecular donor − acceptor (D − A) interactions, resulting in narrow energy gaps. This could produce a photoresponse in the near-IR region in OSCs. Low-lying LUMO and high-lying HOMO levels can contribute to e ﬃ cient carrier injection of electrons and holes from the electrodes, which could enable ambipolar operation in FET devices. Moreover, the thiophene units have di ﬀ erent connecting angles to the main chain axis, which can a ﬀ ect the molecular orientation in  ﬁ lms. Systematic investigation of the structure in the  ﬁ lms and the performance in FETs and OSCs should elucidate the contribution of molecular orientation to the structure − properties relationship in organic electronics.   Thus, we include the copolymer with bithiophene (PNDTI-BT) from our previous work for comparison.

Figure 3  shows 2D grazing incidence X-ray di ﬀ raction (GIXD) patterns of the PNDTI polymer  ﬁ lms after thermal treatment at 320  ° C for 1 h. PNDTI-T and PNDTI-DTT showed a strong di ﬀ raction peak for  π − π  stacking at 1.8 Å − 1   in the out-of-plane direction, indicating that the orientation of these polymers was face-on to the substrate. In contrast, PNDTI-TT showed strong lamella peaks at 0.25 Å − 1   in the out- of-plane direction and its  π − π  stacking appeared at 1.8 Å − 1   in the in-plane direction, indicating an edge-on orientation to the substrate. This di ﬀ erence in the orientation may originate from the linearity of the polymer main chain. PNDTI-TT has a straight main chain, whereas the main chains of PNDTI-T and PNDTI-DTT are curved owing to the angle of the thiophene rings (29.0 °  and 28.4 ° , respectively). The straight main chains may have stronger intermolecular  π − π  interactions and interactions of the alkyl chains with the substrates, leading to the edge-on orientation. This result agrees with the previous data for PNDTI-BT with a linear main chain, which also showed strong edge-on orientation in the  ﬁ lms. In contrast, the main chain with the curved structure could have the opposite balance between the intermolecular interactions and the interaction with the substrates, resulting in the preferred face- on orientation.

The orientation of the polymer chains can strongly a ﬀ ect the charge transport. The PNDTI-DTT and PNDTI-T  ﬁ lms showed low mobility in FETs owing to their preferred face- on orientation to the substrate. Thus, we expect that in a solar cell, in which the carriers  ﬂ ow in the out-of-plane direction, PNDTI-DTT and PNDTI-T may show superior performance, assuming that the orientations of the polymers are similar in the mixed BHJ. To elucidate the e ﬀ ect of the molecular orientation in OSCs, 2D GIXD of the PTB7:PNDTIs  ﬁ lms were measured without thermal treatment ( Figure S8 ). Compared with the pristine thin  ﬁ lms after annealing, the di ﬀ raction peaks were weaker although PNDTI-T and PNDTI-TT exhibited their original preferred orientations (face-on and edge-on, respec- tively) with the in-plane lamellar peaks at 0.25 Å − 1   even in the presence of PTB7. The out-of-plane peak at 1.7 Å − 1   was attributed to PTB7 with a face-on orientation, similar to the pristine PTB7  ﬁ lms. In contrast, PNDTI-DTT showed low crystallinity with low preferred orientation and showed only a weak broad peak in the out-of-plane direction at 1.6 Å − 1 , which could be attributed to PTB7. Although AFM observations showed a uniform  ﬁ lm surface, the GIXD results suggest that PTB7 and PNDTIs formed a phase-separated structure on a scale smaller than the detection limit of AFM. However, because of the decrease of the crystallinity upon mixing with PTB7, the e ﬀ ects of the preferred orientation in PNDTIs are still not clear.  

To see the e ﬀ ects of orientation on the charge transport in the out-of-plane direction, space charge limited current (SCLC) was measured on pristine  ﬁ lms and BHJ  ﬁ lms of PTB7:PNDTIs, and the electron mobility was evaluated by using the Mott − Gurney law ( Figure S9  and  Table S3 ). The calculated average mobility of the pristine  ﬁ lms was in the range of 7.6  ×  10 − 4 − 14  ×  10 − 4   cm 2 /(V s), which may not be a signi ﬁ cant di ﬀ erence considering the standard deviations of the measurements. However, for the mixed  ﬁ lms, PTB7:PNDTI- DTT exhibited a mobility about 1 order of magnitude higher than the other  ﬁ lms, which was the opposite trend to the electron and hole mobilities in the FET measurements. This may be attributed to the preferred face-on orientation of PNDTI-DTT in BHJ  ﬁ lms, even though direct observation of the orientation was di ﬃ cult because of the low crystallinity.  

Among the PNDTI devices, the PTB7:PNDTI-DTT devices showed the highest performance despite their low crystallinity and unclear preference for face-on orientation in the PNDTI- DTT domain. This suggests that the increase of the crystallinity in the BHJ structure could enhance the performance of OSCs further. We optimized the fabrication conditions for PTB7:PNDTI-DTT devices with di ﬀ erent annealing temper- atures and concentrations of solvent additive ( Table S4 ). However, the treatments did not improve the performance of this system substantially, and the highest PCE of 3.6% was achieved for the PTB7:PNDTI-DTT device without thermal annealing or an additive. The performance of the device with an inverted con ﬁ guration (ITO/ZnO/active layer/MoO x /Ag) was similar ( J SC  of 10.7 mA/cm 2 ,  V OC  of 0.67 V, FF of 0.50, and PCE 3.6%), and thermal annealing also slightly decreased the device performance. Thus, simple thermal annealing to increase the crystallinity of the polymers was not an appropriate method for improving the polymer blend OSCs with PNDTI-DTT.  

# CONCLUSION  

New NDTI-based polymers with di ﬀ erent thiophene units were synthesized and their use in FETs and OSCs were systemati- cally investigated in relation to the  ﬁ lm structures. The linear polymers with a preferred edge-on orientation in the pristine ﬁ lms (PNDTI-TT and PNDTI-BT) had a clear advantage in FETs with the highest electron mobility of 0.26 cm 2 /(V s). Our results demonstrate that the NDTI moiety is a promising building block for organic semiconductors. In contrast, the e ﬀ ects of the molecular orientation were less clear in OSCs because of the lower crystallinity of the polymers in the BHJ structure. The performance of the polymers could be improved if mixed  ﬁ lms of PNDTI-DTT derivatives with a highly ordered face-on orientation can be fabricated through chemical modi ﬁ cations of the polymers or the deposition process.

Device Fabrication and Measurement of Solar Cells.  A patterned indium tin oxide (ITO)-coated glass substrate was cleaned by sequential ultrasonication in detergent solution, water, 2-propanol, and acetone, followed by UV-O 3  treatment. For the conventional device, PEDOT:PSS (Clevios AI 4083) was spin-coated onto ITO at 4000 rpm for 30 s, and the resulting  ﬁ lm was annealed at 150  ° C for 10 min in an N 2 - ﬁ lled glovebox. The solution of PTB7:PNDTI polymer (1:1 w/w) in CHCl 3  was successively spin-coated onto the PEDOT layer. The Ca (20 nm) and Al electrodes (80 nm) were deposited by thermal evaporation under vacuum (10 − 4   Pa) through a metal mask. For the inverted con ﬁ guration, the ZnO layer was prepared by the sol − gel method. Zinc acetate dihydrate (100 mg) was dissolved in EtOH (1.6 mL). 2-Aminoethanol (28.4  μ L) was added to the solution to stabilize the precursor. The solution was spin-coated onto ITO at 3000 rpm for 30 s in an N 2 - ﬁ lled glovebox. After spin-coating, the substrate was annealed at 200  ° C for 30 min in air.  (about 10 − 4   Pa) through a metal mask. The device size was determined by the overlapping area between the ITO and the metal electrodes (0.2 cm 2 ).
