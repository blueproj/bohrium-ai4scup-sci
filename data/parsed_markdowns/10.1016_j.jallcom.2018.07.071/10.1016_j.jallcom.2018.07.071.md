Abstract:  The research of Nickel-based superalloys with excellent mechanical  properties is of significance for aerospace. Here we report the effect of sintering  temperature of 850 °C, 900°C and 950 °C with the heating rate of 100 °C /min via spark  plasma sintering (SPS) on Inconel 718 (IN718) superalloy reinforced with 0.25 wt. %  MANUSCRIPT reduced oxide graphene (RGO). The microstructures and mechanical properties of  IN718-RGO composite were investigated by scanning electron microscopy (SEM) and  MTS servo hydraulic testing machine, respectively. SEM results demonstrate that the  RGO is almost uniformly distributed into the IN718 powders after 3D rock-milling for  8h. The mechanical properties of the composite increase with increasing the sintering  temperature. When the sintering temperature is 950 °C, a relative density of 92.5 %, a  microhardness of 375 Hv and an ultimate compressive strength of 1748.20 MPa after  aging heat treatment was obtained for the IN718-0.25 wt. % RGO, respectively. The  fracture mechanism of the composite changes from intergranular fracture to  transgranular fracture with the sintering temperature increasing.  

Keywords:  Inconel 718; Reduce graphene oxide (RGO); Spark plasma sintering;  

# 1 Introduction  

Nickel-based superalloys are mostly used for the aerospace of the turbine disc,  where specific strength is one of significant consideration. [1, 2] Nickel-based  superalloys of Inconel 718 (IN718, also named as GH4169 in China) is a widely used  MANUSCRIPT high temperature material that consists of two types of strengthening precipitates of the  metastable body centered tetragonal  γ′′ coherent  γ′  (Ni 3 plastic anisotropy which is inherent in nickel-based superalloys, amounts of approaches  and experiments have been carried out. For instance, texture modification, through  addition of rare earth elements or others reinforcements as allotropes of carbon, [3-5]  and careful selection of the melting technology [6], and control of heat treatment  process [7] were applied to improve the mechanical properties of IN718. Normally, the  ACCEPTED production processes of Nickel-based superalloys include casting-forging, directional  solidification and powder metallurgy. Powder metallurgy process is usually divided  into two stages: powder preparation and powder consolidation. [3] Gas atomization and  rotary electrode method were applied to produce powders of superalloys. [8]  Nickel-based superalloys generally adopt two-step heat treatment: the first step of  solution treatment is to eliminate eutectic and tissue homogenization; the second step of  aging heat treatment is to form a desired amount of certain microstructural phases. [9,  10].  

Different kinds of mechanical milling were used for the powder preparation in the  previous researches. [11-13] Mechanical milling is a kind of processing method, which  can be applied to synthesize fine-grained or nanocrystalline materials in relatively large  quantities. [14-16] So far, mechanical milling has been successfully employed in  combination with various consolidation methods to obtain significant performance in a  variety of metals, alloys and composites. [17, 18] It is interesting to note that in the  majority of these kinds of studies, a route including mechanical milling and SPS was  used to fabricate ultrafine lamellar structured Al2024 alloy by Zheng.[19] The  structured alloy exhibited significantly enhanced yield strength of 375 MPa and tensile  strength of 456 MPa, respectively.  

Compared with the traditional process of preparing metal matrix composites, SPS  is based on the simultaneous application of high sinter temperature, high pressure and  high current associated with an electric current passes through a powder specimen.  Owing to the advantages of SPS, it has the potential for consolidation of powder  ACCEPTED materials rapidly and effectively. [20-22] SPS uses uniaxial force and ON-OFF DC  pulse which facilitating joule heat throughout the specimen to prepare metal and  ceramic materials. A rapid heating rate at the particle contacts causes surface melting  and facilitates diffusion. Interestingly, in view of the large number of publishing  literatures, high-energy milling followed by subsequent spark plasma sintering has  been studied through grain refinement to improve the mechanical properties of MMCs,  particularly the strength and hardness. However, there are few academic studies of the  compressive behaviour of nickel-based superalloys reinforced by graphene through 3D  rock-milling and SPS.  

In this paper, the effects of different sintering temperatures on the microstructures  and mechanical properties of IN718 superalloy reinforced by RGO through SPS were  investigated. The 3D rock-milling was applied to uniformly mix raw materials before  SPS process. We used scanning electron microscopy (SEM) and energy dispersive  spectrometer (EDS) to probe the microstructure and texture of the composite powders  MANUSCRIPT and sintered samples. Microhardness and compressive tests were performed on bulk  samples and the results showed that the mechanical properties of as-sintered specimens  were significantly related to the sintering temperature. We also found that transgranular  fractures govern the deformation processes at 900 °C and 950 °C, in contrast to the  intergranular fractures at 850 °C.  

# 2 Experimental section  

# 2.1 Materials  

Central Iron & Steel Research Institute provided nickel-based superalloy IN718  ACCEPTED m. Reduced oxide graphene (RGO) was  from Nanjing XFNANO Materials Tech Co., Ltd, which was a purity of > 99 %, single  m and thickness of 0.8\~1.2 nm. All the chemical  reagents utilized in the experiments were of analytical grade purity without further  purification. Table 1 summarizes the elemental compositions of the powder of IN718.  

# 2.2 Preparation of IN718-RGO composite powders  

In order to make the 0.25 wt. % RGO homogenously disperse in the IN718-RGO  composite powders, the 3D rock-milling machine (ROCKINGMILL, Hispeed mixing  shaker machine, RM-05) was adopted. Commercial gas atomized IN718 powder and  RGO were the raw materials. The rock machine is equipped with the stainless steel  304L milling fixture to place the polypropylene bottle. The composite powders were  milled for 8h at 30 Hz and the ball-to-powder ratio of 10:1. At the end of milling, the  composite powders were taken out of the polypropylene bottle and quickly transferred  into the graphite die for spark plasma sintering process.  

# 2.3 Spark plasma sintering  

Spark plasma sintering system is from Japan (SPS, LABOXTM-325S, SINTER  MANUSCRIPT LAND INC, JAPAN), and the system of maximum current and voltage is 2500 A and  10 V, respectively. The column of SPS system chamber includes a graphite die  equipped with two plungers and eight spacers. Graphite foils were set at both  extremities of the SPS column between graphite spacers and the electrodes. The same  diameter of compressive carbon paper (Ø 20 (O.D) × 0.2 mm thick) was adopted  ACCEPTED between IN718-RGO composite mixtures and graphite punches. IN718-RGO  composite mixtures (30g) were added into graphite die of Ø 20 (O.D) × H 40mm.  After pre-pressuring by LAB Mini Press in the pressure of 10 MPa, the composite  mixtures were put into the graphite die. The composite samples were heated to 850 °C,  900 °C and 950 °C at a heating rate of 100 °C /min with a pressure of 30 MPa and a  holding time of 5 min. # 4 Conclusions  

In summary, the samples at sintering temperature of 850 °C, 900 °C and 950 °C on  the heating rate of 100 °C /min of IN718-0.25 wt. % RGO composite were successfully  fabricated by 3D rock-milling and spark plasma sintering, respectively. The main  results achieved are as follows.  

(1) The sintering temperature has a great impact on the IN718-0.25 wt. % RGO  composite of microstructure, relative density, and microhardness. The densification and  consolidation of the IN718-RGO composite increased with increasing sintering  temperature, promoting the neck formation of IN718-RGO composite. A relative  density of 92.5 % and a microhardness of 375 Hv were obtained at the sintering  

(2) Compressive behaviour and fracture morphology of IN718-0.25 wt. % RGO  ACCEPTED composite were also relative to the sintering temperature. The ultimate compressive  strength of the composite at sintering temperature of 950 °C was 1480.64 MPa without  aging heat treatment. However, a better ultimate compressive strength of 1748.20 MPa  

(3) The compression fracture type of IN718-0.25 wt. % RGO composite transformed  from intergranular to transgranular fracture due to improving the densification of the  IN718-RGO composite when the sintering temperature ranged from 850 °C to 950 °C.
