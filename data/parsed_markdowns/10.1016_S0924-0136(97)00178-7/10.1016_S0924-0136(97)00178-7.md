# Abstract  

A method was developed for the dynamic behavior modeling of P / M superalloys during hot isostatic pressing (HIP). The power dissipative efﬁciency  h  during the HIP process was deﬁned as a function of the relative density  R  and the strain-rate sensitivity index  m . Based on the results of isothermal constant strain-rate tests, the microstructure evolution of P / M Rene95 alloy during the HIP process was predicted using the proposed method. The predicted results indicate that HIPing within the temperature range of 1100–1120°C and range of strain rate of 10 − 4 –10 − 3   may eliminate the residual dendrite and ensure consistency in the microstructures and properties of HIP products, these indications being in good agreement with practical experience. © 1997 Elsevier Science S.A.  

Keywords : P / M superalloy; Hot isostatic pressing; Dynamic behavior  

# 1. Introduction  

At the present time, P / M superalloys are being used widely for critical components of high-performance aero-engines. Because of the severe working conditions, consistency in the microstructures and properties of P / M superalloys components is essential for safety and reliability. The hot isostatic pressing (HIP) process is an indispensable step for the manufacturing of P / M super- alloys components. Thus, the investigation of micro- structure evolution during the HIP process of P / M superalloys can not only improve understanding of the HIP process, but can also provide guidance for deter- mining the optimal parameters of the HIP process.  

The dynamic material modelling (DMM) method was proposed by Gegel for dense materials [1] and has been applied successfully in the prediction of mi- crostructure evolution during hot forming processes and in the determination of the stable regions of ther- momechanical parameters [1,2]. In this paper, referring to the DMM method, a new approach is proposed to model the dynamic behavior of P / M superalloys during the HIP process in order to reveal the microstructure evolution and control the quality of as-HIPed products.  

# 2. Method of dynamic behavior modeling for P / M superalloys  

From the thermodynamic viewpoint, hot-forming processes can be regarded as irreversible processes far away from equilibrium conditions. The various mecha- nisms in operation during the processes are related to the energy dissipative processes according to the dissi- pative structure theory. During hot-forming processes, the workpiece is the basic energy dissipative unit. Through it, the input deformation power is divided into two portions: plastic deformation energy and potential energy, the later being stored in the workpiece through microstructure evolution. Accordingly, the dynamic be- havior of materials during hot-forming processes de- pends on the ratio of the above two portions.

To sum up, it is appropriate that the HIP process of P / M Rene95 alloy is performed at a temperature of 1100–1120°C and at a strain rate of 10 − 4 –10 − 3   s − 1   in order that the DRX can develop completely. There are two advantages for the above selection. First, it can lower the external load whilst the applied pressure is greater than the yield pressure of the materials. Second, full development of DRX during the HIP process may eliminate the residual dendrite and result in the obtain- ing of as-HIPed parts with consistency in microstruc- tures and properties. It is reported that for the HIP process of P / M Rene95 alloy carried out at tempera- tures of 1100, 1120 and 1150°C, the residual dendrite in the primary powders disappeared and the microstruc- ture consisted of stable DRX grains at temperature of 1120 and 1150°C, while, the applied pressure had no signiﬁcant effects. Because the gamma prime solvus temperature for Rene95 alloy is 1160°C, if the tempera- ture of HIP process is selected close to the temperature, grain growth or other defects can occur easily, affecting the performance of HIP products. Therefore, the HIP temperature is generally selected as 1120°C, which tal- lies with the remarks about DRX and the results avail- able in Ref. [5].  

# 5. Conclusions  

(1) A method of dynamic behavior modeling for P / M superalloys has been proposed and the relation- ship between the dissipative efﬁciency and the relative density and strain-rate sensitivity index has been formu- lated.  

(2) The process window for the HIP process can be determined using the proposed method, to ensure con- sistency in the microstructures and properties of HIP products and to lay the foundations for the HIP pro- cess becoming intelligent manufacturing.  

(3) Using the proposed method, the optimum pro- cess window for the HIP process of P / M Rene95 alloy is as follows: 1100–1120°C and 10 − 4 –10 − 3   s − 1 .
