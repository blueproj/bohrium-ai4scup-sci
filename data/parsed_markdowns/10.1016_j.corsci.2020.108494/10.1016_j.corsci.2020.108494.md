Many reports showed that the common base alloy constituents Ti  and/or Ta diffuse from the superalloy through the coating and may affect the properties of the  alumina scale on the aluminide coatings due to the formation of Ti, Ta-rich oxide and/or mixed  oxides with Ni, Co and Cr in the scale. # 6   Conclusions  

The composition and thickness of the as-received aluminide coatings on alloy 625 and Rene 80  are very similar. Both coatings form alumina based surface scales during 1050 °C air exposure up  to 1000 h whereby the coating on Rene 80 exhibit only slightly higher oxide growth rate and  spallation.

The results of the study illustrate that using the coupled thermodynamic-kinetic modelling it is  possible to quantitatively predict the coating microstructural changes and lifetime as a result of a  change in the base alloy composition without very extensive test programs.
