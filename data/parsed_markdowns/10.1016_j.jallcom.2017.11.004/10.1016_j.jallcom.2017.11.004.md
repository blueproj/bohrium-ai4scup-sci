# 1.  

Inconel 718 (IN718) super-alloy is widely used in aircraft industries due to its good weldability,  excellent creep resistance, high yield strength, and high fatigue strength at elevated temperatures  IN718 components with complex structures, such as turbine blisk, can be repaired or even  manufactured by laser metal deposition (LMD) due to its high efficiency and relatively low cost  .  Therefore, investigations on the laser metal deposited (LMDed) IN718 have been attracting more and  more attentions . These previous studies provide a solid foundation for the further research, and  

In the recent years, in order to satisfy the growing economical expectations of forming large-scale  components in industries, high-deposition-rate laser metal deposition (HDR-LMD) technology has  been developed  . Compared with conventional LMD with a deposition rate less than 0.5kg/h, the  deposition rate for HDR-LMD is more than 2kg/h. This significantly different deposition rates lead to  different thermal cycle processes between HDR- and conventional LMD, which results in their  different microstructures, such as the volume fraction of Laves phases  , and the residual stress.  Therefore, the heat treatment regime used for conventional LMDed IN718 may be not suitable for  HDR-LMDed IN718.  

The currently used heat treatment regimens for HDR-LMDed IN718 are the ones used for wrought or  cast IN718. The problems is the microstructures of HDR-LMDed IN718 are different from that of  wrought or cast IN718: columnar grains grow epitaxially along deposition direction in the HDR-LMDed  IN718, whereas wrought IN718 owns equiaxed grains; Laves phase is formed in the HDR-LMDed  IN718 at the termination of solidification due to the niobium segregation, but no Laves phase existed  in the wrought IN718; Laves phase in cast IN718 is much larger than that in HDR-LMDed IN718; only  micro-segregation exists in the HDR-LMDed IN718, while macro-segregation exists in the cast IN718.  Therefore, the heat treatment regimens for wrought or cast IN718 may be not suitable for  HDR-LMDed IN718  

In order to obtain a more suitable heat treatment regime for HDR-LMDed IN718, it is necessary to  study the evolutions of microstructures and properties of HDR-LMDed IN718 heat treated with  different processes. The two main functions of heat treatment for LMDed IN718 are: to eliminate  anisotropy, Niobium segregation and hard and brittle Laves phases by solution heat treatment; to  induce strengthening phase precipitation by aging heat treatment. This means, solution heat  treatment plays the main roll for the modification for the microstructures of IN718 which are different  from that of wrought or casted IN718. Therefore, in this paper, different solution heat treatments have  been used and their effects on microstructures and tensile properties have been studied.

Fig. 11 shows the fractures of type-S and type-L. The fracture surfaces of both samples can be divided  into two zones: fibrous zone and shear lip zone. Dimples in the fibrous zones indicate that the fracture  mechanism is ductile rupture. It is interesting to note that the depth of the dimple is connected with  the austenite grain morphology for both samples. In the columnar grain, dendrite morphology is  remained after tensile test as shown in Fig. 11(b)-A and (e), which means that the recrystallization s of  these two specimens are not completed. No dendrite morphology can be found in the recrystallized  equiaxed grain as shown in Fig. 11(b)-B and (d). Dimples in this region are slightly deeper than that in  the former region, which indicates that the plastic deformation capability of equiaxed grain is superior  to that of columnar grain in the loading direction of this tensile test.

In our previous work, we found that the fragmentation of the micron Laves phase was the main  reason for the formation of micro-voids  . This is mainly  related to particle size. Sundararaman   indicated that the formation of micro-voids was associated  with intense and localized slip. Shiozawa and Weertman   illustrated that these  particles, such as NbC, behaved as nondeformable inclusions when the materials underwent plastic  deformation. When the particle size is large, the incoordination of deformation will cause large stress  in the particles, which induce the fragmentation. When the particles size is small, moving with the  matrix during the plastic deformation   can reduce the stress in the particles. Therefore, small size  particles are not easily broken. In this condition, the interfaces between these small size particles and  matrix are weak areas. Differences in the elastic constants of the particles and of the matrix lead to a  concentration of shear stress at the interface and this may result in separation  causes the formation of micro-voids and induce fracture.

# 5.   Conclusion  

The effects of different solution heat treatment times on Inconel 718 (IN718) formed by  high-deposition-rate laser metal deposition (HDR-LMD) have been studied in this current work. The  two employed solution heat treatment regimes are 1100 ć ×0.5 h/WQ+720 ć ×8 h/FC to 620 ć ×8  h/AC (Type-S) and 1100 ć ×1 h/WQ+720 ć ×8 h/FC to 620 ć ×8 h/AC (Type-L). Metallurgical analysis  and tensile property tests on these samples have been conducted and the results have been  evaluated.  

It has been observed that both type-S and type-L samples contain a large number of small grains  (≤20μm) and a certain amount of large grains (≥20 µm). The recrystallization degree of type-L sample  is higher than that of type-S and twins only exist in the former one. In addition, niobium segregation  and Laves phases can almost be eliminated by both heat treatment regimes.  

Moreover, the remained granular sub-micron particles in both sample types are investigated. These  particles are TiN, NbC, undissolved Laves and (Ti, Nb) phase. NbC particles occupy a large fraction of  the total volume in both types of specimens. The tensile tests result show that both types of samples have similar yield strength and tensile  strength, whereas the elongation of type-L is superior to that of type-S.
