# 2.   INTRODUCTION  

Hot corrosion has been reported for different aircraft and land turbine components. In most of the  cases failures are localised in the blades and vanes of the first stage [1-3]. However, the second or  third stages of the rotor blades have also shown to be affected by high temperature corrosion [4]. # 3.   EXPERIMENTAL PROCEDURE  

The 4th generation Ni-based single crystal superalloy TMS-138 was used as a substrate material. The  composition in wt.% is summarised in table 1. The superalloy was heat treated following the typical  solution (at 1340┬░C) and ageing (at 870┬░C) procedure before sample preparation [28]. The  

Two methodologies were followed for tests at 700┬░C. Octagonal shaped specimens were exposed to a  900┬░C pre-heated gas. The slow gas flow helps in reducing the temperature from 900┬░C to 700┬░C over  the length of the reactor glass tube and in reaching the equilibrium before contacting the samples. In  

this case no catalyst was used to assure the equilibrium of SO 3  at 700┬░C. The single orientation disks  were exposed to a catalysed gas. In this case the full length of the quartz reactor was maintained at  700┬░C and a Fe 2 O 3  powder-containing honeycomb catalyst was placed in front of the samples.
