# Heat treatment of codeposited aluminium – silicon coatings on ferritic steels by CVD-FBR technology  

⁎ J.M. Brossard, M.P. Hierro, J.A. Trilleros, M.C. Carpintero, L. Sánchez, F.J. Bolívar, F.J. Pérez  

Universidad Complutense de Madrid, Dpto CC, Materiales e Ingeniería Metalúrgica, Avenida Complutense s/n, Facultad de Ciencias Quimicas, 28040 Madrid, Spain  

Received 2 August 2005; accepted in revised form 10 March 2006 Available online 27 October 2006  

# Abstract  

Ferritic steels are often used in boiler or supercritical steam turbines which operate at temperatures between 600 – 650 °C under pressure. Nowadays generation energy industries are interested in increasing the efficiency of the turbines. Increase of the operation temperature should meet this objective. In this study Al/Si protective coatings are developed by CVD-FBR at moderate temperature to respect substrates mechanical properties. Al – Si codeposition had been formed on three ferritic steels (P-91, P-92 and HCM-12 A). The initial parameters of the process were optimized by thermodynamic calculations using Thermo-Calc software. Then, those parameters were used in the experiments to obtain Al – Si coatings at low temperature and atmospheric pressure. Fe – Al intermetallic coatings containing Cr and Si were obtained. The effect of diffusion heat treatment was studied in order to allow the phase transformation from Fe 2 Al 5  (brittle phase) to lower Al content phases such as FeAl (more fracture toughness).

# 1. Introduction  

Research in high temperature materials science including the study of corrosion and protection behaviour of materials in aggressive environments is widely stimulated by the aim to increase efficiency of clean electric-power generation plants [1,2] . An increase of steam temperature (from 580 °C to 630 °C or 700 °C) and/or the use of biomass or waste as fuel is expected to reach this goal with a reduction in the costs of energy conversion as well as to pollutants gas emission (CO 2 , SO x , NO x ). However the key problem which has to be considerate is to design suitable materials for boilers components such as heat exchangers that are resistant to creep, gas corrosion and hot corrosion at the operating temperature  [3 – 5] .  

Although, austenitic steels and nickel-based superalloys  [6 – 8] appear as attractive materials for their corrosion resistance, fer- ritic – martensitic steels are preferable due to their low thermal- expansion coefficients, higher low-cycle fatigue resistances, high- er thermal conductivities and lower costs. Recently modified ferritic – martensitic steels 9% Cr steels (P-91, P-92) and 12% Cr (HCM12-A) are interesting candidates with adequate creep strengths up to possibly 630 °C. Addition of suitable coating containing Al and/or Si for example can increase corrosion re- sistance of these materials to temperatures up to their mechanical property limits. Diffusion coating containing a sufficient amount of Al and Si capable of developing a protective scale in conditions oxidation are particularly interesting  [9 – 11] . Nevertheless tem- perature treatment of the selected coating process has to be below temperature of martensite transformation (700 °C  [12] ) in order to conserve mechanical properties of base materials. To achieve this goal, Chemical Vapour Deposition in Fluidized Bed Reactor (CVD-FBR) is particularly interesting because diffusion coatings can be obtained at relatively low temperatures ( T b 700 °C)  [13] and it has already been demonstrated to be an effective method to develop Al – Si coatings on AISI 304  [14] .  

The aim of this paper is firstly to develop and characterize Al – Si diffusion coatings obtained from Al and Si powder in fluidised reactor using H 2 /HCl mixing gas as activator. Ther- modynamics calculation were performed and used as guideline

The samples (10 mm×10 mm× ξ  with 1.5 b ξ b 2.5) were po- lished from 240-grit SiC paper up to 600-grit SiC paper and then ultrasonically cleaned in alcohol, dried and weighed prior to coating experiment.  

# 2.2. Coatings procedure  

# 2.2.1. Thermodynamic calculation  

Thermodynamic study of phase equilibrium (composition, partial pressure) during CVD were performed using Thermo- Calc computer program to determine the feasibility of metal deposition and provide a useful guideline to optimise deposition condition. Calculations are based on the free Gibbs energy minimisation code and mass conversion rule. SSUB3 and SOL2 databases (Scientific Group Thermodata Europe) were used to define chloride precursors and substrate respectively.  

# 2.2.2. Experimental conditions  

The fluidized bed reactor characteristics used in this study have been presented in previous works  [15] . The bed was composed of powder mixture of Al (99.5% purity, grain size 200  μ m) – silicon (97.5% purity, grain size about 40  μ m) as metallic donor. Bed − 1 particles were fluidized using an important Ar flow (1.6 L min ). Al- and Si-chloride precursors were generated at coating temperatures by reaction between metallic donor powder and an input gas mixture 2.4 vol.% HCl and 28 vol H 2 . Coatings were obtained using a two temperature steps procedure at 550 °C and 490 °C for 1 h and 30 min respectively. Some of the coated samples were heat-treated in a vertical furnace at 700 °C for 2 h under the inert gas Ar at atmospheric pressure.

# 3. Results  

# 3.1. Thermodynamics approximation  

Thermodynamic calculations were performed for system Al (s) /Si (s) /Ar/HCl/H 2 . Fe, Cr, Mn, W and Cu was also included in the system to consider the interaction between gas phase and BCC substrate.( Table 1 ). Input conditions used for calculation was guided by previous experimental studies  [14,15] . Between 400 °C and 600 °C, the main Al- and Si-chlorides precursors − 8   (partial pressure N 10 atm) obtained from this calculation are Al 2 Cl 6 , AlCl 3 , AlCl, AlCl 2 , AlCl 2 H, SiH 3 Cl. The partial pressures of the main Al and Si precursors determinated at 490 °C and 550 °C are presented in  Table 2 . AlCl 3  precursor is the main and the most stable chloride precursor present in all the temperature range studied whereas Al 2 Cl 6  partial pressure decreases with temperature. SiH 3 Cl partial pressure starts to decrease below the AlCl one around 540 °C, but is higher at lower temperatures (two times higher at 490 °C). We note that when BCC substrate is taking into account for calculations, no drastic effect is observed on the partial pressure of Al- and Si- chloride precursors. Nevertheless, new gaseous species from these calculations appear ( Table 3 ) to indicate to substrate attack by the gaseous atmosphere. Partial pressures of these species are relatively low and are assumed to no perturb Al – Si deposition.

These calculations provide interesting information about gaseous chloride precursors formed in the reactor starting from solid donor and about the dependence of these partial pressures on temperature, but are still insufficient for optimising coating conditions. In the case of conventional CVD process, deposition condition can be approximated using a so called CVD phase diagram  [16] . This calculation consist of determinating domain of thermodynamic stability of solid phase (deposit) susceptible to be deposited at varying initial input partial pressures of one metallic precursor versus initial partial pressure of the other one. In this study the main Al-precursors (AlCl 3 , AlCl and AlCl 2 H) and Si-precursors (SiH 3 Cl) determinate from the first calcula- tion ( Table 2 ) had been used to carry out a similar CVD phase diagram. Results obtained from each Al-precursors versus SiH 3 Cl are superposed in  Fig. 1 a and b for calculations per- formed at 490 °C and 550 °C respectively. Domains are se- parated by the line corresponding to yield formation of solid phase equal to zero. Shift inside of one domain will lead to an increase or decrease of the amount of the corresponding phase. Approximately the same domain shapes are observed for the two temperatures with a slight move of the boundary resulting in an increase or a decrease of the domain area.  

The diagram phase obtained with AlCl 3  (dash line) show only two possible domains. With high SiH 3 Cl partial pressure for- mation of Si solid can be expect whereas for low partial pressures no solid phase are promoted. Only gas mixture is present at equilibrium. At 490 °C, the map resulting from AlCl 2 H precursors is separated in two domains corresponding to Si+gas and Al+gas whereas a new region corresponding to Al+Si+gas appear bet- ween the former ones at 550 °C. From this condition of input gas it is impossible to find an equilibrium gas composition close to the one determinate in the reactor from reactions between solid donor (Al powder) and H 2 /HCl activator ( Table 2 ). So in this work it was postulated, as a guideline to optimising coating condition, which equilibrium partial pressures of the main precursors in the reactor were close to the initial pressures used to perform the conven- tional CVD map. Deposition domains identified change with the Al-chloride precursors considered and a competitive mechanism between each one can be assumed ( Table 4 ).  

In this way, the AlCl precursor is supposed to promote only Si deposition at 490 °C whereas Al/Si codeposition is expected to be favoured at 550 °C. In addition, the partial pressure of AlCl 2 H precursor is considered to favour only Al deposition at both temperatures whereas with AlCl 3  only gas phase is present at equilibrium. This observation can be correlated with the high stability of AlCl 3(g)  ( Δ G 500 °C = − 268  V 6 kJ) in this range of temperature.  

Based on this calculation it was chosen to apply a two step procedure at 550 °C and then 490 °C for 1 h and 30 min respectively. The aim of this procedure was to encourage Si deposition in the external surface of the diffusion coating.

# 4. Discussion  

In situ  reactions of Al and Si donor powder with H 2 /HCl activator lead to generation of Al- and Si-chloride mixture where partial pressure of each precursor fluctuates with tem- perature. From different studies on pack cementation coating process  [21,22] , it is now commonly accepted that codeposition

In this study ther- modynamic simulation were used to guide the choice of Al – Si codeposition condition in CVD-FBR at temperatures lower than 700 °C. Calculation had firstly been applied to determine equi- librium partial pressures of Al-and Si-precursors in the reactor versus temperature. It was found that temperatures ranging from 475 to 575 °C presented a particular interest because SiH 3 Cl precursors was more or less in the same range of AlCl and ACl 2 H chloride precursors. Additional calculations based on conven- tional CVD phase diagram at 490 °C and 550 °C showed that the SiH 3 Cl precursor with AlCl and/or AlCl 2 H will promote code- position of Al and Si whereas no codeposition domain where found with the AlCl 3  precursor. As no Al- and Si-chloride pre- cursors are used as input gas in CVD-FBR, this phase diagram cannot be used directly to determine deposition conditions. Con- sequently we assimilate equilibrium partial pressure of SiH 3 Cl with Al-chloride (AlCl 3 , AlCl, AlCl 2 H successively) determined at 490 °C and 550 °C as initial input precursors. This ap- proximation leads us to consider that a competitive mechanism between precursors can occur. As such, the AlCl precursor is supposed to promote only Si deposition at 490 °C and Al/Si codeposition at 550 °C. AlCl 2 H precursor is supposed to favour

The mechanism leading to the deposition of Al and Si at the surface is still widely discussed. As mentioned by Voudouris et al.  [23]  decomposition of lower halides can lead to the deposition of aluminium in the considered range of temperature.  

The following reactions of disproportionation also appear to be an important possible mechanism of deposition as partial pressure of involved precursors is appreciable.  

Furthermore, different studies  [9,24]  speak about possible displacement reaction between metallic element in the substrate and chloride precursors.  

In case of silicon, decomposition of SiH 3 Cl precursor to SiHCl+H 2  [25]  and then direct dissociation of SiHCl to Si and HCl is favoured ( Δ G 550 °C = − 111.946 kJ). Secondary, SiH 4 precursors can also participate in deposition through direct dissociation or by a mechanism close to the ones proposed for deposition of silicon from silane precursors  [26] .  

The deposited Al and Si atoms can then diffuse into the metallic substrate. Assuming that the high rate of mass transfer are encountered in the fluidised bed reactor the step of transport

It was confirmed that both Al and Si were deposited and diffused into the substrate. The Al powder used in the bed, which led to a high activity of Al in the vapour phase close to substrate surface, and the low temperature of the process resulted in the formation of Al-rich intermetallic phase as in the case of so-called  “ high activity coating ”  in pack cementation [28,29] . In this case coating is assumed to grow dominantly by Al inward diffusion. As no Fe – Si or Fe – Al – Si phases were identified it is probable that the Si deposited is present in the form of solid solution of Fe 2 Al 5 − x Si x  [17] . The coatings obtained in this study are thinner than those obtained previously on AISI304  [14]  probably because a lower HCl amount and lower temperature were used but also because of the ferritic structure of substrate. The subsequent diffusion treatment ap- plied at 700 °C on the coated substrate led to an increase of the coating thickness and to a transformation of the brittle Fe 2 Al 5 phase into lower Al intermetallic phases, principally Fe 3 Al. The occurrence of precipitates during this treatment is certainly linked to the mechanism of phase transformation and coating growth that may occur by both inward Al and outward Fe diffusion  [28,29] . Composition of these precipitates had to be analysed more in detail but they may be formed of slow diffusing element (such as Cr) in addition to elements having a low solubility in the corresponding intermetallic phase. As an example, Palm  [18]  reported for the Fe – Cr – Al system a maxi- mum solubility of 6.2 at.% of Cr in the Fe 2 Al 5  and Maitra and Grupta  [17]  gave Si a solubility ranging from to 1.48 to 7.57 in the Fe 2 Al 5  phase with an increasing value for the Fe-rich phase. The higher amount of Si at the external surface of coatings still observed after heat treatment seems to indicate that Si is not a fast diffusing element within the Fe – Al intermetallic phase. As  

Al/Si deposition had been performed successfully at low tem- perature on ferritic substrate, this coating had now to be evaluated in high temperature oxidation environment.  

# 5. Conclusions  

The aim of the work reported in this paper was to obtain coating Al/Si diffusion on three ferritic steels through a low temperature coating process ( b 700 °C) in order to conserve the mechanical properties of the base material. In this way, Chemical Vapour Deposition in Fluidized Bed Reactor (CVD-FBR) tech- nique had been chosen and thermodynamics calculation was used as a guide to optimise CVD condition to perform Al- and Si codeposition in the desired range of temperature. Based on this calculation, a two temperature step procedures (550 °C and then 490 °C) was applied to promote deposition of Si in the external surface of diffusion coatings. Diffusion coatings obtained on P-91, P-92, HCM-12 ferritic steels are approximately 3.5, 2.7 and 3.5  μ m thick respectively and was principally constituted of Fe 2 Al 5  containing around 4 – 5 Si at.% and 4 – 6 Cr at.%. During a subsequent heat treatment the brittle Fe 2 Al 5  phase was transformed by diffusion into lower Al-content interme- tallic phase.
