Abstract—The formation of precipitate free zones(PFZ)along large and small angle grain boundaries has been investigated for bi- and polycrystals of the nickel-base superalloy NIMONIC PE16. Since titanium is a constituent ofthey'-precipitates,its depletion along the grain boundaries leads to the dissolution of they'-particles andhencetoPFZs.The former belief that they are due to chromium-rich grain boundary carbides is disproved.

# 1.INTRODUCTION  

Grain boundaries (GB) may affect the yield strength of precipitation hardened polycrystals via two different channels:  

(i) GBs may act as dislocation sources and they limit the slip length. (ii) There may be precipitate-free zones (PFZ) along the GBs. These PFZs soften the material [1-7]. toPFZs: (i)Lossofquenched-in excess vacancies from the interior of the grains totheGBs[8-15].The vacancies are considered as necessary for the nucleation of the precipitates. (ii)Some element E which is essential for the formation of the desired precipitates Pn is lost from the grains because E precipitates in the GBs.The index “H'” of Ph stands for  

"hardening" precipitates. E may be "essential" intwoways:direct andindirect.
