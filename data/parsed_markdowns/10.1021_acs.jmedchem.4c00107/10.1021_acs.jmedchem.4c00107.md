ABSTRACT:  The epidermal growth factor receptor (EGFR) tertiary C797S mutation is an important cause of resistance to Osimertinib, which seriously hinders the clinical application of Osimertinib. Developing proteolysis-targeting chimeras (PRO- TACs) targeting EGFR mutants can offer a promising strategy to overcome drug resistance. In this study, some novel PROTACs targeting C797S mutation were designed and synthesized based on a new EGFR inhibitor and displayed a potent degradation effect in H1975-TM cells harboring EGFR L858R/T790M/C797S . The represen- tative compound  C6  exhibited a DC 50  of 10.2 nM against EGFR and an IC 50  of 10.3 nM against H1975-TM. Furthermore,  C6  also showed potent degradation activity against various main EGFR mutants, including EGFR Del19/T790M/C797S . Mechanistic studies revealed that the protein degradation was achieved through the ubiquitin − proteasome system. Finally,  C6  inhibited tumor growth in the H1975-TM xenograft tumor model effectively and safely. This study identifies a novel and potent EGFR PROTAC to overcome Osimertinib resistance mediated by C797S mutation.  

# INTRODUCTION  

Lung cancer remains a global health challenge with high mortality and morbidity, and the epidermal growth factor receptor has been proven to be an important target in the − 4  

Inhibitors targeting EGFR have been greatly developed, and three generations of EGFR inhibitors have been approved for marketing.   Among them, the third-generation EGFR inhibitor Osimertinib can effectively inhibit the T790M mutant by covalently binding to Cys797, overcoming the drug resistance problem caused by the T790M mutation.   Although Osimertinib has successfully overcome T790M mutation- mediated resistance and achieved remarkable success, a newly acquired resistance inevitably appears during the treatment process. The C797S mutation significantly reduces the efficacy of Osimertinib by hindering its covalent binding to EGFR.   Therefore, there is an urgent need to develop a new therapeutic strategy to overcome intractable drug resistance.  

PROTAC (proteolysis-targeting chimera) technology has recently become one of the most popular technologies in new drug development. − 12   PROTAC is a bifunctional molecule that includes a POI (protein of interest) ligand, an E3 ligase ligand, and a linker connecting the two ligands. Unlike the traditional “occupancy-driven” approach of small-molecule inhibitors, PROTAC adopts an innovative “event-driven” approach.   The significant feature of this strategy is that it can effectively degrade target proteins through catalytic amounts of drugs to achieve therapeutic functions. Compared with traditional small-molecule inhibitors, PRO- TAC has the advantages of overcoming drug resistance, targeting undruggable proteins, and high target selectivity. ,  

Given the advantages of PROTAC, many PROTACs targeting EGFR have been developed. ,   However, most EGFR PROTACs are designed based on first-, second-, and third- generation small inhibitors, which selectively degrade EGFR Del19 , EGFR L858R , or EGFR L858R/T790M , but lack effective degradation activity against the C797S mutant ( Figure 1 ). − 27  

Currently, a few PROTACs targeting the C797S mutation have been reported and they are generally designed based on the allosteric inhibitor EAI001 and the orthosteric inhibitor Brigatinib ( Figure 2 ). ,   Despite the efficacy of these PROTACs in degrading the C797S mutant  in vitro , there have been no approvals for clinical trials. Therefore, the EGFR PROTACs targeting C797S mutation still need further development.

Binding Model Analysis of the Ternary Complex.  To investigate the effect of compound  C6  on the formation of the ternary complex, we conducted molecular docking and MD (molecular dynamics) simulations. The results indicated that the system tended to stabilize after 100 ns, with  C6  recruiting CRBN to the surface of EGFR protein to form a tight ternary complex ( Figure 6 A,B). In the complex, compound  C6  formed two hydrogen bonds with Met793 of EGFR and two hydrogen bonds with His378 and Trp380 of CRBN, which contributed to formation of a stable ternary complex and was the reason for the potent degradation ability of PROTAC.  

In Vitro  Antitumor Efficacy of Compound C6 on H1975-TM.  We used the H1975-TM cell line harboring EGFR L858R/T790M/C797S   to evaluate the  in vitro  antitumor efficacy of compound  C6 . First, we evaluated the effect of C6  on colony formation of H1975-TM. The results in  Figure 7 A demonstrated that compound  C6  inhibited the growth of H1975-TM significantly. When treated with  C6  at a concentration of 33 nM, the colony formation of H1975-TM was completely abolished, which was remarkably more potent than that of Brigatinib and  D51 . In addition, compound  C6 arrested H1975-TM cells in the G0/G1 phase and promoted cell apoptosis in a concentration-dependent manner ( Figure 7 B,C). Furthermore, we conducted a Western blot assay to investigate the impact of  C6  on EGFR and downstream signaling pathways ( Figure 7 D). Compound  C6  dose-depend- ently suppressed EGFR phosphorylation and downstream ERK and AKT activation, which was also significantly more potent than  D51 . All these results indicated that compound  C6 exhibited promising  in vitro  antitumor efficacy.

# CONCLUSIONS  

In this study, we designed and synthesized a series of EGFR PROTACs targeting C797S mutation and conducted a series of biological evaluations. These PROTAC molecules demon- strated significant degradation activity against EGFR L858R/T790M/C797S   protein and exhibited potent antiproli- ferative activity against H1975-TM harboring EGFR L858R/T790M/C797S . The top compound  C6  displayed a DC 50  of 10.2 nM against EGFR L858R/T790M/C797S   and an IC 50  of 10.3 nM against H1975-TM. Mechanistic studies revealed that compound  C6  degraded the EGFR protein through the ubiquitin − proteasome system. Furthermore, compound  C6 also exhibited potent degradation activity against various EGFR mutants, including EGFR Del19/T790M/C797S , while sparing the EGFR WT . Compound  C6  exerted a profound inhibitory effect on the clonogenicity of H1975-TM, arrested the cell cycle in the G0/G1 phase, promoted apoptosis, and blocked the activation of EGFR downstream signaling pathways. Finally, the oral administration of  C6  effectively and safely suppressed tumor growth in the H1975-TM xenograft tumor model. In conclusion, we have identified a novel and effective EGFR PROTAC targeting the C797S mutation for the treatment of Osimertinib resistance.

The CDOCK module (Discovery Studio 2019) was used to perform the semi-flexible docking of complex between compound  6  and EFGR (PDB: 7VRE) with the default values of the protocol. The docking results were presented by the DS visual interface using “Hydrophobic surface”.  

To model the structure of the ternary complex, we used the ZDOCK module with default parameters and protocol to obtain the protein − protein complex of CRBN (PDB: 4TZ4) with EGFR (PDB: 7VRE). Then, AutoDock Vina was used in receptor − ligand docking, and the pocket was defined in the binding surface of the two proteins. The number of points in each dimension of XYZ was defined as 30, and the grid space was set as 0.375 Å. The value of exhaustiveness in the docking process was defined as 8.  

The docking model of the ternary complex was used as the initial structure for MD simulations. MD was performed with GROMACS 2020.6. The structure of the protein − protein complex was handled via the AMBER14sb force field whereas the general AMBER force field (GAFF) was employed for the PROTAC, and Multiwfn was used to fit the restrained electrostatic potential 2 (RESP2) charge.  

Periodic boundary conditions were used in the calculation process, and the ternary complex was placed in the center of a cubic box with a boundary distance of 1 nm. The structure was solvated with the TIP3P water model and neutralized with chloride ions. NVT and NPT equilibration were performed after energy minimization of system with the steepest descent method. Production MD simulation was performed for 200 ns with V  − rescale temperature coupling and Parrinello − Rahman pressure coupling at NPT in 300 K. The van der Waals and electrostatics in the calculation were treated as cutoff and PME, respectively.  

Microscale Thermophoresis Binding Assay.  The binding affinities of the compound with the EGFR L858R/T790M/C797S   were measured using the Monolith NT.115. The EGFR L858R/T790M/C797S was kept in the reaction buffer (PBS-P, 1 mM TCEP) at a concentration of 10  μ M and then labeled according to the protocol of Protein Labeling Kit RED-NHS Second Generation (Cat #  MO- L011). A range of concentrations of the compound (range from 100 to 0.00153  μ M) was mixed with labeled EGFR L858R/T790M/C797S   and incubated in the dark for 10 min. Then, the mixtures were loaded into 16 hydrophilic glass capillaries, and binding was measured by monitoring the thermophoresis with 60% LED power on Monolith NT.115.  

Plasmids and Lentivirus Infection.  H1975, PC-9, and A549 cells were purchased from the American Type Culture Collection (ATCC) and cultured in the recommended culture medium. Stable cell lines H1975-TM (EGFR L858R/T790M/C797S ) and PC-9-TM (EGFR Del19/T790M/C797S ) were constructed by lentivirus infection. Plasmids harboring EGFR L858R/T790M/C797S   and EGFR Del19/T790M/C797S were first packed in HEK-293T cells to produce lentivirus. Following a 48 h lentivirus infection, the H1975-TM cells and PC-9-TM cells were chosen using 2 and 3  μ g/mL of puromycin, respectively. PCR and Sanger sequencing were then used to confirm the stable cell lines. In Vitro  Cell Proliferation Inhibition Assay.  Cells were seeded in a 96-well culture plate (NEST Biotechnology), cultured overnight, and treated with compounds of different concentrations for 72 h. The cell counting kit-8 (CCK-8) assay evaluated the inhibitory activity of compounds on growth. Absorbance values were measured at 450 nm with a microplate detector (Bio Tek, USA).  

Colony Formation Assay.  H1975-TM cells were seeded in a six- well plate (NEST Biotechnology) at 2000 cells/well and incubated overnight at 37  ° C. The next day, the original medium was discarded and treated with the indicated compounds. Medium with fresh drugs was changed every 3 days. After 14 days of culture, cells were fixed with methanol and stained with crystal violet.  

Cell Apoptosis and Cycle Assay.  H1975-TM cells were seeded into six-well plates for 24 h and then treated with compound for another 24 h. Apoptosis of the H1975-TM cell line was measured by Annexin V and propidium iodide (PI) dual staining using an Annexin V-FITC Apoptosis Detection Kit (Vazyme,  # A211-02). Assays were performed according to standard protocols, and signals were detected using a FACSCelesta (Becton Dickinson). For the cell cycle assay, cells were collected and treated with 70% ethanol at  − 20  ° C for 24 h. The cells were then washed twice with cold PBS and stained with RNase A and PI (Beyotime, C1052).  

Western Blot.  Cells were lysed in RIPA buffer, and protein concentrations were quantified using the BCA Kit (Vazyme,  # E112- 01). After heating for 3 min at 95  ° C, samples were loaded onto 8 or 12% SDS-PAGE gels, followed by transfer onto PVDF membranes. Membranes were blocked with 5% BSA-TBST and then blotted with primary antibodies against EGFR (Proteintech, 66455-1-Ig), phospho-EGFR (Tyr1068) (CST, 3777s), ERK1/2 (Proteintech, 16443-1-AP), phospho-ERK (Thr202/Tyr204) (CST, 4370), AKT (Proteintech, 10176-2-AP), phospho-AKT (Ser473) (Proteintech, 66444-1-Ig), and GAPDH (Proteintech, 60004-1-Ig), followed by treatment with HRP-conjugated secondary antibodies.  

Proteomics Assay.  H1975-TM cells were seeded in 10 cm dishes for 80 − 90% confluency and treated with 50 nM  C6  or DMSO for 24 h. Each group contains three independent replicates. Subsequently, the cells were harvested and lysed in SDT lysis buffer (4% SDS, 100 mM Tris − HCl, pH 7.6). The samples were subjected to subsequent experimental procedures and bioinformatic analysis, which was performed at Shanghai Applied Protein Technology Co., Ltd. The proteins were extracted and quantified with a BCA Protein Assay Kit (Bio-Rad, USA). Digestion of protein was performed according to the FASP procedure. LC-MS/MS analysis was performed on a timsTOF Pro mass spectrometry (Bruker) that was coupled to Nanoelute (Bruker). The MS raw data for each sample were combined and searched using the MaxQuant 1.6.14 software for identification and quantitation analysis. The volcano plot of the  − log10 ( P  value) vs log2 (fold change of control group vs  C6 ) is presented. Proteins with − log10 ( P  value) > 2 ( P  value <0.01) with fold change >1.3 or <0.77 were considered to be significantly changed.  P  values were analyzed by two-tailed unpaired  t  tests. The down-regulated proteins were blasted against the online Kyoto Encyclopedia of Genes and Genomes (KEGG) database ( http://geneontology.org/ ) to retrieve their KEGG Orthology identifications and were subsequently mapped to pathways in KEGG.  

Animals.  The experimental animal and protocol were carried out according to IACUC of China Pharmaceutical University. The animals used for pharmacokinetic study were 10 male SPF SD rats (6 − 8 weeks, 200 − 230 g), and the animals used for the efficacy study were 18 female SPF BALB/c nude mice. All animals used in studies were purchased from Chang Zhou Cavens Laboratory Animal, Ltd. (Changzhou, China). Experimental animals were subjected to 1 week of acclimatization after receiving, followed by research. The temperature of the animal room was 20 − 26  ° C, the humidity was 40 − 70%, 12 h of light and darkness alternately, free drinking and feeding.  

P harm a co kinetics (PK) Study.  The 10 male SPF SD rats were divided into two groups, and compound  C6  was given by gavage (50 mg/kg) and tail vein injection (5 mg/kg). About 0.4 mL of blood was collected from the orbit at 5 min, 15 min, 30 min, 1 h, 2 h, 3 h, 5 h, 7 h, 9 h, 12 h, and 24 h after administration. The concentration of  C6  in rat plasma samples was determined by LC-MS/MS, and the pharmacokinetic parameters were calculated by WinNolin software.  

In Vivo  Antitumor Efficacy Assay.  Under sterile conditions, H1975-TM cells in the logarithmic growth stage were inoculated subcutaneously into the right armpit of BALB/c nude mice, and the cell inoculation volume was 5  ×  10 6 . The vernier caliper was used to measure the diameter of the transplanted tumor in nude mice. When the tumor grew to about 80 − 90 mm 3 , 18 nude mice with good growth status and good tumor size uniformity were selected and randomly divided into three groups of six mice each, which was the control group (saline), 25 mg/kg  C6  group, and 100 mg/kg  C6 group, and administered according to the dose setting table. The dosage form was a liquid solution, and the administration route involved oral gavage with a once-daily frequency. The antitumor effect of the compound was dynamically observed by measuring the tumor diameter. The tumor diameter and body weight of the nude mice were measured once every other day. After 31 days of administration, the nude mice were sacrificed and the tumors were removed and weighed.  

H&E and Ki67 Staining.  Major organs and tumors of mice were fixed with 4% formaldehyde and then embedded in paraffin and sliced. Sections were stained with hematoxylin and eosin or anti-Ki67 and photographed by light microscopy.
