# A B S T R A C T  

# A R T I C L E I N F O  

Study on semi-solid processing of superalloy is little reported due to the di ﬃ culties in fabricating semi-solid billets. In this manuscript, a novel method called semi-solid isothermal treatment of wrought superalloy (SSITWS) was  ﬁ rstly proposed in order to fabricate semi-solid billets of superalloy. The e ﬀ ects of isothermal temperature and soaking time on microstructure of Ni-based superalloy GH4037 in the semi-solid state were investigated. The results show that the near-globular microstructure of wrought GH4037 superalloy can be obtained by the semi-solid isothermal treatment. The average grain size increased and the roundness decreased with the prolonged soaking time at 1350 °C and 1360 °C. When the isothermal temperature was elevated to 1370 °C or 1380 °C, the microstructure evolution was complex due to the combination of breaking up mechanism and coarsening mechanism. At 1390 °C, the dendritic grains were obviously observed in the microstructure. With the increasing of isothermal temperature and soaking time, the liquid boundary  ﬁ lm coarsening behavior oc- curred in the microstructure. With the increasing of soaking time, the size of intragranular liquid droplets in- creased, but the amount of them decreased. When the isothermal temperature was high, the intragranular liquid droplets disappeared. The coarsening rate constant at 1360 °C was lower compared to that at 1350 °C. However, when the isothermal temperature was higher than 1370 °C, the coarsening kinetics was not suitable for de- scribing the microstructure evolution. The discrete carbide precipitates appeared in the grain boundaries. The distribution of Ti and Mo were higher in the grain boundaries than that in solid grains, and the distribution of other elements was homogenous in the grain boundaries and inside the grains.

# 1. Introduction  

Nickle-based superalloys are widely utilized in gas-turbine engines and turbine blades because of their good performances of high-tem- perature strength, toughness, oxidation resistance and corrosion re- sistance [ 1 , 2 ]. There are various microstructure features including grain size,  γ 'size, element distribution, carbide-phase content and grain- boundary morphology which can in ﬂ uence the mechanical properties of Ni-based superalloys [ 3 ]. Although cast and wrought Ni-based su- peralloys are commonly used in many  ﬁ elds, there are still some defects of them fabricated by conventional forming processes. The micro- structure of Ni-based superalloys by traditional casting forming pro- cesses is dendritic morphology and exhibits a strong tendency of mi- crosegregation [ 4 – 6 ]. Traditional forging processes show improved performances of parts and elongated lifetime of moulds because of the appropriate plastic deformation at low temperatures, while the geo- metric complexity of the components limits the application of forging processes [ 7 ].  

complex parts can be formed by SSP [ 11 ].  

Semi-solid forming involves rheoforming and thixoforming [ 11 ]. Rheoforming of high melting point alloys has not been widely in- vestigated because of hard fabrication of the semi-solid slurry. As for Ni-based superalloys, it is possible for them to be fabricated semi-solid parts by thixoforming and the preparation of semi-solid billets plays the key role in this process. The strain-induced melting activation (SIMA) [ 12 ] and the recrystallization and partial remelting (RAP) [ 13 ] are two promising methods to fabricate semi-solid billets with near-globular microstructure. The main di ﬀ erence between SIMA and RAP method is the deformation temperature before the semi-solid isothermal treat- ment. SIMA involves a hot working process where the treatment tem- perature is above the recrystallization temperature, while RAP involves a warm working process where the treatment temperature is below recrystallization temperature. As for SIMA, a cold deformation in room temperature such as upsetting also need to be applied to the hot worked materials before semi-solid isothermal treatment. It indicates that the SIMA method needs a longer procedure than RAP. The semi-solid billets fabricated by SIMA method lead to high cost due to the cold de- formation and complex procedures, and the semi-solid billets fabricated by RAP method involve the procedure of warm working. A large number of low melting point alloys have been investigated, including aluminum alloys [ 14 – 16 ] and magnesium alloys [ 17 , 18 ]. Nowadays, the usage of some high melting point alloys has gradually become a new hot spot in the  ﬁ eld of semi-solid processing. However, the researches on the semi-solid processing of high melting point alloys are mainly focused on steels [ 19 – 21 ] and titanium alloys [ 22 , 23 ]. Microstructure evolution on steel and titanium alloy has received more attention due to its importance to fabricating semi-solid slurry. Yi Meng et al. [ 24 ] in- vestigated the microstructure evolution of the hot-working tool steel SKD61 in the semi-solid state, and validated the feasibility of RAP process for the fabrication of SKD61 stock with a uniform spherical microstructure. Microstructure evolution of Ti14 in semi-solid state showed that more Ti 2 Cu precipitated on grain boundaries at a higher deformation temperature [ 25 ]. Compared with research on SSP of steel and titanium alloy, research on SSP of Ni-based superalloys was little reported. It may be due to the high forming temperature and complex forming processes. The only investigation from E.D. Manson-Whitton et al. [ 26 ] showed the microstructures of IN718 Ni-based superalloy in semi-solid state consisted of rounded solid grains surrounded by grain boundary liquid  ﬁ lms and the rate of semi-solid grain coarsening in- creased with decreasing temperature.  

In order to decrease the cost and shorten the forming processing of SIMA and RAP, it is necessary to develop a new method without complex hot and warm working in the fabricating process of semi-solid billets. Therefore, in this paper, a novel method called semi-solid iso- thermal treatment of wrought superalloy (SSITWS) for fabricating semi- solid billets is  ﬁ rstly developed to reduce the cost and shorten the procedures. Based on SSITWS method, the semi-solid samples of wrought superalloy GH4037 were fabricated, and the e ﬀ ects of iso- thermal temperature and soaking time on microstructure evolution of the billets were investigated.

# 3. Results and Discussion  

3.1. A Novel Method Called Semi-solid Isothermal Treatment of Wrought Superalloy (SSITWS)  

As shown in  Fig. 2 , conventional SIMA method involves hot working above recrystallization temperature and cold deformation at the room temperature before the semi-solid isothermal treatment. While RAP method includes warm working below recrystallization temperature before semisolid isothermal treatment. These two preparation methods of semi-solid billets requires a long processing procedure, so the cost is relatively high to fabricate semi-solid billets. In this paper, a new pre- paration method called semi-solid isothermal treatment of wrought superalloy (SSITWS) of semi-solid billets is put forward. It de ﬁ nes a process for fabricating semi-solid billets of superalloy, in which super- alloy  ﬁ rstly undergoes a hot working procedure, then directly heated to semi-solid temperature and soaked for a period of time to obtain semi- solid billets with spheroidal grains.  

In contrast to SIMA, this method does not need a cold deformation procedure before the isothermal heat treatment. It indicates that this novel method will reduce the fabrication cost of semi-solid billets. The commercial superalloys are commonly supplied in the hot working state such as hot rolling state and hot extruding state. In other words, the commercial hot-rolling or hot-extrusion superalloys with hot working state can be directly used for semisolid isothermal treatment. It in- dicates that shorter procedures and lower cost are achieved in the SSITWS method. Lower resistance to deformation can be achieved in the SSITWS method compared with RAP. Furthermore, commercial warm-deformation superalloys are di ﬃ cult to be obtained directly due to dominant supply of hot-deformation superalloys. Therefore, it also increases the procedures and cost for RAP process of superalloys.

Fig. 5  shows the microstructure evolution of GH4037 samples after semi-solid isothermal treatment at 1350 °C for di ﬀ erent soaking times. It can be seen that the semi-solid microstructure consists of solid phase and liquid phase. The solid phase almost exists in the form of solid grains. The liquid phase mainly exists in the form of liquid drops (LD) inside of solid grains, liquid  ﬁ lm (LF) on grain boundaries and grain boundary liquid (GBL). As shown in  Fig. 5 (a), the solid grains of the samples soaked for 10 min at 1350 °C exhibited extremely irregular shape and non-homogeneous grain size, and a large number of liquid droplets dispersed inside the solid grains. When the soaking time in- creased to 30 min, as shown in  Fig. 5 (c), the grain sizes were still in- homogeneous, and some distinct polygonal grains remained. When the soaking time increased to 40 min or more, as shown in  Fig. 5 (d) – (f), the grains became approximately spherical, the grain sizes were roughly the same. With the increasing of soaking time, the number of liquid droplets inside the grains became less while the size of them became larger, and the grain boundary liquid  ﬁ lm became thicker.  

Fig. 6  shows the microstructure of GH4037 samples after isothermal treatment at 1360 °C for di ﬀ erent soaking times. It can be found that the microstructure evolution is nearly similar to 1350 °C. With the in- creasing of soaking time, the grains became larger and more globular. By comparison, the liquid boundary  ﬁ lm coarsening behavior was more obvious in the microstructure at 1360 °C. When the soaking time was long, this behavior was particularly noticeable as shown in  Fig. 6 (e) and (f). The grain morphologies of the grains were more spherical even at the beginning of isothermal treatment at 1360 °C. Compared with the microstructure of samples at 1350 °C, the intragranular liquid droplets were larger and the number of them decreased at 1360 °C.  

Fig. 7  shows the microstructure of GH4037 samples after semi-solid isothermal treatment at 1370 °C for di ﬀ erent soaking times. Similarly, the liquid boundary  ﬁ lm coarsening behavior was obvious with the increasing of soaking time. As shown in  Fig. 7 , the number of the in- tragranular liquid droplets decreased at 1370 °C compared to them at 1350 °C and 1360 °C. The microstructure evolution at 1370 °C was di ﬀ erent with these two temperatures. There are two trends in the microstructure, coarsening trend caused by grains coalescence or Ost- wald ripening [ 28 ] and re ﬁ ning trend due to the in ﬂ uence of in- tragranular liquid droplets. As shown in  Fig. 7 (a), some adjacent liquid droplets inside the solid grains gathered, and some liquid droplets near to grain boundaries combined with the grain liquid  ﬁ lm (indicated in red arrows in  Fig. 7 (a)). Meanwhile, a large number of the large ag- glomerates existed inside the solid grains. When coarsening trend was dominant, average grain size of solid grains increased, resulting in coarsening of solid grains ( Fig. 7 (b) – (c)). At the same time, the larger liquid droplets broke the grain boundaries resulting in the formation of smaller isolated grains ( Fig. 7 (d) – (e)). With the increasing of soaking time, some new isolated grains formed. Because of the formation of new isolated grains, the average grain size was relatively smaller, but the grain morphology was extremely irregular. The similar results were reported [ 24 ]. In addition, it can be noted that when some new isolated grains created the number of intragranular liquid droplets obviously decreased, as shown in  Fig. 7 (d) – (f). It is because intragranular liquid droplets moved and contacted with liquid  ﬁ lm in grain boundaries.

The essence of GH4037 semi-solid microstructure evolution can be explained by the in ﬂ uences of isothermal temperature and soaking time on near-globular grains. As shown in  Figs. 5 and 6 , when the samples were treated in the semi-solid state with low liquid fraction, the liquid phase  ﬁ rstly appeared at grain boundaries. The grain boundary coar- sening behavior occurred at the same time. Meanwhile, with the melting of equilibrium eutectic structures, the isolated liquid droplets appeared inside the solid grains. With the increasing of soaking time, the grain boundaries coarsened extremely, and the grains became globular. As shown in  Fig. 10 , the average grain size increased with the prolonged of soaking time at 1350 °C and 1360 °C. The coarsening be- havior can be explained by the mechanism of Ostwald ripening or coarsening [ 27 ]. This mechanism was observed in the semi-solid state where the liquid phase dispersed in solid phase. During coarsening, the average size of solid grains increased and the number of solid grains decreased. In this process, the driving force was the decrease of inter- facial energy due to the decrease of interfacial area. When isothermal temperatures were in the semi-solid region, the interfacial energy in- evitably increased with the melting of low meting point components. A considerable amount of free energy was stored as the form of interfacial energy in the semi-solid system. According to the principle of minimum free energy, when external conditions stayed unchangeable, the system with constant components could achieve a stable energy balance only in the state of the lowest free energy. Due to the existence of large amount of interfacial energy in the semi-solid state, the free energy was rela- tively high, which resulted in an unstable balance state. It will auto- matically change to equilibrium until reaching to the minimum free energy. Because the semi-solid system contained partial liquid phase, the small solid grains would be melting gradually, while the large grains would become coarsening and spherical under the in ﬂ uence of interfacial energy. So in order to decrease the interfacial energy, the average size of solid grains increased. Consequently, the area of the solid-liquid interface in the semi-solid system reduced and the energy of the system decreased. According to the Ostwald ripening theory, the process of grain spheroidization was slow just in terms of atomic dif- fusion. Therefore, it took a period of time to promote the roundness at 1350 °C and 1360 °C.  

With the increasing of temperature, the microstructure evolution was complex. As shown in  Fig. 10 , the changes of average grain size and roundness are not regular at 1370 °C and 1380 °C. It could not be ex- plained only by the mechanisms such as coalescence and Ostwald ri- pening, the breaking up mechanism also occurred in this process. As the isothermal temperature was 1370 °C, at the beginning of reheating, a portion of the adjacent liquid droplets inside the solid grains assembled and formed new isolated grains, while some liquid droplets near the grain boundaries combined with the grain liquid  ﬁ lm and broke the former grain boundaries. The large solid grains were divided into tiny grains, so the roundness of grains was very large. With the combination of breaking up mechanism and coarsening mechanism, the average grain size was complicated. The similar results were observed at 1380 °C. When the soaking time was less than 30 min at 1380 °C, the breaking up mechanism was dominant in this process. The average grain size decreased and the roundness increased. With the increasing of soaking time, the breaking up mechanism ended and the coarsening mechanism played a major role in this process. As a result, the average grain size increased and the roundness decreased.  

In the case of low liquid fraction, a large number of liquid droplets existed in the solid grains. The intragranular liquid droplets formed because the incipiently melted solid grains were inhomogeneous be- cause of the composition segregation [ 29 ]. With the increasing of soaking time, the size of liquid droplets increased and the number of liquid droplets decreased. It can be analyzed by the coarsening theory. With the prolonged soaking time, some adjacent liquid particles got together into large liquid droplets and became spherical while some

It clearly describes the grain morphology and participates in the grain boundaries. As shown in  Fig. 12 , the grain boundaries were  ﬁ lled with discrete carbide precipitates. The energy dispersive spectrometer (EDS) analysis results of three positions (as shown in  Fig. 12 ) are listed in Table 3 . It reveals that the discrete carbide precipitates in the grain boundaries and inside the cells are much the same. However, the chemical composition in region A is di ﬀ erent from that in region C. As for the references [ 32 ,  33 ] and the EDS results, the grain boundaries were mainly  ﬁ lled with Cr 23 C 6  carbides, meanwhile the TiC carbides probably dispersed inside the grains. So the liquid phase  ﬁ rstly appeared in grain boundaries during the isothermal treatment. Besides, these elements could cause compo- nent segregation, which resulted in the di ﬀ erent melting point of phases in the solid grains and grain boundaries.  

Fig. 13 (a) shows the scanning electron microscopy (SEM) image of GH4037 alloy after semisolid isothermal treatment at 1370 °C for 40 min. The coarse grain boundaries were observed which indicated the serious coarsening behavior.  Fig. 13 (b) shows the EDS analysis across the grain boundary. It can be seen that the contents of Ti, Al and Mo were higher in grain boundaries than that inside the solid grains. Al- though the content was small, the element of C was relatively higher in the grain boundaries. The distribution of the Cr and W was relatively homogeneous according to the EDS line scanning results.  

Fig. 14  shows the map scanning of GH4037 alloy after semi-solid isothermal treatment at 1370 °C for 10 min. According to the map scanning analysis, the distribution of main elements in the solid grains and the boundaries is obvious. It can be seen that the element Ti dis- tributed more at the grain boundaries and dispersed inside the grains in the form of TiC carbides. The elements of Mo, Al and C were rich in the grain boundaries, the distribution of Cr and W were homogenous in the grain boundaries and inside the grains.  

# 4. Conclusions  

In this work, the semisolid isothermal treatment experiments were carried out on the samples of the initial GH4037 superalloy bar for gaining the near-globular microstructure. The e ﬀ ects of the soaking time and the isothermal temperature on the microstructure evolution in the semi-solid state were investigated. The following conclusions can be obtained.  

(1) The near-globular microstructure can be obtained by semisolid isothermal treatment. The average grain size increased and the roundness decreased with the prolonged soaking time at 1350 °C and 1360 °C. When the isothermal temperature reached 1370 °C and 1380 °C, the microstructure evolution was complex due to the combination of breaking up mechanism and coarsening mechanism.
