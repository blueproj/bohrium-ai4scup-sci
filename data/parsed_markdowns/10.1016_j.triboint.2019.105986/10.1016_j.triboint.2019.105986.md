# ABSTRACT:  

Fretting fatigue failure behavior of DD6 Nickel-based single crystal superalloys dovetail  attachment at 600    has been investigated. The contact surface material is oxidized at high  temperature and the oxides are compacted on the contact surface. The contact surface is  delaminated and many pits are formed, which result in stress concentration near the wear pits and  promote crack initiation. All the dovetail specimens fail at the contact leading edge area and  along (111) [ 110  ] and (  111  ) [110] slip systems, which are resulted from the large resolved  shear stress at the contact edge. Crystallographic plasticity finite element method is used to  obtain the contact stress and strain, and the calculated maximum damage failure area agrees well  with the experimental results.  

Keywords:  Nickel-based single crystal, Dovetail attachment, High temperature fretting fatigue,  Fretting wear.

# 1.   Introduction  

The blade-disk connecting structure (such as tenon/mortise structure and dovetail attachment)  in gas turbine engine is prone to fretting fatigue damage due to high contact stresses and small  displacements on the blade/disk interfaces induced by varying centrifugal load and vibration [1- 3]. Fretting fatigue promotes crack initiation and has been identified one of the important cause  for the reduced service life in gas turbine engine [4]. Nickel-based single crystal (NBSX)  superalloys are commonly used in turbine blades for its superior fatigue resistance at high  temperature [5, 6]. However, the working environment with high temperature and high rotate  speed complicates the fretting fatigue behavior of NBSX superalloys turbine blade. Therefore, it  is of great significance to carry out research on fretting fatigue of NBSX blade under real  working conditions.  

Extensive experiments have been conducted to investigate the fretting fatigue behavior of  different metals by means of  Single clamp fretting fatigue test  or  Bridge-type fretting fatigue test   etc. [7]. It has been found that more than 50 different factors affect fretting fatigue behaviors [8,  9], such as slip amplitude [8], tangential force [10] and coefficient of friction [11]. However, the  interfacial stresses in blade-disk structure are oscillatory under real working condition, which  greatly affect the fretting fatigue process [12]. Two typical blade-disk connecting structures are  shown in  Fig. 1 , the stress states on the contact surface of the two kinds of structure are similar.  Due to the simple structure of dovetail attachment, it is often applied to investigate the fretting  fatigue behavior of blade-disk connecting structure. The earlier fretting fatigue test of dovetail  attachment was designed by Ruiz et. al [13], the dovetail specimen was constrained by a  rectangular sheet, and then two hydraulic actuators were respectively applied to the test specimen  and the plate to simulate the centrifugal load and the remote disk loading. Although this kind of  fixture is similar to the real dovetail attachment, it is too expensive to perform a single test.  Subsequently, Golden et.al [14] developed a fixture with removable contact pads, which had  been used to study the effects of flank angles [14], surface treatments [15, 16], etc. Obviously,  the removable contact pads provide a significant cost advantage compared to Ruiz’s. In order to  extend fretting fatigue study to high temperature environments, Golden developed the original  fretting fatigue fixture by using the furnace igniters to control the experimental temperature,  many cooling holes were manufactured to cool the fixture [17]. However, a significant  temperature gradient will form in the contact region that can’t be eliminated, and the additional  cooling holes increased processing difficulties. Since NBSX superalloys exhibit distinct fretting  fatigue characteristic at different temperatures, a uniform experimental temperature is required.

Fig. 1.  Schematic of: (a) tenon/mortise structure (b) dovetail attachment.  

Using the above or similar fixture, most of the researches were focusing on the fretting  fatigue behavior of Ti-6Al-4V, which is commonly used in the fan and compressor stages of gas  turbine engine [1, 15, 18-21]. However, the mechanical behavior of nickel-based superalloys  depends on the microstructure [22]. Besides, the fretting fatigue characteristics of NBSX  superalloys are closely related to the crystal orientation, which are different from conventional  polycrystalline materials. Specifically, the fretting fatigue crack tended to initiate along the  direction of slip lines for the crystal orientation and grow along the slip plane [23]. Many  experimental and numerical analysis also concluded the similar results [24, 25]. In addition, the  fretting direction was considered to have a great influence on fretting fatigue behavior, the crack  angle was different when the fretting direction was inconsistent with the crystal orientation [23].  Despite considerable efforts have been made to study the crystal orientation effects on fretting  fatigue behavior of NBSX superalloys, most were focused on the material responses, there are  still lacking of researches about complex geometries and loading conditions, such as the dovetail  attachment. On the other hand, researches on high temperature fretting fatigue of NBSX  superalloys have also been conducted in the past few years. Crystallographic cracking occurred  on both octahedral planes and cube planes at elevated temperature, but at room temperature,  cracking was most likely to occur on octahedral planes [25]. Nevertheless, the effects of  temperature on fretting fatigue damage have not been clarified [25-27].  

It is well known that fretting wear will occur during the fretting fatigue process due to the  relative displacements between contacting parts [28]. Surface adhesion and delamination are  generally observed in the regions, however, the fretting wear characteristics are discriminating  for different locations on the contact surface (e.g. contact edge and center area) [1]. Fretting wear  mechanism is complicated and affected by many factors, such as material properties, surface  hardness, temperature, etc. [29-31]. In addition, oxides will form during high temperature  fretting fatigue process, which greatly affects the fretting wear and crack initiation [30].Some  researchers have proposed some solid lubricants with a low friction coefficient to reduce wear  [32].In our previous work, the interaction mechanism between fretting wear and fretting crack  initiation of NBSX superalloys have been preliminarily investigated, the effects of fretting wear  on crack initiation depends on the fretting condition (stick, partial slip or gross sliding) [33].  

However, the evolution of fretting wear and crack initiation mechanism of NBSX superalloys  dovetail attachment have not been studied.  

The purpose of this paper is to investigate the fretting fatigue behavior of NBSX dovetail  attachment at high temperature. To study the effects of temperature and load on fretting fatigue  damage, a dovetail attachment fretting fatigue apparatus is developed equipped with high  temperature furnace, and the effects of loading levels are investigated. The worn morphologies,  fracture morphologies and microstructural evolution are observed through Optical microscope  observation and Scan Electron Microscopy (SEM). Energy Dispersive Spectrum (EDS) is  applied to analyze the chemical composition of the contact surface. Besides, Crystallographic  plasticity finite element method (CPFEM) have been applied to obtain the stresses and strains of  the contact region, the actuations of crystallographic slip systems have been studied. Finally, the  fretting fatigue damage mechanism of NBSX superalloys is discussed based on the experimental  and numerical results.

# 2.2   Fretting fatigue test method  

The dovetail attachment is subjected to the cyclic centrifugal loads and moments when in  service ( Fig. 1 ), which are caused by blade rotation and aerodynamic forces, respectively. In the  previous researches, the influences of aerodynamic forces on fretting fatigue behavior of dovetail  were often neglected to simplify the complexity of the testing apparatus [12, 13]. Therefore, the  uniaxial fatigue test system was utilized to simulate the actual centrifugal loads. The developed  fretting fatigue test apparatus equipped with high temperature furnace is as shown in  Fig. 4 . The  cyclic loads applied on the bottom of the dovetail specimen are provided by Instron 8801 servo- hydraulic system, which is capable of a maximum applied force of 100 kN. The fixture is  connected with the 8801 servo-hydraulic system through 2 pins. The high temperature furnace  can provide a temperature range of 3   to 1000  , which can provide more uniform temperature  environment than furnace igniters [17] and induction heating coil [38]. Three K-type  thermocouples are employed to measure and control experimental temperature, which are fixed  on the specimen surface, as shown in  Fig. 4 (marked in +) . The dovetail specimen is in contact  with the two removable contact pads, which are embedded in the fixture and can reduce the  experimental cost. The contact pads and the slots are the same size to ensure that the contact pads  are fixed in the slots. Besides, the fretting fatigue test at room temperature have shown that there  is no micro-movement between contact pad and fixture slot under cycle axial load. Moreover,  there will be an “interference fit” between contact pads and fixture at high temperature due to  thermal deformation. Hence, this fretting fatigue test method has been applied in many studies  both at room temperature and elevated temperature [14-17].  

![](images/8a4bcc05c02fdc3d59cd782af08b8654373c9432060a3a5a63f588789ee6c85b.jpg)  
Fig. 4.  Fretting fatigue test apparatus of dovetail assembly  

Before fretting fatigue tests, each dovetail specimen is ultrasonically cleaned with absolute  ethanol to remove stains or impurities form the surface. In order to reproduce the fretting  conditions of dovetail attachments used in gas turbine engine, the experimental temperature is set  as 600  , which approximates the operating temperature turbine blade tenon [26]. The  temperature would maintain for an hour after it reaches the target value.  

In this paper, we focus on the fretting fatigue behavior of dovetail attachment under low  frequency load. All fretting fatigue tests are run in force control, and three loading levels are  investigated with stress ratio of 0.1, the test frequency is 10 Hz. These parameters are typically  used during fretting fatigue test [35, 39]. The loading level is defined as the maximum load  applied to the dovetail specimen.  Table 4  presents the specific experimental parameters.  

Microstructural SEM observations are conducted by using the FEI Verios G4 equipped with  EDS. Cross section SEM samples are prepared from the test specimens sectioned in the parallel  direction to the loading axis. The SEM samples will be mechanically ground, polished and  chemically corroded using 5ml nitric acid, 10ml hydrofluoric acid and 15ml hydrogen peroxide.

# 2.3   Numerical analysis method  

In order to have a better understanding about the failure causes of the NBSX dovetail  attachment, finite element method (FEM) is used for numerical analysis. FEM is preferred in  fretting fatigue research for its ability to obtain accurate stress and strain of contact area [40, 41].  The contact analysis of dovetail attachment is simulated by the commercial software ABAQUS.  Fig.5  shows the finite element model of dovetail attachment with boundary and loading  condition. Besides, a quasi-3D model was used to improve the calculation accuracy and  efficiency, the thicknesses of the dovetail specimen and contact pad are 0.1mm, and the front and  back of the model constrained using axisymmetric boundary condition. Mesh refinement is  conducted at the contact edge area to capture the accurate stress gradient, where the stress is  larger than the contact center area according to Hertz theory. The distribution of contact stresses  along fretting direction is as shown in  Fig. 6 , it can be seen that there is a fluctuation of contact  stress when the mesh size is 0.02mm.  Table 5  lists the maximum contact stress of different mesh  size and the relative errors. The minimum mesh size is determined to be 0.04mm based on the  convergence analysis results. The element type is eight-node brick elements with incompatible  modes (C3D8I) that is commonly used in contact analysis [42, 43].

The general purpose and non-linear finite element analysis code are used to solve the  problem. Lagrange multiplier contact algorithm is applied because that it could strictly enforce  the stick condition. The master-slave algorithm is used with surface-to-surface discretization, in  which the flat-rounded surface of the pad is defined as a master surface and the surface of the  dovetail specimen acts as a slave surface. A constant COF of 0.35 is used to investigate the  fretting fatigue behavior for all loading conditions, which is determined according to the friction  test at 600  . 3.   Results  

# 3.1   Displacements and life cycles  

The overall fretting fatigue failure life (OFL) can be divided into crack initiation life (CIL)  and crack propagation life (CPL). However, there is no standard method to distinguish between  crack initiation and crack propagation. Sunde [49] defined the CIL when the visible crack length  reached a set value, however, the added high temperature furnace hinder the direct observation of  cracks. Shi [12] analyzed the variation of displacement along with the cycles during the testing  process, and he defined the initiation life as the displacement changes of 0.05mm.  Fig. 7  shows  the variation of maximum displacement (during one loading cycle) along with the cycles. The  peak displacement is fluctuating during fretting fatigue process, and an abrupt change in peak  displacement occurs at the end of the test. The fluctuation of peak displacement may be induced  by fretting wear. In this paper, we define CIL as  ∆ D  (the difference of adjacent peaks) exceeds  0.03mm. The red dotted line in  Fig.7  indicates the boundary between the crack initiation stage  and the crack propagation stage. Six effective specimens are tested and the results are  summarized in  Table 6 . Experimental CIL and fitted CIF are shown in  Fig. 8 , with the increase  of maximum fatigue load, the CIL decreases. It can be seen from  Fig. 7  that the maximum displacement oscillates periodically, which  indicates the relative position between the dovetail specimen and fixture changes along with  cycles. The change in maximum displacement is mainly due to wear behavior on the contact  surface. During the fretting fatigue process, wear debris generation and ejection occur  simultaneously, the increase in maximum displacement represents that the amount of wear debris  generation is greater that wear debris ejection, and it can just assume that there is only the wear  debris generation process. In contrast, the decrease in maximum displacement means that only  wear debris ejection occurs. However, fluctuations in the peak displacement indicate that the  generation and ejection of wear debris are not dynamically balanced. In addition, when the  maximum fatigue load is 13805N, the maximum displacement has an increasing trend, whereas  there is no obvious trend to increase when the maximum fatigue load is increased to 15975N. On  the contrary, when the maximum fatigue load is 18105N, the maximum displacement tends to  decrease along with cycles. Hence, it can be deduced that wear is more sever under greater load  accompanied with more wear debris. Besides, the longer OFLs for Case 2 and Case 5 might be  attributed to crack elimination induced by fretting wear after crack initiation.  

# 3.2   Surface damage  

The fretting wear morphology of the contact surface under different loading conditions are  shown in  Fig. 9 . The dovetail specimen failed at the contact edge, and many wear pits are  generated on the contact surfaces due to fretting wear. Wear becomes severe as the fretting  fatigue load increases ( Fig. 9 (a), (d), (g) ), which are resulted from the increased alternating  stresses in the contacting interface, then wear debris are formed when the surface is broken. The  observed wear phenomena are consistent with the results derived from the maximum  displacement-cycle curve in Section 3.1. Close to the fracture, wear debris are refined due to the  relative high contact stresses at the fretting contact edge, as shown in  Fig. 9 (e) . The wear debris  could be either adhered to the contacting surface or further oxidized and refined into smaller  particles. Large number of fine debris likely worked as the solid lubricant that partly mitigated  the abrasion damage in the fretting region [1]. Furthermore, the relative displacement between  the dovetail specimen and contact pad can result in obvious fretting trace, especially at the  contact edge ( Fig. 9 (c) ). However, as the fatigue load increases, the fretting trace becomes  unclear ( Fig. 9 (f), (i) ).  

![](images/95a54be63d01eede8ecd3d20177e916303d437e2fcfa3132924f1cbd249b9358.jpg)  
Fig. 9 . Wear morphology of contact surface under three loading conditions.  

Fig. 10  shows the detailed wear characteristics at different areas of Case 6. Many irregular  wear pits are distributed at the contact center area and surface peeling could be observed ( Fig.  10(A) ). The chemical composition of Region 1 and Region 2 are obtained through EDS analysis,  as listed in  Table 7 . The contents of oxygen are very high that exceeds 25%, which indicates that  the contact surface has undergone severe oxidation. It is noticed that the outline of the wear pit is  nearly not damaged ( Fig. 10 (C) ), thus the crack should be initiated after the appearance of wear  pit. A long contact crack is found in the contact edge area ( Fig. 10 (B) ). This kind of crack was  reported as the potential cause of failure under fretting fatigue condition, which would propagate  towards the substrate under fatigue loading, eventually leading to the failure of the specimen [1,  50]. Due to the presence of wear pits at the contact edge, the effective contact area is reduced,  the actual contact stress would be greater than the initial value. Therefore, cracks are prone to  initiation in heavily worn regions.

Fig. 14  shows the representative crack path of failed dovetail specimen observed by using a  3D depth of field microscope. # 4.2   Fretting fatigue mechanism  

The fretting fatigue process of NBSX dovetail attachment is accompanied by cracking and  wear. Wear occurs on the contact surface and the degree of wear is different between the contact  center area and contact edge area. According to the wear behavior and failure characteristics of  NBSX dovetail specimen, the fretting fatigue damage mechanism is as schematically illustrated  in  Fig. 22 . The specimen surface can be divided into three regions according to damage  mechanism, namely the stable wear region (Region I), crack initiation region (Region II), and  undamaged region (Region III). In Region I, the contact surface is oxidized due to the high  temperature, then the oxides will be compacted to form a glaze layer (oxide layer) on the contact  surface. Because of the shear stress at the contact interface, wear will occur. The stress state  dramatically changed at the contact edge area (Region II), where stress concentration occurs. The  large shear stress in Region II results in the remove of oxide layer. Besides, the large contact  stresses and relative displacements cause the generation of wear pit, which will lead to the  reduction of effective loading area and larger stress compared with the unworn specimen. The  large RSS will promote the slip system to actuate and cause plastic deformation. As the result,  crack will easily initiate and propagate under fretting fatigue loading condition. In addition, the  crack growth direction is nearly 80 degrees to the fretting direction, which is agree well with the  numerical results, and it validate that the fretting fatigue damage characteristics of NBSX  dovetail attachment is the actuation of crystallographic slip systems.  

When the maximum fatigue load increases, the fretting wear is becoming severe and fretting  fatigue life decreases. On the one side, the larger fatigue load produces higher RSS, which will  promote crystallographic slip and plastic deformation at the contact edge area. Cracks will easily  initiate with the accumulation of plastic deformation. On the other side, fretting wear is more  severe under the larger fatigue load, which is accompanied by surface delamination and wear pits  generation. As a result, the effective loading area is reduced and the actual contact stress is larger  than the numerical results. Hence, the fretting fatigue life is shorter under larger fatigue load.

# 5.   Conclusion  

In this paper, a dovetail attachment fretting fatigue experiment system is developed that can  provide a uniform high temperature environment. Fretting fatigue tests of dovetail attachment are  carried out at 600 . Numerical analysis is conducted to study the stress state at the contact  region. The effects of loading condition on fretting fatigue damage are investigated, and the main  conclusions are summarized as follows:  

(1) The dovetail specimens fail at the location where in contact with pads, and the fretting  fatigue crack initiates at the contact edge area. With the increase of fatigue load, fretting fatigue  life decreases. Obvious fretting scars can be observed on the contact surface of both test  specimens and fretting pads, but the morphologies are not affected by the loading condition.  

(2) Numerical results based on the crystallographic constitutive model shows that the  maximum von Mises stress and RSS occurs at the contact leading edge area. The large RSS will  lead to crystallographic slip and plastic deformation along (111) [ 110  ] and (  111  ) [110] slip  systems. The actuations of the two slip systems are consistent with the fracture surfaces.  

(3) NBSX superalloys will be oxidized at the contact surface, then the oxides are compacted  and a thin oxide layer is formed. Due to the existence of large tangential stress, surface  delamination will occur at the contact surface. In particular, the larger contact stress and relative  displacement will result in more severe wear near the contact edge area compared to the center  area.  

(4) No obvious fatigue strips can be observed on the fracture surface due to crack opening- and-closure. The oxide layer at the contact edge area is removed because of large shear stress.  Wear pits reduce the effective loading area of specimen and cause further stress concentration at  the edge of wear pit, which will promote crack initiation and propagation.  

(5) With the increase of fatigue load, plastic deformation will be more likely to occur at the  contact edge area due to the larger RSS. Then cracks will be easily initiated with the  accumulation of plastic deformation. Besides, fretting wear is becoming more severe that is  accompanied by surface delamination and wear pits generation. As a consequence, the effective  loading area is reduced and the stress concentration is getting serious, thus the fretting fatigue  life is short under the small fatigue loading conditions.
