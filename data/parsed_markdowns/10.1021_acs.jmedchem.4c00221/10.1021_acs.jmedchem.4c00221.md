ABSTRACT:  Natural product evodiamine is a multitargeting antitumor lead compound. However, clinical development of evodiamine derivatives was hampered by poor water solubility and limited  in vivo antitumor potency. Herein, a series of evodiamine − glucose conjugates were designed by additional targeting glucose transporter-1 (GLUT1). Compared with the lead compound, conjugate  8  exhibited obvious enhancement in water solubility and  in vivo  antitumor efficacy. Furthermore, the effect of GLUT1 targeting also led to lower cytotoxicity to normal cells. Antitumor mechanism studies manifested that conjugate  8  acted by Top1/Top2 dual inhibition, apoptosis induction, and G 2 /M cell cycle arrest, which selectively targeted tumor cells with a high expression level of GLUT1. Thus, evodiamine − glucose conjugates showed promising features as potential antitumor agents.

Natural products (NPs) are the foremost source for the generation of new molecular entities with therapeutic efficacy. Nearly one-half of the approved small-molecule antineoplastic drugs were associated with NPs. − 3   Nevertheless, unmodified NPs are usually limited by moderate activity and unsatisfactory physicochemical properties. Thus, the discovery of NP derivatives with favorable  in vivo  activities remains a momentous challenge. − 8  

Starting from 3-fluoro-10-hydroxyl evo dia mine, herein, a series of evodiamine − glucose conjugates were designed and assayed. In particular, conjugate  8  displayed improved water solubility, excellent  in vitro  and  in vivo  antitumor activity, and lower toxicity to normal cells.  

# RESULTS AND DISCUSSION  

Evodiamine, an indolequinone alkaloid derived from Chinese herbal medicine  Evodia rutaecarpa , represents a promising antitumor lead compound. − 12   Previously, a number of evodiamine derivatives were designed, − 24   and we identified 3-fluoro-10-hydroxyl evo dia mine (compound  2  in  Figure 1 B) with excellent  in vitro  antitumor activity.   However, compound 2  possesses poor water solubility and limited  in vivo  antitumor activity, which has hampered further development in clinical treatment. Therefore, it is highly required to design novel evodiamine derivatives with improved antitumor activity and reduced toxicity.  

Design and Chemical Synthesis of Evodiamine − Glucose Conjugates.  Our previous studies have confirmed that 3-fluoro-10-hydroxyl evo dia mine (compound  2 ) was a potent antitumor lead compound.   Extensive structure − activity relationship studies revealed that the 10-hydroxyl group provided an appropriate reactive site for derivatization with little impact on the antitumor activity. , ,   Thus, the GLUT1 ligand glucose was introduced on the 10-hydroxyl group through ether (compounds  8  and  15 ) or ester linkers (compounds  13a − b ). The designed conjugates were expected to simultaneously improve the  in vivo  antitumor activity and decrease the toxicity through targeting GLUT1 ( Figure 1 B).  

Tumor cells predominantly rely on anaerobic glycolysis (known as the Warburg effect) 25 ,   rather than the mitochon- drial oxidative phosphorylation pathway in normal cells. Glucose transporters (GLUTs), such as GLUT1, are generally overexpressed in tumor cells for enhancing glucose uptake to achieve the high energy demand for cell proliferation. , Specifically, GLUT1 was reported to be overexpressed in breast, ,   lung,   gastric,   and colorectal cancers, − 34   which was regarded as an ideal target for antitumor therapy. Migration Inhibition Effect of Conjugate 8 against HCT116 Cells.  Tumor metastasis plays a vital role in the deaths caused by cancer.   Therefore, the wound healing assay and the transwell assay were performed to determine the antimetastasis capacity of conjugate  8  against the HCT116 cell line. As demonstrated in  Figure 3 A,B, the scratched area in the blank control declined evidently, with a wound-healing rate value of 62%. In contrast, conjugate  8  efficiently repressed the wound heal in a concentration-dependent manner with the wound- healing rate ranging from 37 to 1.1%. Interestingly, conjugate  8 almost suppressed the cancer cell migration completely at the concentration of 0.05  μ M. Furthermore, the result of the transwell assay manifested that conjugate  8  demonstrated remarkable anti-invasion capability against HCT116 cells, which dose-dependently diminished the number of cancer cells migrating into the lower compartment ( Figure 3 C,D). These results indicated that conjugate  8  could effectively inhibit the migration and invasion of cancer cells and protect healthy tissue.

Topoisomerase Inhibitory Activity of Conjugate 8.  To further clarify the modes of action of conjugate  8 , the topoisomerase I/II (Top1/2) inhibitory activity was determined by using the topoisomerase-mediated DNA relaxation assay. As illustrated in  Figure 4 G, conjugate  8  exhibited moderate Top1 inhibitory activity at a concentration of 100  μ M, which was comparable to the parent compound. In contrast, conjugate  8 showed stronger Top2 inhibition activity than parent compound 2  at the concentration of 50  μ M ( Figure 4 H), which was more active than positive control etoposide (Eto). The above results confirmed that conjugate  8  acted as a dual Top1/Top2 inhibitor, whose mechanism was similar to the parent compound. Furthermore, the binding modes of conjugate  8 with Top1 and Top2 α  were investigated by molecular docking. As shown in  Figure 4 I, the hydroxy group of glucose was found to form two hydrogen bonds with Asp533 and DG12 of Top1 (PDB ID: 1T8I 12 − 14 , ), respectively. Besides, the  π − π interaction was observed between the indole ring of the evodiamine core and DNA skeletons. In addition, the hydro- phobic interaction with amino acid residues Ile535, Glu356, and Lys425 was also involved in the interaction of Top1 with conjugate  8 . As for Top2 α  ( Figure 4 J, PDB ID: 5GWK 12 − 14 , ), the interactions were mainly observed on the glucose part. The hydroxyl group in the glucose formed three hydrogen bonds with Lys614, His758, and His759, respectively, and a coordinated bond was observed between the glucose and Mg 2+ . Similar to the binding mode of Top1, the indole ring of the evodiamine part also formed the  π − π  interactions with the DNA skeleton. Moreover, the hydrophobic interactions with the surrounding amino acid residues Met762 and Gly615 were also observed.

# CONCLUSIONS  

In summary, a series of evodiamine − glucose conjugates were designed to ameliorate  in vivo  antitumor activity. By introducing a GLUT1-targeted structural motif, conjugate  8  was proven to possess potent antitumor activity both  in vitro  and  in vivo . Compared with the parent compound, conjugate  8  exhibited an obvious improvement of water solubility, leading to enhanced  in vivo  antitumor efficacy. Moreover, conjugate  8  exhibited lower cytotoxicity to normal cells by selectively targeting cancer cells with high expression levels of GLUT1. Preliminary mechanism studies manifested that conjugate  8  acted by a dual Top1/Top2 inhibition, apoptosis induction, and arresting the cell cycle at the G 2 /M phase. Taken together, targeting GLUT1 was proven to be an effective strategy to enhance the  in vivo  efficacy of evodiamine derivatives and compound  8  represents a promising antitumor lead compound for the treatment of colon cancer.

Stability Test.  The excess test compounds were dissolved in PBS buffer and incubated in an incubator at 37  ° C. Subsequently, 100  μ L of the solution was added to 900  μ L of methanol after the coincubation for 24 and 72 h. The mixed solution was filtered and analyzed by LC − MS with UV light.  

Solubility Test.  The methanol solution samples of tested compounds were prepared at 0.01, 0.02, 0.05, 0.75, 0.1, 0.125, and 0.15 mg/mL, respectively. The maximum absorption wavelength of the tested compound was obtained by full wavelength scanning of the UV − visible spectrophotometer, and the absorbance of each sample at the maximum absorption wavelength was collected to establish a standard curve. Then the diluent of the saturated aqueous solution of the tested compound is prepared, and the dilution times are 5, 10, 20, and 30 times. The absorbance of the diluent at the maximum absorption wavelength was collected by the UV − vis spectrophotometer, the appropriate absorbance was selected (the absorbance range was below 0.15 mg/mL methanol solution), and the water solubility of the tested compound was calculated by a standard curve.  

Small Interfering RNA Treatments.  The siGLUT1 HCT116 cells were obtained by the transfection of siRNA (cat no: GM-SI-137480, purchased from Genomeditech) into HCT116 cells using the liposomal transfection reagent. The siRNA interference efficiency was determined by Western blot analysis.  

Annexin V-PI Flow Cytometry Assay.  In order to detect the apoptotic cells, HCT116 or siGLUT1 HCT116 cells were seeded in 6- well plates at a density of 2  ×  10 5   cells/well, followed by incubation with the specific concentration of the test compounds for 48 h. Subsequently, cells were collected and washed twice with cold PBS. After centrifugation, the precipitate was resuspended in 200  μ L of 1  × binding buffer, and then the mixture of Annexin V-FITC (3  μ L) and PI (3  μ L) was added and incubated at room temperature for 15 min in the dark. Finally, cells in each sample were analyzed by flow cytometry (BD Accuri C6).  

ROS Measurement.  The fluorescent probe DCFH-DA (Beyotime, China) was employed to determine the intracellular ROS. HCT116 cells (4  ×  10 5   cells/well) were seeded into 6-well plates and cultured overnight before the incubation of various concentrations of the test compounds for 12 h. The cells were then treated with DCFH-DA (10 μ M) in a serum-free medium for another 30 min. Finally, the ROS levels in HCT116 cells were measured by fluorescence microscopy and a Biotek Synergy H2 reader.  

GSH Detection Assay.  HCT116 cells were seeded in a 6-well plate at 5  ×  10 5   cells, followed by the treatment with test compounds at the indicated concentration for 12 h. After treatment, cells were lysed to collect the total proteins, and then the reduced GSH content was measured using the GSH detection assay kit (A006-2-1, Nanjing Jiancheng Bioengineering Institute). All samples were assayed in triplicate, and the GSH level was normalized against the total protein concentration.  

Transwell Migration Assay.  For the cell migration assay, HCT116 cells were suspended in serum-free DMEM and incubated in the upper chambers at a density of 5  ×  10 4 , and 600  μ L of DMEM containing 10% FBS was added to the lower chambers. The medium in the lower chambers contained the specified concentration of compounds  8 . After 24 h, the inner side of the upper chamber membrane was swabbed to remove cells that did not penetrate the membrane, and the transwell chambers were fixed with 5% poly formaldehyde and then stained with 2.5% crystal violet.  

In Vivo  Antitumor Activity.  The experimental procedures and animal use and care protocols were approved by the Committee on Ethics of Medicine, Navy Medical University. The  in vivo  antitumor activities of compound  2  (5 mg/kg, bid) and compound  8  (10 mg/kg, bid and 20 mg/kg, bid) were evaluated. BALB/c nude female mice (certificate SCXK-2019-0004) were obtained from Hangzhou ZiYuan Experimental Animal Technology Co., Ltd. Suspensions of HCT116 colon cancer cells were implanted subcutaneously into the right axilla region of the mice. Treatment began when the implanted tumors reached a volume of approximately 100 mm 3   (about 10 days later). The animals were randomized into the appropriate groups (five animals/ group), and the treatments were administered by intraperitoneal injection for 21 consecutive days twice per day. Tumor volumes were determined by using calipers to measure the length and width and calculating the volume using the formula TV = ( AB 2 )/2, where  A  is the tumor length and  B  is the width. Tumor volumes and body weights were monitored every 2 days over the course of treatment. Mice were sacrificed after the end of the administration, and tumors were removed and recorded for further analysis. The H&E staining, Ki67 staining, and TUNEL staining of the tumor tissue were performed by Shanghai Shycbio Biological Technology Co. Ltd.
