# SNRMPACDC: computational model focused on Siamese network and random matrix projection for anticancer synergistic drug combination prediction  

Tian-Hao Li, Chun-Chun Wang , Li Zhang and Xing Chen Corresponding author. Xing Chen, Artificial Intelligence Research Institute, China University of Mining and Technology, Xuzhou 221116, China. E-mail: xingchen@amss.ac.cn; xingchen@cumt.edu.cn  

# Abstract  

Synergistic drug combinations can improve the therapeutic effect and reduce the drug dosage to avoid toxicity. In previous years, an in vitro  approach was utilized to screen synergistic drug combinations. However, the  in vitro  method is time-consuming and expensive. With the rapid growth of high-throughput data, computational methods are becoming efficient tools to predict potential synergistic drug combinations. Considering the limitations of the previous computational methods, we developed a new model named Siamese Network and Random Matrix Projection for AntiCancer Drug Combination prediction (SNRMPACDC). Firstly, the Siamese convolutional network and random matrix projection were used to process the features of the two drugs into drug combination features. Then, the features of the cancer cell line were processed through the convolutional network. Finally, the processed features were integrated and input into the multi-layer perceptron network to get the predicted score. Compared with the traditional method of splicing drug features into drug combination features, SNRMPACDC improved the interpret ability of drug combination features to a certain extent. In addition, the introduction of convolutional networks can better extract the potential information in the features. SNRMPACDC achieved the root mean-squared error of 15.01 and the Pearson correlation coefficient of 0.75 in 5-fold cross-validation of regression prediction for response data. In addition, SNRMPACDC achieved the AUC of 0.91  ±  0.03 and the AUPR of 0.62  ±  0.05 in 5-fold cross-validation of classification prediction of synergistic or not. These results are almost better than all the previous models. SNRMPACDC would be an effective approach to infer potential anticancer synergistic drug combinations.  

Keywords:  cell line, drug, random matrix projection, synergistic drug combination, Siamese network  

# Introduction  

Biological systems are complex, with many interacting subsys- tems operating at different levels of function. The cooperative operation of these subsystems can effectively protect the organ- ism from malfunction [ 1 ]. Therefore, human disease itself is a very complex phenomenon. Because of this, treating disease should address multiple challenges rather than focusing on a single one [ 2 ]. The use of multiple drugs to improve the therapeutic effect, namely combination therapy, has the following advantages over single-drug therapy. First of all, compared with the traditional single-drug therapy with one drug and one target, the combina- tion therapy can effectively improve the efficacy [ 3 ,  4 ]. Secondly, since the dose of combination therapy is usually lower than that of a single drug, the toxicity and adverse side effects to patients may be reduced [ 5 ]. Thirdly, combination therapy can reduce or even overcome resistance [ 6 ,  7 ]. As a result, drug combinations are increasingly applied to treat different diseases, such as cancer [ 8 ], viral disease [ 9 ], fungal and bacterial infections [ 10 ]. However, not all drug combinations are effective, and blind drug combina- tions are likely to be detrimental and even shorten the patient’s survival, which are called antagonist drug combinations [ 11 ]. Therefore, identifying synergistic drug combinations is crucial for the treatment of diseases.  

At present, researchers are conducting a lot of biological exper- iments to find synergistic drug combinations and have achieved certain results [ 12 ]. For example, experiments have confirmed that the combination of docetaxel, trastuzumab and pertuzumab can effectively treat HER2-positive metastatic breast cancer [ 13 ]. Compared with traditional monotherapy, this regimen extended patient survival by about 16 months [ 13 ]. In addition, the com- bination of lamivudine and Zidovudine has been shown to be effective in the treatment of acquired immunodeficiency syn- drome [ 14 ]. Furthermore, there is evidence that combination of docetaxel (Taxotere) and cisplatin (TC) can effectively treat gastric cancer [ 15 ]. However, identifying potential synergistic drug com- binations through traditional biological experiments is not only laborious but may lead patients to suffer unnecessary and even harmful treatments. Nowadays, with the accumulation of high- throughput biological data, computational models could be con- structed to predict potential synergistic drug combinations. The prediction would facilitate further experimental verification and help advance precision medicine [ 16 ]. Computational approaches can be used as a supplement to the  in vitro  methods of screening drug combinations and greatly decrease the experimental work- load.  

Cancer is a complex and deadly disease with a high fatality rate and easy to develop resistance to single drug, which poses a great threat to human life. Thus, many researchers are trying to use computational models to find potential synergistic drug combinations that could effectively treat cancer [ 17 ,  18 ]. Celebi et al.  [ 19 ] proposed a novel model for predicting potential synergistic drug combinations by using multi-omics data. They first integrated DNA methylation, copy-number alterations, somatic mutations and gene expression profiles measured before drug treatment as the features of cell lines. Then, chemical structures and target pathways were combined to represent drugs. Finally, they spliced the features of the drug and the cell line as feature vector to represent the pair of the drug combination and cell line and used it as the input of the XGBoost model to obtain the final prediction score. Gayvert  et al . [ 20 ] proposed a computational model that used existing high-throughput drug screening data as features and random forest as a classifier for prediction. It was illustrated that the model had significant predictive power for mutant BRAF melanoma. In addition, to more accurately measure synergies between drug combinations, Malyutina  et al . [ 21 ] proposed a novel drug combination sensitivity score (CSS) to quantify the efficacy of drug combinations. They adopted three machine learning-based methods to demonstrate the accuracy of the CSS definition. In addition, they also defined a CSS-based synergy score to evaluate the degree of drug interaction and demonstrated its ability to successfully discover antagonistic and synergistic drug combinations. Moreover, for melanoma, a common cancer in humans, a linear model based on Bliss scores was developed by Amzallag  et al.  [ 22 ] to combine dose– response data for different drugs to produce effective estimates of drug synergy. Different from the traditional method of using Bliss hypothesis directly on the original experimental data, they first directly identified the statistically significant collaborative events from the drug combination data and reduced the noise of the dataset. Furthermore, because of the rapid development of deep learning, researchers are beginning to consider applying it to the prediction of synergistic drug combinations. Preuer  et al.  [ 23 ] proposed a deep learning-based model named DeepSynergy. They calculated and integrated three different chemical characteristics that characterize the drug: counts of extended connectivity fingerprints with a radius of 6 (ECFP_6), physicochemical properties and toxicophores. Then, they described the cell lines through their gene expression profile and spliced the features of the drug and the cell line as the final features. Finally, they used the processed features as the input of the multilayer perceptron to get the final prediction result. Different from others, Cheng et al.  [ 24 ] developed a network-based model to predict synergistic drug combinations. The advantage of using network algorithms to infer drug combinations is that they can enhance biological interpret ability to some extent compared with the black-box characteristics of machine learning and deep learning algorithms. This model first integrated protein–protein interactions and drug–target interactions to construct a network of drug targets associated with different drugs. Then, the affinity between two drugs was further integrated by defining the relationship between drugs as topological overlap or topological separation. Finally, a drug–drug–target complex network was formed, and drug combinations that follow a specific network topology were found in this network.  

Not only for cancers but also for many other different diseases, researchers also found that the efficacy of drugs can be improved by combining them. In this respect, researchers have also done a lot of research work and achieved some results. For combinations of antifungal drugs, Chen  et al.  [ 10 ] released a novel method named Network-based Laplacian regularized Least Square Syn- ergistic drug combination prediction. They first divided the drugs into principal and adjuvant drugs and constructed three different drug similarities based on drug chemical structures, drug–target interactions and known synergistic drug combinations. Next, they constructed two distinct classifiers in the principal drug and adjuvant drug spaces, respectively. Finally, the two classifiers were combined to predict synergistic drug combinations. In addition, Ding  et al.  [ 25 ] proposed a new Ensemble Prediction framework of Synergistic Drug Combinations. First, they constructed feature vectors for drug pairs by integrating three types of drug similar- ity calculated based on multiple sources of information. Then, feature-based predictor can be used to obtain feature-based score for drug pair. Next, transductive learning was utilized to obtain network-based score based on drug–target network. Finally, the feature-based and network-based score were integrated by two ensemble rules to infer potential drug combinations. In addition, Ji et al.  [ 26 ] proposed a model that uses XGBoost to predict potential synergistic drug combinations. They used five distinct data pro- files, namely pathways, human phenotypic information, chemical structure information, protein–protein interactions and protein targets to make predictions. Furthermore, through denoting drug pairs as combinations of pharmacological features of investigated drugs, Zhang  et al . [ 27 ] developed a novel heterogeneous network named SyFFM based on symmetric meta-path. Firstly, features of drug combinations were established based on drug–target, drug– enzyme and drug–ATC associations. Next, field-aware factoriza- tion machines were utilized on these features to compute the synergistic scores between drugs. The innovation of this method is that it can be applied to not only two-drug combination, but also to three-drug combination, which is a meaningful attempt.  

Although the above calculation methods have their own advan- tages, they still need to be further improved. For example, the datasets used in many studies are relatively incomplete, and the description of the features of drugs and cell lines is not sufficient. Besides, some studies only pay attention to drug combi- nations, but they do not target any specific disease for these drug combinations. In recent years, deep learning was successfully adopted in many fields, such as speech and image recognition [ 28 ]. Deep learning can extract important features from a large amount of data, making it an ideal choice for identifying new drug combinations. However, the previous methods of predicting synergistic drug combinations using deep learning did not fur- ther consider biological interpret ability. For example, DeepSyn- ergy method simply spliced the features of two drugs together as the features of drug combination. In our work, we developed a new model named Siamese Network and Random Matrix Projection for AntiCancer Drug Combination prediction (SNRMPACDC). First of all, the features of the two drugs were taken as the input of the Siamese network, and the processed results were projected by two matrices symmetric to each other along the central axis to get two new vectors. The multiplication of the two vectors was used as the final vector representing the drug combination. Then, convolutional neural network was used to process the features of cell lines. Last, the features of drug combinations and cell lines were integrated and then processed through the multi-layer perceptron network to obtain the synergy value of the drug com- bination on the cell line. SNRMPACDC can find the combination of drugs that has the greatest therapeutic effect on cancer cell lines. The primary innovation of the model is the construction method of drug combination features. Unlike other models that simply spliced the features of two different drugs together as the features of a drug combination, SNRMPACDC first processed the features of the two drugs by using a Siamese network, which is a common network in the field of image similarity recognition used to measure the similarity degree between two identical things. Therefore, the features of the two drugs processed by Siamese net- work more reflect the similarity between the two drugs. However, in our problem, getting the similarity between two drugs through the Siamese network is not what we ultimately want. What we need is a degree of interaction between the two drugs. Therefore, we introduced a random matrix projection module, which can convert similarity into interaction to a certain extent. In addition, the introduction of convolutional networks can better extract the potential information in the features. In this study, we verified the accuracy of the model from the perspective of regression and classification. As a result, from the perspective of regression of response data, the average root mean-squared error (RMSE) and standard deviation of SNRMPACDC in 5-fold cross-validation were

 15.01 and 1.27, and the average Pearson correlation coefficient

 (PCC) and standard deviation of SNRMPACDC were 0.75 and 0.03. Besides, in terms of the classification of synergistic or not, the average area under the receiver operating characteristic curve (AUC) and area under the precision recall curve (AUPR) in 5- fold cross-validation were 0.91 and 0.62. Compared with previous methods, the prediction accuracy of the model proposed in this paper is improved under the above two groups of experiments. Finally, we further divided the drugs into new drugs and old drugs and predicted potential synergistic drug combinations containing new drugs for further biological experiment validation.  

# Results Performance evaluation  

We employed 5-fold cross-validation to evaluate the performance of SNRMPACDC and competitive methods. Different from the traditional 5-fold cross-validation, in the 5-fold cross-validation adopted in this paper, all drug combinations were divided into five parts to ensure that drug combinations appearing in the test set will not appear in the training set. In addition, we also compared SNRMPACDC with a variety of advanced algorithms. Since the synergistic value of the drug combination on the cell line is con- tinuous, the prediction of synergistic values of drug combinations can be considered as a regression problem. Therefore, the PCC and RMSE were used as evaluation indexes in this experiment.

As a continuous value, the synergy value of drug combination on cancer cell line does not clearly show whether this combi- nation is worth to be noticed or not. Therefore, drug combina- tions were further divided into synergistic and non-synergistic combinations in this paper. In a conventional sense, the syn- ergistic value greater than 0 can be regarded as a synergistic combination. However, in clinical practice, scientists need combi- nations with strong synergistic effect. Therefore, after statistical analysis of the known synergistic value range, we chose 30 as the threshold to distinguish whether there is synergistic or not. In other words, those with a synergy value  < 30 in the training set were considered to have no synergistic effect, while those with a synergy value  > 30 were considered to have synergistic effect. At the same time, due to the large number of additive combinations in the training data, the model tends to make more conservative predictions. Thus, an appropriate threshold should be selected to binarize the predicted synergistic effect. We adopted the most extensive way to select the appropriate threshold at present, that is, the threshold value when the  F 1-score is the maximum value is taken as the appropriate threshold. SNRMPACDC (adding new feature data) achieved an ACC, BACC, PREC, TPR, TNR and Kappa of 0.93, 0.77, 0.64, 0.56, 0.97 and 0.56. These results are mostly higher than the previous methods and further demonstrate the accuracy and advancement of SNRMPACDC.

# Discussion  

Predicting potential drug combinations can help us understand the mechanisms by which drugs treat disease and recommend potentially effective drug combinations for biologists to better treat disease. In this paper, we added a random matrix projection module to Siamese network to process the two drug features into drug combination features and further combined with the pro- cessed cell line features to infer the potential synergistic effect of drugs through the multi-layer perceptron network. SNRMPACDC mainly includes three parts. The first part is the network used to deal with drug features, which is a combination structure that integrates Siamese convolutional network and random matrix projection. The second part is to use the structure of the con- volutional network as the main body to process the cell line features. In the third part, the treated drug combination and cell line features are combined to make the final prediction using the multi-layer perceptron network. SNRMPACDC obtained higher performance than comparison models in term of both RMSE and PCC value. In addition, when this problem was turned into a binary classification, SNRMPACDC was also superior to most pre- vious models in terms of indicators such as AUC and AUPR. These results demonstrated the reliable performance of SNRMPACDC.  

SNRMPACDC performs well due to the following aspects. First of all, for drugs and cell lines, it is a new attempt to integrate mul- tiple pieces of information as the final signature vector. Especially, the copy number variation and mutation data were introduced into cell line features. Secondly, SNRMPACDC provided a feature extraction approach, which enhanced the interpret ability of drug combination features. Researchers can adopt this approach to extract features of drug combination before constructing machine learning-based prediction module in the field of synergistic drug combination prediction. In addition, the feature extraction method in SNRMPACDC can also be adopted in other bioinformat- ics fields such as drug–drug interaction prediction. Thirdly, SNRM- PACDC innovatively combined two different modules, namely Siamese network and random matrix projection. Siamese network was commonly used to deal with the relationship between two entities of the same kind, which can measure the similarity of two entities well. The random matrix projection model can well transform the similarity problem into the correlation problem, which is exactly what we need. Therefore, SNRMPACDC is suitable for our data. In addition, in both the Siamese network and the network dealing with cell lines, we utilized the one-dimensional convolutional network as the main part, which can better mine the implicit information in vectors than the conventional multi- layer perceptron network, so as to make the model more accurate. Finally, SNRMPACDC can predict synergy scores for new drugs that have no known synergistic drug combinations by feeding their feature vectors into the trained model.  

However, SNRMPACDC also has some limitations and needs to be improved. Firstly, after we got the features of drug combi- nations and cell lines, we multiplied the feature vector directly as a feature of the drug combination’s effect on the cell line, which does not have a good biological explanation and deserve to be improved. Secondly, there was no clear explanation for the hyperparameter selection of neural network, such as the size of the convolutional layer and the size of the pooling layer. Lastly, there was no perfect mathematical way for using the random matrix projection module to convert the similarity of features into mutuality. For the future research, the model of SNRMPACDC may be improved by introducing more reasonable method to integrate feature vector of drug combination with cell line feature vector or replacing the classifier module in SNRMPACDC. In addition, more and more effective models should be proposed to predict the synergistic combinations composed of three or more drugs in the future.

# Materials and methods  

# Materials  

In our study, we used data from large-scale cancer screening recently released by Merck & Co [ 33 ]. There were a total of 23 052 samples in the dataset, with two different drugs and a cell line for each sample. The dataset covered 39 different cell lines from seven different tissue types (ovary, prostate, lung, large intestine, skin, breast and pleura) and covered 38 diverse anticancer drugs. Measuring the synergistic effects of drug combinations is usually dependent on  in vitro  drug sensitivity tests. By culturing cancer cell lines in culture medium supplemented with drug combina- tions, the promotion or inhibition effect of drug combinations on cell proliferation is measured by certain methods, such as Zero Interaction Potency [ 34 ], highest single agent [ 35 ], Bliss indepen- dence [ 36 ] or Loewe Additivity [ 37 ]. In our study, we computed Loewe Additivity values by the batch processing model named Combenefit [ 38 ].  

In addition, it is necessary to obtain the features of drugs and cancer cell lines. For drugs, we used three different chemical features to represent the features of drugs. Firstly, ECFP_6 were obtained with jCompoundMapper [ 39 ]. Secondly, predefined physicochemical properties were calculated through ChemoPy [ 40 ]. The third kind of chemical features was binary toxicophore features based on a set of toxicophores collected from literature [ 41 ]. In total, the three chemical pieces of information were com- bined as feature information of drugs, with a total of  k  dimensions ( k  =  4387). The drug feature matrix of  DF  with  m  rows and  p columns was employed to denote the drug feature information, where  m  represented the number drugs and  p  was equal to  k . Besides, the element of  DF ( i , j ) denoted the  j -th feature value of the drug  i.  In addition, for cell line, we also used three different kinds of cell line features to represent cell lines. Firstly, gene expression profiles of cell lines were downloaded from the ArrayExpress database [ 42 ]. We further screened the expression data through microarray analysis [ 43 ] and finally obtained the genomic fea- tures with the dimension of 3984 denoted by  l . Here, the genomic feature matrix of  GF  with  n  rows and  q  columns was used to record genomic feature information of cell lines, where  n  was the number of cell lines and  q  was equal to  l . In addition, the element of  GF ( i , j ) denoted the  j -th genomic feature value of the cell line  i. Secondly, cell lines may not be well represented by only using the expression profile data of cell lines. For a more comprehensive and detailed representation of cell lines, copy number variation and mutation data with dimensions of 48 270 denoted by  r  were further obtained from the Cancer Cell Line Encyclopedia (CCLE) database as supplementary features. The copy number variation and mutation feature matrix of  CMF  ( n  rows and  h  columns) were utilized to denote the supplementary feature information of cell lines, where the element  CMF ( i , j ) represented the  j -th feature value of the cell line  i  based on copy number variation and mutation data. Here,  h  was equal to  r . It is worth noting that only 31 of the previous 39 different cell lines were found in the CCLE database.  

# Siamese Network and Random Matrix Projection for AntiCancer Drug Combination prediction  

The prediction model of SNRMPACDC was constructed based on Siamese network and random matrix projection (See  Figure 1 ). Firstly, we preprocessed the feature data of drugs and cell lines. Secondly, for investigated drug–drug combination and cell line, we first utilized the different drug feature vectors as the input of the Siamese network to obtain the new drug feature representations, which were further used as the input of random matrix projec- tion module to obtain the finally representation of drug–drug combination. Then, the convolutional networks were employed to extract the new representation of the cell line from original cell line feature vector. Lastly, we combined the new representations of investigated drug–drug combination and cell line as the input of a multi-layer perceptron network to get the synergistic score of the drug–drug combination on the cell line.  

Firstly, we preprocessed the feature data of drugs and cell lines. Because zero variance features can be considered useless features in machine learning, thus we first removed the zero variance features of drugs. Specifically, we calculated the variance for all column vectors in  DF ,  GF  and  CMF  and removed the columns with zero variance from these matrices to obtain the new matrices of  DF 1,  GF 1 and  CMF 1. Then, we adopted the same operation including first standardization and then a tanh followed by a second standardization as used in previous model of DeepSynergy to further process the feature data of drugs and cell lines. Secondly, Siamese network is a specific neural network architecture and the main structure of the Siamese network is two identical subnetworks that share the same configuration, parameters and weights, which can learn a complex relationship between two samples, especially the similarity relationship [ 44 ]. It was widely used to measure the similarity between two different pictures [ 45 – 47 ]. Besides, Jeon  et al.  [ 48 ] utilized a Siamese network to measure the response similarity of the two drugs. After inputting feature vectors of two drugs into the trained Siamese network, they can obtain two embedding vectors for the two drugs and calculate the cosine similarity of the embedding vectors as the drug response similarity. In addition, Su  et al.  [ 49 ] constructed a Siamese network for anti-cancer drug recommendation through directly ranking the drugs instead of predicting the exact value of drug response. In their study, Siamese network was utilized to measure the relative position between drugs for each cell line. Through minimizing the difference between the predicted relative position and the real relative position, an optimal Siamese network can be constructed to provide the rank for all the candidate drugs for investigated cell line. Different from previous study, we designed a novel and distinct Siamese network architecture (see  Figure 2 ) and combined the Siamese network module with random matrix projection module to learn the feature vector of drug–drug combination. Specifically, in the Siamese network, each subnetwork contained five convolution modules, one flatten layer, one dropout layer and one full connection layer. In each convolution module, we adopt one-dimensional convolutional neural network as the main structure, including convolutional layer, batch normalization

Finally, the vector was fed into a multi-layer perceptron network to obtain the predicted synergy value of the drug combination on the cell line.  

# Key Points  

• For drugs and cell lines, it is a new attempt to integrate multiple pieces of information as the final signature vec- tor. Especially, the copy number variation and mutation data were introduced into cell line features. • SNRMPACDC innovatively combines Siamese network and random matrix projection. • In both the Siamese network and the network dealing with cell lines, we take the one-dimensional convolu- tional network as the main part, which can better mine the implicit information in vectors than the conven- tional multi-layer perceptron network. • We verified the accuracy of the model from the perspec- tive of regression and classification.
