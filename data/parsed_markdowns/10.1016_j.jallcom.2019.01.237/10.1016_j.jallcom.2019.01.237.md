# Hot deformation characteristics and dynamic recrystallization mechanism of GH4730  Ni-based superalloy  

Jinglong Qu  1 , Xingfei Xie  , Zhongnan Bi  1 , Jinhui Du  1 , Maicang Zhang

Abstract:  The hot deformation behavior of GH4730 Ni-based superalloy above  temperature was studied in this work. The constitutive equation of this alloy was  established and the dynamic recrystallization mechanism was studied. The results indicate  that the recrystallization mechanism of the GH4730 superalloy is dominated by  discontinuous dynamic recrystallization nucleation, involving grain boundary bulging. The  dislocation substructures transform from dislocation cells and walls into sub-grain  boundaries during further deformation. The grain boundaries produce step-shaped  structures in order to maintain the low-energy surface state during the dynamic  recrystallization.  

Key words:  High-temperature alloys; Grain boundaries; Microstructure; Step-shaped  structure; TEM  

# 1. Introduction  

Ni-based superalloys have been successfully used for critical structural components in  gas turbine engines for several decades due to their excellent mechanical properties and  good oxidation resistance at high temperatures up to 650    [1,2,3]. With the fuel efficiency  improvement of gas turbine engines, it is imperative to develop new Ni-based superalloys  MANUSCRIPT to withstand severe environmental attacks which reduce the service life at higher  temperature. Because of the high production cost of powder metallurgy superalloys, an  urgent requirement to develop a commercial cast-wrought superalloy for turbine disc  applications at higher temperature occurs [4,5]. GH4730 Ni-based superalloy was  developed because of its superior combination of good service properties at higher  temperatures from 700 to 750   cast-wrought superalloys.  

Hot deformation behavior of Ni-based superalloys plays a critical role in the turbine  disk manufacture and is influenced by many factors [6,7]. The relationship between grain  boundaries and  γ ’ phases influences the deformation characteristics below the  temperature in U720Li superalloy [6]. The strain levels provide enough driving force to  ACCEPTED complete the recrystallization in AD730 superalloy below the  γ ’ solvus temperature [7].  The  γ ’ solvus temperature of AD730 superalloy is estimated to be about 1100    [4,8]. The  hot deformation behavior and recrystallization mechanism of the AD730 superalloy above  γ ’ solvus temperature remain unclear. Moreover, the hot forging technology has been  effectively improved, and the numerical modeling has been widely applied on manufacture  processing and production quality control. Due to the lack of understanding on the hot  deformation behavior during manufacture processing of the GH4730 superalloy, the  establishment of the hot deformation constitutive relationship and the dynamic  recrystallization model for the GH4730 superalloy is urgently required.  

The aim of this study is to understand the hot deformation constitutive relationship,  hot deformation behavior and dynamic recrystallization mechanism of the GH4730  superalloy through thermal simulation experimentation. The dislocation substructure of the  GH4730 superalloy during recrystallization was studied through transmission electron  microscopy (TEM). The recrystallization mechanism of the GH4730 superalloy above the  γ ’ solvus temperature was further discussed.

# 3.2. Dynamic recrystallization behavior  

Fig.3(a) shows the initial microstructure of the GH4730 superalloy before isothermal  compression test. The average grain size of superalloy before compression test is determined  to be about 35  µ m by the mean linear intercept method. The recrystallized grains are formed  during hot compression deformation, and the size of recrystallized grains increases with  increase in temperatures under the identical strain rate, as shown in Fig. 3(b) and (c).  

Under various hot deformation conditions, the grain boundary bulging phenomenon is  observed in the GH4730 superalloy subjected to compressive deformation, as presented in  Fig. 4(a)-(d). This indicates that the dominant dynamic recrystallization mechanism of the  GH4730 superalloy is discontinuous dynamic recrystallization. As the temperature increases  MANUSCRIPT to 1100   , the  γ ’ phases effectively inhibiting grain boundary migration have been  completely dissolved. The recrystallization nucleation is induced by grain boundary  migration accompanied by grain boundary bulging. The local grain boundary migration  caused by the stress unbalance in high dislocation density areas produces the bulging shape.  The severe strain gradient at the grain boundary could lead the grain-boundary dislocation  sources to emit dislocations towards the interior of grains during the compression  deformation. These dislocations interact with the basal plane dislocations and then form the  sub-grain boundaries, interrupting the grain bulged part. Subsequently, the annihilation and  rearrangement of dislocations cause recovery, and the low dislocation density areas are  formed in the center of sub-grains. The sub-grains continuously absorb dislocations and then  gradually are transformed into high angle grain boundaries (HAGB). The dynamic recrystallization is controlled by the dislocation substructure evolution and  ACCEPTED grain boundary migration, as presented in Fig. 4 and 5. The dislocation cells could transform  into sub-grain structures through the rearrangement and annihilation of dislocations, and then   Wusatowska-Sarnek  et al. [21]  and Chen et al. With increasing strain, the shape of initial grain boundary fluctuates, and the new  grains could be nucleated by bulging of the initial grain boundary. As the deformation further  proceeds, the sub-grain structures would spread all over the deformation structures through  the transformation from dislocation cells to sub-grain boundaries.  

During the deformation of polycrystalline materials, the grain boundary can effectively  inhibit the dislocation movement. When the dislocation density at the grain boundary exceeds  the corresponding absorption ability, the grain boundary bulging could occur [22]. Fig. 6  presents the initial stage of grain boundary bulging, i.e. the dislocations are absorbed by the  grain boundary. A mass of dislocations are piled up, and the stress concentration is produced  at the grain boundary and could further lead to grain boundary bulging. In order to reduce the  stress concentration at the grain boundary, the dislocations piled up near the grain boundary  would rearrange and form high amounts of dislocation cells, which would also lead to the  formation of sub-grains near the bulged grain boundary [18,23].  

When the stress along the grain boundary is uniform, the grain boundary would be in  equilibrium and maintain a straight morphology. The strain energy is concentrated near the  MANUSCRIPT grain boundary where the shear stress is applied, and the grain boundary is in  non-equilibrium state. When strain differences exist at the grain boundary, if the grain  boundary was subjected to stress or/and action, it would exist in a pushed state. Consequently,  the adjacent portion would be in tension state in order to rebalance the force. In order to  release the non-equilibrium shear strain, the grain boundary would be in a tension or pushed  state, leading to grain boundary migration and bulging. Winning et al. # 3.3. Step-shaped structure formation  

Fig. 7 shows that the step-shaped grain boundary appeared in the GH4730 superalloy  after hot deformation under different conditions. The grain boundary is inclined to be along  the coincidence site lattice (CSL) close packed direction to contain the maximum coincident  nodes or the shortest period, whereas a certain amount of coincident lattice atoms exist within  the high angle grain boundary structure [25]. In contrast, if the grain boundary orientation is  not along the close packed direction, the grain boundary could be dissociated into some  step-shaped grain boundaries along the close packed plane, and the secondary interface  dislocations would exist at the junction of two close packed planes. The step-shaped structure  could be generated at grain boundary because of the dislocation interactions, as shown in Fig.  7(a)-(c).  

The appearance of step-shaped grain boundary suggests that the crystals in the GH4730  MANUSCRIPT superalloy after hot deformation have relatively higher dislocation density. The formation of  steps accompanied by grain boundary dislocations is indispensable to the grain boundary  structure. The misorientation between grains is high, and the recrystallization has not been  fully completed. The amount of atoms at the grain boundary shared by two grains increases  with increase of the coincident sites existing at the grain boundary, whereas the atom  arrangement distortion degree reduces with increase of the coincident sites existing at the  grain boundary. Consequently, the corresponding grain boundary energy is decreased due to  ACCEPTED the increase of the coincident sites existing at the grain boundary. In order to retain the  low-energy surface state, the step-shaped structures are formed at the interface of the grain  boundary and the close packed plane {111} having the minimum surface energy, as indicated  in Fig. 7. The steps with different heights implies that these steps are produced by different  numbers of {111} planes. Gleiter et al. [26] also observed that the interaction between the  {111} planes in the grain and the grain boundary induced the formation of a step-shaped  structures in certain face-centered cubic (FCC) materials. Though the grains with FCC crystal  structure usually induce four series of steps due to four different {111} planes, not all steps  can be visible. The strong contrast could only be obtained for the steps formed by the {111}  planes nearly parallel to the grain boundary. In contrast, the higher-sized {111} plane  deviation from the grain boundary could lead to less apparent step morphology. The step  plane is a low-surface-energy crystal plane such as {111} planes, and the step-shaped or  serration grain boundary more preferably forms than the straight grain boundary [24]. In  addition, the grain boundary surface atoms are easy to be absorbed because of the relatively  high energy state and the corresponding residual bonds, resulting in the surface energy  reduction. The step-shaped grain boundary could induce the atom surface diffusion and the  surface absorption. The mechanism of step-shaped grain boundary migration is considered as  the atom absorption and emission process [27]. Under the action of the driving force, the  atoms could be emitted from the step and then be absorbed by the grain boundary. The grain  boundary migration is driven by the different stored energy between the recrystallized region  and the initial region, and this migration process would stop when this driving force is  consumed. The grain boundary movement is terminate when the grain boundary migrates into  a completely recrystallized grain [24].  

According to the above results, the step-shaped model of the grain boundary during  dynamic recrystallization for GH4730 superalloy is illustrated in Fig. 8. These step-shaped  structures are formed by the {111} planes, which had the minimum deviation degree from the  grain boundaries. The step distances are increased with decrease in the deviation degree  between these {111} planes and grain boundaries. The boundaries containing steps are  formed through the interaction between the {111} planes and the grain boundaries. A step  height with the same dislocation Burger’s factor is generated at the location where the  ACCEPTED dislocation line reaches the grain boundary surface [28]. If the grain is free of dislocations,  the boundary surface steps would form through the close packed plane nucleation. The atom  deposition at these boundary surfaces causes the step movement and grain growth. The  growth rate of recrystallized grains strongly depends on the growth direction. The boundary  of the growing grain is usually parallel to a certain crystallographic plane, e.g., {111} planes  [28]. Since the {111} planes in the FCC materials such as GH4730 superalloy is apt to form  steps, the growth rate in the direction perpendicular to {111} planes is the minimum. The hot deformation behavior of GH4730 Ni-based superalloy above the  γ ’ solvus  temperature was studied in this work. The main conclusions are summarized as follows.  

(1) The hot deformation constitutive equation of the GH4730 superalloy is:

The Q value of hot deformation obtained through experimentation is relatively lower,  which further confirms the excellent deformability of the GH4730 superalloy.  

(2) The dynamic recrystallization mechanism of the GH4730 superalloy above the  γ ’  solvus temperature is mainly based on the discontinuous dynamic recrystallization through  grain boundary bulging. When the dislocation density exceeds the absorbability of the grain  boundary or the dislocation absorption process required a certain incubation period, the grain  boundary bulging occurs. The migration ability of the grain boundaries increases with the  deformation temperature. The discontinuous dynamic recrystallization easily occurs at higher  deformation temperature.  

(3) The grain boundary could form a certain angle with the {111} close-packed planes  during dynamic recrystallization. The step-shaped structures are produced at the grain  boundary in order to retain the low-energy surface state.
