# Discussion  

From the above mentioned results, after partial substitution of Ni by Co, γ’  phase precipitates become more cuboidal and both the hot workability and  DRX of IN713C superalloy are enhanced significantly.  

γ/γ’ phase lattice misfit is an important factor determining the morphology  of γ’ precipitate. It has been reported that the precipitate is close to spherical if  the γ/γ’ misfit is near-zero [20-21], and appearance of γ’ phase transforms to  cuboidal characteristics when misfit value are between 0.002 and 0.005.

# Conclusions  

Substitution of Ni by Co leads to lower driving force for formation of γ’ phase.  With the increase of Co fraction, the γ’ phase precipitates turned finer and more  cuboidal shape. As a result, the hot workability of IN713C alloy is improved  significantly by Co addition. This can be ascribed to the changed γ/γ’ interface,  the decrease in activation energy, misfit between γ/γ’ phase lattice, the stacking  

fault energy as well as the decrease fraction of γ’ phase after adding Co.
