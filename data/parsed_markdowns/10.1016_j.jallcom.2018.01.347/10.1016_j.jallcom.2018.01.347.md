The in ﬂ uences of continuous cooling rate on microstructure and microhardness of the weld simulated heat-affected zone (HAZ) of Ti-22Al-25Nb (at.%) alloy were investigated in this study. The continuous cooling process of the weld HAZ of Ti-22Al-25Nb alloy was simulated using a Gleeble3500 thermo- mechanical simulator. The phase constituent investigated by X-ray diffraction showed that the weld simulated HAZs were all composed of lath-shaped O phase, equiaxed  a 2  particle and  b /B2 matrix at different continuous cooling rates. Microstructural observations by scanning electron microscopy indi- cated that the size of O phase signi ﬁ cantly decreased and the shape of  a 2  particle did not obviously change with the increase of continuous cooling rate. Quantitative analysis presented that with increasing continuous cooling rate the volume fraction of O phase decreased, while that of  a 2  and  b /B2 phases increased. Phase transformation temperatures were determined by using dilatometric analysis and the weld simulated HAZ continuous cooling transformation diagram was determined. The microhardness of the weld simulated HAZ increased with the increase of continuous cooling rate.

# 1. Introduction  

As is well-known, weld heat-affected zone (HAZ) subjected to a severe and short weld thermal cycle during welding process pro- duces high temperature and causes very steep temperature gradient, which affects the phase transformation in the weld HAZ [ 1 , 2 ]. For certain alloys, weld HAZ after welding has high hardness, low ductility, and is easy to create crack [ 3 ], therefore, weld HAZ is harm to the mechanical property of weld joint [ 4 e 6 ]. Grain size and microstructure are often the main factors affecting the mechanical property of weld joint and these two factors are in turns related to the cooling rate of the welding process [ 7 e 9 ]. Therefore, it is necessary to study the evolution of microstructure and micro- hardness of the weld simulated HAZ at various continuous cooling rates and construct weld simulated HAZ continuous cooling transformation (WSHCCT) of alloys.

Therefore, it is feasible that the Gleeble thermo- mechanical simulator was used to simulate the weld HAZ of alloys.  

The ordered orthorhombic Ti 2 AlNb-based titanium interme- tallic alloys, due to their high speci ﬁ c strength, excellent oxidation resistance, high speci ﬁ c modulus, good creep resistance, high temperature performance and superior processability, have been widely studied for potential application in aircraft engines [ 16 e 19 ]. The alloy with a nominal composition of Ti-22Al-25Nb (at.%), a second-generation Ti 2 AlNb-based alloy (Nb    25%), is a signi ﬁ cant member of the Ti 2 AlNb-based alloy group because of its excellent mechanical properties [ 20 e 22 ]. In order to further explore the commercial potential of Ti 2 AlNb-based alloys in aircraft, many re- searchers have studied the welding of Ti-22Al-25Nb alloy [ 23 e 28 ]. HAZ is the main region affected the mechanical properties of weld joint. However, reported research work on continuous cooling process of the weld HAZ region of Ti-22Al-25Nb alloy is very scanty. The present work is planned to study the evolution of micro- structure and microhardness of weld simulated HAZ of Ti-22Al- 25Nb alloy at various continuous cooling rates and construct WSHCCT curves by using thermo-mechanical weld HAZ physical simulation.

# 3.2. Effect of cooling rate on the microstructure  

Fig. 3  shows the microstructures resulting from phase trans- formation in the weld simulated HAZ of Ti-22Al-25Nb alloy due to thermal cycles (hold at peak temperature of 1450    C for 2 s, and then cooled down to 1100    C, followed by continuous cooling rates from very low to high). As shown in  Fig. 3  a1, b1, c1, d1 and e1, the prior  b /B2 grain sizes in the weld simulated HAZ at different continuous cooling rates are all coarse even at very slow continuous cooling rate. The microstructure is very sensitive to the cooling rate, the morphologies of the weld simulated HAZ at different contin- uous cooling rates are various within the prior  b /B2 grains (as shown in  Fig. 3  a2, b2, c2, d2 and e2).  Fig. 3  a3, b3, c3, d3 and e3 show the size of lath-shaped O phase decreases with the increase of continuous cooling rate, and the shape of  a 2  particle has not obvious change with increasing continuous cooling rate. The area fractions of different phases were determined by image analysis on SEM images using ImageJ software. Each measured value was the average of 10 readings obtained from 10 different images.  V v  ¼  A A [ 32 ],  V v  is the volume fraction of structure measured in the speci- ﬁ ed area;  A A  is the area fraction of structure measured in the same area. The volume fractions of O phase,  a 2  phase and  b /B2 matrix were determined from the area fraction. The quantitative analysis results of O phase,  a 2  phase and  b /B2 matrix in the weld simulated HAZ at different continuous cooling rates were presented in  Fig. 4 . Results of image analysis ( Fig. 4 ) con ﬁ rmed that the volume fraction of O phase decreased with the increase of continuous cooling rate, while the volume fractions of  a 2  and  b /B2 increased with increasing continuous cooling rate.

# 3.4. Effect of cooling rate on the microhardness  

The microhardness test is a simple, yet powerful test that re- ﬂ ects materials' processing performance and allows prediction of materials' mechanical properties since it is shown to have a linear relationship with yield stress [ 35 , 36 ]. Therefore, it is necessary to investigate the microhardness in the weld simulated HAZ of Ti- 22Al-25Nb alloy at various continuous cooling rates, and the re- sults are shown in  Fig. 7 . The data plotted on the graph ( Fig. 7 ) is an average value of  ﬁ ve measurements. From  Fig. 7 , it can be seen that the microhardness of the weld simulated HAZ depends on the microstructure and increases gradually with increasing continuous cooling rate. The hardness of the existing phases in Ti 2 AlNb alloys follows the order:  a 2  phase  >  b /B2 phase  >  O phase [ 16 , 37 ]. The microhardness of Ti-22Al-25Nb alloy gradually increases with the increase of continuous cooling rate since the volume fraction of O phase decreases and the volume fractions of  a 2  and  b /B2 phases increase with increasing continuous cooling rate. In addition, the microhardness of the weld simulated HAZ of Ti-22Al-25Nb alloy is higher than that of the base material (380 HV) [ 38 ]. Based on the above results, it indicates that softening of the HAZ is not a problem in welding of Ti-22Al-25Nb alloy by controlling the cooling rate of the weld joint.  

# 4. Conclusions  

A series of heat treatment experiments for the weld simulated HAZ of Ti-22Al-25Nb alloy was conducted to understand the evo- lution behaviors of microstructure and microhardness with increasing continuous cooling rate. The following conclusions can be drawn from the present work:  

(1) The weld simulated HAZs of Ti-22Al-25Nb alloy at the various continuous cooling rates are all composed of lath- shaped O phase, equiaxed  a 2  particle and  b /B2 matrix. (2) In the weld simulated HAZs of Ti-22Al-25Nb alloy, the size of O phase decreases with increasing continuous cooling rate and the shape of  a 2  particle does not obviously change with the increase of the continuous cooling rate; the volume fraction of O phase decreases with the increase of continuous cooling rate, while the volume fractions of  a 2  and  b /B2 phases increase with increasing continuous cooling rate.
