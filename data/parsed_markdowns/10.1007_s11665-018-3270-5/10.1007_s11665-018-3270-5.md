# Characterization of the Hot Deformation Behavior of a Newly Developed Nickel-Based Superalloy  

Zhaoxia Shi, Xiaofeng Yan, Chunhua Duan, Cunjiang Tang, and Enxiang Pu (Submitted September 7, 2017; in revised form January 10, 2018)  

To clarify the microstructural evolution and hot workability of GH4282 during hot forming processes, the hot deformation behavior of this superalloy was investigated by isothermal compression tests in the tem-   2 1   perature interval of 950-1210 C and the strain rate range of 0.01-10 s with a true strain of 0.7. The results show that the ’¼éow stresses decrease with an increase in the deformation temperature and a decrease in the strain rate. The characteristic of dynamic recrystallization is revealed by the ’¼éow curves. The variation rule of the ’¼éow stress can be well described by the hyperbolic sine type equation, and the thermal deformation activation energy is determined to be 498.118 kJ/mol. The optimum hot working parameters   2 1 are 1100-1180 C and 0.01-0.1 s , under which the ’¼üne and uniform microstructure can be obtained.  

Keywords constitutive equation, dynamic recrystallization, ’¼éow behavior, processing map, superalloy GH4282  

# 1. Introduction  

It has been well known that  c ┬ó -strengthened nickel-based superalloys are specially developed for structural applications at elevated temperature owing to their favorable combination of good high-temperature strength, excellent creep resistance and outstanding corrosion resistance. Waspaloy, Rene ┬ó 41 and Inconel 740H are known to be representative nickel-based superalloys and have been widely used in gas turbines, combustion systems and other critical applications related to nuclear technology and power generation from coal (Ref  1 - 3 ). GH4282 (Haynes 282), a newly developed  c ┬ó -strengthened nickel-based superalloy, is designed to meet the demands for performance at a high service temperature while maintaining weldability and fabricability (Ref  4 ,  5 ). For this alloy, moderate amounts of Al and Ti not only can form  c ┬ó  precipitation to strengthen the matrix but can also prevent the deterioration of the welding performance. Co and Mo are used as solution strengthening elements. Meanwhile, the addition of Co would be effective for the precipitation of the  c ┬ó  phase and increase the superalloys stability at higher temperatures. Cr plays an important role in providing a resistance to hot corrosion and oxidation. C, together with Cr, Mo and Ti, is used to form M 23 C 6 , M 6 C and MC carbides, which appear at grain boundaries in the desired morphology to produce good creep strength and ductility. Furthermore, B, Ce and Mg are usually added in trace amounts to strengthen the grain boundary. Therefore, GH4282 exhibits good comprehensive mechanical properties. In particular, the new alloy has a unique combina- tion of excellent creep strength and thermal stability that approach or transcend those of commonly used superalloys, such as Waspaloy and Rene ┬ó 41 (Ref  5 ).  

As a relatively new alloy, the previous research on GH4282 superalloy mainly focused on its microstructure characteriza- tion during a heat treatment or welding process, the fatigue properties and the oxidation behavior (Ref  6 - 9 ). It is well known that for superalloys, the desirable microstructure and required mechanical performance are largely dependent on the hot working process. However, the hot deformation process of GH4282 superalloy is very complicated due to the increase in the number of alloying elements and the complex system of phases. Thus, it is necessary to investigate the hot deformation behavior of the alloy to obtain the improved microstructure and mechanical performance.  

The analysis of the ’¼éow stress and the constitutive modeling equations are commonly used to describe hot deformation behaviors of superalloys. According to Momeni  s ’¼éow curve analysis of Incoloy 901 (Ref  10 ), the exceptional variation of strains related to the peak points of the ’¼éow curves with respect to the strain rate was caused by the change in the kinetics of the dynamic softening mechanisms. Wang et al. (Ref  11 ) deduced the dependence of the peak stress on the deformation temper- ature and the strain rate of the 740H alloy and compared its activation energy with those of other superalloys to illustrate the alloy  s workability. To reasonably conduct a hot working process (i.e., hot forging, rolling and extrusion), it is of great importance for the designer to understand the deformation mechanisms and microstructural evolution of metal materials. According to the deformed microstructure analysis, the mech- anism of dynamic recrystallization (DRX) of the nickel-based superalloys GH690 (Ref  12 ), 617B (Ref  13 ) and Nimonic 80A (Ref  14 ) was analyzed, which was proved to be closely associated with the deformation temperature and strain rate. Among the many methods, the processing maps established using the dynamic material model (DMM) (Ref  15 - 17 ) can effectively re’¼éect the hot deformation behavior under various deformation conditions and assess the workability of the metal materials. For instance, Cheng et al. (Ref  18 ) constructed the processing map of IN718 superalloy for plane strain compres- sion in the temperature range of 900   C to 1050   C and put forward two favorable hot working regions. Shi et al. (Ref  19 ) analyzed the microstructure of deformed specimens to verify the processing maps established for GH4945 superalloy and found that there were two preferable hot working regimes to conduct hot workings (1082-1131   C, 0.004-0.018 s  1   and 1134-1150   C, 0.018-0.213 s  1 ), in which the homogeneous and ’¼üne-grained microstructure was obtained.  

In the present work, the isothermal compression tests of GH4282 superalloy were conducted over wide temperature and strain rate ranges. The hot deformation behavior was investi- gated by analyzing ’¼éow curves, developing the constitutive equation and establishing processing maps. The microstructures of the specimens were observed by optical microscopy (OM), ’¼üeld emission scanning electron microscopy (FESEM) and electron backscatter diffraction (EBSD). Additionally, the optimized hot working parameters were proposed for improv- ing the workability and controlling the microstructure.

From the ’¼éow stress curves (Fig.  5 a, b), it can be observed that the alloy has a great deformation resistance at low deformation temperatures with high strain rates, which is attributed to the precipitation strengthening of the  c ┬ó  phase on the  c  matrix when the deformation temperature is lower than the solvus temperature of the  c ┬ó  phase (1008   C).
