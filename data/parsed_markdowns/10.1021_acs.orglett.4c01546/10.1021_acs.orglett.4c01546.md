ABSTRACT:  Because of the three-dimensional bioisosteric feature, bicyclo[1.1.1]pentylamines (BCPAs) are valuable scaffolds in synthetic chemistry and medicinal chemistry. Here, we report a Halogen Atom Transfer (XAT) mediated radical C − N coupling between C3-iodo-BCPs and diazonium salts in the presence of base. Similarly, a multicomponent reaction (MCR) enables the simulta- neous construction of the C − C bond and C − N bond simultaneously. Versatile roles of diazonium salts were also explored.

Recently, a diverse array of efficient strategies to synthesize these unique BCPAs through 1,3-function aliz ation of bicyclo[1.1.1]pentanes (BCPs) with nitrogen-contained pre- cursors have been developed.   In 2016, the Baran group demonstrated a pioneering method to prepare the mono- substituted BCPAs employing nucleophilic “turbo amides” as the aminating reagents.   A similar study on one-pot aminoalkylation protocol has also been reported by the Gleason group.   In 2017, the Uchiyama group developed an iron-catalyzed radical three component carboamination of BCPs with  α -iodoaziridines, opening up new avenues to access multifunction aliz ed BCP derivatives.   Notably, MacMillan and co-workers illustrated a modular methodology utilizing metal la photo redox catalysis to afford the corresponding  N - heterocycles substituted BCPAs.  

C3-halo substituted BCPAs have been recognized as another appealing structure in organic synthesis.   The core strategy is to construct C − halogen and C − N bonds simultaneously based on nitrogen-centered radical mediated cascade addition of highly reactive [1.1.1.]propellane. Pioneering examples have been illustrated by Leonori,   Hong,   Anderson,   Ai ssa, Jiang 5e   and others 5f   ( Figure 1 B). Moreover, the reactive site of the C − halogen bond on C3-halo substituted BCPAs allows for downstream diversification to access molecular diversity.  

Among the numerous studies on activation of alkyl halides including fluoroalkyl halides,   Halogen Atom Transfer (XAT) provides a powerful tool to construct a series of valuable compounds. The process is distinctively facilitated by the difference of bond dissociation energy (BDE) between the reactants and products while traditional approaches employ harsher reaction conditions or specific catalysis matches the varied redox potentials.   Very recently, Leonori 9   and our group 10   independently developed XAT-mediated C − N cou- pling of alkyl iodides. The diazonium salts serve as not only the precursor of aryl radical generated from the interaction with promoter but also the source of diazo substituent on the final products.  

As an important version of nitrogen-contained scaffolds, diazo compounds have attracted particular attention because of diverse applications in material science.   Here, we envision direct C − N coupling of tunable and bench-stable C3-halo substituted BCPs with readily available diazonium salts can smoothly occur via a base-induced XAT process ( Figure 1 C). On the other hand, an XAT-mediated multicomponent reaction (MCR) 14   involving [1.1.1.]propellane can be established because of the extremely high reactivity of these strained frameworks ( Figure 1 C). Notably, this approach allows assembly of the C − C bond and C − N bond simultaneously. Furthermore, it also enables quick construction of two quaternary centers, which is very challenging in traditional synthetic chemistry. Overall, two rapid and complementary entries toward the preparation of diverse azo-substituted BCPs bearing fluorinated motifs or alkyl groups will be developed under transition-metal-free reaction conditions.  

With the above hypothesis in mind, we initially investigated the reaction between readily available C3-iodo-BCPs and diazonium salts in the presence of Cs 2 CO 3  as the promoter and methanol as the solvent ( Scheme 1 ). To our delight, a number of functional groups including H, Me, F, Cl, Br, CF 3 , and ketone were well tolerated, furnishing the corresponding azo-substituted BCPs ( 3a - g ) in moderate yields. The compatibility of sulfonated BCP derivates ( 1b - e ) was also evaluated under standard reaction condition. Substrates bearing (hetero)aryl substituents could smoothly participate in this mild C − N coupling. The challenge with current methodology is the low efficiency on the cleavage of C − I bond since only about half conversion of C3-iodo-BCPs was obtained even under the optimized conditions. It is found that the strength of the C − I bond in C3-iodo-BCP is stronger than the one in tertiary alkyl iodide (for details, see the Supporting Information ).  

Next, we turned our attention to the multicomponent reactions with high step- and atom-economy which proceeded through a cascade radical addition driven by a strain-release and sequential trapping process. Fortunately, acceptable yields of azo-substituted BCPs were obtained when the solvent was simply replaced by acetonitrile ( Scheme 2 ). Delightedly, a wide range of diazonium salt derivatives bearing electron- donating or -withdrawing groups underwent alkylation smoothly to give the corresponding products ( 6a − h ). Recently, development of methodologies to prepare fluoroalkyl-substituted BCPs is of great interest in medicinal chemistry due to the fine-tuned physicochemical properties of drug candidates.   Indeed, the established conditions could be applied to readily available fluoroalkyl iodides as the sources of fluoroalkyl radicals via the Halogen Atom Transfer (XAT) process. The io do di flu oro acetamide s as well as iododifluor- oacetate underwent the coupling efficiently to furnish the desired products ( 7u − z ); even the amides installed with a free proton were suitable with the reaction conditions. Sulfonyl- based substrate ( 4z ) was also a competent coupling partner. Moreover, di- or trifluoroethyl patterns were successfully incorporated along with azo-groups into [1.1.1.]propellane to furnish the azo-substituted BCP derivates ( 7ab-ac ) in moderate yields. Notably, the reaction of trifluoromethyl iodide worked well to give the corresponding product ( 7ad ) in 90% yield.  

To better showcase the synthetic utility of the current protocol, we first employed alkyl-substituted C3-iodo-BCP ( 9 ) as the starting material since the coupled product could be potentially regarded as a bioisostere of 1,4-substituted arene. As a result, the desired product ( 10 ) was obtained in 65% yield ( Figure 2 A). Late-stage function aliz ation of alkyl iodide derived from bioactive molecule  DL -Menthol was conducted, and the corresponding product ( 12 ) was obtained in 68% yield ( Figure 2 B). The reaction could be scaled-up with slightly decreased yields to furnish the desired products ( 6d − e ) as synthetic surrogates which allowed us to explore the further transformations ( Figure 2 C). Palladium-catalyzed Miyaura borylation from azo-substituted BCP ( 6e ) afforded the corresponding boronic ester ( 13 ) in 50% yield ( Figure 2 C, eq 1). Reductive C − S coupling employing phthalimide-derived thiolating reagent as the electrophile was also achieved ( Figure 2 C, eq 2).   Similarly, Suzuki or Sonogashira coupling of azo- substituted BCP ( 6d ) occurred smoothly, leading to the formation of desired products ( 15 ,  16 ) in 77% and 88% yields, respectively ( Figure 2 C, eqs 3 − 4).  

In conclusion, we have developed a base-induced Halogen Atom Transfer (XAT) process to prepare a series of azo- substituted BCPs from C3-iodo-BCPs under transition-metal- free conditions. Importantly, a multicomponent reaction employing alkyl iodides, diazonium salts, and [1.1.1.]- propellane has been established, displaying high efficiency
