The microstructure–property relationship of deformed parts has been becoming very critical, especially for aerospace components. The design of manufacturing process to achieve the unique and required microstructure and property is thus critical. For turbine disc, ﬁne microstructure is required for the bore region of the disc to provide high tension strength and fatigue resistance at low temperature (420–650   C). The coarse microstructure is also needed for its rim region to possess the excellent rupture life and crack growth resistance at high temperature (>760   C). A unique heat treatment method was developed to produce the gradient microstructure from the bore to the rim regions. In this study, the gradient temperature heat treatment process (GTHT) was carried out on FGH 4096 superalloys under the conditions of (1080   C 260   C /30 min, AC) and (1140   C 300   C /30 min, AC), and two structural-gradient- materials (SGMs) were obtained. The SGM produced by GTHT under the condition of (1080   C 260   C / 30 min, AC) has gradient microstructure from the equiaxed grains to the ﬂatted grains. In between the equiaxed and ﬂatted microstructures, a unique transition region is found. The dual-size gradient material was thus successfully fabricated by the GTHT process under the condition of (1140   C 300   C /30 min, AC). The grain size of coarse microstructure is approximately 40  l m, while the grain size of ﬁne microstruc- ture is about 4  l m. The microstructural mechanisms of the SGMs during hot working process are iden- tiﬁed in this research. Furthermore, the developed dual-size gradient material has potential application in dual-property turbine disc.

Investigation on the relationship between microstructure and mechanical property is a critical and fundamental issue in materi- als science  [5] . It becomes especially crucial for aerospace compo- nents such as turbine disc since the microstructure–property relationship affects the design of manufacturing processes  [6–8] . For turbine disc, ﬁne microstructure is required for the inner region of the disc (bore) to provide high tension strength and fatigue resistance at low temperature (420–650   C)  [9,10] . The coarse microstructure, on the other hand, is also needed for its outer region (rim) to possess the excellent fatigue life and crack growth resistance at high temperature (>760   C)  [11,12] . The gradient structural material is thus an ideal material for such components working in severe strain condition and wide temperature range from one part to another within one component.  

With the rapid development of materials science and engineer- ing technologies, structural-gradient-materials (SGMs), which especially refer to the gradient materials with different mechanical properties, have been paid more and more attentions for their tai- lor-made features requested by some unique applications. Many new manufacturing and processing techniques have thus long been explored to fabricate quality gradient materials conveniently and economically. These include powder metallurgy (PM)  [1] , chemical  

To study the gradient material in this research, FGH 4096 super- alloy is chosen as the case study material. The alloy is generally regarded as one of the most important powder metallurgy superal- loys for fabrication of aerospace components such as turbine disc for its unique combination of mechanical and physical properties, i.e., high strength, excellent crack resistance, and microstructural stabil- ity up to 750   C  [13,14] . Recently, the hot deformation behavior [15] , dynamic recrystallization (DRX)  [16] , recrystallization mecha- nism  [17,18]  of FGH 4096 superalloy have been extensively ex- plored and investigated. The research reveals that the dislocations in the alloy facilitate the occurrence of DRX at the previous particle boundary (PPB) of the alloy. When the PPB is entirely covered with DRX grains, the ﬁrst layer of the necklace structure is formed. In the course of hot forging process, DRX grains form a single layer at the low strain and evolve to several layers at the large strain. Deforma- tion temperature has a great effect on the recrystallization nucle- ation and grain growth. To obtain ﬁne grains, hot forging should be carried out at the temperature of 1050–1080   C. After two passes of multiple forging, the grain size is signiﬁcantly reﬁned from 30.0 to 2.2  l m, and the reﬁnement mechanism is found to be the repeated DRX. If the coarse grain is needed, the annealing treatment of the as- deformed superalloy needs to be carried out at the temperature of 1140–1170   C.  

However, how to prepare the gradient microstructure of the al- loy via controlling its recrystallization is a critical and most practi- cal problem. This research tried to address this issue. This paper articulates how to prepare the gradient microstructure by gradient temperature heat treatment (GTHT) technique and the microstruc- tural mechanisms of the gradient microstructure is also explored in this research.

The nominal chemical composition of the hot isostatic pressed (HIPed) FGH 4096 superalloy is: C, 0.02; Al, 2.2; W, 4.0; Nb, 0.8; Mo, 4.0; Ti, 3.7; Cr, 16.0; Co, 13.0; Ni–bal. The prealloyed powder particles with the diameter from 50 to 150  l m were prepared by plasma rotating electrode process (PREP). The powder was encapsulated in a stainless steel capsule and the HIPed billet was fabricated by duplex HIP under the condition of 1030   C/120 MPa/1 h + 1170   C/140 MPa/ 2 h. The multiple forging (upsetting with a height reduction of 30% and drawing with a cross-section reduction of 60%) was carried out at the temperature of 1110   C and the strain rate of 0.15 s  1   in a hydraulic press, and then followed by subsequent oil quenching (OQ). Two experimental bars were machined from the multiple forged superalloy.  

For experimental bar 1#, GTHT was then employed using the setup shown in Fig. 1 b and the parameters listed in  Table 1 . The inside and outside temperatures ( T in  and  T out ) of the experimental bar 1# are about 1080 and 260   C, respectively.  

However, the annealing treatment of experimental bar 2# was conducted before GTHT. The detailed procedure is soaking at 1080 ± 3   C for 30 min and cooling down to room temperature via water quenching (WQ). GTHT was then carried out. The inside and outside temperatures of the experimental bar 2# are about 1140 and 300   C, respectively. After GTHT, the experimental bars were air cooled (AC) to room temperature.  

Optical microscope (OM) observation was done for the experimental bars, espe- cially in the transition region of the gradient microstructure. The gradient micro- structure was observed using ZEISS SUPRA 55 scanning electron microscope (SEM), and the grain fraction analysis was performed using an Oxford electron backscatter diffraction (EBSD). Oxford electron backscatter diffraction patterns (EBSPs) were obtained using 20 kV accelerating voltage, 45 nA beam current and 25 mm working distance. High-resolution EBSD was conducted using the grid spac- ing of 0.50  l m. EBSPs were indexed automatically using the software package CHANNEL 5, and the microstructure was reconstructed by calculating the orienta- tion difference (misorientations) between neighboring data points. Energy disper- sive spectrometer (EDS) was used to analyze the chemical composition and in order to understand whether it has composition mutation or not. The objective of these researches is to verify whether the gradient microstructure has only micro- structure variation, but does not have composition mutation.  

Furthermore, the nano-indentation test was also conducted in the Tribo inden- ter with a Berkovich indenter. The tip truncation of the indenter was calibrated using a reference specimen of fused silica and the tip radius is 300 nm. Measure- ment was performed in a 3    5 pattern with a constant distance of 50  l m in the transition zone of the gradient-structured material. A peak load of 8000  l N was used with a loading/unloading rate of 500  l N/s. The positions of the indents were checked in situ by OM.  

# 3. Results and discussion  

# 3.1. SGM produced by GTHT under the condition of (1080   C 260   C / 30 min, AC)  

The Typical microstructure of the SGM produced by GTHT under the condition of (1080   C 260   C /30 min, AC) is shown in  Fig. 2 a. The inside region of the bar has an equiaxed microstructure with a number of recrystallization grains. These equiaxed grains repre- sent the static recrystallization occurred in this side of the experi- mental bar 1#. However, the outside region exhibits a ﬂatted structure. The ﬂatted grains reveal that the static recrystallization did not occur in the outside of the experimental bar as the temper- ature is lower than the recrystallization temperature. Meanwhile, a transition region between the above-mentioned two regions is found. With a higher magniﬁcation, a number of equiaxed grains are found among the ﬂatted grains in this region, as shown in Fig. 2 b. In general, a novel SGM with an obvious equiaxed-ﬂatted gradient microstructure was produced GTHT under the condition of (1080   C 260   C /30 min, AC). However, whether this SGM has only microstructure variance and does not have composition mutation needs to be further investigated.  

The EDS results obtained from the gradient microstructure of experimental bar 1# is shown in  Fig. 3 . It indicates that the equi- axed-grain area has the same chemical composition compared to the ﬂatted-grain area. No signiﬁcant difference in Ni (Cr, Co, Ti, Mo, Al, and W) concentration is observed in the gradient micro- structure. The SGM obtained under the condition of (1080   C 260   C / 30 min, AC) does not have the composition mutation in the whole microstructure variance regions, e.g., equiaxed, transition and the ﬂatted regions. This is the real SGM with the homogeneous chemical concentration and the obviously different microstructures.

# 3.2. SGM produced by GTHT under the condition of (1140   C 300   C / 30 min, AC)  

The typical microstructure of the SGM produced under the con- dition of (1140   C 300   C /30 min, AC) is shown in  Fig. 5 . The dual-size gradient material was also successfully fabricated by the GTHT manufacturing process. The grain size of the coarse microstructure is approximate 40  l m, and the grain size of the ﬁne microstructure is about 4  l m. Obviously, the grain size has been greatly reﬁned after the multiple passes of forging and annealing treatment. The ﬁne grains show the dynamic and static recrystallization occurred in the multiple forging and annealing treatment, respectively. However, the ﬁne grains did not grow up in GTHT process. Mean- while, the coarse grains represent the grain growth occurred in this side of experimental bar 2# in GTHT process.  

Prior art  [18]  shows that microstructure evolution in annealing process obviously exhibits three characteristic periods, e.g., incu- bation, nucleation, and grain growth periods. Furthermore, anneal- ing temperature has a great effect on microstructure evolution. When the annealing treatment is carried out below the tempera- ture of 990   C, only individual boundary migration is observed, but most of the boundaries survive in migration. However, a gen- eral boundary migration takes place when the annealing tempera- ture is 1020–1080   C and nucleation occurs. In this period, severe boundary migration ﬁnally leads to the nucleation of recrystalliza- tion. When the annealing temperature is above 1110   C, nuclei grow up to equiaxed grains and recrystallization occurs in the grain growth period. If ﬁne grain is needed, annealing treatment should be carried out under the condition of (1080   C/30 min, WQ).

# 3.3. Microstructural mechanisms of the SGM during hot working process  

Fig. 7  depicts the microstructural mechanisms of the SGMs dur- ing hot working processes. Four hot working processes (hot iso- static pressed, HIPed; multiple forged, MFed; annealing treated, ATed; gradient temperature heat treated, GTHTed) obviously pres- ent ﬁve characteristic structures, including previous particle boundaries (PPBs) network, necklace microstructure, ﬁne-grained microstructure, equiaxed-ﬂatted gradient microstructure and dual-size gradient microstructure, respectively.  

The typical microstructure of HIPed FGH 4096 superalloy con- sists of grains with the average grain diameter of about 30.0  l m, which reveals a prior particle boundary (PPB) net existing in the al- loy. After multiple forging process, the prior particles were de- formed and the DRX took place at PPBs  [17] . The equiaxed grains surrounding the non-recrystallized grain is called necklace micro- structure. Gottstein proposed the authoritative mechanism of the necklace formation  [19] .  

Based on the gradient in heating temperatures (1080   C inside and 260   C outside), the entirely different microstructure evolu- tions took place in the experimental bar 1#. The controlled static recrystallization occurred in the inside region of the bar. However, the ﬂatted microstructure remained in the outside region since the temperature is lower than the recrystallization temperature. SGM with obviously equiaxed-ﬂatted gradient microstructure was pro- duced by GTHT under the condition of (1080   C 260   C /30 min, AC).  

Through microstructure observation  [18] , it is found that the necklace is the intermediate microstructure in grain reﬁnement process. During the multiple forging process, dislocations stimu- late the occurrence of DRX at PPBs. When PPB is entirely covered with the recrystallized grains, necklace microstructure is formed. Meanwhile, the grains have been reﬁned for the ﬁrst time. For the experimental bar 2#, there is a subsequent annealing treat- ment process after the multiple forging. The controlled static recrystallization occurred in the annealing treatment at 1080   C, that leads to grain reﬁnement. When several necklaces were formed, grains were reﬁned for many times. # 4. Conclusions  

The gradient temperature heat treatment processes were car- ried out under the conditions of (1080   C 260   C /30 min, AC) and (1140   C 300   C /30 min, AC), and two structural gradient microstruc- tures (SGMs) were prepared. The microstructural mechanisms for SGMs in hot working processes have been developed. The SGM produced by GTHT under the condition of (1080   C 260   C /30 min, AC) has gradient microstructure from the equiaxed grains to the ﬂatted grains. In between the equiaxed and the ﬂatted microstruc- ture, a unique transition region is found, which reveals the engaged characteristics with an obvious S-shape. The dual-size gradient material was successfully fabricated by GTHT process under the condition of (1140   C 300   C /30 min, AC). The grain size of coarse microstructure is about 40  l m, and the grain size of ﬁne microstructure is approximately 4  l m. This novel SGM process has a potential application in fabrication of dual-property turbine disc.
