# 1. Introduction  

If the main concern is the resistance against oxidation then continuous, dense, well-adhered, stable  film formation on the surface of the alloy is needed such as jet engines and power plants where  working temperatures may reach to three-quarters of the alloys’ melting temperatures [1, 2]. The  alloys with these protective oxide films are generally classified as alumina (Al 2 O 3 ) or chromia  (Cr 2 O 3 ) forming alloys as they have a considerable amount of stable oxide forming elements of Al  or Cr [2-4]. cyclic oxidation tests of NiAlCr alloys with the addition of 0, 1, 2.5, 3.8 at. % Mg at 1027°C.
