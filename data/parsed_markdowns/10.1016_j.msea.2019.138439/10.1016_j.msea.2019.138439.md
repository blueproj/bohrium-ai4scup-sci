# A B S T R A C T  

The precipitation and evolution of intersected plate-like  γ 0   ( γ 0 P ) phase within interdendritic  β  during long-term  aging and its relevant influence on the creep behavior of a multiphase Ni 3 Al-based intermetallic alloy are  investigated. Results show that the precipitation and growth of the  γ 0 P  precipitates during 800   C aging of  original as-cast alloy are significantly rapid, which can be fully precipitated in 60 min to form the intersected  morphology and continue to coarsen and grow during the extended aging and subsequent 800   C creep processes.  The  γ 0 P  precipitates have the Bain orientation relationship (OR) of (100) γ 0 P //(100) β  and [0-11] γ 0 P //[001] β  with  the interdendritic  β -matrix. The precipitation of intersected  γ 0 P  phase is sensitive to the elemental fluctuations in  β -matrix, and the preceding high-temperature annealing treatment can significantly reduce the elemental  in homogenization and inhibit its generation within the interdendritic  β . Precipitation of adequate intersected  γ 0 P  phase can effectively inhibit the dislocation proliferation and crack initiation in the brittle interdendritic  β  during  creep, resulted in the improved creep properties. Slightly rafted  γ 0   after long-term aging at 800   C transitioned  into R-type  γ 0 -raft structure during creep at 800   C/220 MPa, and can induce larger creep resistance than N-type  γ 0 -raft structure.

# 1. Introduction  

Ni 3 Al-based intermetallic alloys have attracted increasing research  attention as aero-engine materials, due to their high temperature resis­ tance (above 1100   C), excellent high temperature oxidation and  corrosion resistance, low density and cost [ 1 – 5 ]. Ni 3 Al-based interme­ tallic alloys have higher volume fraction of  γ 0   phase (80\~ vol.%)  compared to traditional Ni-based superalloys [ 4 ], even though their  poor ductility at ambient temperature and oxygen embrittlement at  medium temperature have been successfully solved by alloying with  trace boron and moderate chromium, respectively [ 6 , 7 ], the relative  poor weldability and inferior fabricability still limit their further ap­ plications [ 8 – 10 ]. Hence, the NiAl- β  phase was introduced by adding  abundant amount of Fe and Cr elements to solve this problem [ 4 , 10 , 11 ].  

Many studies [ 11 – 13 ] proved that the introduction of  β  phase pro­ motes the formation of complex multiphase structure in Ni 3 Al-based  intermetallic alloy, thus it could be successfully welded by vacuum  electron beam welding and transient liquid phase bonding. The studies

# 2. Experimental procedure  

The experimental multiphase Ni 3 Al-based intermetallic alloy inves­ tigated in present work was as-cast ingot produced via VIM   ESR  þ processes [ 17 ]. Its actual chemical composition (in wt.%) is listed in  Table 1 . From the as-cast ingot, two equal groups both consisting of  cuboidal specimens and tensile creep samples (revealed by  Fig. 1  [ 17 ])  were obtained. One group of the specimens was subjected to  1160   C/10 h annealing and cooled in furnace (FC). The heating rates  used in this work were all 10   C/min. Then, the two groups of specimens  were subjected to 800   C-1000 h long-term aging treatment and cooled  by water (WC). The original as-cast specimens plus long-term aging were  designated as AC-LTA, as illustrated in  Fig. 2 (a). The 1160   C/10 h  annealed specimens plus long-term aging were designated as AT-LTA, as  illustrated in  Fig. 2 (b).  

Creep tests of the AC-LTA and AT-LTA samples were performed on a  RDL100 model creep testing machine and the creep conditions are  controlled constantly at 800   C and uniaxial tensile stress of 220 MPa.  Before imposing tensile stress, creep specimens were heated to the  testing temperature of 800   C on 10   C/min, then isothermal heating for  30 min to eliminate temperature gradient. Three specimens were tested  for each of the AC-LTA and AT-LTA cycles under the same condition, the  obtained results appeared to be within limited error range. After failure,  crept samples were cooled slowly in furnace to ambient temperature.  The crept specimens were cut near fracture surface along the transverse  direction to determine fracture morphologies, and the deformed struc­ tures parallel to tensile stress were characterized in detail.  

Specimens for microstructure observations were chemically etched  at 20   C using a solution of 60 ml HCl  þ  20 ml H 2 O  þ  10 g CrO 3  for 0.5 s  after mechanical polishing. The S-4800 and S-7800 scanning electron  microscopes (SEM) were applied for structure observations before/after  creep as well as the fracture surfaces, the relevant operations were  conducted at a voltage of 15 kV. The dislocation substructures evolved  during creep deformation and plate-like  γ 0   precipitates were character­ ized using a Tecnai G2 F30 S-TWIN transmission electron microscope  (TEM) equipped with energy-dispersive spectroscopy (EDS) operated at  300 kV. For TEM observations, specimens were firstly mechanically  grinded and punched into thin disks (thickness of 45   5  μ m, 3 mm in   diameter), subsequently electro polished at    25    5   C using a solution  5% HClO  95% C H OH (in volume) at a voltage of 38 V. Quantitative  3  þ 2 5 measurement of the  β  and  γ 0   precipitates was performed using the Image  Pro-Plus 6.0 software on more than ten similar SEM images for one  specimen.

# 3.1. Microstructures of AC-LTA and AT-LTA alloys  

Fig. 3 (a) and (b) show the SEM microstructures of the AC-LTA and  AT-LTA specimens, respectively, which indicate that the structures  consist of interdendritic  β  phase with the similar width around 10.8  μ m  and volume fraction around 5.18 vol.%.  Fig. 4  reveals the SEM mor­ phologies of  γ 0 þ γ  dendrite (a, b) and interdendritic  β  phase (c, d) in the  AC-LTA and AT-LTA specimens.  Fig. 4 (c) and (d) illustrate the similar  width around 5.6  μ m of the  γ 0 -envelope [ 23 ] serving a bonding role  between interdendritic  β / γ 0 þ γ  dendrite in the AC-LTA and AT-LTA  specimens. However, the primary  γ 0   phase in the  γ 0 þ γ  dendrite and  quantity of the intersected plate-like  γ 0   ( γ 0 P ) phase within the interden­ dritic  β  appear with varied characteristics. In AC-LTA specimen, the  primary  γ 0   phase in  γ 0 þ γ  dendrite is calculated to be 0.51    0.11  μ m in  size and has begun to form the rafting structures (see  Fig. 4a) , and a large  number of intersected  γ 0 P  precipitates is generated within the inter­ dendritic  β  (as arrowed in  Fig. 4 c). In contrast, the primary  γ 0   phase of  AT-LTA specimen coarsened to a larger average size about  0.92   0.11  μ m but still remain the cuboidal shape (see  Fig. 4 b), and   relatively less intersected  γ 0 P  precipitates are observed within inter­ dendritic  β  (arrowed in  Fig. 4 d). The above results prove the fact that the  preliminary annealing treatment (1160   C, 10 h) can effectively inhibit  the precipitation of intersected  γ 0 P  phase within  β  and rafting of primary  γ 0   during long-term aging (800   C, 1000 h).  

# 3.2. Creep properties and microstructure evolution during creep  deformation  

Creep curves for the AC-LTA and AT-LTA specimens possessing  different primary  γ 0   morphology in  γ 0 þ γ  dendrite and different  γ 0 P  quantity within  β  measured at 800   C/220 MPa are discovered in  Fig. 5 Compared with the AT-LTA alloy with larger sized (0.92   0.11  μ m)   primary  γ 0   phase, the AC-LTA alloy with smaller sized (0.51    0.11  μ m)  primary  γ 0   phase suffered reduced steady-state creep rate ( ε _ ss ) and  extended creep rupture life, these changes seem to be inconsistent with  our previous results [ 17 ] that larger primary  γ 0   phase leads to smaller  ε _ ss  value, since the smaller  γ 0 / γ  lattice mismatch results from larger primary  γ 0   phase, thus higher resistance to creep deformation [ 24 , 25 ]. The  contrast results are due to the existence of intersected  γ 0 P  precipitates  within  β  in varied amounts for the two specimens, which may play an  important role in creep performance of this experimental multiphase  alloy. 3.6. Discussion: precipitates in  β  during 800   C aging  

In this investigated alloy, interdendritic  β  phase is introduced to  enhance the weldability and hot workability via adding abundant  amount of Fe and Cr elements [ 4 , 17 , 23 ]. The multiphase structure of  this alloy primarily consists of dominating heteroid austenitic  γ 0 þ γ  dendrite and complementary island-shaped interdendritic  β  phase  (about 19.37 vol %) surrounded by  γ 0 -envelope [ 23 ]. In the interden­ dritic  β  which is unfavorable to creep properties of this alloy [ 17 ], three  types of precipitates coexist [ 18 ]. The level of thermal stability is  γ 0 P  >  α -Cr  >  Cr 3 C 2  [ 18 ]. Few rod-like Cr 3 C 2  carbides are detected in the  experimental alloy during aging ( Fig. 10 ) and creep, the fully precipi­ tated semi-spherical  α -Cr particles after 1160   C annealing is metastable,

During 800   C aging, only the  γ 0 P  phase could generate and grow  rapidly (in 60 min) and in large quantities to form the intersected  morphology within interdendritic  β  ( Fig. 10 c), which may continue to  coarsen and propagate with the aging time prolonging and the subse­ quent 800   C creep deformation process ( Figs. 4 c and  6f ). The precipi­ tated  γ 0 P  phase has the OR of (100) γ 0 P //(100) β  and [0-11] γ 0 P //[001] β  with the interdendritic  β -matrix ( Figs. 11  and  12 ), consistent with the  previously reported Bain OR of  γ 0 / β  in NiAl-based alloy [ 20 ]. The  observed favorable OR of (100) γ 0 P //(100) β , [0-11] γ 0 P //[001] β  and  coherent  γ 0 P / β -matrix (fcc/bcc) interface without any misfit compensa­ tion ( Fig. 12 b) are different with the semi-coherency character of the  γ 0 -envelope/ β  interface [ 23 ] in this experimental alloy and can facilitate  the dislocation proliferation in interdendritic  β . # 4. Conclusions  

The precipitation mechanism and evolution of intersected  γ 0 P  phase  within the interdendritic  β  during long-term aging and its effect on the  creep behavior of a novelly designed multiphase Ni 3 Al-based interme­ tallic alloy are investigated. The obtained conclusions are as follows:  

(1) The precipitation and growth of  γ 0 P  precipitates in the original as-  cast alloy during 800   C aging process are significantly rapid,  which can be fully precipitated in 60 min to form the intersected  morphology and continue to coarsen and grow during the  extended aging and subsequent 800   C creep processes. The  precipitated  γ 0 P  phase has the Bain orientation relationship (OR)  of (100) γ 0 P //(100) β  and [0-11] γ 0 P //[001] β  with the interdendritic  β -matrix.  

(2) The precipitation of intersected  γ 0 P  phase is sensitive to the  elemental fluctuations in  β -matrix, and the preceding 1160   C  annealing treatment significantly reduced the elemental inho­ mogenization so inhibited the precipitation of  γ 0 P  phase within  the interdendritic  β .  (3) The precipitation of adequate intersected  γ 0 P  within the inter­ dendritic  β  can effectively inhibit the dislocation proliferation  and crack initiation in the brittle  β  phase during creep deforma­ tion at 800   C/220 MPa, benefited in improving the creep rupture  life from 261 h to 390 h of this experimental multiphase Ni 3 Al-  based intermetallic alloy.   (4) Slightly rafted  γ 0   after long-term aging at 800   C transitioned into  R-type  γ 0 -raft structure during creep at 800   C/220 MPa, and the  R-type  γ 0 -raft structure provided stronger resistance to disloca­ tion slipping than N-type  γ 0 -raft structure during creep deforma­ tion for this multiphase Ni 3 Al-based intermetallic alloy.
