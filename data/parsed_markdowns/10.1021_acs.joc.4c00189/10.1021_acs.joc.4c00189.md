# Organocatalyzed Photo electrochemistry for the Generation of Acyl and Phosphoryl Radicals through Hydrogen Atom-Transfer Process  

Hong He, Cai-Mi Pan, Zhong-Wei Hou, \*  Manman Sun, and Lei Wang \*  

![](images/95bfe3e098f82bd04d9baac787071954fe5eac33cd3076fa9b132c766508b8e3.jpg)  
ABSTRACT:  An organocatalyzed photo electrochemical method for the generation of acyl and phosphoryl radicals from formamides, aldehydes, and phosphine oxides has been developed. This protocol utilizes 9,10-p henan th rene quin one (PQ) as both a molecular catalyst and a hydrogen atom-transfer (HAT) reagent, eliminating the requirement for external metal-based reagents, HAT reagents, and oxidants. The generated acyl radicals can be applied to a range of radical-mediated transformation reactions, including C − H carbamoylation of heteroarenes, intermolecular tandem radical cyclization of CF 3 -substituted  N -arylacrylamides, as well as intramolecular cyclization reactions. The use of acyl radicals in these transformations offers an efficient and sustainable approach to accessing structurally diverse carbonyl compounds.  

# INTRODUCTION  

Carbonyl compounds, ubiquitous and vital structural units in organic chemistry, are widely encountered in drug molecules and bioactive molecules and play a significant role in organic synthesis. The synthesis of carbonyl compounds via acyl radicals is garnering increasing attention. Acyl radicals can be efficiently generated through various methods, including radical carbonylation of C − X bonds and CO,   Norrish I reaction of ketones,   homolysis of C(O) − Y (Y = SeR, TeAr, SR, POPh 2 , [Co]) bonds,   oxidation of C(O) − Z (Z = COOH, SiMe 3 , NHNH 2 , Hantzsch ester) bonds,   as well as reduction of C(O) − L (Z = Cl, SR, OCOOR, OCOR, COONPhth) bonds 5   ( Scheme 1 ). However, these methods often require severe reaction conditions and the prefabrication of acyl radical precursors. C(O) − H compounds, including formamides 6   and aldehydes,   are particularly well suited as acyl radical precursors due to their stability and ready availability. Acyl radicals generated via hydrogen atom transfer (HAT) from C(O) − H compounds are frequently employed as key intermediates for the synthesis of carbonyl compounds under mild conditions. Nevertheless, the success of oxidation reactions involving acyl radicals often hinges on the use of complex multicatalytic systems and sacrificial oxidants.  

Organic electrochemistry offers a direct and powerful approach to organic synthesis by harnessing electrical energy for chemical reactions.   These reactions eliminate the need for chemical reagents and exhibit green and high-efficiency characteristics.   Electrochemical generation of acyl radicals from thiolesters,  α -keto acids, 4-acyl-1,4-di hydro pyr i dines, and acyl hydrazines has been achieved to access acylated structures under oxidizing or reducing conditions.   Photo electro chem- istry,   a field that combines the advantages of electrochemistry and photocatalysis, enriches the types of catalysts available and provides new avenues for executing complex reactions.   This emerging discipline holds tremendous potential for addressing the ongoing challenges in organic synthesis. The acyl radical-mediated reactions, such as Minisci-type C − H acylation and carbamoylation,   tandem radical cycliza- tion and addition,   represent a straightforward and powerful strategy for the construction of high-value molecules containing a carbonyl group. A simple and oxidant-free pathway for achieving these radical reactions from readily available materials is highly desirable. These acyl radicals were successfully applied to a range of radical-mediated trans- formation reactions, including C − H carbamoylation of heteroarenes, intermolecular tandem radical cyclization of CF 3 -substituted  N -arylacrylamides, intramolecular cyclization reactions etc.

# CONCLUSIONS  

We have displayed an organocatalyzed photo electrochemical approach for generating acyl radicals and phosphoryl radicals using formamides, aldehydes, and phosphine oxides as radical precursors. These radical intermediates can participate in a variety of chemical transformations such as C − H carbamoy- lation of heteroarenes and tandem radical cyclization reactions. The key to the success of these reactions lies in the dual role of PQ as both a photosensitizer and HAT reagent, avoiding the need for additional metal-catalysts, HAT reagents, or oxidants. Various carbonyl compounds are synthesized with acceptable efficiency under green and sustainable conditions.

General Procedure for the C − H Carbamoylation of Heteroarenes.  A 20 mL tube ( Figure S1A  in Supporting Information; dimensions: height 9.5 cm, outer diameter 2.5 cm, inner diameter 2.2 cm) was outfitted with a reticulated vitreous carbon (RVC, 100 PPI, dimensions: 1.2 cm  ×  0.8 cm  ×  0.8 cm) anode and a platinum plate cathode (dimensions: 1 cm  ×  1 cm  ×  0.1 mm) ( Figure S1B  in Supporting Information). The tube was charged with the following reactants: heteroarene (0.3 mmol, 1 equiv), PQ (6.2 mg, 10 mol %), and nBu4NClO4 (103 mg, 0.3 mmol, 1 equiv).Subsequently, MeCN (7 mL), formamides (1.5 mmol, 5 equiv), and CH 3 SO 3 H (20  μ L, 0.3 mmol, 1 equiv) were added. The resulting solution was purged with argon for 10 min. The RVC anode was securely fixed onto a sharpened graphite rod (diameter: 0.6 cm), maintaining a distance of approximately 0.5 cm between the RVC and platinum electrodes. The reaction was conducted at room temper- ature (cooled with water) under a constant current of 2 mA and irradiated with 420 − 425 nm LEDs (10 W) for 16 h ( Figure S1C  in Supporting Information). The distance from the light source to the tube was about 0.5 cm. The reaction mixture was concentrated, and the residue was chromatographed through silica gel to isolate the desired product.

General Procedure for the Amplification Synthesis of 3.  An amplification synthesis of  3  was conducted on 1.5 mmol at 10 mA under irradiation with a Kessil PR160L LED lamp (427 nm, 30 W) for 25 h by using a piece of RVC (1.2 cm  ×  2 cm  ×  2 cm) as the anode and a Pt plate (1.5 cm  ×  1.5 cm  ×  0.3 mm) as the cathode. The tube was charged with the following reactants:  1  (203 mg, 1.5 mmol, 1 equiv), PQ (31.2 mg, 10 mol %), and   n Bu 4 NClO 4  (515 mg, 1.5 mmol, 1 equiv). Subsequently, MeCN (35 mL), formamide (0.3 mL, 7.5 mmol, 5 equiv), and CH 3 SO 3 H (100  μ L, 1.5 mmol, 1 equiv) were added. When the reaction was completed, the reaction mixture was concentrated under reduced pressure. Then, the residue was chromatographed through silica gel, eluting with ethyl acetate/ petroleum ether (1:5 v/v) to give the desired product  3  as a white solid (156 mg, 0.87 mmol, 58% yield).
