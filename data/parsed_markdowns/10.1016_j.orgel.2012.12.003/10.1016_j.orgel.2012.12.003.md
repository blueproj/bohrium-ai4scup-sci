Two new donor–acceptor (D–A) polymers composed of benzo[1,2-b:4,5-b 0 ]dithiophene (BDT) as donor and thiadiazolo[3,4-c]pyridine (PyTZ) as acceptor were designed and synthesized. Compared to the polymer based on BDT and 4,7-dithien-2-yl-2,1,3-benzothia- diazole (DTBT), the planarity, energy levels, and band-gaps of the new polymers were ﬁne adjusted by incorporating conjugated alkylthienyl side chains to BDT and substitute a stronger acceptor PyTZ for 2,1,3-b enzo thi a diaz ole. The new polymers exhibit broad absorption from 300 to 800 nm in both solution and ﬁlm state. The polymer band gaps and energy levels are close to the optimal values. As a result, power conversion efﬁciencies (PCEs) of 4.84% and 5.11% were obtained for inverted polymer solar cells based on these new polymers. The PCEs are signiﬁcantly higher than those of the BDT–DTBT based poly- mers (2–4%).

# 1. Introduction  

Polymer solar cells (PSCs) using p-type conjugated poly- mers as donors and n-type fullerene derivatives as accep- tors are considered a promising alternative for producing clean energy at low costs  [1,2] , and considerable progress has been made in this area as shown by the improvements of device power conversion efﬁciency (PCE) from below 1% to over 9%  [3–6] . The donor polymer for an efﬁcient bulk hetero-junction (BHJ) solar cell should posses a low band-gap, a high hole mobility and suitable energy levels matching those of the fullerene acceptors  [1b–1c,2] . Given the lowest unoccupied molecular orbital (LUMO)/highest occupied molecular orbital (HOMO) energy levels of the donor polymers, we can theoretically calculate the maxi- mum PCEs of the BHJ solar cells. Assuming a ﬁll factor of 0.65 and external quantum efﬁciency (EQE) of 65%, the donor polymer should theoretically be able to offer a PCE  

chains on BDT with conjugated alkylthienyl chains and substitute a stronger acceptor thiadiazolo[3,4-c] pyridine (PyTZ) for 2,1,3-b enzo thi a diaz ole (BTZ). The introduction of alkylthienyl enhances the conjugation of BDT in 2- dimensions and provides a high degree of coplanarity of the polymer backbone  [6,11] . More importantly, alkylthie- nyl chains aid in achieving a lower HOMO energy level, thus obtaining a larger open circuit voltage  [6a,6b] . Pyridyl is more  p -electron deﬁcient than phenyl , which makes PyTZ a stronger acceptor than BTZ, thus decreasing the polymer band gap. In this work we report the synthesis of two new copolymers PBPT-8 and PBPT-12, which are composed of alkylthienyl substituted BDT as the donor and PyTZ as the acceptor with different alkyl-chains intro- duced to the BDT unit to adjust polymer solubility and intermolecular packing. The optical band gaps of PBPT-8 and PBPT-12 are 1.56 eV and 1.53 eV, and the HOMO en- ergy levels of the two new polymers are   5.41 eV and  5.44 eV, respectively, which are all in accordance with the values of the efﬁcient polymer donor materials  [7] . Fi- nally, inverted polymer solar cells using the two polymers as the donors and PC 61 BM as the acceptor were fabricated. PCEs as high as 5.11% were achieved for the optimized devices, which demonstrated that the new polymers based on BDT-T-PyTZ are promising materials for OPV applications.

# 4. Conclusion  

Two new D–A polymers, PBPT-8 and PBPT-12, com- posed of BDT with two thiophene bridges as donor and PyTZ as acceptor were designed and synthesized, aiming to further improve the performance of BDT–DTBT based polymers. The new polymers exhibit broad absorption from 300 to 800 nm in both solutions and ﬁlm state. The optical band gaps are 1.56 eV for PBPT-8 and 1.53 eV for PBPT-12, and the HOMO energy levels are   5.41 eV for PBPT-8 and   5.44 eV for PBPT-12. The polymer band gaps and energy levels are close to the theoretical values of the efﬁcient donor polymers. As a result, PCEs of 4.84% and 5.11% were obtained for PBPT-8 and PBPT-12 based in- verted PSCs, which are signiﬁcantly higher than the PCEs of the BDT–DTBT based polymers (2–4%).
