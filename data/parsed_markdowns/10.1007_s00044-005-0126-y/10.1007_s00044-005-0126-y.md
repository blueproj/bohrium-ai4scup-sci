Abstract.The Plasmodium falciparum cysteine proteases,falcipains,have been established as novel targets for antimalarial drug design. Using the de novo design approach, several trisubstituted thiazole analogs were generated as potential inhibitors of these enzymes. A general and convenient synthetic approach for these novel trisubstituted thiazoles is reported here. Substituents at the 4th and 5" positions of the target thiazoles were introduced by a Hantzsch reaction,and the chain at the second position was extended through a Sandmeyer reaction formylation, and Wittig olefination. In vitro enzyme inhibition studies have identified three inhibitors (14, 16, 23) of the falcipains with one (14) showing dual activity against both falcipain-2 and falcipain-3 and ICsovalues of 6.6 and 29.4 μM, respectively.  

Malaria is one of the 10 most prevalent and deadly diseases in the world. Increasing resistance of malarial parasites to common therapies demands a serious search for novel targets.' Plasmodial cysteine proteases have been established as appropriate chemotherapeutic targets for antimalarial drug design.' Falcipain-2 and falcipain-3 have been characterized as the key cysteine proteases implicated in hemoglobin degradation in P. falciparum.? Several vinyl sulfone-based peptide inhibitors have been shown to inhibit falcipain-2 and falcipain-3 and exhibit antiparasitic activity.2 Recently, we have reported model structures of falcipain-2\* and falcipain-3? which can be used to design novel inhibitors of these enzymes. Several isoquinoline derivatives were designed using the model structure of falcipain-2 and were shown to inhibit the enzyme. .46  

Thiazoles occupy a prominent position among heterocycles. In nature, the thiazolium ring is the chemically active center in the coenzyme-derived from vitamin B (thiamin). A large number of thiazoles obtained from microbial and marine origins exhibit important biological effects such as antitumor, antifungal, antibiotic, and antiviral activities.' Synthetic, substituted thiazoles have also been shown to exhibit a wide variety of biological activity.  

The present work deals with the design and synthesis of trisubstituted thiazoles to inhibit cysteine proteases in Plasmodium falciparum. This represents a phase 2 design strategy with the goal of increasing the number of hydrogen bond interactions between inhibitor and enzyme to develop potent, dual inhibitors of both falcipain-2 and 3. Although several synthetic routes toward short chain trisubstituted thiazoles? are reported, synthesis of extended chain, trisubstituted thiazoles has not been studied extensively."° Herein we describe a general and convenient synthesis of several rationally designed, extended chain, trisubstituted thiazoles.

De novo design of inhibitors. In the absence of a crystal structure, sequence-based homology modeling is a well accepted alternative to derive a 3D structure for use in structure-based drug design.F  For example, in a study based on homology models of kinases, it was shown that the models could pick up some of the crucial selectivity information."" In another report, homology models of CDK2 and factor VIla were used to dock databases of chemically diverse structures to identify active compounds. It was reported that when the sequence homology in the binding site region was more than 5o%, the performance of the homology model-based docking was The two cysteine proteases from Plasmodium falciparum, falcipain-2 and falcipain-3, share a reasonable (>30%) sequence homology with cruzain, the cysteine protease from Trypanosoma cruzi. Several crystal structures of cruzain with inhibitors are available, while such experimental structures are yet to be reported for the falcipains. Hence, homology models of falcipain-2 and falcipain-3 were developed by us previously.,s  The models  were  validated by various structure/geometry verification tools as well as docking studies of either substrates or inhibitors. The correlation coefficients of more thanO.9between docking-based interaction energies and experimentally obtained binding affinity related parameters of the ligands provided additional support for the models. The model structures of the falcipains have been shown to be quite similar tothat of cruzain,and the important catalytic residues in the binding pockets appear to be structurally conserved across these enzymes with more than 90% sequence identity in this region.4,s  

Vinyl sulfone-based peptides are known to be irreversible inhibitors of cysteine proteases like cruzain' and falcipains, forming covalent bonds with the thiolate of the catalytic cysteine. The binding mode of vinyl sulfone inhibitors in the model structures of falcipains, as proposed by the docking studies, appeared to be similar to that in cruzain. Thus, in the absence of crystal data for the falcipains, it may be reasonable to utilize the information gleaned from cruzain-inhibitor complexes for structure-based drug design of inhibitors of homologous proteins like the falcipains As an example, important interactions of one of the vinyl sulfone inhibitors with cruzain (as seen in crystal structures) and the model structure of falcipain-2 (as proposed by the docking studies) are shown schematically in Figure 1. There were several other compounds that inhibited falcipain-2 and falcipain-3 at higher levels than is routinely conducted due to solubility or handling issues related to the high throughput-screening format (96 well plates). These were compounds 15, 17, 29 and 30, of which 17 was cytotoxic above the enzyme assay cutoff concentration. Interestingly, 15 had in vitro ICso values against P. falciparum in the range of 20 μM, above the 12 μM cutoff. Furthermore, 15 showed activity against L. donovani in the range of the cytotoxic effects. Upon close inspection it is not apparent that 15 should be cytotoxic, if anything, one should suspect the potentially electrophilic enone 14. However, 14 is not only one of the more potent compounds in both P. falciparum and L. donovani in cell culture, it also demonstrates enzyme inhibition (ICso) within the range of its in vitro EC50  

Altogether, 14, 15, 20, 22 and 30 were roughly as active against P. falciparum as they were against L. donovani in cell culture, showing μM ECso values against these organisms. The close similarity in potencies across organisms also supports the hypothesis that it would be possible to design broad spectrum inhibitors of parasitic cysteine proteases. These results clearly show that it is possible, although the potencies in vitro would have to be brought into the nM range for these to be considered as potential clinical candidates. Also, further studies are required to assess whether these compounds are selective for the parasitic cysteine proteases over the homologous human proteases. Although these compounds are designed to be active site directed reversible inhibitors advanced biological studies on the current set of compounds would help to support this claim.  

The micromolar potency across the series of analogs is disconcerting considering how well the target molecules seem to fit into the receptor site. It seems likely that there are simply too many rotatable bonds in these molecules when counterbalanced against the relatively open aqueous accessible face of the protease catalytic site, a significant entropic and thermodynamic disadvantage.  

An indication of this effect might be argued for several of the compounds that show somewhat better activity relative to their close chemical congeners. For example, 14 and 15 differ only in the placement of a double bond between the side chain ketone and the thiazole ring.This modification is much more meaningful than addition of an isolated double bond to a hydrocarbon chain. In this case, the double bond conjugates the carbonyl with the aromatic ring inducing a great deal of rigidity now across several contiguous atoms. The number of rotatable bonds in 14 is 12 while in 15, it is 16. Compound 14 is somewhat more potent and less toxic than 15. Another example of a generally more potent analog in the Table is 22 or 23, in which now a double bond connects two aromatic rings while at the same time a thiazolanilide side-chain replaces the highly flexible 5- thia-8-phenyloctan-3-one found in most of the analogs presented in the Table. Together, these modifications reduce the rotatable bonds down to (arguably) 9. This should also explain the poor correlation between the interaction energies of the ligand-enzyme complex and the corresponding ICso values since the docking calculations were performed in vacuum where entropy and solvent related issues cannot be addressed completely. Thus, although the interaction energy values for the compounds active against falcipain-2 (14, 16 and 23) appear to be in the same rank order as that of the corresponding ICso values, several other compounds having interaction energy values in the similar range are inactive.  

The untoward entropic effects of drug (ligand) flexibility on drug binding (potency) at the recepton level could be considered axiomatic in the field of drug design but is an oversimplification when one considers the receptor type. Highly flexible molecules fit well into nuclear hormone receptors with high affinity despite the entropic disadvantage perhaps because the hydrophobic-hydrophilic drug-water repulsion is relieved upon burial of the hydrophobe in the hydrophobic center of the receptor cavity. The potent binding enjoyed by entropically negative flexible peptide ligands in solvent-exposed catalytic sites is no doubt due to a combination of hydrogen bonds (backbone and side-chain), ionic and van der Waals forces.  

Another interesting, unplanned addition to the Table is the ester 30. While this compound has a full complement of rotatable bonds (\~15), it nonetheless has a comparable level of potency across the two organisms without cytotoxicity. While the ICso is not in accord with the ECso, there are many possible explanations for this including selective uptake of the compound by the organism or involvement of some additional unknown mechanism of action.  

# CONCLUSION  

We have utilized homology models for falcipains to conduct a simple foray into de novo design The thiazole ring was chosen as a common motif in this series as it placed the side-chains into proper orientations to avoid bumping interactions with the protein active site. We have developed convenient synthetic routes for the synthesis of the designed trisubstituted thiazoles and believe that these synthetic methods will serve as useful methodological additions towards synthesis of complicated trisubstituted thiazoles.  In vitro enzyme assays identified the degree of enzyme inhibition of Plasmodium falciparum cysteine proteases, falcipain-2 and falcipain-3. In parallel, the analogs were examined in standard cell culture assays of P. falciparum and L. donovani. Compound 23 was shown to clearly inhibit both falcipain-2 and falcipain-3. Many of the other compounds 14, 15, 20, 22, and 30 displayed lower potency in enzyme assay, but were roughly as active against P. falciparum as they were against L. donovani in cell culture, showing μM EC50 values against these organisms. The close similarity in potencies across organisms supports the hypothesis that it should be possible to design broad-spectrum antiparasitic agents that act through a mechanism of reversible inhibition of parasitic cysteine proteases.
