ABSTRACT:  A unique process for the photoinduced platinum-catalyzed reductive allylation of  α -diketones with allylic carbonates has been developed. This allylation reaction was found to proceed selectively at the more electron-deficient carbonyl group of the diketone to afford an  α - keto homoallylic alcohol. Such products could be further derivatized by transformation of the remaining carbonyl group. T he allylation of carbonyl compounds represents a straightforward means of synthesizing homoallylic alcohols, which are versatile synthetic intermediates for a wide range of pharmaceuticals and natural products.   The use of non-nucleophilic allylic compounds, such as allylic halides or carboxylates, in this process may be especially helpful. Such reactions would not require allylic metal compounds, which are often unstable and must be prepared in advance of the synthesis. Conventional reductive allylation reactions catalyzed by transition metals utilize either metals or labile organic compounds as reductants ( Figure 1 a).   However, recent progress in the development of photoinduced transition  

metal catalysis has enabled the use of stable, readily available amines as reductants ( Figure 1 b). Titanium,   cobalt,   nickel, and chromium 6   catalysts have so far been applied to photoinduced reductive allylation in combination with photo- redox catalysts. Even so, these catalytic systems have been used mainly for the allylation of aldehydes, and only a few examples of the allylation of ketones have been reported to date.   The utility of transition-metal-catalyzed reductive allylation could be improved by designing novel systems that expand the range of applicable substrates.  

Platinum is a group 10 element and exhibits unique reactivity when it is used as a homogeneous catalyst for various bond-forming reactions. Despite this, there have been only a few reported examples of photoinduced platinum- catalyzed reactions,   even though other group 10 elements, namely nickel and palladium, have been extensively studied with regard to such reactions.   The present work demon- strates the photoinduced platinum-catalyzed reductive allyla- tion of ketones with allylic carbonates ( Figure 1 c). These reactions present a unique example of photoinduced platinum catalysis in conjunction with a single platinum catalyst without a photoredox catalyst. This process is applicable to various  α - diketones and even to fluorenone. It should also be noted that α -diketones were found to undergo selective monoallylation in the more electron-deficient carbonyl group. Assessments of other metal catalysts established that tetrakis(tri phenyl phosphine)platinum [Pt(PPh 3 ) 4 ] was the most effective at promoting the photoinduced allylation reaction. The applicability of this novel reaction was investigated by performing trials using a variety of  α -diketones ( Table 2 ). Electron-donating substituents, such as methyl ( 3ba ) and methoxy ( 3ca ) groups, on the aromatic ring of the diketone were tolerated, as were halogen substituents ( 3da − 3fa ). Interestingly, the C(sp 2 ) − Br bond, which is typically labile in palladium-catalyzed reactions, remained intact under the present conditions.   A diketone having an electron-deficient trifluoromethyl group ( 1g ) gave a slightly decreased yield, while a substrate having a furyl group ( 1i ; furil) afforded the corresponding homoallylic alcohol ( 3ia ) in a 71% yield.  

Asymmetrically substituted diketones were also examined to investigate the regio selectivity of the allylation reaction. When the reaction was carried out with diketones possessing a phenyl group and an alkyl group on each carbonyl group, allylation occurred selectively at the benzoyl group (>95:5 regioselec- tivity for  3ja − 3ma ). Monosubstituted benzil derivatives were also examined. In these experiments, 4-eth oxy carbonyl benz il underwent the allylation reaction with formation of a C − C bond solely at the carbonyl group substituted with the more electron-deficient aryl group ( 3na / 3na ′  = 84:16).  

Both diketones and monoketones could be converted to the corresponding homoallylic alcohols through this process. As an example, the reaction of fluorenone ( 1o ) with allyl carbonate 2a  afforded the corresponding alcohol ( 3oa ) in 24% yield ( Scheme 1 , top). However, benzophenone, which has no absorption in the visible region of light, failed to give a homoallylic alcohol under the present reaction conditions. These results suggested that the  α -diketone structure was not essential for the present allylation reaction, and the photo- excitation of the starting ketone would be involved in the reaction mechanism. Several allylic carbonates were also applied to this reaction. The use of  β -methallyl methyl carbonate ( 2b ) gave the corresponding product ( 3ab ) in a 73% yield ( Scheme 1 , bottom). Unfortunately, the reaction of 1a  with crotyl methyl carbonate resulted in an inseparable mixture of isomers of homoallylic alcohols.  

A series of experiments was performed to obtain insights into the mechanism associated with this photoinduced platinum-catalyzed allylation reaction. When a mixture of  1a , 2a , and HE was exposed to blue light in the absence of a platinum catalyst, the allylation product ( 3aa ) was not produced. Rather, benzoin  4  and its dehydrated dimer  5 were obtained in 47% and 15% yields, respectively ( Scheme 2 a). In prior work,   the reduction of  1a  to  4  was found to proceed via a double hydrogen atom transfer pathway involving an  α -hydroxy alkyl radical intermediate. The palladium-catalyzed allylation of  4  has also been reported, and so the allylation of  4  with  2a  in association with a platinum catalyst was examined ( Scheme 2 b). However, allylation did not occur under the present reaction conditions, which indicated that neither benzoin  4  nor the corresponding enolate functioned as reaction intermediates. The participation of a ( π - allyl)platinum(II) complex as an intermediate was assessed by carrying out the stoichiometric reaction of benzil ( 1 ), ( π - allyl)platinum(II) complex  6 ,   and a reductant with photo- irradiation. During this trial, the allyl group of the platinum complex ( 6 ) was transferred to  1a  to furnish homoallylic alcohol  3aa  in the presence of sodium methoxide (NaOMe). On the basis of the above results, we propose a reaction mechanism involving an  α -hydroxy alkyl radical and a ( π - allyl)platinum(II) species as intermediates ( Scheme 3 ). Initially, allyl carbonate  2a  undergoes oxidative addition to a platinum(0) complex to produce the ( π -allyl)platinum(II) alkoxide complex  A  in conjunction with the elimination of carbon dioxide. The photoexcitation of benzil ( 1a ) causes the abstraction of a hydrogen atom from the HE to generate  α - hydroxy carbon radical intermediate  B  together with radical species  C .   The subsequent deprotonation reaction of  B  with A  results in the formation of a ( π -allyl)platinum(II)-ketyl ion pair ( D ). This then undergoes C − C bond formation to furnish platinum(I) homoallylic alkoxide  E . This process has been established for palladium and nickel catalysis. In summary, this work demonstrated photoinduced platinum-catalyzed reductive allylation reactions of  α -diketones with allylic carbonates. These reactions occurred preferentially in the more electron-deficient carbonyl group of the starting diketone. The resulting homoallylic alcohols could also be further derivatized by the transformation of the remaining carbonyl group. Mechanistic investigations suggested that a ketyl radical generated from the starting ketone as a consequence of photo irradiation was likely the key inter- mediate and reacted with a ( π -allyl)platinum(II) complex to form a C − C bond.
