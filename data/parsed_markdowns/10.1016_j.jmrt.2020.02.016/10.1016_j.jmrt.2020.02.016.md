# a b s t r a c t  

T wo novel low melting pointing metals (LMPMs), Bi-21In-18Pb-12Sn alloy and Ga-13.5Sn alloy as    phase change materials (PCMs), were ﬁrstly proposed to work for thermal manage- ment    in aerospace applications. Their phase change characteristics and thermal stability were investigated in comparison with that of conventional parafﬁn PCMs (i.e. C 26 H 58  and C 17 H 36 ). The results indicate that the latent heat per unit volume in the LMPMs is higher than that of the parafﬁn PCMs, leading to much better thermal capacity of the former, and this can effectively reduce the    volume proportion of LMPMs in use.    LMPMs exhibit excel- lent thermal conductivity compared with the parafﬁn PCMs, and this contributes to rapid absorption of the heat released by the temperature-controlled target, and consequently the temperature of the target could remain stable near the phase change temperature. Besides, the Al 2 O 3  coating by the spraying method could act as    effective corrosion inhibitor in pro- tecting Al alloy container used for electronic packaging from the    liquid Ga-Sn alloy. The temperature control characteristic and temperature gradient of LMPMs consequently could suit the applied requirement of spacecraft device operated in vacuum condition. Therefore, low melting point metals with better thermal conductivity and stability would be promising candidates for PCMs applied in the ﬁeld of aerospace.

# 1. Introduction  

The phase change energy storage technology has been regarded as one of the effective ways in thermal manage- ment of electronic device, especially in the ﬁeld of spacecraft [1,2] . In recent years, phase change materials (PCMs), as    energy storage materials in latent heat storage systems, are becom- ing increasingly attractive due to their high energy storage density and isothermal behavior when storing and retriev- ing heat [3–5] . Solid PCMs absorb the external heat and lead to the phase change, with the increasing temperature during heating process, and ﬁnally the heat are stored in the terms of liquid PCMs. When the temperature decreases, this heat stored before are released during the re-solidiﬁcation of PCMs. Therefore, the periodic operation of PCMs consists of two types of thermal effect: one is the cooling effect originated from absorption of the external heat due to the melting of PCMs; the other is the heating effect generated from the released heat when PCMs solidify again [6,7] .  

PCMs are usually classiﬁed into three types: organic, inor- ganic, and mixed ones [8–11] . In latent thermal energy storage systems, inorganic compounds and organic compounds are two kinds of conventional PCMs widely used for the stor- age and release of heat latent. Although the storage densities of inorganic PCMs are usually high, supercooled phenomena and precipitated appearance of inorganic PCMs lead to insufﬁ- cient thermal stability and inferior service life. Organic phase change materials, represented by the most widely used paraf- ﬁn PCMs in space technology, also have problems such as low thermal conductivity and low density [12] . The main conven- tional parafﬁn PCMs still cannot satisfy the core criteria of spacecraft design, such as    dimensional stability, tight temper- ature control, high ﬂux heat acquisition and minimization of volume [12] .  

Numerous methods have been tried by researchers to improve this situation. The investigation on the thermal storage performance of metals were ﬁrstly conducted by Birchenall et al. in the 1980s [13] . Because metal has    no supercooling phenomenon, in addition to high thermal conductivity, its volume changes little in the process of solid- liquid phase change [14] . Therefore, metals as    PCMs show great potential in phase change energy storage technology. Furthermore, the low melting point metals (LMPMs) as PCMs can narrow the charge/discharge process and promote the heat transfer efﬁciency with the contribution of their excel- lent thermal conductivity [15,16] . Therefore, the role of LMPMs becomes increasingly signiﬁcant in the thermal management and temperature control [17,18] . Owing to excellent thermal conductivity ( ∼ 10 1   W/m · K) and high latent heat of unit [8] , gal- lium (Ga) and bismuth (Bi) based alloys as    LMPMs had proved to be quite useful to improve the efﬁciency of thermal manage- ment and enhance the cooling capability of thermal control components in electronic devices of spacecraft [19] . The effect of LMPMs on the temperature control of electronic device was investigated by numerical and experimental studies [20,21] . The thermal-control operation in electronic device of space- craft is intermittent, periodic and long-term during a space exploration mission [22] . To achieve tiny ﬂuctuations within a desired temperature range, PCMs are required with ideal

# 2. Experimental procedure  

The properties of the LMPMs and the selected parafﬁn PCMs are shown in Table 1 . These four PCMs are classiﬁed into Group A and Group B according to the similar phase change tem- perature: n-hexacosane (C 26 H 58 ) and Bi-21In-18Pb-12Sn alloy are    included in Group A, while n-heptadecane (C 17 H 36 )    and Ga-13.5Sn alloy are included in Group B.  

Fig. 1 shows the experimental schematic diagram. This experiment procedure was divided into two parts: the heating and the cooling process. In the heating process, a container with PCM was heated by a resistance heater. The temperature change of PCM in container was measured by a    temperature measuring unit, and recorded by the temperature inspector and the computer. Finally, the T-t curve of PCM during the heating process was drawn. The process of cooling experiment was similar to that the heating experiment. The difference was that PCM in the container should be pre-heated to the speci- ﬁed temperature through thermostat, and then cooled in the air.  

In both heating and cooling processes, the two kinds of PCMs were placed in the cylinder glass containers with an inner diameter of 15.0 mm and a depth of 40.0 mm. The tem- perature measurement units were inserted to the bottom of the container along the axis of cylinder. In order to better

# 3. Results  

# 3.1. Heat storage and heat release curves of phase change of alloy  

The temperatures of PCMs in Group A and Group B during the heating experiment were recorded by the temperature inspec- tor and the computer. The heating T-t curves obtained are shown in Fig. 3 . The T-t curves could be clearly divided into three parts: (1) the heat generated from the resistance heater was all absorbed in the form of PCMs’ sensible heat, result- ing in the rapid rise of the temperature of PCMs due to the relatively low sensible heat capacity of PCMs; (2) when the temperature of PCMs rose rapidly to the phase change tem- perature, the heat released by the resistance heating sheet was mainly absorbed in the forms of latent heat and sen- sible heat of PCMs. Since the latent heat capacity of PCMs was far greater than the sensible heat capacity, there formed an obvious platform near the phase change temperature of PCMs; (3) the heat generated by the resistance heating sheet was no longer absorbed in terms of PCMs’ latent heat but the sensible heat when PCMs were completely melted. There- fore, the temperature of PCMs rapidly rose again at this stage.  

The cooling T-t curves of PCMs in Group A and Group B are shown in Fig. 4 . Similar with the heating process, the cooling T-t curve of PCMs could also be clearly divided into three stages: (1) the heat of PCMs stored in sensible heat was released rapidly in terms of natural convection and heat radiation, owing to an    obvious gap between PCMs’ tempera- ture and ambient temperature. Furthermore, its temperature decreased rapidly due to the relatively low heat capacity of PCMs; (2) when the temperature of PCMs rose up to the phase change temperature, the heat of PCMs stored in terms of latent heat was released, but the temperature changed mildly. Similar with the heating process, there also formed an    obvious “platform” near the phase change temperature of PCMs; (3) the release of latent heat had been ﬁnished when solidiﬁed completely. However, the heat of PCMs stored in terms of sensible heat began to release. Therefore, the obvi- ous decline in temperature of PCMs appeared again. With the further decrease of PCMs’ temperature, the temperature gap between PCMs and environment became much narrower while the speed of heat release also reduced. Finally, the temperatures of PCMs were slowly decreased to the ambient temperature.

# 4. Discussion  

Although PCMs have different thermophysical properties, the phase change latent heat and thermal conductivity are the two main factors of phase change materials since they have great effect on the phase change process of PCMs. Therefore, this section will further analyze the inﬂuence of phase change latent heat and thermal conductivity on phase change prop- erties of PCMs.

# 4.2. Inﬂuence of thermal conductivity on phase change characteristics  

The thermal conductivity of PCMs inﬂuences the internal temperature distribution and heat transfer, and further deter- mines the phase change process of PCMs [25,26] . The effect of thermal conductivity on the phase transformation process of PCMs will be discussed below. Fig. 6 shows variations in internal temperature difference of PCMs during the heating process. The rectangular symbol represents variation in tem- perature difference of Point A and Point C with increasing time, which can be considered as the temperature difference between the bottom and the top along the axis of PCMs.  

Fig. 7 shows variations in the temperature difference of PCMs in Group A    and Group B during the cooling process. Com- pared with the heating process, the temperature difference of PCMs is relatively small during the cooling process, owing to the natural convection as the main way of PCMs cooling. Furthermore, there is no large temperature gradient within PCMs due to the small cooling power in this process. The ther- mal conductivity of PCMs determines the temperature control characteristics of phase change temperature control devices, and further signiﬁcantly determines temperature distribution of the device in the operation process. Based on the above results, the heat released by the temperature-controlled target can be quickly absorbed by PCMs with good thermal conduc- tivity, and then the temperature of the target can keep stable near the phase change temperature. Therefore, low melting point materials with good thermal conductivity should be the prime candidate for PCMs applied in the ﬁeld of aerospace, since the thermal conductivity of LMPMs is usually hundreds or even thousands times larger than that of parafﬁn PCMs [1] .

# 5. Conclusions  

This study investigated phase change characteristics of two novel low melting point alloys (i.e. Bi-21In-18Pb-12Sn alloy and Ga-13.5Sn alloy), compared with that of two conventional parafﬁn PCMs (i.e. C 26 H 58  and C 17 H 36 ).  

The latent heat of per unit in conventional parafﬁn phase change materials is much less than that of low melting point metals, and this indicates the higher unit efﬁciency of low melting point metals than that of parafﬁn phase change mate- rials. Under the same heating condition, the temperature gradients in the low melting point metals are much smaller than that of parafﬁn phase change materials. According to T-t curves, parafﬁn materials could not control the temper- ature to remain in small range under large heating power, but the low melting point metals could in the same process of phase change. In contrast, the latter exhibited better thermal conductivity under the same condition. Besides, this study also found the narrow variation of phase change tempera- ture and phase change latent heat measured after 100 and 500 melting/solidiﬁcation cycles, and the low melting point metals shows excellent stability in phase change temperature and latent heat. In addition, the Al 2 O 3  coating by the spraying method can effectively retrain the corrosion behavior induced by the liquid Ga-Sn alloy. Furthermore, in the simulated exper- imental environment, the temperature control characteristic and temperature gradient of low melting point metal could meet the applied requirement of spacecraft device operated in vacuum condition.  

Ongoing further investigation should be focused on more related space environment tests of LMPCM such as    zero grav- ity and strong radiation. The effect of space environment on the thermal properties of phase change thermal control mate- rials Bi-21In-18Pb-12Sn and Ga-13.5Sn with low melting point should be further investigated, so that the better application of low melting point metals in the ﬁeld of aerospace can be realized.
