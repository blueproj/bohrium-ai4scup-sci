# Oxidation behavior of sputtered Ni–Cr–Al–Ti nanocrystalline coating  

G. Chen \*, H. Lou  

State Key Lab for Corrosion and Protection, Institute of Corrosion and Protection of Metals, Academia Sinica, Shenyang 110015, People’s Republic of China

# Abstract  

Magnetron sputter deposition was used to prepare Ni–9.7Cr–5.5Al–7Ti (wt % ) nanocrystalline coatings on the substrates of the same material. The oxidation behavior of the cast Ni–Cr–Al–Ti alloy and its sputtered coating at 1000 ° C in air was investigated. An  a -Al O  scale with TiO  dispersions was formed on the sputtered coating after 200 h of oxidation; even so good 2 3 2 oxidation resistance was exhibited. The formation of TiO  was attributed to the high ratio of Ti / Al in the coating. The high 2 content of Cr and the precipitation of Ni (AlTi) in the sputtered coating during oxidation could restrict the formation and growth 3 of TiO . Therefore, good oxidation resistance was shown by the sputtered Ni–Cr–Al–Ti coating despite the formation of TiO . 2 2

# 1. Introduction  

Ni-base superalloys are widely used as turbine blade material for advanced engines that are required to operate at elevated temperatures and greatly depend on the presence of Al or Cr to form a protective oxide scale of alumina or chromina. However, a certain amount of Ti is contained in superalloys. The existence of Ti in superalloys can impair the oxidation resistance by form- ing TiO , which can destroy the continuity of the 2 protective oxide scale [1,2]. Recently, nanocrystalline coatings prepared by the magnetron sputtering tech- nique have attracted much attention. The nanocrystal- line coatings on the substrates of the same materials such as Ni-based superalloys K38G, K17F and LDZ125 showed excellent oxidation resistance by developing a unitary layer of Al 2 O 3  [3–5]. TiO 2  was not formed on these coated superalloys; therefore, no evidence can be used to evaluate the e ﬀ ect of TiO  on the oxidation 2 resistance of these sputtered coatings. Nano- crystallization can promote the selective oxidation of Al due to the low free energy of formation of Al 2 O 3  [6]. It has been suggested that the free energies of formation of TiO 2  and Al 2 O 3  are almost the same [7]. Therefore, TiO 2  and Al 2 O 3  would have almost the same propensity to form. The results that no TiO 2  was formed may be due to the low ratio of Ti / Al in the sputtered superalloys coating. Because TiO 2  could be formed when the ratio of Ti / Al was increased slightly, this can be evidenced by the studies of the rehealing ability of sputtered K17F and K38G coatings [8,9]. However, TiO 2  could not be found on the oxide scale. The results suggested that TiO 2  was well dispersed in the oxide scale. It is important to study the oxidation behavior of the sputtered nanocrystalline coating when TiO 2  can be formed and observed on the coating so as to evaluate the e ﬀ ect of TiO 2  on the oxidation resistance of the sputtered coating. In this paper, based upon the contents of Cr, Al and Ti in the superalloy LDZ125, by increasing the content of Ti in order to promote the formation of TiO , a simple 2 Ni–Cr–Al–Ti alloy was developed. The oxidation resis- tance and oxide formation of this alloy and its sputtered Ni–Cr–Al–Ti nanocrystalline coating have been investigated.

# 4. Discussion  

The formation of TiO 2  can impair the oxidation resistance of the cast alloy in that TiO 2  can destroy the continuity of the formed oxide scale [1]. It has been indicated that the formation and growth of TiO 2  were directly related to the presence of TiC in the superalloys [2]. The lack of formation of TiO 2  on the coated K38G, K17F, LDZ125 superalloys was probably attributed to the absence of TiC in the sputtered superalloys coatings. The present results indicated that TiO 2  was formed on the cast Ni–Cr–Al–Ti alloy and its sputtered coating. No TiC existed in the Ni–Cr–Al–Ti alloy; therefore, the harmful e ﬀ ect of TiO  was not apparent for the cast 2 Ni–Cr–Al–Ti alloy. This was due to the formation of a complete Al 2 O 3  subscale preceded by the formation of transient oxides such as Cr O , TiO  and NiCr O . The 2 3 2 2 4 high ratio of Ti / Al contributed to the formation of TiO 2  on the sputtered Ni–Cr–Al–Ti coating. The appear- ance of TiO 2  on the protective coating may contribute to the acceleration of mass gain [10]. However, in

Therefore, the selective oxidation of Al was more pre- dominant than Ti, and an Al 2 O 3  scale with dispersed TiO 2  particles was formed on the sputtered coating.

The oxide scale will be subjected to thermal and growth stresses generated during oxidation process. Spalling may be induced by these stresses, resulting in accelerated oxidation. No spallation was observed in the oxide scale formed on the sputtered coating. Therefore, the stresses must somehow have been relieved. The plastic deformation of the oxide scale formed on the nanocrystalline coating could be easily generated, and then these stresses may be relieved by plastic deformation of the oxide scale [6]. The curved oxide scale shown in Fig. 5(b) provides evidence for such deformation.

Good oxidation resistance was shown by the sputtered Ni–Cr–Al–Ti coating. The pres- ence of TiO 2  did not impair the continuity of the Al O  scale, which resulted from the e ﬀ ect of Cr and 2 3 the precipitation of Ni (AlTi).
