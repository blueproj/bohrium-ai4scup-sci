ABSTRACT:  By using cyclic (vinyl triflate)iodonium salts, a novel triple Suzuki − Miyaura reaction was accomplished for the synthesis of polyaromatic ethylene derivatives in the presence of palladium catalysts. The reaction exhibits extensive compatibility with a wide range of readily available arylboronic acids, giving triaryl- substituted ethylenes in good yields.  

P olycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons (PAHs), due to their unique optical and electrical properties, have received significant attention over the past decade.   The conjugated  π - system of PAHs allows charge migration across the stacked disk-shaped molecules, rendering them beneficial in various organic electronic applications such as field effect transistors (OFETs), organic light-emitting diodes (OLEDs), and photo- voltaic devices (OPVs).   Polyarylated ethylene as a popular precursor for PAHs, undergoes multiple dehydrogenation coupling processes, resulting in annulative  π -extension (APEX) reaction for graphene fragments.   Meanwhile, polyarylated ethylenes exhibit unique aggregation induced emission (AIE) effect, stemming from its unique molecular structure and optical properties.   Recently, the exploration of diaryliodonium salts (Ar 2 IX) has witnessed notable advance- ment in facilitating ary lo cyclization s and polyarylations, significantly contributing to the development of synthetic methodologies for constructing these polyaromatic skeletons.

Vinyl(aryl)iodonium salts, serving as efficient vinylating agents, were frequently introduced in metal-catalyzed reac- tions.   Particularly, linear (vinyl triflate)iodonium salts were synthesized with phenylacetylene and aryliodine (III)   reagents as illustrated in  Scheme 1 a. This process is facilitated by the addition of tri flu oro methane s ulf onic acid (TfOH) or trime- thylsilyl tri flu oro methane s ulf on ate (TMSOTf), which intro- duces the OTf group and anionic moieties to the desired iodonium salts. The (vinyl triflate)iodoniums exhibit versatile reactivity. For instance, the olefin portion can participate in a Sonogashira reaction with terminal olefins. Under strong alkaline conditions at a low temperature, substitution with iodobenzene facilitates the synthesis of diaryliodonium salts. Additionally, when the aromatic component is benzoic acid, the OTf can be replaced by azide to generate pseudocyclic derivatives. As shown in  Scheme 1 b, cyclic (vinyl triflate)- iodonium salts have been highlighted in recent reports.   The preparation of these salts involves the activation of the aryl C −  

H bond through oxidation, leading to the formation of cyclic hypervalent iodoniums. These salts are utilized as carbene precursors aimed at yielding phenylacetylene or styrene derivatives. Our research interest is particularly focused on employing Ar 2 IX for constructing polyaromatic compounds.  

We have previously reported on the dual-arylations of cyclic diaryliodonium salts, as well as b enzo cyclization reactions involving linear diaryliodonium salts under transition-metal catalysis.   In this Communication, we introduce a novel synthetic approach for cyclic (vinyl triflate)iodonium salts. Thus, palladium-catalyzed triple Suzuki-Miyaura reactions utilizing these cyclic (vinyl triflate) iodonium salts were carried out in one pot. This approach opens a new avenue for synthesizing polyarylated ethylene derivatives by multiple coupling reactions ( Scheme 1 c).

With the optimized reaction conditions in hand, we started to investigate the structural diversity of various substituted arylboronic acids toward polyarylated ethylenes. Initially, substrates of 4-alkyl substituted arylboronic acids were examined under the standard conditions. As shown in  Scheme 2 , substituents such as methyl,  tert -butyl, and  iso -propyl were generally well-tolerated, affording the desired products  3ab − 3ad  in moderate to good yields of 56% − 71%. Notably, products  3ae − 3ah  bearing long-chain alkyl groups (butyl, amyl, and hexyl) proved to be useful synthons by improving the solubility of conjugated structures in the synthesis of functional materials. The 4-cyclohexyl-substituted phenyl- boronic acid was also compatible, affording  3ai  in 50% yield. Subsequently, we also investigated the reactivity of 3-alkyl substituted phenylboronic acids. 3-Substituted phenylboronic acids bearing methyl,  iso -propyl,  tert -butyl, and 3,4-/3,5- methyl, were all successful in producing their corresponding products ( 3aj − 3an ) in good yields (42% − 51%). 4-Alkoxy- substituted phenylboronic acids underwent smooth reactions to yield the desired products of  3ao − 3aq  in 47% − 55% yields. Remarkably, the triarylation product of  3ar  derived from 4- phenoxy-substituted phenylboronic acid was obtained in 60% yield, which emerged as potential candidates for  π -extended material design. In addition, 3-alkoxy-substituted phenyl- boronic acids were employed, giving  3as  (60% yield) and 3at  (38% yield). The successful synthesis of multideuterated aryl product of  3au  in 66% yield was observed by using phenyl- d 5 -boronic acid. Note that deuterated organic compounds play a crucial role in medicinal and material science.   In order to obtain graphene fragments with larger conjugated structures, we studied the reactivity of 1-pyreneboronic acid and 4- (diphenylamino)phenylboronic acid, and the desired triple- arylation products  3av − 3aw  were afforded in 36% − 40% yields, showcasing the potential for  π -extended material design. Moreover, the desired product bearing 4-(trans-4- n - propylcyclohexyl)phenyl ( 3ax ) was afforded in 41% yield, in which the 4-(trans-4- n -prop yl cy clo hex yl)phenyl group could be utilized in the construction of liquid-crystal materials.   Of note, arylboronic acids containing electron withdrawing groups ( − CF 3 , etc.) or halogen groups ( − Cl,  − F, and  − Br) cannot furnish the corresponding products.

The oxidative method with tri methyl benzene (MesH) did not furnish the desired product but instead obtained  3aa  in a yield of 91% ( Scheme 4 a). Next, we tried to explore the reaction scope for potential utility, and  3aa  was chosen to undergo a further intramolecular dehydrogenative coupling reaction (Scholl reaction). In summary, we have developed a novel method for synthesizing a series of cyclic (vinyl triflate)iodonium salts. Subsequently, we successfully conducted triple Suzuki − Miyaura reactions between these iodonium salts and arylboronic acids, utilizing a palladium catalyst to produce a diverse array of polyarylated ethylenes. We consider this method to be an exceptionally valuable platform for efficiently accessing PAH molecules in the future, showcasing its utility and potential in complex synthesis. Further research is currently underway in our laboratory to refine the reaction conditions and expand the scope of this methodology.
