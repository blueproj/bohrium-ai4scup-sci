Biological Target.  alpha 7 nicotinic acetylcholine receptor ( α 7 nAChR)  

Summary.  Cholinergic signaling is a neurotransmitter system that modulates the balance of excitation and inhibition in the brain. In order for this signaling system to operate, it utilizes the neurotransmitter acetylcholine (ACh) and a cholinergic receptor (AChR) such as the  α 7 receptor, as well as the two enzymes choline acetyl transfer as e (ChAT) and acetylcholine st erase (AChE). Cholinergic signaling is crucial in controlling cognitive processes in the brain. It is also important in neuroimmune communication to transmit information regarding the peripheral immune status to and from the central nervous system (CNS). Dysfunction of the cholinergic signaling has been implicated in cognitive impairments in neurological and psychiatric diseases such as Alzheimer’s disease (AD), schizophrenia, and Parkinson’s disease (PD).  

The alpha 7 nicotinic acetylcholine receptor ( α 7 nAChR), also known as the  α 7 receptor, is a fast-desensitizing ligand- gated ion channel with marked calcium ions’ permeability. It is highly expressed in the cortex and hippocampus regions of the brain, which are associated with cognition.  α 7 nAChR is localized in both pre-synaptic and post-synaptic structures of neurons, where its activation can modulate neurotransmitter release, neuronal excitability, and intracellular signaling. Dysfunction of  α 7 nAChRs has been associated with multiple neurologic diseases such as AD, PD, schizophrenia, and other CNS disorders.  

AD is a neuro degenerative disease that is usually characterized by a slow onset of memory loss and a late development of disorientation and behavioral difficulties. Studies have shown that the pathogenesis of AD is associated with the loss of cholinergic neurons in the basal forebrain nuclei. The inhibition of acetylcholine esterase prevents the breakdown of acetylcho- line to promote cholinergic transmission, which is the reason acetylcholine esterase inhibitors are used as a standard treatment for the cognitive symptoms of AD. Recent clinical studies have shown that encenicline, a partial agonist of the  α 7 nAChR, improves cognition symptoms in AD patients. Therefore, the modulation of  α 7 nAChR represents an attractive therapeutic strategy for the treatment of cognitive impairments associated with AD.

Schizophrenia is a chronic and disabling mental disorder that affects patients’ thinking and how they perceive reality and relate to others. Patients may also suffer from cognitive impairment. Studies have provided evidence implicating  α 7 nAChRs in the etiology of schizophrenia. This evidence came from the following observations and findings:  

•  Neuronal  α 7 nAChRs expression is decreased in the brains of schizophrenic patients. •  Many schizophrenic patients frequently smoke, which is believed to be a form of self-medication to improve cognition (nicotine from cigarette smoking is a non- selective agonist of nAChRs and possibly acts to improve cognition). •  Symptoms of schizophrenia are associated with variants in the promotor region of the CHRNA7, the gene encoding α 7 nAChR protein, •  Accumulating evidence from clinical trials has indicated that activating  α 7 nAChR with agonists may have beneficial effects on cognition.  

Therefore, the modulation of  α 7 nAChR represents a potentially viable therapeutic strategy for the treatment of schizophrenia.  

The neuro degenerative PD is characterized by progressive deficits in motor functions, such as tremor, bradykinesia, rigidity, and impaired postural reflex. The main pathological finding associated with PD is the degeneration of dopaminergic neurons in the substantia nigra, resulting in loss of dopaminergic tone in the striatum. L-DOPA is the current standard treatment for the motor symptoms of PD. However, long-term therapy of PD patients with L-DOPA induces dyskinesia as a side effect. Emerging evidence indicates that activating  α 7 nAChRs acutely alleviates dyskinesia in several animal models. Other studies suggest that pretreatment with  α 7 nAChR agonists may protect against neuro degeneration in nigrostriatal neurons and activation of  α 7 may have disease-modifying properties. Therefore, the modulation of  α 7 nAChR may provide a viable therapeutic target for both ameliorating PD progression and managing dyskinesia.  

The  α 7 nAChR is also widely expressed in peripheral immune cells including macrophages, monocytes, dendritic cells, and B and T cells. Activation of peripheral  α 7 nAChRs is critical for inhibiting the release of pro-inflammatory cytokines via the cholinergic anti-inflammatory pathway. Therefore,  α 7 nAChR is a potential therapeutic target for the treatment of several inflammatory diseases such as rheumatoid arthritis and atherosclerosis.  

Cough is a common symptom of many allergies, diseases, and respiratory infections. When cough persists more than 8 weeks it is defined as chronic. Treatment of chronic cough is currently limited to the use of antitussive agents, which can decrease cough frequency and/or severity. However, antitussives suffer from limited efficacy, and their use may be restricted by low safety and potential abuse liabilities. Recent studies performed on healthy human volunteers indicate that activation of nAChR may be a novel, safe, and effective approach to treat cough. Other preclinical studies suggest that targeting  α 7 nAChR may be an attractive strategy to identify novel therapy for cough patients.  

With the promising potential of  α 7 nAChR as highlighted above, researchers have characterized and developed several agonists of  α 7 nAChRs. While many of them have advanced to clinical trials, most were discontinued due to lack of efficacy or unfavorable adverse effects. They may also have displayed time- or concentration-dependent receptor desensitization.  

In recent years,  α 7-selective positive allosteric modulators (PAMs) have emerged as potentially beneficial therapeutic alternatives to  α 7 agonists in the treatment of  α 7 nAChR-related diseases and disorders. PAMs increase the potency of the endogenous cholinergic agonist such as acetylcholine and choline without perturbing the temporal and spatial integrity of neuro transmission. This is distinct from the  α 7 agonists, which activate the channel irrespective of the endogenous agonist. There are two types of  α 7 PAMs, distinguished according to their ability to modulate the peak currents generated by ion channels. Type I PAMs predominantly affect the peak current generated by the ion channel with little or no effect on receptor desensitization, while type II PAMs markedly delay desensitization of the receptor. Additionally,  α 7 nAChR PAMs may possess improved selectivity over related channel targets, presumably through binding to non-conserved regions of the receptor.  

The disclosed compounds of formula  1  in this patent application exhibit activities as positive allosteric modulation of the  α 7 nAChR and may be beneficial in preventing, treating, or ameliorating diseases and disorders of the CNS, such as cognitive impairments in AD, PD, schizophrenia, and L-DOPA- induced dyskinesia, as well as inflammation and cough.
