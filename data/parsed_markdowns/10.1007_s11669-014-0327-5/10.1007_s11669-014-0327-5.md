# Experimental Investigation and Thermodynamic Modeling of the Co-Rich Region in the Co-Al-Ni-W Quaternary System  

J. Zhu, M.S. Titus, and T.M. Pollock (Submitted February 26, 2014; in revised form June 17, 2014; published online August 13, 2014)  

The stabilities of the  c -(Co),  c ¢ -Co 3 (Al,W),  l -Co 7 W 6 ,  b -CoAl and  a -(W) phases in the Co-Al-W and Ni-Al-W ternary systems were investigated at 900, 1000, 1100 and 1300   C. Electron probe microanalysis was used to determine compositions of the phases. Scanning electron microscopy was used to determine area fractions of the phases present after heat treatments. Thermody- TM   namic modeling of the Co-Al-Ni-W quaternary system, using Pandat software, was incor- porated to determine relevant thermodynamic parameters for phases in the Co-rich region of the system. Phases were modeled using the substitutional solution model and compound energy formalism. Excellent agreement was found between experiments and thermodynamic modeling. This new thermodynamic database enables predictions of stable  c - c ¢  compositions for a new class of high temperature, high strength Co-base superalloys.  

Keywords CALPHAD, experimental phase equilibria, thermo- dynamic assessment  

# 1. Introduction  

Among all structural materials, nickel-base alloys are unique for their ability to operate at 90% of their melting point with substantial mechanical loads in chemically severe environments. Such metallic alloys reinforced with inter- metallic precipitates form the basis of a wide variety of high temperature and high strength structural materials. The recent discovery of a stable ternary Co 3 (Al,W) intermetallic [ 1 ]   compound with an ordered L1 2  structure provides a possible pathway for development of a new class of load- bearing Co-base high temperature alloys. The thermody- namic coexistence of the  c -Co solid-solution phase with the c ¢ -Co 3 (Al,W) phase, and the similarity of the lattice parameters of the two phases result in microstructures with submicron regularly aligned, coherent cuboidal  c ¢  precipi- 1 , ]   tates. This microstructure is morphologically identical to the microstructure of Ni-base superalloys, which enables Co-base alloys to possess high-temperature strength and creep resistance comparable to that of the commercial Ni- 3 - 6 ] base superalloys.  

Based on the fact that the  c ¢ -Ni 3 Al with the L1 2  structure is very stable and the  c ¢  phase exists in a wide composition 7 ]   range in the Ni-Co-Al ternary system, it is expected that the thermodynamic stability of the  c ¢  phase may be increased by Ni substitution for Co. Knowledge of phase equilibria and thermodynamic properties of the Co-Al-Ni-W quaternary system is of fundamental importance for better understanding the microstructure and subsequent property enhancement of Co-base alloys, as well as possible improvements to the current Ni-base superalloys with Co additions.  

The purpose of the present investigation is to develop a reliable thermodynamic description of the Co-Al-Ni-W system in the Co-rich zone, which is an essential step forward for full thermodynamic description of the Co-Al-Ni-W-X (X = Cr, Re, Ta, etc.) system. New experimental assessments combined with prior knowledge of relevant ternary and quaternary systems provide an improved understanding of phase equilibria in this quaternary system. The database will provide a useful guideline for new classes of Co-base alloys and processing development. This paper is organized as follows: ﬁrst, the information available on relevant ternary and quaternary systems is reviewed. Next, experiments aimed at resolving stable phases in uncertain regimes of the diagrams are reviewed. Finally, a thermodynamic assessment of the quaternary Co-Al-Ni-W system is given.  

# 2. Survey of Relevant Ternary and Quaternary Systems  

# 2.1 Co-Al-W Ternary System  

Although there is no stable Co 3 Al compound formed in the Co-Al binary system, a metastable Co 3 Al phase with the L1 2  structure was observed in a Co-Al alloy with 14 at.% 8 ]   Al. First principles calculations also indicate the strong ordering tendency of both Co 3 Al and CoAl 3  to form the L1 2 [ 9 ]   structure and the formation enthalpies for these two compounds are 19.9 and 22.6 kJ/mol, respectively.  

Metastable Co 3 W precipitates with the L1 2  structure have 10 ] been reported in Co-W alloys aged at 873 and 973 K. First principles calculations suggested that Co 3 W has an ordering tendency to form the L1 2  structure, while CoW 3 11 ] does not.  

The ternary  c ¢ -Co 3 (Al, W) phase with almost equiatomic 1 ]   ratio of Al and W was ﬁrst identiﬁed by Sato et al. The compositions of the matrix (A1) and that of the precipitate (L1 2 ) were determined using a ﬁeld emission electron probe microanalyzer (FE-EPMA). The L1 2  phase ﬁeld exists only over a narrow range in the Co-Al-W ternary, resulting in a narrow  c  +  c ¢  two-phase region. Two isothermal sections at 900 and 1000   C were proposed, showing that the  c ¢  phase     1 ]   is stable at 900 C but metastable at 1000 C. The thermal stability of the  c ¢  phase was investigated by differential scanning calorimetry (DSC). The solvus temperature of the c ¢ phase in Co-(9-10)Al-(7.5-10)W (at.%) alloys is   1 , , ] 990 C.  

14 ]   Dmitrieva et al. investigated the phase stabilities in the Co-CoAl-W part using optical light, differential thermal, x-ray diffraction and electron microprobe analysis. A liquidus projection including four primary phase regions: Co, CoAl, Co 7 W 6  and W, was reported and two four-phase invariant reactions with participation of liquid were estab- lished: L =  > Co + CoAl + Co 7 W 6 at 1380   C and L + W =  >  CoAl + Co 7 W 6  at 1490   C. The vertical sec- tions for 75 at.% Co and equiatomic Al and W were constructed based on the liquidus, solidus surfaces and the isothermal sections at 1200 and 900   C.  

[ 15 - 18 ] First-principles SQS calculations investigating phase stability and elastic properties of the L1 2 -Co 3 Al 0.5 W 0.5  compound found it to be unstable at 0 K. The authors suggested that its presence at high temperatures was stabilized by entropy as well as short- 19 ]   range ordering effects. Tsukamoto et al. studied the stability of  c ¢ -Co 3 (Al,W) at 900   C through microstructure and EPMA analysis on a cold rolled bulk alloy followed by heat-treatment from 100 to 2000 h in evacuated silica tube. The  c ¢  phase was observed to form in contact with the  c phase discontinuously and dissolved in the vicinity of the CoAl and Co 3 W phases with increasing annealing time. Additionally, the area fraction of the  c ¢  phase decreased with annealing time. Based on these observations, which are consistent with those in a diffusion-couple experiment by 20 ]   19 ]   Kobayashi et al., Tsukamoto et al. concluded that the c ¢  phase is metastable and the  c , CoAl, and Co3W phases are thermodynamic ally stable at 900   C.  

# 2.2 Ni-Co-Al Ternary System  

7 ]   Kainuma et al. investigated the Ni-Co-Al ternary system in the composition range Ni-(0-70)Co-(20-30)Al (at.%). The microstructure was examined by optical micros- copy and the compositions of  c ,  c ¢  and  b  phases were determined by energy dispersion x-ray spectroscopy (EDX). Three isothermal sections at 900, 1100 and 1300   C were obtained and martensitic transformation temperatures were determined using DSC. The  c ¢  phase ﬁeld is stabilized and the  c  +  b  two-phase region widens with decreasing tem- perature. Mechanical properties were also examined and a good combination of strength and ductility was observed in the multi-phase alloys composed of  c ,  c ¢  and  b  phases.  

# 2.3 Ni-Al-W Ternary System  

Experimental investigations of the phase equilibria in the Ni-Al-W system are limited to the Ni-rich range of 21 ]   compositions. Korniyenko et al. reviewed this system based mainly on the studies of Ref.  22 ,  23 ,  24 . The partial 22 ] pseudo-binary section NiAl-W given by Nash et al. suggested a very lower solid solubility of W in  b -NiAl and the composition of the eutectic point is at 1.4 at.% W, in which electron probe microanalysis (EPMA) was used to determine the phase compositions. Using diffusion couples 24 ]   method, Jia et al. also conﬁrmed a small solubility of W in the  b  phase, less than 0.53 at.% in the temperature range 900 to 1300   C. The experimental phase compositions 23 ]   given by Udovskii et al. are in good agreement with the   20 ] isothermal section at 1250 C obtained by Nash et al. [ 25 ]   Alekseeva proposed a reaction scheme based on the 23 ] experimental data by Udovskii et al.  

Popovic ˇ et al. 26 ]   obtained new experimental data on the phase equilibria in Ni-rich alloys at 1100, 1000 and 900   C and computed three isothermal sections at these tempera- tures. Three alloys were prepared from pure elements and annealed in vacuum for 10 h at 1100   C, 100 h at 1000   C, and 1300 h at 900   C. Energy dispersive x-ray spectroscopy (EDS) in scanning electron microscopy (SEM) was used to examine the homogeneity of samples and their nominal chemical composition. The microstructure and chemical composition of phases present were studied using EDS in transmission electron microscope (TEM). The experimental data obtained by Popovic ˇ et al. 26 ]   were conﬁrmed with calculated results obtained using a commercial database for Ni-base superalloys. They obtained a new thermodynamic description via optimization, which is in better agreement with their own experimental observation.  

# 2.4 Co-Ni-W Ternary System

Nikolskii et al. established two isopleths along the 10 wt.% (Co + Ni) and 20 wt.% (Co + Ni) lines, and later modiﬁed the 10 wt.% (Co + Ni) 28 ]   isopleth. They used powder metallurgy techniques to prepare two sets of W-rich alloys and heat-treated them ﬁrst under a hydrogen atmosphere at 1380 to 1400   C for 1 h and then annealed between 1460 and 1700   C in vacuum. The heat-treated samples were further annealed at different temperatures following the schedule: 1400   C (24 h)  ﬁ 1200   C (26 h)  ﬁ 800   C (720 h)  ﬁ 575   C (1680 h). Metallography, x-ray diffraction (XRD), and EPMA were used for phase characterization. With the data of these two 28 ]   isopleths, Nikolskii et al. constructed four partial iso- thermal sections at 575, 800, 950 and 1050   C. Most of the binary compounds showed little ternary solubility at these temperatures.

# 2.5 Co-Al-Ni-W Quaternary System  

13 ]   Shinagawa et al. determined the phase equilibria between  c  and  c ¢  phases at 900   C in the Co-(10-70)Ni-Al- W system. Alloys were prepared from pure elements by induction melting under an argon atmosphere. Small pieces of specimens were heat-treated at 1300   C for 24 h, followed by quenching in ice water, and then cold-rolled at a reduction rate of 20-50% before the heat treatment at 900   C for 168 h. The equilibrium compositions of the  c and  c ¢  phases at 900   C were determined by EPMA, and the phase transition temperatures were examined by DSC at a heating rate of 10   C/min. The lattice parameters of the  c and  c ¢  phases were also determined by x-ray diffraction (XRD).  

The  c ¢  phase was observed to exist continuously from the Co side to the Ni side in the Co-Ni-Al-W system and the c  +  c ¢  two-phase region was broadened with increasing Ni content. The  c ¢  solvus temperature monotonically increases while the solidus is not strongly inﬂuenced by increasing Ni content in two series of alloys. The  c ¢  morphology changes from cuboidal to spherical with increasing Ni content in the Co-rich region.  

[ 13 , ]   While the aforementioned study and others have experimentally determined equilibrium compositions of various phases in the Co-Al-W and Co-Ni-Al-W systems, a full thermodynamic description has not yet been devel- oped for the four-component system. Using previous experimental data from literature and three experimental alloys in the Co-Al-W and Ni-Al-W systems along with experiments described in the following sections, a thermo- dynamic description of the Co-Al-Ni-W system in the Co- rich region has been developed, and further optimized using TM   32 ] Pandat software.

# 6. Conclusions  

The Ni-Al-W and Co-Al-W ternary systems in the W-rich region were studied experimentally and theoretically at 900, 1000, 1100 and 1300   C. The thermodynamic description of the Co-Al-Ni-W quaternary system was optimized and a good agreement of the calculated results with the experi- mental data was obtained. The thermodynamic description will provide a useful guideline for further experimental investigation of this system as well as the effects with other element additions, such as Cr, Re, Ta and Ti.
