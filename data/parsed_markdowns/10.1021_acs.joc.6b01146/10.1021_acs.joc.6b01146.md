ABSTRACT:    Two-armed imidazolium-based anion receptors have been prepared. The central 2,7-disubstituted naphthalene  ring features two photoactive anthracene end-capped side arms with central 2-bromo- or hydrogen-bonding imidazolium  1 31 receptors. Combined emission,  H and  P-NMR studies carried out in the presence of a wide variety of anions reveal that  3– – 2– – 2+ – only HP 2 O 7 , H 2 PO 4 , SO 4  and F   anions promoted noticeable changes. The halogen receptor  6 ·2PF 6   acts as a selective  – fluorescent molecular sensor for H 2 PO 4  anions since only this anion promotes the apparition of the anthracene excimer  emission band, whereas it remains unchanged in the presence of the others tested anions. In addition this halogen receptor  3– behaves as a chemodosimeter toward HP 2 O 7  anion, through its transformation into the corresponding bisimidazolone after  debromination by the action of the basic anion. The association constant values of the halogen bonding complexes in the  – 2– competitive solvent  CD 3 CN/MeOD (8:2) mixture  with H 2 PO 4 , SO 4  anions are higher than those found for the hydrogen- bonding counterpart.  On contrary, in the less competitive CH 3 CN solvent higher binding affinity for anions corresponds to the  hydrogen bonding receptor  7 2+ ·2PF 6 – . Among the well-established noncovalent intermolecular interactions, halogen bonding (XB)  “a net attractive  interaction between an electrophilic region associated with a halogen atom in a molecular entity and a nucleophilic region in an- other, or the same, molecular entity” 2  is the least exploited noncovalent interaction, specially in the solution phase.  It is known that  XB is generally of the same order of strength as hydrogen bonding (HB) and can either compete or cooperate with HB in crystal  structures. In this context, in 1999 it was reported an interesting investigation into the interplay of XB with HB in solid-state organ- ic salts.  Several scenarios have been found in this field, in some cases it has been showed the ability of XB to dominate over HB in  driving the self-assembly processes of several cocrystal under appropriate conditions,  whereas recently it has been demonstrated  the complementarity and ability of HB and XB to interact orthogonally in the preparation of several cocrystals with various XB and  HB acceptor molecules.  The bonding mechanism of the halogen bonds XB···A and the hydrogen bonds HB···A is very similar  taking into account the electrostatic and covalent contribution. The main differences between the XB···A and the HB···A is that the  former are generally associated with a weaker electrostatic attraction, due to a smaller and in some cases less favorably oriented  polarization, additionally the HOMO-LUMO interaction is more stabilized due to the lower energy of the  σ \*  LUMO in the halogen- halogen compared with the respective hydrogen-hydrogen. Taking this into account the strength of the halogen bonds could be  stronger or weaker than the analogous hydrogen bonds.  However, comparative studies of the anion sensing properties in solution  of analogous charge-assisted XB and HB receptors remain almost unexplored.  

In this context, it has been reported that in halogen- and hydrogen-bonding triazolium- 8  and imidazolium-based receptors,  substi- tution of the hydrogen atom by halogen in the anion receptor has a great influence in the anion recognition event due to electronic  and geometric consideration. With the aim of acquiring more knowledge about the behavior of halogen bonding-based receptors in  solution we report here the synthesis and a comparative study of the anion sensing properties of a series of novel two-armed charge- assisted ditopic receptors: imidazolium and haloimidazolium bidentate receptors, which incorporate two end-caped photoactive  anthracene rings as fluorescent signaling units into their host frameworks  

Synthesis .   The bis-imidazolium receptors  6 2+ ·2PF 6 –  and  7 2+ ·2PF 6 –  were prepared by a stepwise sequence which involves initial  alkylation of 2-bromo-4,5-dimethyl-1 H -imidazole  2 10  or 4,5-dimethyl-1 H -imidazole  3 11  with 2,7-bis(bromomethyl)naphthalene  1  in  basic medium to give the corresponding bis-imidazoles  4  and  5 9a  respectively. Further alkylation of  4  or  5  with 9- (bromomethyl)anthracene afforded the expected halogen- or hydrogen-bonding bis-imidazolium receptors as bromide salts in mod- erate yield (47 and 14 % respectively). These bromide salts were readily converted by anion exchange after several washing with  saturated aqueous solution of NH 4 PF 6  into the corresponding hex a flu oro phosphate salts  6 2+ ·2PF 6 –  and  7 2+ ·2PF 6 –   in almost quantita- tive yield (Scheme 1). All the prepared compounds have been fully characterized using standard techniques:  1 H NMR,  13 C NMR  and FAB mass spectrometry.

Anion binding studies . The anion sensing properties of receptors  6 2+ ·2PF 6 –  and  7 2+ ·2PF 6 –  were initially investigated by fluores- cence toward the set of anions: HP 2 O 7 3– , H 2 PO 4 – , SO 4 2– , HSO 4 – , NO 3 − , F – , Cl – , Br – , I – , AcO – , ClO 4 – , BF 4 –  and C 6 H 5 CO 2 –  as tetrabu- tylammonium salts and the organic phosphate derivatives ATP, ADP and AMP as sodium salts.  

The emission spectra of receptors  6 2+ ·2PF 6 –  and  7 2+ ·2PF 6 –  were measured in CH 3 CN using an excitation wavelength at 370 nm,  both receptors exhibit only the characteristic anthracene monomeric emission bands at 396, 418 and 442 nm. The hydrogen receptor  7 2+ ·2PF 6 –  exhibits a   quantum yield (Φ = 0.370), which is considerably higher, almost 22 times, than that showed by the halogenated  one  6 2+ ·2PF 6 –  (Φ = 0.017) . Probably, the quenching in the fluorescence emission bands of the halogenated receptor  6 2+ ·2PF 6 –  could  be attributed to an intramolecular heavy-atom effect 12  caused by the bromine atoms present in the receptor  

Evaluation of the sensing properties by fluorescence were realized by stepwise addition of increasing amounts of the previously  mentioned set of anions in CH 3 CN, except ATP ADP and AMP anions which were added water, to a solution of the corresponding  receptor  6 2+ ·2PF 6 –  and  7 2+ ·2PF 6 –  in CH 3 CN. The results reveal important differences in the sensing behavior between the halogenat- ed receptor  6 2+ ·2PF 6 –  and the hydrogen receptor  7 2+ ·2PF 6 – .  

Titrations data obtained from the halogenated receptor  6 2+ ·2PF 6 –  ( c  = 1·10 –5  M in CH 3 CN) showed that addition of HSO 4 – , NO 3 − ,  Cl – , Br – , I – , AcO – , ClO 4 – , BF 4 –  and C 6 H 5 CO 2 –  anions had no effect on the emission spectrum of this receptor, whereas the addition of  HP 2 O 7 3– , H 2 PO 4 – , SO 4 2–  and F –  anions promoted significant changes in the emission bands of  6 2+ ·2PF 6 – . Interestingly the changes  resulting were strongly dependent on the anion added, and three different responses have been observed (Figure 1).

First, addition of increasing amounts of SO 4 2–  or F –  promoted a remarkable decreasing of the monomer emission bands of the re- ceptor  6 2+ ·2PF 6 –  and no other additional  spectral changes were observed (Figure 2a). Second, addition of H 2 PO 4 –  anions promotes a  progressive increasing of a new broad and structureless emission band at  λ  = 465 nm, assigned to the anthracene excimer emission  band, with concomitant decreasing of the monomer emission bands at  λ  = 396, 418 and 442 nm (Figure 2b). Third, addition of  HP 2 O 7 3–  anions induces two different effects: addition of up to one equivalent of HP 2 O 7 3–  anion causes a weak increasing of the  excimer emission band at  λ  = 465 nm along with a decreasing of the monomer emission bands (Figure 2c), subsequent addition of  more than one equivalent promotes only a continuous and remarkable increasing of the monomer emission bands  λ  = 396, 418 and  442 nm (Figure 2c inset), being the quantum yield two-fold increased from Φ = 0.017 to Φ = 0.037. Additionally the inc reasing of  the monomer emission bands is not only dependent on the number of equivalents added but also depends on the time, which sug- gests that a chemical modification could be taking place during the recognition event. In other words, the receptor  6 2+ ·2PF 6 –  could  be acting as chemodosimeter, further investigations about this process will be detailed in the next sections.

The different responses observed with the receptor  6 2+ ·2PF 6 –  in the presence of HP 2 O 7 3– , H 2 PO 4 – , SO 4 2– , and F –  anions could be  due to the fact that the receptor  6 2+ ·2PF 6 –  can act through different mechanisms depending on the nature of the anion added. Thus,  receptor  6 2+ ·2PF 6 –   acts by photo-induced electron transfer (PET) mechanism   in the presence of SO 4 2–  and F –  anions which diminish  the intensity of the emission bands.  The broad emission band observed at  λ  = 465 nm in the receptor  6 2+ ·2PF 6 -  with the presence  of H 2 PO 4 -  anions could be attributed to the well known p-staking formation of the two anthracene moieties 14  present in the receptor  after the addition of the anion as a consequence of the XB interactions between the receptor and the anions.  

Competition experiments were performed to test the specificity of the receptor  6 2+ ·2PF 6 -  ( c  = 1 x 10 –5  M in CH 3 CN) for H 2 PO 4 -   anions. The intensity of the excimer emission band at  λ  = 465 nm of the complex  6 2+ ·2   H 2 PO 4 -   remained practically unaltered with  the addition of two equivalents of HSO 4 – , NO 3 − , F – , Cl – , Br – , I – , AcO – , ClO 4 – , BF 4 –  and C 6 H 5 CO 2 –  while the presence of two equiva- lent of HP 2 O 7 3–  and SO 4 2–  anions promote a weak decrease of 13 and 26 % respectively in the intensity of the excimer band (Figure  3).

The behavior observed during the titration experiment of the halogen bond donor receptor  6 2+ ·2PF 6 –   with HP 2 O 7 3–  anions was  quite different: first of all, addition of up to 1 equivalent promotes similar changes to those observed in the receptor  7 2+ ·2PF 6 – : the  signals corresponding to  H e ,  H a , and  H b  protons were downfield shifted Δ δ  = + 0.25 ppm, Δ δ  = + 0.08 ppm and Δ δ  = + 0.08 ppm  respectively, and the methyl proton were upfield shifted Δ δ  = – 0.06 ppm, suggesting that a recognition process is taking place. The  addition of more than one equivalent induced the appearance of two new sets of signals (Figure 7, peaks in blue and in red) and the  progressive disappearance of the signals assigned to the complex  6 2+ ·HP 2 O 7 3– . Finally, after addition of five equivalents only one set  of signals (Figure 7, peaks in red) was present in the  1 H-NMR spectrum which clearly indicates than an intermediate species (Fig- ure 7, blue peaks) was generated during the addition process. The titration was also monitored by mass spectrometry, which showed  that the two new species were the result of the loss of one or two bromine atoms in the receptor  6 2+ ·2PF 6 –  and the subsequent gener- ation of the corresponding mono- and bis-imidazolone  8 + ·PF 6 –  and  9  respectively. Although halogen displacement in 2- haloimidazolium derivatives by the action of active methylene compounds, amines and sulfur derivatives is well-documented,   conversion into the corresponding 2-imidazolones by merely the action of oxoanions under extremely mild reactions conditions is  unprecedented. To this end, the bis-imidazolone  9  was prepared in bulk in 44% yield by treatment of receptor  6 2+ ·2PF 6 –  with 10  equivalents of tris(tetra but yl ammonium)hydrogen pyrophosphate in acetonitrile at room temperature for 24 h.

The analysis of the obtained association constant values in CH 3 CN, by UV-Vis and fluorescence, and in CD 3 CN/MeOD (8:2) by  1 H NMR reveal that the halogen bonding bis-imidazolium receptor  6 2+ ·2PF 6 –  exhibits higher binding affinity for the tested anions  than the hydrogen bonding receptor  7 2+ ·2PF 6 –  in the more competitive solvent mixture CD 3 CN/MeOD (8:2), which is in agreement  with the most of the studies reported to date on halogen bonding receptors in very competitive solvent mixtures . On the contrary,  the hydrogen receptor  7 2+ ·2PF 6 –  shows higher binding affinity for anions than the halogen bonding receptor  6 2+ ·2PF 6 –  in the less  competitive and aprotic CH 3 CN solvent . Note that, as expected, the presence of methanol always decreases the association con- stants, moderately in the 1:1 complexes (about 10 2  times) and strongly in the 1:2 complexes (about 10 7  times).

Upfield shifts of Δ δ  \~ -3 ppm have been  reported for hydrogen pyrophosphate trianion with other receptors.  

When the reaction course between the receptor  6 2+ ·2PF 6 –  and 6 equivalents of  HP 2 O 7 3–  anions was followed by  31 P NMR spec- troscopy, it was found that the final spectrum displayed a sharp signal at -8.12 ppm due to a complexed H 2 PO 4 –  anion, along with a  doublet centered at -9.16 ppm attributable to the tetraphosphate a small signal at -15.7 ppm due to the cy clo diphosphate anion.   Support for the assignment of the signal at -8.12 ppm to a complexed H 2 PO 4 –  anion is the fact that similar signal in shape and posi- tion is found when 1 equivalent of this anion is added to a solution of the bisimidazolone  9  in CD 3 CN/CD 3 OD (9:1, v/v). The overlay of the bright field and the fluorescence images obtained by confocal microscopy at different channels ( λ  = 450 ± 10  nm,  λ  = 510 ± 10 nm and  λ  = 610 ± 10 nm) when excited at  λ  = 405 nm (Figure 9) shows that the fluorescence was located in the  intracellular area indicating a good cell membrane permeability and the capability of the sensor  6 2+ ·2PF 6 –  to be used as imaging  agent in living cells in a wide range of wavelengths emission.

CONCLUSION  

The synthesis of novel two-armed charge-assisted anion ditopic receptors   has been reported. The central 2,7-disubstituted  naphthalene core  incorporates two end-caped photoactive anthracene rings as fluorescent signaling units into their  side arms,   with  central 2-bromo-  (XB) or hydrogen (HB) imidazolium bidentate receptors.  Several anion-binding experiments have been car- ried out in order to make a comparative study of sensing capabilities of the XB receptor  versus  the HB one. Evaluation of the sens- ing properties by fluorescence reveals important differences in the sensing behavior between the halogenated receptor  6 2+ ·2PF 6 –  and  the hydrogen receptor  7 2+ ·2PF 6 – . In the presence of SO 4 2–  or F –  anions a remarkable decreasing of the monomer emission bands of  the receptor  6 2+ ·2PF 6 – were observed, whereas XB receptor acts a selective fluorescent molecular sensor for H 2 PO 4 –  anions being the  unique anion which promotes a progressive increasing of a new broad and structureless emission band assigned to the anthracene  excimer emission band in the receptor  6 2+ ·2PF 6 – . In addition, this receptor behaves as a chemodosimeter towards HP 2 O 7 3–  anion,  through its transformation into the corresponding bisimidazolone after debromination by the action of the basic anion. In contrast to  this rich fluorescent behavior of the XB donor receptor, addition of the mentioned anions to the HB donor receptor only promoted  quenching of the monomer emission bands.  1 H NMR titration experiments were performed in order to get additional information  about the binding mode between the receptors  6 2+ ·2PF 6 –   and  7 2+ ·2PF 6 – . Halogen- and hydrogen bond donor receptors have similar  1 H NMR spectra the only exception is the presence of an imidazolium proton on the hydrogen bond donor receptor. Both receptors  show the same set of signals, and the most interesting feature observed during the  1 H-NMR titration experiments was the different  behavior of receptors  6 2+ ·2PF 6 –   and  7 2+ ·2PF 6 –  after addition of HP 2 O 7 3–  anions. The analysis of the obtained association constant  values reveals that  in the competitive solvent mixture  CD 3 CN/MeOD (8:2)  the halogen bonding complexes  display higher val- ues  than those found for the hydrogen-bonding counterpart.  On contrary, in the less competitive and aprotic CH 3 CN solvent the  hydrogen receptor  7 2+ ·2PF 6 –  shows higher binding affinity for anions than  the halogen bonding  receptor.  

The reported experiments using HeLa cells concerning the XB receptor  6 2+ ·2PF 6 –  proved that this sensor has a good cell mem- brane permeability being capable to be used as imaging agent in living cells in a wide range of wavelengths emission; on the other  hand, for the HB receptor  7 2+ ·2PF 6 – , the observed fluorescence was much smaller.  

Taking into account that the capability of a XB or HB to act as a binding site strongly depends on several factors, the assessment  that the integration of a halogen atom into the anion receptor at the expense of one hydrogen-bonding receptor increases the anion  recognition affinity cannot be considered as a general rule.  

On these very flexible structures, localization of minima is a difficult task. The results of the theoretical calculations are not en- tirely satisfying although the main tendencies of the binding energies are reproduced. The most important result is the finding that  open and closed forms exist for the  6 2+ ·(H 2 PO 4 – ) 2   complex, justifying the existence of an excimer emission
