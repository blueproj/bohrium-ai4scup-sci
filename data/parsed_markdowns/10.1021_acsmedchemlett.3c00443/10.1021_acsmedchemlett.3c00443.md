ABSTRACT:  Trans glut a minas es, classified explicitly as “protein-glutamine: amine  γ -glutamyl transferases”, catalyze the formation of isopeptide bonds between the  γ -amino group of lysine and the  γ -glutamyl group of glutamine, leading to the release of ammonia. These enzymes play pivotal roles in diverse medical conditions such as cardiovascular, autoimmune, neuro degenerative, and dermatological diseases, as well as in wound healing and inflammatory diseases. This Patent Highlight presents novel inhibitors of trans glut a minas es, specifically trans glut a minas e 2 (TG2), and their synthetic processes. In addition, these inhibitors are effective treatments for diseases linked to TG2, such as celiac disease and fibrotic disorders. Therefore, these inhibitors can be active agents in pharmaceutical treatments.

Summary.  Trans glut a minas es belong to the transferase category and are technically termed “protein-glutamine: amine γ -glutamyl transferases”. These enzymes facilitate a link between the  γ -amino group of lysine and the  γ -glutamyl group of glutamine, forming an isopeptide bond with a concomitant release of ammonia. Glutamine may undergo deamidation under certain conditions or without suitable amines, producing glutamic acid.  

Trans glut a minas es significantly contribute to various ther- apeutic domains, including cardiovascular disorders (like thrombosis and atherosclerosis), autoimmune disorders (like celiac disease, dermatitis herpetiformis, and gluten ataxia), neuro degenerative diseases (such as Alzheimer’s, Parkinson’s, and Huntington’s), and dermatological conditions (like ichthyosis, psoriasis, and acne), as well as wound healing and inflammatory disorders (such as tissue fibrosis).  

Of particular note is celiac disease, a gluten intolerance disorder. The disease manifests as chronic inflammation in the mucosa of the small intestine, causing progressive destruction of the intestinal epithelium when susceptible individuals are exposed to gluten. This condition leads to impaired nutrient absorption and symptoms like weight loss, anemia, diarrhea, nausea, vomiting, reduced appetite, and fatigue. This situation warrants the development of drugs for celiac disease and other disorders related to tissue trans glut a minas e (trans glut a minas e 2, TG2, tTG). Tissue trans glut a minas e plays a critical role in the pathogenesis, catalyzing the deamidation of gluten/gliadin in the small intestinal mucosa, thus triggering an inflammatory response. Consequently, inhibitors of tissue trans glut a minas e could be potent therapeutic agents.  

Furthermore, TG2 is a multifaceted protein involved in tumorigenesis, influencing its progression or suppression depending on its intracellular location and conformational structure. Acyclic retinoid (ACR), a vitamin A derivative, has been found to target and inhibit TG2 in hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC) cells, suppressing stemness-related genes and selective cell death. This effect is also observed in Alzheimer’s disease (AD), where TG2 contributes to synaptic dysfunctions, increasing levels of harmful amyloid beta peptide (A β ) aggregates. Moreover, TG2 promotes an aggressive cancer phenotype by stimulating CD44v6 activity and forming a TG2/ CD44v6/ERK1/2 complex. Interestingly, cystamine, a TG2 inhibitor, has shown promise in alleviating cardiac fibrosis.  

In addition, TG2 is associated with androgen insensitivity in prostate cancer (PCa) cells, as its silencing results in the upregulation of androgen receptor (AR) transcription, reversing the migratory potential of PCa cells and reducing the level of mucin-1 (MUC1) RNA transcript. These studies suggest a profound influence of TG2 across a range of pathological conditions, reaffirming the potential of TG2 inhibitors as therapeutic targets.  

Fibrotic disorders, characterized by the accumulation of cross- linked extracellular matrix proteins, form another critical group that could benefit from tissue trans glut a minas e inhibitors. Prominent fibrotic conditions include diabetic nephropathy, cystic fibrosis, idiopathic pulmonary fibrosis, and kidney and liver fibrosis.  

This patent shows novel compounds, potentially reversible or irreversible inhibitors of trans glut a minas es, particularly trans- glutaminase 2, and exploration of their potential uses.
