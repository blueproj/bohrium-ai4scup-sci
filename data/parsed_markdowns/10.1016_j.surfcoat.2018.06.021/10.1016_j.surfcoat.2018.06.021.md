# 1. Introduction  

There is a pressing demand to raise operating temperatures of modern gas turbines in  order to increase their efficiency. As the projected temperatures are well above the melting points  of the nickel based superalloys used as gas turbine materials, it would have detrimental effects on  the chemical, thermal and mechanical stabilities of these alloys. TBCs are commonly produced by air plasma spray (APS) and  electron-beam physical vapour deposition (EB-PVD) techniques. TBCs produced by EB-PVD  technique consist of a columnar structure, which makes them highly strain tolerant and gives  them an increased thermal cycling life compared to the APS TBCs. However, EB-PVD is  expensive and has been reported to produce denser TBCs with high thermal conductivity  compared to APS TBCs [1, 9, 10].
