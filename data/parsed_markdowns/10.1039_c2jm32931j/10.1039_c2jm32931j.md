# Design, synthesis and photovoltaic properties of a new D– p –A polymer with extended  p -bridge units †  

Xia Guo,   Maojie Zhang,   Lijun Huo, \* a   Feng Xu,   Yue Wu ab   and Jianhui Hou \* a  

Received 9th May 2012, Accepted 19th August 2012 DOI: 10.1039/c2jm32931j  

New low band gap copolymers  PBDTT–DTTBT  and  PBDTT–DTBT  using BDT-T and BT as donor and acceptor, with different  p -bridge units thieno[3,2- b ]thiophene and thiophene, respectively, were synthesized. The absorption spectra, electronic energy levels, and photovoltaic properties of the polymers were characterized.  PBDTT–DTTBT  and  PBDTT–DTBT  ﬁlms show broad absorption in the visible range with an absorption edge at over 751 nm, and possess relatively low-lying HOMO levels at   5.11 eV and 5.15 eV. A high hole mobility of 1.97    10  3   cm 2   V  1   s  1   was recorded for PBDTT–DTTBT , that is two orders of magnitude higher than  PBDTT–DTBT  (1.58    10  5   cm 2   V  1 s  1 ), which is ascribed to the application of thieno[3,2- b ]thiophene as  p -bridge units. The PCE of the PSC device based on  PBDTT–DTTBT /PC 70 BM (1 : 1, w/w) reached 6.03% with a  J sc  of 12.46 mA cm  2 , a  V oc  of 0.78 V and a FF of 0.62, while  PBDTT–DTBT /PC 70 BM (1 : 1, w/w) only has a PCE of

 2.34% with a  V oc  of 0.82 V, a  J sc  of 5.78 mA cm  2   and a FF of 0.495 under the illumination of AM

 1.5 G, 100 mW cm  2 . These results indicate that  PBDTT–DTTBT  is a promising photovoltaic material. # Introduction  

Bulk heterojunction (BHJ) polymer solar cells (PSCs) have been extensively investigated in both scientiﬁc research and industrial applications during the last decade, because of their potential applications in making large area, light weight, ﬂexible photo- voltaic panels through low-cost solution-processable tech-   niques. Conjugated polymers and fullerene derivatives are commonly used as electron donors and electron acceptor mate-   rials, respectively, in PSCs with a BHJ structure. As it is well known, an ideal polymer electron donor material should possess a broad absorption band with a high extinction coefﬁcient to absorb more sunlight, good mobility to facilitate charge trans- port, suitable molecular energy levels matching those of the fullerene acceptor, and appropriate compatibility with the fullerene acceptor to form a nanoscale bicontinuous inter- penetrating network.  

The design and synthesis of low band gap (LBG) polymers have been proven to be one of the successful strategies to improve the absorption of the conjugated polymers, because a broad absorption band can be realized by molecular structure design. Therefore, high short circuit density values ( J sc ) can be obtained in PSCs based on LBG polymers. One of the broadly adapted strategies to design LBG polymers is to alternate the electron- rich (donor) and the electron-deﬁcient (acceptor) units on the   backbones of conjugated polymers. Conjugated polymers with alternating D– p –A (wherein  p  represents a conjugated bridge) architecture are particularly desirable due to the facile tunability of their electronic structures. In order to get D– p –A polymers for efﬁcient PSCs, much effort has been devoted to the design and   synthesis of new donor and acceptor building blocks. According to the reported work, thiophene or alkylthiophene units are mainly used as  p -bridges in D– p –A polymers with efﬁcient photovoltaic properties. Although the  p -bridge unit has great inﬂuence on the photovoltaic properties of D– p –A polymers,   there are only a few studies which focus on it. LBG polymers based on benzo[1,2- b :4,5- b ’]dithiophene (BDT) and bis(thio- phen-2-yl)-benzothiadiazole (DTBT) have been designed, synthesized and applied in PSCs. A PCE over 5% has been achieved based on some of these PBDTDTBT-based poly-   mers. Different strategies have been employed to improve the photovoltaic properties of this type of polymers. For example, Hou  et al.  reported a PBDTDTBT polymer with a deeper HOMO level; 7   Huang  et al.  used a fused BT named naphtho[1,2-   c :5,6- c ]bis[1,2,5] thiadiazole (NT) to replace the BT unit, You et al.  introduced ﬂuorine atoms to BT units.  

In this work, we designed and synthesized a new D– p –A polymer in which alkylthiophene-substituted benzo[1,2- b :4,5- b ’] dithiophene (BDT-T), b enzo thi a diaz ole (BT) and thieno[3,2- b ] thiophene (TT) are used as donor, acceptor and  p -bridge units, respectively. As shown in Scheme 1, the TT unit was used as the p -bridge instead of thiophene. As it is known, TT has a rigid and coplanar fused ring, which ensures a highly delocalized  p -elec- tron system and strong intermolecular  p – p  stacking, and thus it has attracted much interest for the construction of high perfor- mance organic/polymer semiconductors for applications in   optoelectronic devices. Some conjugated polymers with TT units on their backbones exhibit interesting properties in opto- electronic devices. For example, the ﬁeld-effect transistor (FET) hole mobility of a conjugated polymer containing a TT unit reached as high as 1.95 cm 2   V  1   s  1 ,   and in an earlier report, when the thiophene bridge was replaced by thieno[3,2- b ]thio- phene, the FET hole mobility can be improved by one or two orders of magnitude,   from 8.6    10  5   cm 2   V  1   s  1   to 3.6   10  3   cm 2   V  1   s  1   or from 2.2    10  5   cm 2   V  1   s  1   to 1.5    10  cm 2   V  1   s  1 , and the D–A copolymer PSCs based on TT as   donor unit exhibited a high PCE over 5%. Based on the reported work, we believe that the hole mobility and thus the photovoltaic properties of the polymer will be improved after replacing the thiophene unit by the TT unit. However, fused thiophene oligomers or polymers usually show poor solubility at room temperature. In order to overcome this disadvantage,  n - C 11 H 23  groups were introduced onto the 3-positions of TT, and more bulky groups, hexyldecyl, were employed as substituents on the BDT units. Consequently,  PBDTT–DTTBT , the new D– p –A copolymer, was designed on account of the above points. As comparison, we also synthesized another new polymer  PBDTT– DTBT  (shown in Scheme 1), in which the  p -bridge unit is thio- phene. Consequently, compared with the analogous BT-based polymer, the TTBT-based polymer exhibited higher mobility, higher  J sc  and FF, and a much improved PSC performance, with a PCE of 6%.

# UV-visible absorption and X-ray diffraction  

The ultraviolet-visible (UV-vis) absorption spectra provide information on the electronic structures of the conjugated polymers. Fig. 2 shows the UV-vis absorption spectra of the polymers in chloroform solution and ﬁlms casted from chloro- form solution and  o -DCB solution on quartz substrate.  PBDTT– DTTBT  and  PBDTT–DTBT  exhibit similar optical band gaps, which are 1.65 eV and 1.63 eV, respectively, indicating that the introduction of the extended  p -bridge, as shown in this work, has little inﬂuence on the optical band gap. As shown in Fig. 2, PBDTT–DTBT  shows an absorption peak ( l max ) at 555 nm in chloroform solution and an absorption peak ( l max ) at 619 nm in solid ﬁlm casted from chloroform, and the absorption spectra of the ﬁlms casted from chloroform and  o -DCB solution are almost identical. The absorption spectrum of  PBDTT–DTTBT  in chloroform exhibits a peak at 607 nm, which is   60 nm red- shifted compared to that of  PBDTT–DTBT , indicating that in solution state, the  p -electrons of  PBDTT–DTTBT  can be delo- calized better than in those of  PBDTT–DTBT . Very interest- ingly, for  PBDTT–DTTBT , the ﬁlm casted from chloroform solution displays a different absorption spectrum compared to the ﬁlm casted from  o -DCB solution; the absorption spectrum of the ﬁlm casted from  o -DCB exhibits a strong shoulder peak at 680 nm. According to the reported work, this kind of shoulder peak is observed in the conjugated polymers due to strong   intermolecular  p – p  stacking properties. Since  o -DCB has a much higher boiling point than chloroform, the slower drying speed of the  o -DCB solution gives longer time for the polymer to form a more ordered structure. Compared to the  PBDTT–DTBT ﬁlm processed by  o -DCB, the absorption shoulder peak of the PBDTT–DTTBT  ﬁlm indicates that stronger or/and ordered intermolecular interactions might be formed in the  PBDTT– DTTBT  ﬁlm due to the introduction of the extended  p -bridge units.

# Photovoltaic properties  

Fig. 5 shows the current density–voltage ( J – V ) characteristics of the devices with different D/A (polymer/PC 70 BM, w/w) ratios of PBDTT–DTTBT  and  PBDTT–DTBT  under the illumination of AM 1.5 G, 100 mW cm  2 . Table 1 lists the photovoltaic performances of the PSCs fabricated under different conditions. The best power conversion efﬁciency (PCE) of  PBDTT–DTTBT was obtained from the PSC device with a D/A ratio of 1 : 1, with a  V oc  of 0.78 V, a  J sc  of 12.46 mA cm  2   and a FF of 0.620, leading to a PCE of 6.03%, and the PCEs are reproducible. For example, in more than 20 devices, the average PCE of the PSCs is over 5.9%, and 8 devices are listed (see Table S1 and Fig. S2 in ESI†). Under the same conditions, the  PBDTT–DTBT  based device gives a PCE of 2.34% with a  V oc  of 0.82 V, a  J sc  of 5.78 mA cm  2   and a FF of 0.495, which is quite similar with the reported work.  

The external quantum efﬁciencies (EQEs) and the absorption spectra of the PSC devices under different conditions are shown in Fig. 6a and b. Since the absorption at long wavelength range is mainly from the polymer, the PSC device with a D/A ratio of 1 : 1.5 shows the weakest absorbance at long wavelengths and also the lowest quantum efﬁciency (QE). Since the absorption in the range from 550 nm to 700 nm is mainly PC 70 BM, the PSC device with a D/A ratio of 1.5 : 1 shows weaker absorbance and thus a lower QE in this range; the PSC device with a D/A ratio of 1 : 1 exhibits more efﬁcient QE in the whole response range, and therefore the highest  J sc  was recorded under these conditions. It is worth pointing out that the discrepancy between the current density values calculated from the EQE and the  J – V  measure- ments is within 3%, indicating that the photovoltaic results are reliable in this work. In comparison with the reported work and the results obtained from this work,  PBDTT–DTTBT  exhibits much better photovoltaic performance than the PSCs based on PBDTT–DTBT , indicating that the extended conjugation of the backbone units, as demonstrated in this work, can be seen as a feasible method to improve the photovoltaic properties of conjugated polymers.

# Conclusion  

In conclusion, we designed and synthesized the new copolymers PBDTT–DTTBT  and  PBDTT–DTBT  using BDT-T and BT as donor and acceptor, with different  p -bridge units TT and thio- phene, respectively. The absorption spectra, electronic energy levels, and photovoltaic properties of the polymers were char- acterized.  PBDTT–DTTBT  and  PBDTT–DTBT  ﬁlms show broad absorption in the visible range with an absorption edge at over 751 nm, and possess relatively low-lying HOMO levels at  5.11 eV and 5.15 eV. A high hole mobility of 1.97    10  3   cm 2 V  1   s  1   was recorded for  PBDTT–DTTBT , that is two orders of magnitude higher than  PBDTT–DTBT  (1.58    10  5   cm 2   V  1 s  1 ), which is ascribed to the application of TT as  p -bridge units. The PCE of the PSC device based on  PBDTT–DTTBT /PC 70 BM

 (1 : 1, w/w) reached 6.03% with a  J sc  of 12.46 mA cm  2 , a  V oc  of

 0.78 V and a FF of 0.62, while  PBDTT–DTBT /PC 70 BM (1 : 1, w/ w) only has a PCE of 2.34% with a  V oc  of 0.82 V, a  J sc  of 5.78 mA cm  2   and a FF of 0.495 under the illumination of AM 1.5 G, 100 mW cm  2 . These results indicate that  PBDTT–DTTBT  is a promising photovoltaic material. Furthermore, it can be concluded that extending conjugation of the backbone units, as demonstrated in this work, has little inﬂuence on the molecular energy levels, but can be seen as a feasible strategy to improve the photovoltaic properties of D– p –A conjugated polymers by enhancing intermolecular interactions.

# Fabrication of polymer solar cells  

Polymer solar cell devices with the structure of ITO/PEDOT-PSS/ Polymers:PC 70 BM/Ca(10 nm)/Al(80 nm) were fabricated under the conditions as follows: patterned indium tin oxide (ITO)- coated glass with a sheet resistance of 10–15 Ohm per square was cleaned with a surfactant scrub and then underwent a wet-clean- ing process inside an ultrasonic bath, beginning with deionized water, followed by acetone and isopropanol. After oxygen plasma cleaning for 10 min, a 40 nm thick poly(3,4-ethylene- dioxythiophene):poly(styrene s ulf on ate) (PEDOT:PSS) (Bayer Baytron 4083) anode buffer layer was spin-cast onto the ITO substrate and then dried by baking in an oven at 150    C for 15 min. The active layer, with a thickness in the range of 80–90 nm, was then deposited on top of the PEDOT:PSS layer by casting from a 10 mg ml  1   o- DCB solution. Finally, 10 nm Ca and 80 nm Al layers were successively deposited in vacuum onto the active layer at a pressure of  ca.  4    10  4   Pa. The overlapping area between the 2 cathode and anode deﬁned a pixel size of 4 mm . Except for the deposition of the PEDOT:PSS layers, all the fabrication processes were carried out inside a controlled atmosphere of a nitrogen drybox containing less than 10 ppm oxygen and moisture.
