ABSTRACT:  Activation of AMP-activated protein kinase (AMPK) is proposed to alleviate hyperlipidemia. With cordycepin and N6-(2- hydroxyethyl) adenosine (HEA) as lead compounds, a series of adenosine-based derivatives were designed, synthesized, and evaluated on activation of AMPK. Finally, compound V1 was identified as a potent AMPK activator with the lipid-lowering effect. Molecular docking and circular dichroism indicated that V1 exerted its activity by binding to the  γ  subunit of AMPK. V1 markedly decreased the

serum low-density lipoprotein cholesterol levels in C57BL/6 mice, golden hamsters, and rhesus monkeys. V1 was selected as the clinical compound and concluded Phase 1 clinical trials. A single dose of V1 (2000 mg) increased AMPK activation in human erythrocytes after 5 and 12 h of treatment. RNA sequencing data suggested that V1 downregulated expression of genes involved in regulation of apoptotic process, lipid metabolism, endoplasmic reticulum stress, and inflammatory response in liver by activating AMPK.  

# 1. INTRODUCTION  

Hyperlipidemia is a lipid metabolism disorder characterized by high blood levels of total cholesterol (TC), triglycerides (TG), and low-density lipoprotein cholesterol (LDL-C). ,   There is a proportional relationship between elevated LDL-C and increased risk of cardiovascular atherosclerosis. ,   Statins are the most frequently prescribed lipid-regulating drugs. There is strong evidence that statins reduce the risk of cardiovascular disease by lowering LDL-C. ,   However, as statins have been widely used clinically, awareness of adverse reactions has increased, with almost 10% of patients intolerant to statins. , Furthermore, the wide application of statins has also prompted concerns about drug − drug interactions with other cardiovas- cular drugs, increasing the risk of statin-associated myopathy and hepatotoxicity, particularly in patients receiving multiple medications. − 11   To identify new nonstatin-based small molecules with LDL-C-lowering effects, we have focused on new targets that could regulate other lipoprotein classes closely linked to LDL-C.  

AMP-activated protein kinase (AMPK) is a cellular energy sensor and is central in the maintenance of energy homeo- stasis. − 15   AMPK acts as a switch for glucose and lipid metabolism in major metabolic organs (e.g., the liver, skeletal muscle, and the pancreas) as well as in adipose tissue. , Once activated, AMPK inhibits ATP-consuming processes, such as gluconeogenesis and fatty acid synthesis, and promotes ATP-generating processes, including fatty acid oxidation. , These metabolic effects should ameliorate the pathogenesis of obesity, type 2 diabetes, and other metabolic disorders.   As a master regulator of the lipogenic pathway, AMPK modulates lipid homeostasis through phosphorylation of acetyl-coenzyme A carboxylases (ACC), and 3-hydroxy-3-methylglutaryl coenzyme A reductase (HMGCR), thereby inhibiting lipid and cholesterol synthesis. − 24   In addition, activation of AMPK increases fatty acid oxidation through two distinct mechanisms. ,   Thus, AMPK is a promising target for treating lipid metabolism disorders.  

Since Abbott Laboratory developed an AMPK agonist (A- 769662) in 2005 via high-throughput screening and structure optimization, small-molecule direct AMPK agonists have become a focus of research for various pharmaceutical companies. − 29   However, there are currently no small- molecule direct AMPK agonists on the market, although some promising candidates have been tested in clinical or preclinical trials, for example, AICAR, PXL770, and O304, etc. ( Figure 1 ). − 33   The side effects and poor oral bioavailability have been a barrier to their development as a drug treatment. AICAR can activate various other AMP-dependent enzymes, such as fructose-1, 6-bisphosphatase, whereas A-769662 is limited by poor bioavailability.   Despite some promising

Cordyceps militaris  ( C. militaris ) has traditionally been used not only as a tonic food but also as a restorative drug in traditional Chinese medicine and exerts various beneficial pharmacological effects, including lowering blood pressure, protecting heart and brain tissue, and lowering serum lipids, and sedative, hypnotic, antibacterial, and antihypoxic effects. − 37   Cordycepin and N6-(2-hydroxyethyl) adenosine (HEA) ( Figure 1 ) are biologically active adenosine analogues extracted from  C. militaris .   Our previous study showed that cordycepin could prevent hyperlipidemia via direct activation of AMPK in hamsters fed with a high-fat diet.   However, low permeability, high hepatic first-pass effect, and the deamination by adenosine deaminase (ADA) to form a biologically inactive metabolite 3 ′ -deoxyinosine,   which limits the further application of cordycepin. Therefore, it is desirable to develop effective oral AMPK activators based on HEA and cordycepin for the treatment of hyperlipidemia.  

With cordycepin and HEA as lead compounds, a series of adenosine-based derivatives were designed, synthesized, and evaluated activation of AMPK. Herein, we report our discovery of adenosine-based AMPK activators, optimization to clinical candidate V1, and preclinical data that support its potential as a promising agent for the treatment of hyperlipidemia.  

# 2. RESULTS AND DISCUSSION  

2.1. Design and Synthesis of Adenosine-Based Derivatives.  In the current study, based on the core structure of cordycepin and HEA, five series of adenosine analogs were synthesized, and a structure − activity relationship (SAR) analysis was conducted to develop an AMPK activator with improved efficacy.  

First, the effect of ribose on the activity was investigated, and derivatives I1 − I6 were designed by removing ribose or replacing ribose with bioisosteres, namely, alkyl and benzyl groups. Next, the terminal amino group at the 6-position of the purine ring was modified by introducing different alkoxyl or thiol groups to provide derivatives II1 − II4. Then, derivatives III1 − III22 were obtained by introducing various alkyl or aromatic amino groups at the 6-position of the purine ring.  

Derivatives IV1 − IV8 were designed by introducing acyl groups at the 5 ′ -hydroxyl group of ribose. Finally, all the hydroxyl groups of ribose were acetylated to provide derivatives V1 − V6, in order to investigate the improved hydrophobic effect of compounds on AMPK activation.

2.3. In Vivo and Vitro Evaluation of Potency of Derivatives for Activating AMPK and Reducing the Lipid Content.  EC 50  value of derivatives III14, III20, III21, III22, and V1 on AMPK activation were also determined. Derivatives III14, III20, III21, III22, and V1 exhibited lower EC 50  values than AMP (70.3  μ M). III20 and III22 were the most potent, with EC 50  values of 0.016 and 0.025  μ M, respectively ( Figure S1A ). Next, we evaluated the cellular lipid content of HepG2 cells incubated with these five derivatives at a concentration of 10  μ M, using Oil Red O (ORO) staining. Out of the five compounds, V1 significantly decreased intracellular lipid content (64% vs model,  Figure S1B ), which was at least partly due to AMPK activation. The binding affinity of V1 with AMPK was determined by surface plasmon resonance (SPR), as shown in  Figure S2A . V1 had a clear concentration-dependent response to AMPK, with a calculated K  d  of 17.9  μ Μ  ( Figure S2B ).  

To further study the lipid-lowering effects, we subsequently assessed in vivo efficacies of derivatives in series III, IV, and V on model of high-fat diet Kunming mice. Mice were administered orally with test compounds (50 mg/kg) once daily for consecutive 7 days. Then, serum total cholesterols were collected and detected to evaluate the test compound activities. As illustrated in  Table 5 , derivatives III20 and V1 exhibited more significant ability to reduce serum TC compared with simvastatin or probucol. Unexpectedly, the mice treated with III20 were skinny. Collectively, these data suggest that  V1  is a potent oral AMPK activator and regulates the lipid metabolism in HepG2 cells and High-fat diet mice.  

2.4. Determination of the Interaction of V1 and AMPK by Circular Dichroism.  To verify the interaction of V1 with AMPK, we used circular dichroism (CD), which has been widely used to examine the interaction between ligands and proteins by measuring the conformational change during binding. Detailed information, such as the dissociation constant, was obtained by analyzing the signal from the binding process. An AMPK truncation protein (AMPKr) was applied in this section, with AMP as the control ligand.  

The band positions and the intensities for different V1/ AMPKr ratios obtained by CD analysis are shown in  Figure 2 A. To collect a suitable amount of data, AMPK was titrated with V1 between V1/AMPKr ratios of 0.1 and 6.8. AMPKr induced two negative CD bands at 222 and 208 nm, which is typical of the  α -helix structure. The increase in the V1/AMPKr ratio induced a decrease in band intensity at all far-UV wavelengths without altering the position of the corresponding absorption band, indicating that this compound induced a slight change in the conformation of AMPKr. The association constant of V1 was calculated by using the magnitude of the negative Cotton effect at 222 nm. Applying nonlinear regression analysis, a  K D  value of 0.21  μ M was obtained at pH 7.4 (T = 25  ° C). In contrast, an approximate  K D  value of 0.15  μ M was determined for AMP ( Figure 2 B).

2.6. Lipid Lowering Effect of V1 in Rodent Models of Hyperlipidemia.  Encouraged by the former results, we examined derivative V1 in vivo in C57BL/6J mice ( Figure 4 A). Probucol was previously applied to reduce serum LDL-C levels in patients with hypercholesterolemia and was frequently chosen as a positive drug in the development of metabolic disease drugs.   A high-fat diet (HFD) markedly elevated serum LDL-C in the mice ( Figure 4 B). Compared with the untreated HFD control, derivative V1 decreased the LDL-C level in a dose-dependent manner by 22.2 ( P  < 0.05) and 32.6% ( P  < 0.01) at doses of 20 and 40 (mg/kg)/day, respectively. Obviously, V1 (40 mg/kg) was able to regulate LDL-C as evidenced by the similar LDL-C level between the V1-treated and Probucol-treated group. These results indicate that derivative V1 caused a significant decrease in LDL-C in mice.  

Golden hamsters have been widely used in studies of lipid metabolism disorders because of the similarity of their lipid metabolism to humans. Simvastatin reduces the amount of LDL cholesterol and triglycerides by inhibiting HMG-CoA reductase and is commonly used in the clinical treatments of hypercholesterolemia and in preclinical research.   After 4 weeks of HFD feeding, TC, TG, and LDL-C in HFD hamsters were significantly increased compared with normal hamsters. Simvastatin (6 mg/kg) decreased LDL-C, TC, and TG of hamsters by 27.5, 24.8, and 52.8%. Derivative V1 decreased the serum levels of LDL-C, TC, and TG in a dose-dependent manner at doses of 50 to 200 mg/kg ( Figure 4 C). Compared with the untreated HFD control, derivative V1 reduced LDL-C by 10.5% at 50 mg/kg, by 36.2% at 100 mg/kg, and by 42.0% at 200 mg/kg. The administration of derivative V1 had no effect on HFD-induced body weight gain and food uptake ( Figure S3 ), suggesting that hyperlipidemia was not amelio- rated by decreasing food consumption. Obviously, 200 mg/kg of derivative V1 reduced the LDL-C level as significantly as Simvastatin, while the 200 mg/kg dose exhibited a higher lipid- lowering effect.

2.7. Lipid-Lowering Effect of V1 in Hyperlipidemic Rhesus Monkeys.  To confirm the above results, a 35-day efficacy study was performed on six rhesus monkeys ( Figure 4 A). After a 7-day baseline assessment, three monkeys received oral derivative V1 at a dose of 35 mg/kg for 35 days, followed by a 14-day washout period. The three monkeys in the control group received the same volume of vehicle. The time course of the LDL-C level is shown in  Figure 5 E. During the experimental period, no significant changes in LDL-C levels were detected in the control group. In contrast, the LDL-C level in the V1-treated monkeys ranged from 1.25 to 2.01 mmol/L and decreased by 4.83 − 23.69% compared with the baseline level. The serum LDL-C concentration in V1-treated monkeys was decreased after 7 days of treatment, reduced by 23.69% ( P  < 0.05) after 21 days, and then remained steady during the subsequent period. At the completion of the efficacy study, an 11.93% reduction in the blood LDL-C level was observed in the V1-treated group ( Figure 4 F). The reduced LDL-C level remained during the washout period, indicating the lasting effect of V1 even after the cessation of medication.  

These results demonstrate the hypolipidemic effects of derivative V1 in monkeys.  

2.8. Human Clinical Evaluations of the Phosphor- ylation of AMPK Induced by Derivative V1.  In the first-in- human study, the phosphorylation of AMPK after single doses of V1 was tested with a randomized, placebo-controlled, double-blind, parallel-group design. Ten sequential cohorts of healthy volunteers received compound V1 once at a single dose of 2000 mg. The levels of AMPK phosphorylation in erythrocytes were measured by ELISA after 0 to 24 h of treatment. The phosphorylation of AMPK was significantly increased after 5 and 12 h of treatment ( Figure 4 G). V1 (2000 mg) was given twice daily to 12 healthy volunteers for 7 days. Multiple doses of V1 resulted in a decrease in serum LDL-C and TC ( Figure 4 H,I). Overall, we speculate that V1 treatment regulates human blood lipids by activating AMPK in vivo.  

2.9. Derivative V1 Regulates Lipid-Related Metabo- lism Pathway by Activating AMPK in the Liver.  To gain insights into the involved genes in the beneficial effects of V1- induced AMPK activation on lipid metabolism in vivo, we performed a genome-wide expression analysis in the livers of male wildtype (Model vs V1) and AMPK α LS − / − (Model vs V1) diet-induced obese (DIO) mice. RNA sequencing of liver mRNA in wildtype and AMPK α LS − / − mice fed with a high-fat diet for 13 weeks, followed by orally given vehicle or 200 mg/ kg V1 for 4 weeks. Gene Ontogeny (GO) analysis was performed to identify differentially expressed pathways ( n  = 3). Figure 5 A,B demonstrates the GO analysis of differentially expressed genes (DEGs) involved in the regulation of the apoptotic process, lipid metabolism, endoplasmic reticulum stress, and immune response (inflammatory). There are 20, 12, 10, and 9 DEGs identified in these pathways, respectively, when comparing wildtype DIO mice administrated vehicle or 200 mg/kg V1 for 4 weeks. However, there are only 4, 6, 1, and 4 DEGs, respectively, when comparing AMPK α LS − / − DIO mice administrated vehicle or 200 mg/kg V1 for 4 weeks. Notably, the levels of lipogenic genes such as mono ac yl glycerol O-acyltransferase 1 (Mogat1), acetoacetyl-CoA synthetase

Leptin receptor (Lepr), deficiency which can lead to obesity and reduced insulin sensitivity, was nevertheless increased ( Figure 5 C). Additionally, we noted a marked downregulation of pro-inflammatory genes such as chemokine ligand 2 (Ccl2), C − C chemokine receptor type 2 (Ccr2), and platelet-activating factor receptor (Ptafr), endoplasmic reticulum stress-induced genes such as heat shock 70 kDa protein 5 (Hspa5), E3 ubiquitin-protein ligase synoviolin (Syvn1), DnaJ homologue subfamily B member 9 (Dnajb9) and stromal cell-derived factor 2 like 1 (Sdf2l1), genes involved in the apoptotic process such as connective tissue growth factor (Ctgf) and cysteine rich angiogenic inducer 61 (Cyr61) in 200 mg/kg V1-treated wildtype DIO mice, while hepatic protection genes such as growth arrest and DNA damage-inducible  α  (Gadd45a) and growth arrest and DNA damage inducible  β  (Gadd45b) were correspondingly upregulated ( Figure 5 D − F). 2.11. Caco-2 Permeability Assay of Derivative V1.  We used Caco-2 monolayer cells to investigate the absorption and permeability properties ( Table S2 ). The apparent absorption permeability coefficients (Papp) of V-1 were 8.6  ×  10 − 6   cm/s (2  μ M) and 8.0  ×  10 − 6   cm/s (21  μ M), respectively, which were both in the range of 1 − 10  ×  10 − 6   cm/s, suggesting that V-1 was at a moderate absorption level.   At the same time, we determined the efflux rates (ER) of V-1, which were 2.1 (2 μ M) and 1.9 (21  μ M), respectively, indicating some efflux effect of V-1 in Caco-2 cells.  

2.12. Stability Assay of Derivative V-1.  The IV and V subfamilies contain at least one ester bond, which may be susceptible to metabolic degradation. We supplemented the stability studies of V-1 (10  μ g/mL) in Caco-2 single-layer cells, human liver microsomal, and plasma. As shown in  Figure S4A , V-1 decreases to 77 after 1 h, 63 after 2 h, and 52% after 3 h in Caco-2. The results of the plasma stability assay ( Figure S4B ) showed that V-1 was slightly less stable in human plasma (about 70 − 80% after 30 min). Microsomal stability experi- ments ( Table S3 ) indicated that derivative V-1 (1  μ M) was rapidly metabolized (>80% after 5 min) in human liver microsomes, hepatic clearance (CL hepatic ) was 19.93 mL/min/ kg (>70% hepatic blood flow). In addition, the trend of V-1 metabolism was almost the same in the presence/absence of NADPH in the incubation system, indicating that the hepatic metabolism of V-1 is not dependent on CYPs. Nonetheless, the drug effect of V-1 was evident.  

2.13. Safety Evaluation of Derivative V1 In Vivo. Blockade of the human ether-a-go-go-related gene (hERG) channel is a major obstacle encountered in drug development. Derivative V1 displayed low hERG inhibition, with an IC of >30  μ M ( Table S4 ), indicating that it had low cardiotoxicity potential.  

An acute toxicity test was performed on golden hamsters and rhesus monkeys. Derivative V1 was orally administered in a single-dose experiment at 20 g/kg. Subsequently, the hamsters were closely monitored for 14 days. All hamsters survived during the 14-day treatment without an intense acute toxic response, indicating the acute oral LD50 of V1 was larger than 20 g/kg.  

Derivative V1 was also orally administered to rhesus monkeys at doses of 667, 1500, and 3375 mg/kg. It was well tolerated up to a dose of 3375 mg/kg with only pale bowel movements, from which the monkeys recovered on day 2. All monkeys survived and had no intense acute toxic response during the 14-day treatment. Histological observations showed that derivative V1 had no apparent toxicity in the liver, kidneys, heart, lungs, brain, spleen, and gastrointestinal tract of the monkeys, indicating that V1 was safe at a dose of 3375 mg/kg or lower by intragastric administration. Overall, these results together suggest that V1 is expected to be a safe candidate for lowering serum lipid levels.  

# 3. CONCLUSIONS  

We performed a comprehensive SAR exploration and bio- logical evaluation of adenosine-like AMPK activators. The SAR studies revealed that the ribose moiety and the amino groups were crucial to stimulating lipid metabolism. Derivative V1 was identified and it displayed a serum lipid-lowering effect in hyperlipidemic mice, hamsters, and monkeys. The alteration of the level of AMPK phosphorylation in erythrocytes was also observed in a clinical trial. We also proved that derivative V1 could stimulate the pathway of triglyceride and cholesterol synthesis, endoplasmic reticulum stress, immune response (inflammatory), and apoptotic process in the liver by activating AMPK. The pharmacokinetic properties and toxicity profile showed that compound V1 is a promising agent for treating hyperlipidemia. Next, a series of studies will be performed to identify the precise molecular determinants responsible for AMPK activation and reveal the lipid-lowering mechanism of derivative V1. High blood cholesterol level is one of the main causes of various metabolic syndromes and induces other multiple systemic complications. AMPK activation is able to decrease de novo lipogenesis, promote fatty acid oxidation, and regulate inflammation. As a derivative of cordycepin and HEA and an activator of AMPK, we reasonably speculate that V1 also exhibits a wide range of bioactivities, and can be an efficient therapeutic tool for chronic metabolic disorders, such as obesity, atherosclerosis, nonalcoholic steatosis hepatitis, type II diabetes. V1 elicits hypolipidemic and hypoglycemic activity contributing to the regulation of lipid and glucose metabolism. This work paves the way for the development of a class of direct AMPK activators, which provides a new strategy for anti hyper lipid emi c drugs.

4.6. AMPK Activity Assay.  The total AMPK activity was measured by using a synthetic SAMS peptide substrate and [ γ - 32 P] ATP, as described previously. Briefly, active AMPK ( α 1 β 1 γ 1; 20 ng) was mixed with SAMS peptide (1.2 mM) and MgCl 2  (500 mM). The test compound (10  μ M), ATP (20 mM), and [ γ - 32 P] ATP were added, and the mixture was incubated for 20 min at 30  ° C. The mixture (15  μ L) was spotted onto P81 paper, which was immersed in 1% phosphoric acid. The paper was washed twice with 1% phosphoric acid and once with acetone for 5 min. Washed filters were dried, and the samples were read using a scintillation counter (Wallac Wizard 1470, PerkinElmer).  

The activation activity of test compounds was determined by the reading of scintillation (CPM value) and represented by REA (Relative Enzyme Activity, compared to vehicle), which were calculated by the formula: [(CPM SAMS  − CPM control )  ×  30] /(15  × Incubation time  ×  the volume of AMPK  ×  relative activity of [ γ - 32 P]ATP); 30: the total volume of the reaction system; 15: the volume of reaction mixture spotted onto P81 paper. The relative activity of [ γ - 32 P]ATP was calculated by the formula: (50/15)  ×  (5/ 2)  × Χ /1500;  X  is the CPM reading of the scintillation counter; 50 is the total volume of the reaction system; 15 is the volume of reaction mixture spotted onto P81 paper; 1500 is the picomole number of ATP in the reaction system.  

4.7. Measurement of Lipid Accumulation in HepG2 Cells. Human hepatoma HepG2 cells were maintained in Dulbecco’s modified Eagle’s medium (DMEM) supplemented with 10% (v/v) fetal bovine serum (FBS) at 37  ° C in a 5% CO 2  incubator. A total of 4 ×  10 4   HepG2 cells were seeded per well in white-walled 96-well plates. After 24 h of incubation, the cells were deprived of FBS for 12 h and then incubated for 24 h with the test compounds (10  μ M) in serum-free DMEM.  

HepG2 cells seeded in a 96-well plate were treated with 0.25 mM mixed fatty acids (oleic acid/palmitic acid = 1:1) and various test compounds for 48 h. ORO was dissolved in isopropanol and left overnight. Prior to staining, the ORO solution was filtered through a 0.22  μ m syringe filter. Next, cells were fixed with formalin, and ORO staining was performed. Cells were treated with isopropanol and lipid accumulation was measured using a microplate reader (Epoch, BioTek) that recorded the absorbance at 500 nm. The cell viability results of Cordycepin and V1 are shown in  Table S5 .  

4.8. hERG Testing.  The hERG-expressing HEK293 cell line was grown in a complete DMEM medium supplemented with 10% (v/v) FBS and 0.8 mg/mL G418 in a humidified incubator with an atmosphere of 5% CO 2  at 37  ° C. When the cells were 80% confluent, the culture media was removed and the cells were washed with phosphate-buffered saline (PBS), followed by dissociation with TrypLE Express reagent in a 37  ° C incubator for 0.5 min. Cells were resuspended gently by replacing the reagent with a preheated complete DMEM medium and pipetting them up and down several times. Finally, the cells were harvested by centrifuging at 1000 rpm, adjusted to a cell density of 4  ×  10 3 /mL cells, plated onto 24-well plates, and were used for patch clamp recording 18 h later. Before the assay, 30.186 mM DMSO stock solutions were diluted with an external solution to the desired concentrations. The DMSO concentration was 0.3%, which had no effect on hERG currents. During the automated QPatch recording, the hERG current was recorded in the whole-cell patch clamp configuration. The cells were voltage-clamped at a holding potential of  − 80 mV. The hERG current was activated by depolarizing at  − 30 mV for 0.5 s and then transiting to +30 mV for 2.5 s. Subsequently, the current was taken back to  − 50 mV for 4 s to remove the inactivation and observe the deactivating tail current. The peak size of the tail current was used to quantify the hERG current amplitude. For recording the hERG currents, the intracellular solution contained 20 mM KCl, 115 mM K-aspartate, 1 mM MgCl 2 · 6H 2 O, 10 mM HEPES, 5 mM EGTA, and 2 mM Na 2 - ATP, adjusted to pH 7.2 with KOH; and the internal solution contained 140 mM NaCl, 3.5 mM KCl, 2 mM CaCl 2 · 2H 2 O, 1 mM MgCl · 2H O, 10 mM  D -glucose, 10 mM HEPES, and 1.25 mM 2 2 NaH PO · 2H O, adjusted to pH 7.4 with NaOH.

4.12. Animal Experiments.  All animals were maintained and used in accordance with the guidelines of the Beijing Municipal Ethics Committee for the care and use of laboratory animals, and were approved by the Animal Care & Welfare Committee, Chinese Academy of Medical Sciences and Peking Union Medical College (Beijing, China). Male C57BL/6 J mice, golden hamsters and LDLR − / − golden hamsters (8 − 9 weeks old) were obtained from Beijing Vital River Laboratory Animal Technology Co., Ltd. (Beijing, China). All animals were maintained under standard conditions (temperature of 22  ±  2  ° C, humidity of 50 − 60%, and a 12 h/12 h light/dark cycle) with free access to food and water. The compounds were made to suspension with (sodium carboxyl methylcellulose, Na- CMC) to enhance drug solubility and administered by oral gavage.  

4.13. Kunming Mice Study.  Eight-week-old male Kunming mice were divided into control, model, and test group. In addition to the control group, the remaining groups were fed on high-fat diet (HFD). At the same time, probucol, simvastatin and selected compounds (50 mg/kg) were administered by oral gavage once daily for consecutive 7 days, respectively. The control and model group mice were administered an equivalent volume of distilled water. Blood samples were collected from each mouse and processed to detect the total cholesterol.  

4.14. C57BL/6J Mouse Study.  Eight-week-old male C57BL/6J mice were fed on either a high-fat diet (HFD) or chow diet for 7 days and were then randomly assigned to the following six groups after 1 week of acclimatization: (1) the control group,  n  = 9; (2) the model group,  n  = 10; (3) the probucol group,  n  = 10; (4) the V1 (10 mg/kg) group,  n  = 10; (5) the V1 (20 mg/kg) group,  n  = 10; (6) the V1 (40 mg/kg) group,  n  = 10. In addition to the control group, mice in the remaining groups were fed with an HFD. Probucol and V1 were administered by oral gavage. The control and model group mice were administered an equivalent volume of distilled water. The 24 h food intake and body weight were measured every day individually ( Figure S3A,B ).  

4.15. Golden Hamster Study.  After the acclimatization period

 (1 week), hamsters were randomized into the following seven groups:

 (1) the control group,  n  = 15; (2) the model group,  n  = 16; (3) the simvastatin group,  n  = 16; (4) the V1 (50 mg/kg) group,  n  = 16; (5) the V1 (100 mg/kg) group,  n  = 16; and (6) the V1 (200 mg/kg) group,  n  = 16.  

To induce the hyperlipidemia model, the hamsters were fed an HFD (normal chow supplemented with 0.2% cholesterol, 20% fat w/ w) for 4 consecutive weeks. The simvastatin (6 mg/kg), and derivative V1 (50, 100, and 200 mg/kg) groups were administered the treatment by oral gavage once per day. The control and model group hamsters were administered an equivalent volume of vehicle. The 24 h food intake and body weight were measured every week individually ( Figure S3C,D ).  

After treatment for 4 weeks, blood samples were collected from each hamster and coagulated at room temperature for 30 min and centrifuged at 4  ° C and 2000  ×  g for 10 min to collect serum. The serum was used to estimate the lipid serum levels. The animal experiment procedure is shown in  Figure 4 A.  

There were also seven groups for LDLR − / − golden hamsters: (1) the control group,  n  = 15; (2) the model group,  n  = 15; (3) the simvastatin group,  n  = 15; (4) the V1 (50 mg/kg) group,  n  = 15; (5) the V1 (100 mg/kg) group,  n  = 15; and (6) the V1 (200 mg/kg) group,  n  = 15.  

The process was similar to the former. After treatment for 8 weeks, blood samples were collected from each hamster and processed using the same method as described in the previous paragraph. The food intake and body weight are shown in  Figure S3E,F .  

4.16. Rhesus Monkey Study.  Male rhesus monkeys (age, 8 − 22 years old; weight, 8.01 − 12.41 kg) were obtained from Sichuan PriMed Shines Biotech Co., Ltd. Blood lipid levels were measured twice during the 2 weeks before treatment. Animals were selected for the study based on LDL-c levels (1.70 − 2.34 mmol/L). Animals were housed and maintained at 18 − 26  ° C, 40 − 70% humidity, exposed to 12 h/12 h light/dark cycles, and provided ad libitum access to either chow or HFD and water throughout the experimental period. Animals were randomized into the control and V1 treatment group (35 mg/kg, nasogastric feeding once a day). Animals ( n  = 3) fed on a normal chow diet were the lean control. Animals in the treatment group received V1, nasogastric feeding once a day as a solution in 0.5% CMC as a vehicle for 35 days.

4.18. Toxicity Assay.  The acute toxicity assay was conducted with 6 − 8-week-old golden hamsters weighing 100 − 114 g, which were housed in a conditioned environment for 6 days before the experiment. Later, the hamsters were randomly divided into the control group and the compound V1 treatment group based on body weight. Intragastric administration at a dose of 20 g/kg compound was performed twice with a time interval of 2 − 4 h. The control group was given an equal vehicle. After that, the hamsters were fed normally and observed frequently for 2 weeks. Ultimately, the main organs, including the heart, liver, spleen, lungs, and kidneys, were collected for histological analysis.  

The toxicity of V1 was also determined using three rhesus monkeys (2 − 4 years old, 3.8 − 4.6 kg body weight) obtained from the Laboratory Animal Center, Academy of Military Medical Sciences, Beijing, China. Animals were fed under standard conditions of 12 h/ 12 h of light/dark cycle at a temperature between 16 − 26  ° C for 14 days. The test animals were orally administered a single dose of compound V1 (667, 1500, and 3375 mg/kg). We performed histological observations on the liver, kidneys, heart, lungs, brain, spleen, and gastrointestinal tract of the monkeys to determine the toxicity of compound V1.  

4.19. Pharmacokinetic Evaluation.  The pharmacokinetic profiles of selected compounds were studied using healthy male and female rhesus monkeys (5.0 − 5.5 kg) obtained from the Institute of Beijing Xieerxin Biology Resource Co., Ltd. Animals were housed in a controlled environment (temperature of 16 − 26  ° C, relative humidity of 40 − 70%, and a 12 h/12 h light/dark cycle) on a standard diet with easily accessed drinking water ad libitum. Animals were fasted for 12 h before administration.  

Compound V1 was administered to male and female monkeys by oral gavage (70 mg/kg in 0.5% methylcellulose) or intravenous injection (i.v.) at a dose of 7 mg/kg. Serial blood samples were collected from a forelimb at 2 min (only i.v.), 5, 15, and 30 min, and 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 8, 12, 24, and 36 h after administration, placed in tubes containing heparin (0.5%) and NaF (200 mM), and centrifuged at 14,000 rpm for 5 min twice at 4  ° C to generate plasma. The samples were then analyzed by liquid chromatography-tandem MS.  

4.20. Caco-2 Permeability Assay.  Apparent permeability (Papp) was determined in the Caco-2 human colon carcinoma cell line. Cells were maintained in DMEM containing 20% fetal bovine serum, penicillin and streptomycin in a humidified atmosphere with 5% CO 2 . After several passages, the cells were in the logarithmic growth state, cells were plated onto a cell culture assembly plate (Corning, USA, Transwell permeable supports, 12 mm diameter insert, 0.4  μ m Polycarbonate membrane, area: 1.12 cm 2 ) and monolayer confluency was checked using a TEER electrode prior to the assay. After 21 days of culture, the TERR value reached more than 400  Ω· cm 2 . Medium was washed off and replaced in the appropriate apical (A) and basolateral (B) wells with HBSS buffer (pH 7.4) containing compounds (2  μ M and 21  μ M, respectively). Propranolol and digoxin was used as a positive control, Verapamil was used as P- gp transporter protein inhibitors. To assess compound permeability from the A to B direction, cell monolayers where dosed with 0.5 mL of the compound solution on the A side and 1.5 mL of the HBSS buffer on the B side of the Caco-2 cells. To assess compound permeability from the B to A direction, 1.5 mL of the compound solution was added to the B side, and 0.5 mL of the HBSS buffer was added to the A side. The plate was incubated for 2 h in 5% CO 2  at 37 ° C, 95% humidity condition. Samples were taken at half hour intervals from sides A and B for determination of compound content. All samples were assayed by LC-MS/MS (Agilent 1290 − 6495C, USA) using electrospray ionization. The apparent A − B and B − A permeabilities (Papp) and the efux ratio (ER), B − A/A − B, were calculated.  

4.21. Caco-2 Cell Stability Assay.  Caco-2 cell stability assays were performed in a 35 mm diameter culture dish. Cells were seeded in the culture dish with a logarithmic growth phase, and when the cell confluence reached 80 − 90%, the medium was washed with HBSS buffer several times, and V1 solution (10  μ g/mL in HBSS buffer) was added. The plate was incubated for 2 h in 5% CO 2  at 37  ° C, 95% humidity condition. Samples were taken at an hour interval from the culture dish for determination of V1 content within 3 h. A negative control was included by repeating the above incubation in a culture dish without the cell. All samples were assayed by LC-MS/MS (Agilent 1290 − 6495C, USA) using electrospray ionization. V1 was quantified as a ratio of the analyte response relative to the response of the internal standard.  

4.22. Microsomal Stability Assay.  The in vitro metabolic stability in a liver microsome system was used to assess test compound susceptibility to hepatic degradation. To determine the stability, liver microsomes from mixed-gender humans (XenoTech, USA, buy from IPHASE Co., Ltd.) were preincubated at 0.5 mg/mL in 100 mM phosphate buffer pH 7.4 at 37  ° C for 10 min. The reaction was initiated by the addition of NADPH (nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide phosphate) with 1  μ M of the test compound. A negative control was included by repeating the above incubation without the addition of NADPH. Depletion of the test compound was monitored over a 60 min incubation period, with time point collections taken at 0, 5, 15, 30, and 60 min. At each collection, 30 μ L was precipitated by the addition of 200  μ L acetonitrile containing internal standard. Testosterone, dextromethorphan, and diclofenac were used as the positive controls. Following centrifugation, the supernatants were analyzed by LC-MS/MS (Agilent 1290-6495C, USA). Compounds were quantified as a ratio of the analyte response relative to the response of the internal standard.  

4.23. Plasma Stability Assay.  Plasma stability was analyzed by incubating human plasma (bought from IPHASE Co., Ltd., China) at 37  ° C at 220 rpm with 1  μ M and 5  μ M of V-1, respectively. Depletion of the V-1 was monitored over a 240 min incubation period, with time point collections taken at 0, 10, 20, 30, 45, 60, 90, 120, 180, and 240 min. At each collection, 100  μ L was precipitated by the addition of 200  μ L acetonitrile containing internal standard. Following centrifugation, the supernatants were analyzed by LC-MS/MS (Agilent 1290-6495C, USA). V1 was quantified as a ratio of the analyte response relative to the response of the internal standard.

In a single-dose study, venous blood was drawn from the cubital vein of healthy volunteers. Blood samples were taken at 0, 15, and 30 min, and 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 12, 16, and 24 h. The erythrocyte fraction was extracted immediately by density centrifugation of EDTA blood diluted 1:1 in PBS. 70% Percoll solution (6 mL) was added to a 15 mL centrifuge tube, and the diluted blood was slowly added to the tube. The erythrocytes were obtained after centrifugation at 3000 rpm for 30 min at room temperature. The isolated erythrocytes were used for quantifying the phosphorylation of AMPK via ELISA (PathScan Phospho-AMPK α  (Thr172) Sandwich ELISA kit, Cell Signaling Technology). Briefly, red blood cells were lysed by red blood cell lysis buffer according to the manufacturer’s manual. Horse radish peroxidase (HRP)-linked antibody was used to capture and detect AMPK. To detect endogenous levels of AMPK α  when phosphory- lated at Thr172, rabbit antiAMPK  α  was added to each well. After incubation with erythrocyte lysates, both phospho-AMPK α  and nonphospho-AMPK α  were captured by the coated antibody. Following extensive washing, a phospho-AMPK α  (Thr172) mouse detection antibody was added to detect phosphorylation of Thr172 on the captured AMPK α  protein. Antimouse IgG HRP-linked antibody was used to recognize the bound detection antibody. The HRP substrate, tetra methyl benz i dine, was added to develop the color. The magnitude of the absorbance was measured at 450 nm.  

In the multiple-dose study, 12 volunteers received V1 at a dose of 2000 mg daily for 7 days. Blood samples from each participant were collected before and after the period of treatment. The serum LDL-C and TC levels were examined.  

4.25. Statistical Analysis.  All the data are presented as the mean

 ±  standard error of the mean. GraphPad Prism software (version

 8.3.0, Dotmatics) was used to perform statistical analyses. Parametric variable comparisons between two groups were analyzed by using a two-tailed Student’s  t -test for independent samples.
