the major factors of nodular oxidation are the discontinuity of Al 2 O 3  and the formation of CoO.  During oxidation, the diffusion flux of cations is larger in region 2 than that in region 1 due to the  discontinuous Al 2 O 3 . So it is easy for CoO to nucleate as a result of Cr  depletion  [19, 21] . The  growth of CoO is fast, which leads to more vacancy injected in S/A interface due to the bulk diffusion  in spinel oxides [32-34]. Thus, the vacancy gradient is established, and Co diffusion is promoted. It has  

been reported that O can be trapped by vacancy to form vacancy-oxygen complexes [26-28, 35]. The  interaction between Co diffusion and vacancy-oxygen complexes causes the increasing of the solubility  of oxygen, which accelerates the internal oxidation.  

The process of nodular oxidation can be illustrated in Fig. 8e and f. CoO forms in the region  where Al 2 O 3  scale is discontinuous . The rapid growth of CoO leads to more vacancy generated. The  diffusion of O and Co is promoted under the interaction with the vacancy. Thus, the rates of both  internal oxidation and the growth of CoO are increased. Moreover, the oxygen pressure in S/A

More Cr atoms will be supplied to change to Cr 3+  in  S/A interface. The flux of Cr 3+  will be higher. Moreover, the concentration of Co in S/A interface
