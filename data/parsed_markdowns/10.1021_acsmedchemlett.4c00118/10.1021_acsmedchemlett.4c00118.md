Summary.Acne vulgaris consists of a spectrum of skin lesions including comedones, inflammatory papules, pustules, nodules, and cysts. The disease is classified as mild, moderate, 0r severe depending onlesion severity and anatomical lesion distribution. Disease onset typically occurs at puberty because of elevated sebum production triggered by increased androgen levels.  

The acetyl-CoA carboxylase (ACC) enzyme, which catalyzes the conversion of acetyl-CoA to malonyl-CoA, plays a key role in regulating lipid metabolism. ACC is an essential and rate- limiting step in the de novo synthesis of fatty acids and regulates the oxidation of long chain fatty acids. The terms “de novo lipogenesis" (DNL) and “de novo fatty acid synthesis" are used toaddress the synthesis of fatty acids from non-lipid-based sources.
