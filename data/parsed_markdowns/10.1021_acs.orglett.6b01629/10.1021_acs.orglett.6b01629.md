ABSTRACT:  A general protocol for the synthesis of benzylic amines via side-chain amination of alkylquinones is reported. The reactions are initiated by the tautomerization of an alkylquinone to the corresponding quinone methide, which is subsequently trapped in situ by an amine nucleophile. The synthetic utility of this transformation is also explored.  

B enzylic amines are key constituents of pharmaceuticals, agrochemicals, and materials and are commonly found as intermediates in modern organic chemistry.   Accordingly, a wide variety of methods for their synthesis have been developed. Traditional methods for amine synthesis, such as nucleophilic 2   addition to imines and nucleophilic substitution of benzylic ,   electrophiles, typically require pre function aliz ation of the benzylic coupling partner and/or nucleophile. Transition-metal- − 8   catalyzed reactions, such as the amination of styrene derivatives,   the direct benzylic C − H amination of alkylarenes, the  α -arylation of aliphatic amines,   and imine addition reactions,   o ﬀ er several advantages over traditional methods, including mild reaction conditions and increased functional group tolerance; however, their application is limited by the need to employ expensive and sometimes toxic metal catalysts. Other 9   methods based on the benzylic amination of alkyl- or vinyl- 10   substituted arenes often require super stoic hio metric quantities of oxidants or reagents. Therefore, the development of new technologies for the synthesis of benzylic amines that do not depend on the pre function aliz ation of reaction partners, or the need for transition-metal catalysts or super stoic hio metric reagents, is highly desirable. The utilization of un functional i zed substrates to access reactive intermediates capable of intercepting primary and secondary amines would allow rapid and direct access to benzylic amines. Herein we report a straightforward and e ﬃ cient method for benzylic amine synthesis based on the direct coupling of alkylquinones with primary and secondary amines. This new protocol for benzylic amine synthesis proceeds by way 11   of alkylquinone tautomerization to generate a highly electro- philic quinone methide intermediate that is, in turn, trapped by , an amine coupling partner.

In conclusion, we have developed a general method for the e ﬃ cient synthesis of benzylic amines via alkylquinone amination. This transformation enables the synthesis of complex benzylic amines in a single step under mild conditions. We expect this protocol to be adopted as a useful tool to rapidly access benzylic amines and prepare aminated derivatives of quinone-containing natural products and pharmaceuticals.
