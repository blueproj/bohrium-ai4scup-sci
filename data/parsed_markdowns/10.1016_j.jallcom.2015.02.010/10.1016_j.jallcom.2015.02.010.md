The dual superalloys FGH4096–GH4133B were joined by the electron beam welding. Isothermal compression tests were carried out on electron beam weldments FGH4096–GH4133B alloys at the temperatures of 1020–1140   C (the nominal  c 0 -transus temperature is about 1080   C) and strain rates of 0.001–1.0 s  1   with the height reduction of 50%. The results showed that the true stress–true strain curves are greatly affected by deformation temperature and strain rate. There is an intrinsic and necessary connection between the ﬂow stress and thermal–dynamic behavior, which can be indicated by the true stress–true strain curves. The power dissipation maps at different strains exhibit that true strain has a great effect on processing maps. Processing maps under different strains were constructed for evaluation of the ﬂow instability regime and optimization of processing parameters. The dynamic recrystallization mechanism and microstructure evolution in the welding seam of the studied dual-alloys have been discussed. High temperature and low strain rate are instrumental to dynamic recrystallization. The size of dynamically recrystallized grain decreased with the increase of strain rate and increased with the increase of deformation temperature. Based on the established combine processing map and microstructures, hot deformation process should be carried out under the condition of 1100  1120   C/0.3  1.0 s  1   with high strains to obtain homogeneous and ﬁne microstructure.

# 1. Introduction  

Turbine disk is the heart of aero engine, and it determines the development of space technology. Its work condition is severe, which is caused by the temperature and stress differences between different parts. Because of the turbine disks’ special working cir- cumstance, the working temperatures are different positions. The turbine disks are required to obtain various performances at their various positions. The fast development of spaceﬂight and aviation technology leads to rigorous comprehensive performance require- ment of turbine disks. So the traditional turbine disks with single alloy and single performance already cannot answer the higher purpose. The dual property turbine disk applied to aircraft engine can satisfy the requirements of the comprehensive performance. The turbine disks with dual alloys and dual performances have become a hotspot of research.  

GH4133B alloy is a traditional nickel-based developed by China. This superalloy has the based elements of Ni and Cr, and added the elements of Ti and Al for aging strengthening. This superalloy has high alloying degree, large deformation resistance and narrow deformation temperature range, so the thermal forming is difﬁcult. On the other hand, GH4133B alloy has good microstructure sta- bility, excellent mechanical properties, good ultimate strength and creep performance at elevated temperature. This alloy has been widely applied for manufacturing turbine engine blades or  

FGH4096 superalloy belongs to the second generation of damage tolerance superalloy produced by the way of powder metallurgy. This type of superalloy has excellent high-temperature  

other bearing components under high temperature  [9] . The service temperature of this superalloy is about 700   C. With the develop- ment of modern aviation industry, the working temperature for aircraft engine disk has increased. Therefore, the performance requirements of materials are more and more urgent.  

With the development of modern aviation industry, high per- formance aero-engine application requires that turbine disc should be produced with dual-performance  [10,11] . In order to adapt to the development of aerospace technology, FGH4096 and GH4133B can be connected by electron beam welding technology for getting a dual alloys (FGH4096–GH4133B dual alloys) to satisfy different performance requirements. This dual alloy with multiple performances can meet different requirements on different parts of turbine disc.  

For the dual superalloys connected by electron beam welding technology, because of the different alloy compositions and plastic processing capacity, high temperature deformation becomes a complex and difﬁcult problem. The problems such as blooming large-scale ingots, adiabatic shear band, wedge cracking and prior particle boundary during hot deformation should be resolved. Therefore, it is very important to investigate and conﬁrm the pow- er dissipation map, instability map and microstructure evolution at different deformation temperatures and strain rates. They have been used to research the hot workability of many other alloys [12–16] .  

In this study, the mechanical behaviors of the FGH4096– GH4133B dual alloys during high temperature deformation were investigated by using hot compression tests in the deformation temperature range 1020–1140   C and the strain rate range 0.001–1.0 s  1 . The processing map was employed to study the deformation mechanisms during hot working process and to opti- mize the hot working process. Meanwhile, the effect of true strain on processing map was also investigated. Based on the results, the advanced processing maps are developed. The microstructures after deformation have been studied. Based on the processing map and microstructures evolution, the optimum processing con- dition can be predicted for this dual alloy.

The true stress–true strain curves not only can be used to reﬂect the change of ﬂow stress with the true strain, but also can be used to indicate the intrinsic relationship between the ﬂow stress and thermal–dynamic behavior. At the beginning of hot compression, work hardening caused by the dislocation generation and multipli- cation results in the rapid increase of ﬂow stress and leads to a pro- nounced peak stress. Due to the low stacking fault energy, the dynamic recovery for this studied dual superalloys proceeds slow- ly. With the increase of the deformation degree, the dislocation density and the potential driving force of recovery increases. The continuous ﬂow softening phenomenon can be mainly attributed to the dynamic recovery (DRV), deformation heating and con- tinuous dynamic recrystallization (DRX)  [17,18] . On the one hand,

# 3.2. Approach of processing maps  

Processing map, which as a kind of very important effective and powerful tool has been employed extensively in the study of the hot deformation behavior, control microstructure and optimize the processing parameters in hot working of the metallic materials and alloys. For example, the hot deformation behaviors of superal- loys  [19,20] , Titanium alloys  [21,22]  and magnesium  [23,24]  have been studied by using the processing maps. The dynamic materials model as the basic thesis is used to construct the processing maps. The dynamic materials model assumes that the total instantaneous power  P  absorbed from equipment is dissipated through the tem- perature rise and microstructure change. The dissipated powers caused by the temperature rise and microstructure change are named content  G  and co-content  J . The regime of the processing map could be interpreted based on the characteristic efﬁciency variation associated with the microstructural processes  [25,26] . The regimes marked by the lim- iting conditions are termed ‘‘safe’’ for processing. The deformation mechanisms of the safe domains include DRX, dynamic recovery and super-plasticity. Generally, the efﬁciency value associated with dynamic recovery is about 0.30, the value related to DRX is about 0.30–0.50, while the value related to super-plasticity is very high (about 0.60)  [27,28] . Therefore, for the FGH4096–GH4133B dual alloys, dynamic recovery and DRX are mainly efﬁcient for hot working in the safe process. When the efﬁciency value is less than 0.3, dynamic recovery is the dominating softening mechanism in the hot deformation. The peak efﬁciency of about 0.5 occurring at about 1110   C/1.0 s  1 , dynamic recovery as fraction of DRX grains appears to be minimal, and it has an unconspicuous effect on the hot deformation, and DRX is more efﬁcient than dynamic recovery and is preferred for hot working.  

The hot deformation as a continuous working process, so all processing maps at different true strain should be considered. All processing maps at different strain can be superimposed, and a new combine map is got, as shown in  Fig. 7 . It predicts the insta- bility regimes: the ﬁrst ﬂow instability regime occurred around 1020–1070   C/0.06–1.0 s  1 ; the secondary regime occurred around 1080–1130   C/0.001–0.01 s  1 . We should avoid these regimes in the process of hot deformation.  

Typical microstructures of the FGH4096–GH4133B dual alloys processed after hot compression are shown in  Fig. 8 , which give the evidence on DRV and DRX (including nucleation and grain growth). DRX is the dominating softening mechanism of the stud- ied dual superalloys. The efﬁciency value associated with DRV is about 0.2, and the efﬁciency values associated with DRX is about 0.35.  Fig. 8  (1#) presents many grains are elongated after deforma- tion at 1020   C and 0.001 s  1 , even if the dynamic recrystallization has taken place partially.  Fig. 8  (2#) presents the DRX has taken place partially at 1080   C and 0.1 s  1 , and besides there are still some enlonged grains. Typically, the initiation of DRX occurs with the growing ﬂuctuations of the grain boundary shape. Serration and bulges then develop, and eventually new grains are generated along the prior grain boundaries. Because local stress concentra- tion can be formed easily at the interfaces between the grain boundary during the hot compression, which causes the motion, piling-up, rearrangement and emergence of dislocations, and in turn to form subgrains and subgrain boundaries, during further deformation, DRX grains are formed by the in situ evolution of the subgrains with the growth of low angle boundaries to high angle grain boundaries  [29,30] . When the strain rate drops to 0.001 s  1 , the DRX takes place more fully, as shown in  Fig. 8 (3#). The DRX has occurred fully when the deformation temperature is up to 1110   C and the strain rate is 0.001 s  1 , the microstructure is uniform and the grain is ﬁne, as shown in  Fig. 8 (4#). When the deformation temperature is up to 1110   C and the strain rate is 0.001 s  1 , the continuous dynamic recrystallization has taken place, and the grain growth is apparently facilitated at a relative high temperature and low strain rate, attributed to the prolonga- tion of boundary migration, as shown in  Fig. 8 (5#).  

The results show that at the same strain and strain rate for the FGH4096–GH4133B dual alloys, the higher the deformation tem- perature, the easier the occurrence of dynamic recrystallization. Apparently, at the same deformation temperature and amount, if the dynamic recrystallization has fully occurred in the FGH4096– GH4133B dual alloys, the higher the strain rate, the ﬁner the recrystallized grain.

# 4. Conclusions  

The hot deformation behavior of electron beam welding of superalloy FGH4096 and GH4133B has been investigated at a tem- perature range of 1020–1140   C and a strain rate range of 0.001– 1.0 s  1 . The following conclusions are drawn from this research:  

(1) True stress–true strain curves of FGH4096–GH4133B dual alloys are sensitive to the deformation parameters and strain rate. The true stress–true strain curves also can be used to indicate the intrinsic relationship between the ﬂow stress and thermal–dynamic behavior. (2) True strain has a great effect on processing maps. When the strain is 0.05, there are two instability domains located around 1020–1060   C/0.003–0.1 s  1 and 1100–1130   C/ 0.001–0.01 s  1 , respectively. With the strain increases from 0.15 to 0.40, the instability domains located around 1100– 1130   C/0.001–0.01 s  1   have disappeared, and the other instability domain has shrunk. And the domain with g  > 0.25 expand with the increase of strain. When the strain increases from 0.55 to 0.69, the instability domains located around 1020–1050   C/1.0–0.1 s  1   have disappeared, and the other instability domain located around 1090–1120   C/ 0.001–0.005 s  1 . In the most domain, the value of  g  is larger than 0.25.  

(3) High temperature and low strain rate are instrumental to dynamic recrystallization. The size of dynamically recrystal- lized grain decreased with the increase of strain rate and increased with the increase of deformation temperature. (4) The hot working process can be carried out safely in the region of 1080–1140   C and 0.01  1.0 s  1 . When the true strain is 0.69, the optimum processing condition is around 1090  1130   C/0.1  1.0 s  1   with peak efﬁciency 0.58. Based on the established combine processing map and microstruc- tures, hot deformation process should be carried out under the condition of 1100  1120   C/0.3  1.0 s  1   with high strains to obtain homogeneous and ﬁne microstructure.
