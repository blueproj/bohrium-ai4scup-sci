# Effect of substrate orientation on the formation of equiaxed stray grains in laser surface remelted single crystal superalloys: Experimental investigation  

L. Wang, N. Wang  

The Key Laboratory of Space Applied Physics and Chemistry, Ministry of Education, School of Science, Northwestern Polytechnical University, Xi'an 710072, China  

# a b s t r a c t  

# a r t i c l e i n f o  

Substrate orientation could play a key role in the stray grain (SG) formation in laser welded single crystal (SX) alloys, which is critical to a successful repair of SX components. In a previous work [Acta mater 2015; 88:283 e 292], we studied theoretically the substrate orientation effect on equiaxed SG susceptibility in laser melt pools by altering the orientation via rotations around  x -,  y -, and  z -axis, which coincide with the [100], [010], and [001] crystallographic directions, respectively. It was found that  y -axis rotation leads to a strong variation in SG ability whereas  x - and  z -axis rotations nearly have no effect. In this study, experiments were conducted to verify the calculated results. DD6, a second-generation Chinese SX su- peralloy was chosen as the object of research. The orientation-dependent SG formation was determined and the area-average volume fraction of SGs was employed to evaluate the overall CET tendency. The experimental tendency of the orientation dependent SG agrees well with the theoretical prediction. The effect of the substrate orientation on SG formation was further revealed by cos j  distribution, where  j  is a geometrical parameter which is the angle between normal vector to the solidi ﬁ cation front and preferred crystallographic direction in dendrite domain. Our results provide an in-depth insight into the control of laser processing window for SX repair via adjusting the substrate orientation.

# 1. Introduction  

In one previous paper  [1] , we discussed theoretically the effect of substrate orientation on SG formation in laser remelting process. Such an effect is concerned since during laser repair of single- crystal (SX) superalloy components, epitaxial columnar growth along the substrate orientation needs to be ensured and the for- mation of stray grains (SGs) should be avoided to maintain the SX nature  [2 e 5] . SG formation is closely related to the columnar to equiaxed transition (CET) induced by nucleation and growth of equiaxed grains in constitutional supercooling (CS) zone ahead of columnar dendrite front  [6 e 8] . The extent of CS is determined by solidi ﬁ cation conditions: growth velocity and thermal gradient  [9] . Therefore, the onset of CET needs to be constrained in order to suppress the equiaxed SGs.  

The varying of the substrate orientation can be performed by rotating the sample around different preferred crystallographic directions with respect to the laser beam direction. We proposed a systematic way to investigate it  [1]  by altering the substrate orientation via rotating the substrate around  x -,  y - and  z -axis, which are parallel to the [100], [010], and [001] crystallographic directions respectively, as shown in  Fig. 1 . Our results predicted theoretically that the rotation of orientation around  y -axis can alter SG susceptibility drastically whereas those around  x - and  z -axis nearly have no effect. This paper will carry out the experimental investigation on this aspect to validate the theoretical predictions.

We pay much attention to the contribution of the varied rotation manner on SG formation since there is no explicit conclusion to the varied rotation manner on SG formation although some related experimental works have been carried out. Mokadem et al.  [16] performed laser welding of CMSX-4 SX superalloys along the [100] direction in various crystal planes ranging from (001) to (011), which corresponds to the rotation of the substrate orientation around  x -axis in our discussion  [1] . Their results show that the equiaxed growth regime is extended when the substrate orienta- tion effect was taken into account. Park et al.  [17 e 19]  conducted laser welding of Rene N5 SX superalloys with an asymmetrical orientation and found it led to an asymmetrical distribution of SGs with respect to the centerline. Note that  x - or  z -axis rotation will produce an asymmetrical orientation except that the rotation angle is 45    while all the orientations in  y -axis rotation are symmetrical. Therefore, it can be deduced that  y -axis rotation could be different from the other two in terms of the symmetry of SG distribution.  

Park et al. demonstrated the orientation effect from the sym- metrical point of view without giving details on the susceptibility to the SGs within the melt pool. Later, DuPont et al. made progress on this aspect  [20,21]  by laser melting CMSX-4 superalloys along [100], [110], and [120] crystallographic directions on (001) crystal planes. This corresponds to the  z -axis rotation in our case. Their results showed that the intersections of different dendrite domains are vulnerable sites for SG formation and the location and number of intersections will control the overall SG formation.  

Very Recently, Liu et al.  [22]  studied laser depositions of Rene N5 powders on directionally solidi ﬁ ed DZ125 substrates with the orientation varying in the transverse and longitudinal sections, which correspond to the  x - and  y -axis rotations respectively. They show that the variation of substrate orientation around  y -axis in- ﬂ uences the position of CET, whereas  x -axis rotation primarily af- fects the competitive growth of columnar dendrites.  

As can be seen, the results above have been focused mainly on the  x - and  z -axis rotations whereas that of  y -axis is seldom considered. Meanwhile, the experiments were conducted under different processing conditions and with different SX superalloys. The substrate orientation, sometimes, is believed to have no effect on overall SG formation tendency  [23] . However, as shown in our previous paper  [1] , this is the case only for  x - and  z -axis rotations. For  y -axis rotation, the substrate orientation will alter the SG formation.  

The aim of this work is to conduct experiments to prove our previous theoretical results. The experiments were performed with a speci ﬁ c SX superalloy under  ﬁ xed processing conditions, and the effect of substrate orientation was determined uniquely. It was found that the general trend in experiments agrees well with the theoretical prediction, i.e.  y -axis rotation has an effect on the for- mation of equiaxed SGs. This result is believed to be critical to designing the laser SX repair strategy.  

It should be noted that equiaxed SGs could also be induced by other mechanisms such as dendrite fragmentation or grain detachment when  ﬂ uid  ﬂ ow is strong  [24,25] . However, we concentrate on the heterogeneous nucleation in constitutional undercooling region. For this reason, convection within the fusion zones needs to be depressed. This can be achieved by the reduction of laser power and veri ﬁ ed by melt pool shape  [26] .

# 4.4. Differences among the x-, y-, and z-axis rotations  

We now show why  f  varies strongly with substrate orientation for the  y -axis rotation. As shown in above section, intersections and boundaries are places with high SG formation susceptibility. The number of intersections therefore have an effect on  f  because  f  is area-average volume fraction of SGs in the whole melt pool. The distributions of  f  at  x i  ¼  45    ( i  ¼  x ,  y ,  z ) together with that of the initial state are presented in  Fig. 9 . The domain boundaries are also illustrated in dashed lines. At initial orientation, there are two in- tersections in the weld pool,  Fig. 9(a) . For the rotation around  y - axis, the domain intersections move upwards and downwards with the increases of the rotation angle. At x y  ¼  45  ,  Fig. 9(c) , the in- tersections shift outside the weld pool, therefore,  f  reaches its x   z   minimum value. In contrast, the distributions of  cos j and  cos j at x i  ¼  45    ( i  ¼  x ,  z ) show that there exists at least one intersection in the center part of the weld pool,  Fig. 9(b) and (d) . As a result, the  y - axis rotation will result in a more orientation-sensitive SG forma- tion than  x - and  z -axis rotations, as shown in  Fig. 6 .  

y   The asymmetrical pro ﬁ le of  f with respect to substrate orien- tation lies in the fact that the different variation in the location of the intersection for clockwise and counterclockwise rotations. # 5. Conclusion  

The effect of the substrate orientation varied via rotations around  x -,  y -, and  z -axis on SG formation in laser remelted SX su- peralloys was investigated experimentally and the area-average volume fraction of equiaxed SGs was used to characterize SG sus- ceptibility. Comparisons between experiments and calculations are also made. Based on this work, the following conclusions can be drawn:  

1. Experimentally, the formation of SGs varies strongly with the substrate orientation when the rotation is performed around  y - axis, whereas it changes slightly when the rotation is made around  x - or  z -axis. This agrees well with the previously theo- retical prediction.

 2. Theoretically, since the domain boundaries and intersections have smaller value of cos j , they are the vulnerable places where SGs tend to form.  

3. The domain intersections located at regions of low  G n d = V d  ratio are more susceptible to SG formation than those located in the regions of high  G n d = V d  ratio.

 4. In terms of avoiding equiaxed SG formation,  x y  ¼  45    is a better choice than  x  ¼  0  , which is the orientation used normally for SX laser repair.
