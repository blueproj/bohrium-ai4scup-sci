Microstructures and mechanical properties of Co – 9.5Al – 5W – 16Ni – 6Cr – x Ta (at% where  x ¼ 0, 1.8, 2.8, named as 0Ta, 1.8Ta and 2.8Ta) alloys are investigated. Microstructures of heat-treated samples are composed of  γ  and cubical  γ 0   precipitates. The  γ 0   solvus temperature increases obviously with Ta additions. The yield strength as well as creep strength is improved with the addition of Ta. Ta additions affect the high-temperature tensile deformation mechanism. The  γ 0   phase of the three alloys is rafted along the tensile axis direction during creep tests at 900  1 C. The creep strength of 2.8Ta alloy is approaching the level of a  ﬁ rst-generation Ni-base single-crystal superalloy.

# 1. Introduction  

The discovery of the relatively stable  γ 0 -Co 3  (Al,W) phase with L1 2  structure in the ternary Co – Al – W system by Sato et al.  [1]  has generated substantial interest. A series of experimental  [1 – 6]  and computational  [7]  efforts have been done to study the micro- structure stability and mechanical properties of the new Co-base alloys, suggesting that Co 3 (Al,W) can be practically used as the strengthening phase. However, large amount of W ( 25 wt%) is  added to stabilize Co 3 (Al,W) in the Co – Al – W-base alloys, leading to a high density, and the  γ 0   solvus temperature is lower compared to Ni-base superalloys. A recent investigation by Shinagawa et al. [3]  indicates that substitution of Ni for W can stabilize the  γ 0   phase and increase the  γ 0   solvus temperature. This provides a way to decrease the heavy density. In the ternary Co – Al – W system, with a 2 at% Ta addition, the yield strength as well as the solvus temperature of  γ 0   phase is increased obviously  [4] , whereas a combined addition of certain amount Cr and Ta can signi ﬁ cantly decrease the  γ 0   solvus temperature as well as high-temperature strength  [4] . A combined addition of Cr and Ni to ternary Co – Al – W system can improve the  γ 0   solvus temperature  [5] . These results reveal that the interactions between alloying elements affect the microstructure and mechanical properties. So far, the effect of Ta additions on the microstructure and mechanical behaviors of a multinary Co – Al – W – Ni – Cr alloy system has not been shown systematically. # 2. Experimental procedure

The melting point of alloys and  γ 0   solvus temperature were determined by Differential Thermal Analysis. All single crystal rods were homogenized at 1300  1 C for 8 h in a vacuum, followed by furnace cooling, and then annealed at 850  1 C, 950  1 C and 1000  1 C for 36 h for 0Ta, 1.8Ta and 2.8Ta, respectively. After that, all samples were annealed at 750  1 C for 24 h. Microstructures were examined by Scanning Electron Microscope (SEM). # 3. Results and discussion

For comparison, the transformation temperatures of Co-base super- alloys with  γ / γ 0   two-phase microstructure are also given in Table 1 . It can be seen that with the Ta additions the solidus and liquidus decrease slightly, while the  γ 0 solvus temperature increases from 930 to 1100  1 C obviously. In comparison with 2.8Ta and Co – 8.8Al – 9.8W – 2Ta (at%), with large amount of W substituted by Ni, the  γ 0   solvus temperature of 2.8Ta alloy is higher and the density is lower. These results suggest that Ta is effective to improve  γ 0   solvus temperature in the present alloy system.  

The SEM micrographs of  γ 0   precipitates of samples after heat treatments and creep deformation at 900  1 C are shown in  Fig. 1 . After heat treatments, the three alloys are only constituted of  γ and cubical  γ 0   precipitates. The  γ 0   volume fraction of the three alloys is close to 60% and the average size of  γ 0   precipitates increases from 95, to 241 and 301 nm for 0Ta, 1.8Ta and 2.8Ta. Due to the obvious variation of  γ 0   solvus temperature with Ta additions, the three alloys were implemented with different annealing temperatures. Alloying elements which lower the melt- ing point of an alloy enhance the diffusivity  [8] . As shown in Table 1 , the melting points decrease with Ta additions. Thus, a higher diffusion rate caused by elevated annealing temperature and the addition of Ta results in the increase of  γ 0   precipitates size.  

Directional rafting of  γ 0   precipitates occurred parallel to the applied tensile axis in all crept specimens, and the rafts of the three alloys vary in thickness, associated with the initial precipi- tate size. According to Nabarro ’ s prediction , for  σ  δ 4 0 the raft structure is oriented parallel to the external stress axis while the situation is reversed for  σ  δ o  σ  is the externally applied stress and  δ  is the lattice mis ﬁ t. The rafting microstructures suggest that the three alloys possess a positive  γ / γ 0   lattice mismatch at high temperature.  

Mechanical properties:  The yield strength and creep strength of the three alloys are shown in  Fig. 2 . For comparison, a conven- tional Co-base superalloy (L-605) that contains no  γ 0 -Co 3  (Al,W) phase  [10] , a  ﬁ rst-generation single-crystal Ni-base superalloy (SRR 99)  [11]  and two  γ 0 -strengthened Co-base alloys  [2,4]  are also included. It can be seen that the tensile strength and creep strength of these  γ 0 -strengthened Co-base superalloys are superior compared to those of L-605, probably due to the  γ / γ 0   coherent interface strengthening effect. The 1.8Ta and 2.8Ta alloys also exhibit a three-stage temperature dependence of yield strength. Increasing Ta content, the yield strength and creep life are improved obviously. The yield strength of 1.8Ta and 2.8Ta alloys is higher than that of Co – 9.4Al – 10.7W (at%), and the creep strength of 2.8Ta alloy is comparable with that of Co – 8.8Al – 9.8W – 2Ta (at%), approaching the level of SRR 99. These results suggest that Ta is effective to improve high-temperature proper- ties in a multinary Co – Al – W – Ni – Cr alloy system.

The average  γ 0   precipitates size and channel width of 1.8Ta and 2.8Ta alloys are approaching, while the deformation mechanism is different between the two alloys at 900  1 C. A similar variation of high-temperature deformation behavior with Ta additions in ternary Co – Al – W system at 900  1 C has been reported in Ref.  [4] . It can be inferred that the addition of Ta affects high- temperature deformation behaviors in the present alloy system as well. The stacking fault energy of Co 3 (Al,W) can be increased by Ta additions  [7] , which leads to the increment of the resistance of the precipitate to initial shearing by partial dislocations and the improvement of high-temperature strength. This illustrates the improvement of high-temperature strength with Ta additions.  

The major defect of the three alloys after creep rupture at 900  1 C is dislocations bypassing  γ 0   precipitates. The resistance for the motion of dislocations in  γ  channels is made up of three contributions  [13] , the Orowan stress, solid solution resistance and mis ﬁ t stresses. Ta atoms dissolved in  γ  matrix can increase the solid solution resistance. The addition of Ta can increase the  γ / γ 0 lattice mismatch  [6] , which will result in a higher mis ﬁ t stress. It is dif ﬁ cult to determine the variation of the Orowan stress with Ta additions since the channel width and constants related to the Orowan stress are altered as well. At 900  1 C, the major deforma- tion of 0Ta and 1.8Ta during tensile tests is dislocations bypassing γ 0   precipitates and the yield strength of 1.8Ta is higher than that of 0Ta. This indicates that the addition of Ta can improve the resistance of dislocations ’  motion in  γ  channels, which contributes to the improvement of creep strength.
