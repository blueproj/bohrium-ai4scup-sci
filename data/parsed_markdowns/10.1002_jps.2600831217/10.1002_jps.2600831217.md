# Introduction  

Organic co solvents are among the most powerful so lub i liz ing agents for a large number of weak electrolyte drugs. The mechanism of solubilization by cosolvents has been studied for years. In the absence of a molecular level knowledge of solubilization, a practical approach to understanding cosol- vency is to develop models based on established theories and experimental data. This approach has to make approxima- tions and simplifications at various levels.Such efforts have given rise to several models including the extended regular solution theory,1 the excess energy model,2 and the phenom- enological model.3These models are reported to be applicable towater/co solvent systems.However,they all inevitably involve one or more model parameters which are usually specific to a particular solute--mixed solvent system and must be estimated from experimental data of solubility in mixed solvents.  

Another approach makes use of correlations between co- solvency and available properties of the solutes,cosolvents and water. Models established in this way are often powerful tools for prediction.Since beingintroduced byYalkowsky and Rosemanin1984,4thelog-linear model has found increasing applications due toits wide applicability range and its simplicity. It is useful not only in drug formulation5 but also in modeling the transport of environmental pollutants through soils and natural waterbodies.6.7  

Using the log-linear model, Yalkowsky and Rubino8 stud- iedtheeffectivenessofpropyleneglycolinenhancingthe water solubility of more than fifty drug/co solvent systems. They demonstrated that so lub i liz ation by this co solvent is a function of the polarity of the drug and the concentration of propylene glycolin the mixed solvent.Similar characteristics of so lub i liz ation were also demonstrated for other co solvents suchasmethanolandacetonewithenvironmental“priority pollutants" as solutes.9-12 The purpose of this report is to confirmthe applicabilityof thelog--linearmodel towater/ ethanol systems and to develop a means of estimating the dependency of so lub i liz ation by ethanol upon solute structure as reflected by log Ko w.

# Summary  

Thelog-linearmodel is the simplest theory of cosolvency so far. It discovers general trends and major determinant factors of cosolvency, thus it gives guidelines for solving solubility problem using cosolvents. In this paper, the log-- linear model is demonstrated for ethanol/water systems.It is shown that solubilization or de so lub i liz ation of organic compounds by ethanol strongly depends on the ethanol volume fraction in solvent, as indicated by the model. It is also evident that the extentof solubilization or de so lub i liz ation strongly depends on the hydrophilic it y of the solutes expressed by ClogP, which is the calculated log Kow based on Hansch's fragment method. Deviations from the log-linear model were observed. Generally, positive deviations occur above 0.5 solute-free volume fraction of ethanol and is more significant for hydrophilic solutes.Negative deviations were observed at low ethanol concentrations for hydrophobic solutes. Char- act eri z ation and quant if i cation of such deviations are war- ranted,and will be continuously reported in forthcoming papers.Predictive equations based on the log-linear model are given which calculate the extent of so lub i liz ation or de so lub i liz ation from ethanol concentration in mixed solvent andClogPofsolute
