# INTRODUCTION  

Spiro cy clo hexen one frameworks are widely distributed in natural products and bioactive molecules.   The cyclopropyl spiro cy clo hexen one core, especially, exhibits a broad spectrum of biological activities for potential pharmaceutical and agrochemical applications. For example, CC-1065,   the duocarmycins (such as duocarmycin A),   and yatakemycin 4 compose a class of potent antitumor antibiotics in which the cyclopropyl spiro hex a die none defines their properties with a characteristic DNA alkylation reaction at the cyclopropane ( Figure 1 ). This skeleton is also found in the members of the myrocin family isolated from fungal metabolites (such as myrocin A,  Figure 1 ) with anti pro life rat ive antimicrobial activities.   Cycloclavine,   which belongs to the clavine subset of ergot alkaloids, has been isolated from the seeds of the African morning glory shrub  Ipomoea hildebrandtii  and later from  Aspergillus japonicas , and exhibits promising insecticidal and antiparasitic properties. Until now, the construction of this spirocyclic skeleton has involved four traditional strategies: the [2 + 1] cy clo prop a nation reaction of diaz o cy clo hexane derivatives with olefins; 7   the [2 + 1] cy clo prop a nation reaction of methylene cy clo hexane with diazo compounds, halogenated alkanes, or sulfur ylides; 8   the [4 + 2] cycloaddition of conjugated dienes with a pendant methylene cy clo propane; 9   or the intramolecular dearomative nucleophilic substitution of phenol derivatives containing a leaving group at the phenyl- ethyl position ( Scheme 1 a).   The development of a new, simple, and efficient approach to diverse spiro cy clo hex a die- nones would be highly desirable.  

Dearomatization of phenol and its derivatives, as a powerful strategy to construct (spiro)cyclohexenones, has been studied intensively.   For instance, hypervalent iodine-mediated dearomative spiro cyclization of phenols developed by the groups of Kita and others smoothly construct  ortho - spirolactone cyclohexadienones.   Hamada’s and You’s groups 13   reported the intramolecular allylic alkylation of para -substituted phenols to generate five-membered spirocy- clohexadienone derivatives. Intramolecular arylative dearoma- tization reactions of phenols were developed by Buchwald, You, and Tang to construct benzo 5- or 6-membered

Although dearomative spiro cyclization reactions of phenol derivatives have been described, they mainly involve phenols substituted by carbon or heteroatoms. To the best of our knowledge, dearomative spiro cyclization reactions of halophe- nols are rare. Recently, Luan and co-worker 16   developed the palladium-catalyzed dearomative spiroannulation of bromo- naphthols to provide spirocyclic enone molecules. Very recently, Luan and co-workers demonstrated the elegant dearomative [4 + 1] spiroannulation of halophenols with α , β -unsaturated imines or azoalkenes for the one-step construction of aza spiro cy clo hex a die none derivatives ( Scheme 1 b).   Inspired by Luan’s work, as a part of our ongoing research on dearomative reactions,   we demonstrate herein a mild and efficient dearomative [2 + 1] spiroannulation of bromophenols with electron-deficient alkenes to construct cyclopropyl spirocyclohexadienones ( Scheme 1 c). In addition, from control experiments and DFT calculations, we propose that the intramolecular dehalogenative cyclization step proceeds via a radical-based S RN 1 pathway, though not to the exclusion of the S N 2 pathway.

Mechanistic Studies.  According to the [4 + 1] spiroannulation mechanism proposed by Luan’s group,   we supposed the present [2 + 1] spiroannulation reaction to be initiated by Michael addition of the phenol to the electron- deficient alkene to generate a dearomatized intermediate  IM1 ( Figure 2 ). The debrominative cyclization step of intermediate IM1  to give  7a  would exclude S N 1-substitution via the generation of the  α -carbon cation of the carbonyl group. Therefore, to further determine whether the debrominative cyclization proceeds via a S N 2X, S N 2, or S RN 1 mechanism, the quantum chemical theory was used to explore these processes ( Figure 2 ). As depicted in  Figure 2 , substrates  2a  and  6a generated intermediate  IM1  via the transition state  TS1  with a

Because the activation energy barriers of S N 2 and S RN 1 pathways are the same (9.1 kcal/mol), both pathways are possible in theory.

# CONCLUSIONS  

In summary, we have developed a dearomative [2 + 1] spiroannulation reaction of  p-  and  o -bromophenols with activated olefins (methylene malo nate s) to construct various cyclopropyl spiro cy clo hex a die none skeletons. Furthermore, several other halophenols (X = Cl, I) were also tolerated in this process. Two competing reactions in the reaction system, namely direct Michael addition and dearomative Michael addition following cy clo prop a nation, could be regulated by solvent selection. In addition, the utility of this method is showcased by a gram-scale synthesis and further trans- formations of the dearomatized product into the cycloclavine skeleton. Mechanistic studies show that debrominative cyclization of intermediate  IM1 , the product of dearomative Michael addition of a phenol at its halogenated positions to a methylene malo nate, should involve an intramolecular radical- based S RN 1 mechanism. However, DFT calculations indicated that the intramolecular S N 2 attack, with the same low activation energy barrier in the debrominative cyclization step, cannot be excluded.

General Procedure for Dearomative [2 + 1] Spiroannulation (Represented by Synthesis of Product 3a).  To a dry 10 mL reaction tube equipped with a magnetic stir bar, bromophenol  1a (44.6 mg, 0.2 mmol, 1.0 equiv) and Cs 2 CO 3  (130.3 mg, 0.4 mmol, 2.0 equiv) were added. Then, 2-methylene malo nate  2a  (80.1 mg, 0.4 mmol, 2.0 equiv) in dry THF (2.0 mL) was added. The tube was sealed with rubber plug. The reaction mixture was stirred at room temperature for 3 h. After the reaction was complete (monitored by TLC), the reaction mixture was filtered over a short kieselguhr column and washed with ethyl acetate. The filtrate was concentrated under vacuum to give the crude product, which was purified by silica gel column chromatography (eluent: petroleum ether/ethyl acetate = 20/1) to afford pure product  3a .
