ABSTRACT:  A copper-catalyzed [3 + 2] annulation of  O -acyl oximes with 4-s ulf on ami do phenol s is developed. The advantage of this method lies in the concurrent double activation of two substrates to form nucleophilic enamines and electrophilic quinone monoimines. Q uinone monoimines (QMIs) as excellent electrophiles have been intensively investigated in the [3 + 2]/[3 + 3]/[4 + 2] reaction with electron-rich olefins and 1,3-dipoles, which leads to the formation of di hydro benz of ur an s, benzofurans, di hydro benz ox a zines, 2 H -chromenes, and dihy- dronaphthalene moieties.   QMIs are usually prepared by oxidation of 4-aminophenols with over stoic hio metric oxidants, such as NaIO 4 , Ag 2 O, or PhI(OAc) 2 , and are not easily preserved because of high reactivity. Thus, direct oxidative transformation of 4-aminophenols via the  in situ  generated QMIs has also been explored.   However, a stoichiometric amount of external oxidant is still indispensable.  

On the other hand,  O -acyl oxime is emerging as one of the most shining building blocks in modern organic synthesis 3   via ortho -C Ar -H activation,   aza-Heck-type cyclization,   iminyl radical-initiated 1,5-hydrogen atom transfer/fragmentation, or diradical-initiated di function aliz ation of alkenes.   Because of the oxidative character of N − O bonds,  O -acyl oxime can also act as an oxidant while being used as a reactant, which avoids the use of external oxidant in some cases. The  O -acyl oxime containing an  α -C(sp 3 )-H atom can provide a C − C − N unit to construct structurally diverse  N -heterocycles, including pyr- roles, pyrrolines, pyrazoles, imidazoles, oxazoles, dihydroisox- azoles, thiazoles, 4-pyrrolin-2-ones, pyridines, pyridines, pyrimidines, di hydro pyr imi dines, quinolines, indoles, benzo- thiazoles, thienothiazoles, thieno[3,2- c ]isoquinolines, and 5 H - pyrrolo[2,3- b ]pyrazines via various [3 +  n ] annulations.   The development of new chemistry of  O -acyl oxime in the construction of novel heterocyclic frameworks is still fascinating and challenging.

Under the optimized reaction conditions, the generality of the Cu(OAc) 2 -catalyzed [3 + 2] annulations was examined ( Scheme 2 ). A variety of  O -pivaloyl propiophenone oximes with different substituents on the phenyl ring reacted with  1a to afford the 5-sulfonamidoindoles  3aa − 3ah  in moderate yields. The electron-donating group on the phenyl ring gave a better result than that of the electron-withdrawing group. A sterically hindered substituent on the  ortho -position of phenyl ring led to a decrease in the yield. A primary/secondary alkyl, benzyl, and phenyl group on the  α -carbon of  O -pivaloyl oximes were all tolerated in the reaction with  1a  to furnish  3ai ,  3aj , 3al , and  3am ; however, a  tert -butyl group resulted in the failure of transformation.  O -Pivaloyl heteroaromatic oximes were also compatible with the reaction and provided the furyl- and thienyl-substituted products  3an  and  3ao . The  O -pivaloyl oxime derived from 3,4-di hydro naphtha len-1(2 H )-one gave the product  3ap ′  in 52% yield in which further oxidative de hydro aroma ti z ation occurred. Introducing different func- tional groups on the alkyl chain of  O -pivaloyl oximes could easily synthesize various 3-functionlized alkyl-5-sulfonamidoin- doles  3aq − 3av , including sulfonyl, ester, NHBoc, alkoxyl, and alkenyl group. The 4-(alkyl s ulf on ami do)phenols and different substituted 4-( p -toluene s ulf on ami do)phenols were all compat- ible with the transformation to deliver  3ba − 3fa . Unfortunately, the  O -pivaloyl oxime derived from the aliphatic ketone was not suitable for the reaction and gave a complex mixture.  

When the  O -pivaloyl oxime  4a  having no substituent on the α -carbon was introduced to the reaction with  1a , to our surprise, no anticipated indole product was generated. Instead, the 5-s ulf on ami do benz of ur an-3(2 H )-ones  5a  and  5b  were obtained in 10% and 16% yield, respectively ( Scheme 3 ).

After extensive screening of the reaction conditions ( Tables S2 and S3 ), the optimal conditions were defined as the molar ratio of  1a / 4a /Cu 2 O/ DTBP (di- tert -butyl peroxide) as 1:1.5:0.2:3 and in CHCl 3  at 60  ° C, which afforded  5a  in 33% yield.   Although the yield was not satisfying, this kind of [3 + 2] annulation for the construction of benzofuran-3(2 H )-ones was unprecedented.

O -Acetyl heteroaromatic oximes were also compatible with this transformation to provide the furyl- and thienyl-substituted products  5e  (32%) and  5f  (44%). It was worth noting that halo and alkenyl groups on the alkanoyl chain were also well tolerated in this reaction to afford the products  5h (45%) and  5i  (31%).

For the  α -alkyl/aryl-substituted  O -acyl oxime, intramolecular addition of imine to the carbonyl group followed by dehydration and tautomerization furnishes 5-sulfonamidoin- dole  3 . In summary, we have explored a copper-catalyzed annulation of  O -acyl oximes with 4-s ulf on ami do phenol s on the basis of the double substrate activation mode. In the presence of copper catalyst, the  O -acyl oximes act as an internal oxidant to oxidize the 4-s ulf on ami do phenol s to electrophilic quinone monoimines while reducing themselves to nucleophilic enamines. Interestingly, two different kinds of annulations can be controlled by the  α -substituent of  O -acyl oximes. 5- S ulf on ami do in doles are obtained with  α -alkyl/aryl-substituted O -acyl oximes as the raw material, while 2-amido-5- sulfonamidobenzofuran-3(2 H )-ones are generated with  α - unsubstituted  O -acyl oximes as the reactant.
