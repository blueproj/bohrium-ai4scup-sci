# Flu oro tri flu oro methyl ation of Alkenes Mediated by a Hypervalent Trifluoromethyl-Iodine(III) Reagent  

Mingqin Huang and Chi Zhang \*  

Cite This:  Org. Lett.  2024, 26, 4158−4162  

ABSTRACT:  Herein, we present a novel strategy for synthesizing polyfluorinated compounds by the flu oro tri flu oro methyl ation of olefins, which was achieved through a new trifluoromethyl- iodine(III) reagent  TFNI-1 .  TFNI-1  was readily synthesized via a three-step process, and its structure was characterized by NMR spectroscopy and X-ray crystallography. It is shown by radical trapping and radical clock experiments that the reaction involves the CF 3  radical intermediate.  

F luorinated compounds have attracted considerable attention in various fields, including pharmaceuticals and agrochemicals, due to their beneficial biological activity such as metabolic stability, lipophilicity, and permeability.   Therefore, the development of effective methods for accessing fluorinated compounds is of great importance. The bi function aliz ation of alkenes is a direct approach to the rapid introduction of two functional groups, thereby facilitating the construction of complex molecules.   Flu oro tri flu oro methyl ation of alkenes is one of the most efficient methods for the formation of C(sp 3 ) − F and C(sp 3 ) − CF 3  bonds. In 1979, Zupan et al.   first reported the flu oro tri flu oro methyl ation of styrene using XeF 2 and CF 3 CO 2 H. In 2015, Qing and co-workers 4   reported effective flu oro tri flu oro methyl ation of aliphatic olefins with TMSCF 3  and Selectfluor in the presence of AgOTf, CsF, and PhI(OAc) 2  in DMF. In 2018, Yu and Li et al.   developed the radical flu oro tri flu oro methyl ation of aliphatic olefins employ- ing CsF and Umemoto reagent with the catalysis of Cu(OTf) 2 upon visible light irradiation. In 2019, Ponomarenko and Soloshonok et al.   established the flu oro tri flu oro methyl ation of monosubstituted aliphatic alkenes by a persistent radical perfluoro-3-ethyl-2,4-dimethyl-3-pentyl, which serves as a source of both a trifluoromethyl radical and fluorine atom. In 2020, Cui 7   developed a method for the conversion of aliphatic alkenes to the corresponding flu oro tri flu oro methyl a ted prod- ucts mediated by Mn(OAc) 3 · 2H 2 O with CF 3 SO 2 Na and CF 2 (COOH) 2 . In 2023, Liu and Chen et al.   presented the electrochemical flu oro tri flu oro methyl ation of styrenes utilizing CF 3 SO 2 Na and Et 3 N · 3HF as the CF 3  and F sources, respectively. Although some progress has been made, as mentioned above, the flu oro tri flu oro methyl ation of olefins as a route to polyfluorinated compounds has not yet been fully explored. Since easy access to polyfluorinated compounds would be helpful in increasing the number of polyfluorinated drugs, prodrugs, or drug-like molecules,   there is a need to develop a new protocol for the flu oro tri flu oro methyl ation of

A key feature of hypervalent iodine(III) reagents is their ability to transfer a wide variety of functional groups, including the trifluoromethyl group.   After developing two new hypervalent iodine(III) reagents containing either a fluorine atom 11   or a tri flu oro methyl th io group 12   respectively, we are still interested in synthesizing a novel hypervalent iodine(III) reagent having a trifluoromethyl group. On the basis of the above experimental results and literature reports, we proposed a reaction mechanism ( Figure 2 ). First,  

![](images/809684b9c113fb5c4339051480b10af415cf3b5669cfe82a4e3cfa3ff554cae8.jpg)

Cu(OTf) 2  acts as a single electron transfer (SET) reagent, causing the reduction of reagent  TFNI-1  to produce iodanyl radical intermediate  A , which subsequently decomposed to CF 3  radical and iodine(I) intermediate  B .   CF 3  radical is then captured by the alkene to form the tri flu oro methyl a ted alkyl radical intermediate  C ,   which combines with the fluorine atom from the action of Selectfluor and AgF to give the target flu oro tri flu oro methyl a ted products.  

In summary, we have synthesized two new cyclic CF 3 - containing  λ 3 -iodane reagents,  TFNI-1  and  TFNI-2 , and confirmed their structures.  TFNI-1  can serve as an effective electrophilic CF 3  transfer reagent for challenging fluorotri- flu oro methyl ation of a wide range of terminal alkenes, with Selectfluor being the source of fluorine. The involvement of radicals in this reaction was suggested by TEMPO inhibition and trapping experiments as well as the radical clock experiment. Studies to extend the synthetic utility of  TFNI are currently underway in our laboratory.
