# 1. Introduction  

Organic light-emitting diodes (OLEDs) have developed rapidly  since Dr. Tang prepared the first efficient electro luminescent  devices with low-voltage in 1987  Light weight, high contrast,  wide-viewing angle, high brightness, fast response time, wide  color gamut, low power consumption, and no need for back- light have allowed OLEDs to be a hopeful replacement for other  display technologies, like liquid crystal display. In OLED, the  used materials are mostly organic materials and loose van der  Waals bonds between organic molecules endow OLEDs with  intrinsic flexibility, so OLEDs are viewed as an ideal candidate  for flexible displays.  

Generally, the main roles of the hosts are to transport car- riers, generate excitons, and transfer the exciton energy to the  blue phosphors or TADF emitters. Holes and electrons are  injected from charge transport layers and then transported in  the emitting layer mostly through the hosts although some  carriers are directly trapped by the dopants. Most holes and  electrons recombine in the host and form excitons in the host.  Instead of photon generation by the host, the exciton energy  of the host is transferred to the dopants via Förster and Dexter  energy transfer (FET and DET) mechanisms. Eventually, the  dopant emits light with the assistance of the host suppressing  intermolecular interaction and exciton quenching.  

To fulfil the main functions of the hosts, the host materials  for the blue emitters should meet the following requirements:  i) The host should have a high triplet energy ( E T ) above 2.7  eV to avoid back energy transfer from the dopant to the host;  ii) highest occupied molecular orbital (HOMO) and lowest  unoccupied molecular orbital (LUMO) energy levels are in  close alignment with those of adjacent layers to reduce the  charge-injection barriers and device driving voltages; iii) the  charge mobility of the host materials should be high and bal- anced so that the recombination zone can be widely dispersed  within the emitting layer; iv) the emission spectrum of the  host needs to be overlapped with the absorption spectrum of  the emitter for energy transfer; v) high thermal, morpholog- ical, and chemical bond stabilities are necessary to extend the  device lifetime.  

A lot of hosts have been developed to satisfy the required cri- teria of the hosts for blue phosphors and TADF emitters, which  significantly enhanced the external quantum efficiency and  device lifetime of blue phosphorescent and TADF OLEDs. How- ever, the device lifetime of the high efficiency blue OLEDs is  not long enough and further exploration of the design strategy  and new chemical structure is strongly necessary. Therefore, it  is timely to trace the progress of the high triplet energy hosts  for blue OLEDs and propose the most promising approach to  resolve the device performance issues related with the blue  OLEDs. In this progress report, we systematically summarize  the recent development of host with high  E T  and discuss the  design, properties, and device performances of them. The pur- pose is to better understand the relationship between molecular  structure and properties of host materials and provide valuable  insights into the development of excellent host materials. To  make an easier discussion in this article, we have categorized  the hosts into several groups: bipolar transport host, single  TADF host, TADF assistant host, exciplex host, exciplex free  type mixed host, and electroplex hosts. There is too much work  in bipolar transport host and exciplex host, hence we discussed  the bipolar transport host by n-type groups and the exciplex  host by emission color of the device. Moreover, future possibili- ties of hosts are also proposed.  

# 2. The Categories, Application, and Performance  of High Triplet Energy Hosts  

The high triplet energy host materials can be classified as a  single host and a mixed host. The single hosts include pure- hydrocarbon host materials, hole-transport-type (p-type) hosts,  electron-transport-type (n-type) hosts, bipolar transport hosts,  and TADF hosts, whereas the mixed hosts incorporate exciplex  hosts, exciplex free type mixed hosts and electroplex hosts.  The pure-hydrocarbon host materials, first proposed in 2005,  include no any heterocycles,  and the  E T s of them are usu- ally low, making them suitable for green and red emitters.  

Although several pure-hydrocarbon hosts with high  E T  were  reported recently, the hole and electron mobilities of them  were still low.  The p-type and n-type host materials have a  dominant hole and electron transport nature, respectively.  The bipolar transport hosts can transport hole and electron  evenly relative to the p-type and n-type hosts. Therefore, the  device performances of the bipolar transport hosts are often  better than those of the p-type and n-type hosts due to balanced  charge density and broad recombination zone in the emitting  layer. The TADF host can also be regarded as a bipolar trans- port host because it contains both a hole and an electron trans- port unit. According to its component and function in device, it  can be further classified into a single TADF host, a TADF assis- tant host and an exciplex host. The single TADF host with one  component belongs to intramolecular-type TADF, while the  exciplex TADF host with two components (p-type and n-type  host) are generally defined as an intermolecular-type TADF  host. TADF assistant host means TADF material only acts as  an energy transfer intermediary which harvests the singlet and  triplet excitons of host and transfers these energies to emitters  without any loss. Currently, the TADF-type hosts are the most  effective hosts for highly efficient and long lifetime OLEDs.  Similar to the exciplex host, the exciplex free type mixed host  and electroplex host also consist of p-type and n-type hosts.  However, they are different from the exciplex host in that the  exciplex free type mixed host is just a mixture of a p-type host  and an n-type host without the formation of intermolecular  excited state complex, and the electroplex host is a complex  only generated under an electric field between p-type host and  an n-type host.  Owing to minimized carrier trapping, broad  emission zone and exciton management, the exciton annihila- tion process is restrained effectively, which improves the effi- ciency and lifetime of the electroplex and exciplex free mixed  hosts compared to the single p-type host, n-type host and uni- versal bipolar host. In this work, the p-type and n-type hosts  are not covered separately because they are one component of  the mixed host. Only bipolar transport hosts and TADF type  hosts are handled as the single host.  

# 2.1. Bipolar Transport Host for Blue OLEDs  

Until now, the most successful strategy for the development  of bipolar transport host material is to combine the p-type  group donor (D) and n-type group acceptor (A) directly or  through a  π -linker. The p- and n-type groups can transfer  holes and electrons, respectively, which can improve the  carrier-injection/transport properties and achieve a broad  recombination zone and balanced charge in emitting layer  (EML). Therefore, the D–A or D– π –A molecular structure is  conducive to obtaining the high device performance.  As  shown in  Figure   1 , the common n-type groups include phos- phine oxide (PO), cyano (CN), pyridine, triazole, triazine, car- boline, imidazole, diarylborane, sulfone, etc. The common  p-type groups include carbazole, arylamine, etc. The common  π -linker groups include aromatic ring, thiophene, furfuran,  spirofluorene, etc. In this section, we classify the bipolar  transport hosts in terms of n-type groups and discuss their  performance in blue OLEDs.  

# 2.1.1. Phosphine Oxide-Based Bipolar Host  

The PO moiety with high  E T  and high solubility is known to  transport electrons predominantly. It can deepen the LUMO  level and improve the electron-injection property without  changing the  E T  of the molecule, so it is frequently used in  bipolar transport host materials. The following design strate- gies have been adopted to construct PO-based bipolar host. i)  PO moiety was used as n-type group to attaching to p-type unit  directly; ii) PO moiety was connected with p-type unit through  a linker; iii) PO moiety was served as a secondary acceptor to  combine with other n-type groups; The combination of (ii) and  (iii) was also a useful strategy. iv) PO moiety acted as a non- conjugated  π -linker and n-type group simultaneously and was  inserted between p-type units.  

For the first strategy, PO moiety was mostly attached to car- bazole by different linking fashions and different numbers.  Two PO-based bipolar hosts, PPO1 and PPO2, were devel- oped by modifying 9-phenylcarbazole with one and two diphe- nylphosphine oxide.  The introduction of PO group in host  increased the electron mobility and improved the electron  injection, which resulted in balanced charge transport, EQE max of 18.4% and 17 .1% was achieved for PPO1 and PPO2-based  deep-blue PhOLED, respectively. The quantum efficiency was  further enhanced in mCPPO1 by connecting PO to the carba- zole of  N , N ′ -dicarbazolyl-3,5-benzene (mCP) on account of the  realization of high triplet energy and bipolar charge transport  properties.  The EQE max  of 25.1% was obtained in deep-blue  PhOLEDs, and the EQE can keep above 20% at 2500 cd m − 2 ,  showing small efficiency roll-off. Two soluble PO-based hosts,  CDPO and mCPDPO, were also designed for solution processes  OLED by the integration of PO moiety with 9-phenylcarbazole  and mCP through meta-substitution.  Both hosts showed  similar  E T  ( > 2.80 eV) due to the suppressed  π -conjugation by PO  entity, but the mCPDPO presented enhanced thermal stability  and charge carrier transport. Consequently, the mCPDPO- based device blue TADF OLEDs doped with 2,3,4,5,6-penta(9 H - carbazol-9-yl)benzonitrile (5CzCN) exhibited EQE max  of 15.4%.  

For the second strategy, it usually varies the numbers of  PO moiety and p-type unit, the species of p-type unit and the  linker to optimize the bipolar transport characteristics of host  and improve the performance of the device. The linker can be  nonconjugated unit like fluorene, triphenylamine, tetraphe- nylsilane, etc. or the other high  E T  units. On account  of the sp 3 -hybridized C-9 atom of fluorene breaking conju- gated linkage between functional groups, high  E T  of 2.75  eV  was obtained. Compared with their previous reported unipolar  9-(9-phenylcarbazol-3-yl)-9-phenylfluorene (CF) which was a  PO-free counterpart,  the introduction of the PO moieties  significantly enhanced the amorphous stability and lowered

For the third strategy, PO unit combines with another  n-type group and work as secondary n-type group to enhance  electronic injection and transport. However, to assure a high  E T  value of the host, the other n-type groups need to possess  a weak electron-withdrawing nature to prevent the forma- tion of strong intermolecular charge transfer (ICT). Li and  co-workers modified weak n-type group pyridine with PO unit  and designed two novel PO-based bipolar hosts  m -PyPOmCP  and  p -PyPOmCP.  On account of the unique combination  of PO and pyridine, the LUMO levels of hosts were deepened  while the HOMO levels of hosts were unchanged. Due to large  π -conjugation,  p -PyPOmCP showed slightly lower  E T  (2.78 eV)  than  m -PyPOmCP (2.86  eV). Therefore, by using FIrpic as  a dopant, the  m -PyPOmCP hosted blue PhOLEDs realized  EQE max  of 25.3%, which was higher than  p -PyPOmCP-based  device (EQE max  of 19.1%). The same design strategy was also  adapted to develop  m -POPyCz, and  p -POPyCz hosts through  further modulation of the p-type units, substitution position  and steric effect.  

Di b enzo thi oph ene (DBT) and dibenzofuran with weak elec- tron-withdrawing nature can also combines with PO for con- structing the bipolar PO-based hosts. Certainly, they can also  be viewed as a linker because of their high  E T . Two high triplet  energy hosts (DBT2 and DBT3) were developed by the inte- gration of PO and di b enzo thi oph ene.  It was found that the  ternary host DBT2 showed balanced holes and electrons trans- port. Accordingly, the DBT2-based device exhibited EQE max  of  20.2% when it was used as the host for bis((3,5-difluoro-4-cy- anophenyl)pyridine) iridium picolinate (FCNIrpic) phosphores- cent dopant.  

Recently, Xu and co-workers modified dibenzofuran with  PO and designed four ambipolar transport host materials  (9CzFDBFSPO, 9CzFDBFDPO, 9PhCzFDBFSPO, 9PhCzFD- BFDPO).  The exciton binding energy ( E b ) and molecular  polarity of hosts were tuned by varying the donor and the num- bers of PO units. It was found small  E b  of host accompanied by  strong D–A interactions, which had the advantage in improving  DET form host to the dopants through charge migration. As a  result, in PhOLEDs doped with FIrpic and TADF OLEDs doped  with DMAC-DPS, it was found efficiencies of devices showed  reverse proportion to the  E b  of those hosts. 9CzFDBFDPO per- formed the best device efficiency among all these hosts, pre- senting EQE max  of 23.3% and 20.2% in blue PhOLEDs and  TADF OLEDs, respectively.  

For the last strategy, embedding PO into p-type units can  improve  E T  and carrier transport performance of host effec- tively due to nonconjugated skeleton and electron trans- port properties of PO unit. Chou and Cheng inserted a PO  moiety between two carbazoles and developed host BCPO. The bipolar transport host BCPO showed high triplet energy  ( E T    =  3.01  eV) because PO effectively blocked the electronic  communication between the two  N -phenyl carbazole groups. In the blue PhOLEDs  doped with FCNIrpic, 3DCPO achieved EQE max  of 18.8%.  

As discussed above, the PO moiety was used as the n-type  group to enhance electron transport and nonconjugated  π -linker to improve  E T  of the bipolar transport host in com- bination with various p-type groups like carbazole and triph- enylamine. In particular, the PO moiety was better than any  other n-type group to secure high  E T  while improving the  electron transport properties due to the tetrahedral geometry. However, the PO-based  bipolar hosts have been known to undergo a fatal chemical deg- radation originating from the weak C  P single bond,  which  resulted in decreased operation lifetime of OLEDs with the PO  derived hosts. # 2.1.2. Cyano-Based Bipolar Host  

CN is a widely used electron withdrawing unit to induce elec- tron deficiency and enhance the electron transport character  when it is introduced in organic molecules. Moreover, it has  large bond dissociation energy (BDE) values, which is beneficial  to enhance stability of host materials with little degradation of  the triplet energy by extended conjugation. In the design of the  bipolar hosts, the CN unit was introduced in several different  ways. One of the most common approaches was to modify elec- tron rich carbazole with one CN unit or two CN units to make  the carbazole ring electron deficient. Another method was to  substitute the CN unit to the  π -linker connecting electron rich  carbazole units with other aromatics. Simultaneous modifica- tion of the carbazole and  π -linker with CN unit by combining  the first and second approaches was also reported. The other  method is to design the electron deficient aromatic acceptor  by adding the CN unit to the various aromatic moieties like  dibenzothiophene.  

The carbazole with substitution of CN unit shows moderate  electron deficient nature. Generally, CN unit is modified at  3- or 6-positions of carbazole due to high activity of that posi- tions. And the cyanocarbazole (CN modified at 3-position of  carbazole) combined with other p-type and aromatic groups  through different linking modes to afford bipolar host. Wong  and co-workers reported a well-known bipolar host, mCPCN,  that incorporates one cyano group onto 3-position of carba- zole of mCP.  The introduction of the CN group significantly  improved the balance of hole–electron injection and trans- port, while maintaining the high  E T  of mCP. Blue PhOLEDs  achieved EQE max  of 26.4% using mCPCN as the host material  and FIrpic as the triplet emitter. In another report, nicotinoni- trile was introduced in mCP to afford 5-(9-(3-(9 H -carbazol-9-yl) phenyl)-9 H -carbazol-3-yl)nicotinonitrile (3-PyCNmCP).  How- ever, the  E T  of the 3-PyCNmCP hosts was reduced from 3.03 eV  of mCPCN to 2.76  eV, respectively, due to strong electron  deficiency of the nicotinonitrile compared to CN. The 3-PyC- NmCP were used as the hosts for green phosphorescent tris[2- phenyl pyr id in a to-C 2 ,N] iridium(III) (Ir(ppy) 3 ), and the device  obtained EQE max  of 18.4%.

To reduce the number of weak C  N bonds in  the main backbone unit, the cyanocarbazole was connected via  3-position rather than the 9-position of carbazole. It revealed  that the distorted molecular configuration in  o -CzCN(1) with  ortho linkage mode reduced degree of conjugation, thereby  increasing  E T  by 0.02  eV compared to  m -CzCN(1) with meta  linkage mode. However, the distorted backbone structure of  o -CzCN(1) hampered hole and electron transport due to diffi- cult orbital overlap between molecules. In blue TADF OLEDs  based on 5CzCN as dopant,  o -CzCN(1) and  m -CzCN(1) showed  EQE max  of 16.4% and 15.0%, respectively, which was superior  to that of 3,3-di(9 H -carbazol-9-yl)biphenyl (mCBP). How- ever, the  m -CzCN(1)-based device showed longer lifetime than  mCBP- and  o -CzCN(1)-based devices because of good electron  injection, broad recombination zone and chemical bond sta- bilization effect. Two new bipolar transport host materials, mBFCzCN and  dBFCzCN, were developed by merging the cyanocarbazole with  dibenzofuran which has high  E T  and rigidity.  The effects of  the number of cyanocarbazole units on photophysical and EL  performance of the compounds were investigated. Compared  to dBFCzCN, mBFCzCN with one cyanocarbazole exhibited  higher  E T  and better bipolar character. Therefore, the mBF- CzCN-hosted devices demonstrated high EQE max  of 19.0%  and 21.2% in the FIrpic and Ir(ppy) 3  doped blue and green  PhOLEDs, respectively.  

Instead of dibenzofuran, dimethyl modified terphenyl was  also combined with cyanocarbazole to prepare the bipolar host.  Two host materials,  m -CzCNDMT and  p -CzCNDMT, were syn- thesized by Lee and co-workers  The introduction of methyl  group in the central phenyl unit distorted the backbone struc- ture, leading to high  E T  in the host. In blue PhOLEDs, the  m -CzCNDMT and  p -CzCNDMT demonstrated similar EQE and  relatively small EQE drop at high luminance because CN unit  in the molecule structure improved carrier balance.  

In addition to substituting the CN unit to p-type carbazole,  substituting the CN unit to various  π -linker also provided a  lot of bipolar hosts. The excited state energy levels and charge  transporting ability of the host can be tuned effectively by the  different substitution positions and numbers of CN unit. Duan  and co-workers designed four hosts named 2,4-2CzBN, 2,6- 2CzBN, 3,5-2CzBN, and 3CzBN by attaching CN to benzene  bridge connecting electron rich carbazole units.  The intro- duction of small-sized CN unit had little effect on triplet energy,  but it affected the charge transporting mobilities of the hosts.  It was observed that 3,5-2CzBN and 2,4-2CzBN showed higher  electron mobilities than 2,6-2CzBN, which was attributed to  limited steric effect of the carbazolyl units. Twisted structures  between the carbazole unit and the benzene ring induced the  spatially separated frontier orbital distributions, resulting in  relatively low  Δ E ST  (0.23–0.41 eV).  The 2,3,5,6-tetrakis(3,6-di- tert-butyl-9 H -carbazol-9-yl)benzonitrile (4TCzBN) doped 2,4- 2CzBN TADF device realized EQE max  of 21.5% and ultralow  onset voltage of 2.8 V. Whereas, a record-low operation voltage  of 4.9 V at a practical luminance of 1000 cd m − 2  was acquired in  the 4TCzBN doped 3,5-2CzBN TADF device. The work provided  a strategy for designing high triplet hosts with small  Δ E ST .  

In another report, CNPhCz and DCNPhCz were designed  by connecting different numbers of cyanophenyl groups to  benzene bridge connecting to carbazole.  The  E T  of CNPhCz  with one cyanophenyl was estimated to be 2.67 eV, which was  lower than that of DCNPhCz (2.74 eV), but CNPhCz exhibited  better charge transport abilities than DCNPhCz. In blue and  green PhOLEDs, CNPhCz host device exhibited superior per- formance. However, the  T g  of CNPhCz (49  ° C) was too low in  this work, which might cause crystallization and phase separa- tion and would affect stability of device.  

Li et al. modified  π -linker terphenyl with CN unit and varied  the position of carbazole substitution, developing three iso- meric bipolar hosts, namely  o -CzCN,  m -CzCN, and  p -CzCN. It was observed that the carbazole substitution position affected  the  E T  and charge transporting ability of the hosts. The  m -CzCN  and  o -CzCN hosts revealed higher triplet energies and more  balanced charge transportation. As a result, blue PhOLEDs  with the  m -CzCN host and FIrpic dopant exhibited EQE max  of  23.14%, while the blue TADF OLEDs with  o -CzCN host and  4,5-di(9 H -carbazol-9-yl)phthalonitrile (2CzPN) dopant achieved  EQE max  of 14.98%.  

The position of the CN unit on  π -linker also has an effect  on BDE of hosts, which further affects the device lifetime.  Three CN-based host,  o CzBN,  m CzBN, and  p CzBN with dif- ferent linkage modes relative to carbazole, were prepared to  investigate the effect of BDE of host on the device lifetime of  PhOLEDs.  In blue and green PhOLEDs,  p CzBN with para  positioned CN unit performed the best in terms of lifetime,  while  m CzBN and  o CzBN performed similarly. The BDE cal- culation in the singlet excited state, triplet excited state, anionic  state, and cationic state proposed that BDE in the triplet excited  state was well correlated with device lifetime. This work high- lights importance of the BDE of host in the triplet excited state  on device lifetime.  

Simultaneous modification of the p-type carbazole and  π -linker biphenylene with CN unit afforded four bipolar hosts,  CNCzCN1, CNCzCN2, CNCzCN3, and CNCzCN4.  Carbazole  and cyanocarbazole were linked at ortho position of biphenyl  core to obtain high triplet energy. Carbazole and cyanocarbazole  acted as a hole-transport and an electron transport unit, respec- tively. The CN unit modified biphenyl core strengthened the  electron transport properties and managed the photophysical  properties. It was observed the order of the  E T  was in agree- ment with the dihedral angle of the biphenyl core. CNCzCN1  with CN at C1 positions and CNCzCN4 with CN at C4 positions  showed large dihedral angle because of the steric effect, thereby  accounting for their higher  E T . However, the distorted structure  hindered carrier transport, decreasing the current density of  CNCzCN1 and CNCzCN4. In spite of reduced current density,  CNCzCN1 and CNCzCN4 achieved high EQE max  when they  worked as host for fac-tris(3-(1-(2,6-di isopropyl phenyl)-1 H -im- idazol-2-yl)benzonitrile) iridium (Ir(CNpi) 3 ) phosphor because  of balanced carrier density. In the case of the FIrpic doped blue  PhOLEDs, CNCzCN2 and CNCzCN3 devices exhibited bal- anced carrier density, which lead to higher EQE max .  

The CN unit was also added to aromatic di b enzo thi oph ene  group to design bipolar hosts.  Lee and co-workers compared  the effect of CzDBTCN host with CN-substituted dibenzo- thiophene and DBTCzCN host with CN-substituted carbazole  on device performances. It was observed that CzDBTCN with  the CN substituent on the di b enzo thi oph ene unit showed  a relatively shallow HOMO and a deep LUMO. When it was  employed as host for hole-trapping dopant Ir(dbi) 3 , CzDBTCN  demonstrated less strong hole-trapping effect by the shallow  HOMO level. Therefore, the carrier balance factor endowed  CzDBTCN-based device a high EQE max  of 26.9% and small effi- ciency roll-off. Similar to above work,  it was also observed that  hole transport type mCBP host device presented better opera- tional lifetime than CzDBTCN- and DBTCzCN-based devices  by using hole-trapping dopant Ir(dbi) 3  because of narrow emis- sion zone in the latter devices. To suppress the carrier trapping,  the authors replaced Ir(dbi) 3  with an electron-trapping blue  emitter Ir(CNpi) 3 . As a result, CzDBTCN-based device obtained  a superior lifetime to mCBP-based device. Therefore, it can be  concluded from this work that hole transport type host is pre- ferred in the hole trapping type device, while bipolar type host  is favored in the device with little carrier trapping.  

In short, the CN unit was introduced into the donor,  π -bridge  or other aromatic units to strengthen electron injection and  transport and was effective to allow bipolar charge transport  and high EQE in the devices. Additionally, it was useful to  extend the device lifetime of the blue OLEDs because of high  BDE of the CN unit. Although the CN unit had little effect on  triplet energy, the number and substituting position of the CN  unit were critical to charge transport characteristics and device  lifetime. Therefore, the CN substitution needs to be properly  managed to guarantee high  E T , low driving voltage, high EQE  and long device lifetime. # 2.1.3. Pyridine-Based Bipolar Host  

Pyridine is a common electron-transporting moiety which has  good electron affinity, and relatively deep LUMO level, and  has been successfully used in electron-transporting material.  Therefore, the pyridine unit was incorporated into the mole- cular structure with p-type moieties to provide bipolar hosts.  Carbazole has been popular as the p-type moiety of the pyri- dine derivatives because the combination of weak n-type pyri- dine with p-type carbazole can readily balance carriers for high  efficiency.  

Cheng and co-workers integrated pyridine unit into an  Indolo[3,2-a]carbazole core with high triplet energy, devel- oping a series of host materials (4ICPPy and 4ICDPy). Although the numbers of pyridine units in those three hosts  was dissimilar, their  E T  was similarly because of large twisted  angle between the indolocarbazole moiety and pyridine unit.  4ICPPy and 4ICDPy had relatively balanced electron and hole  mobility and improved thermal stability due to the existence of  pyridine moiety. When they were employed as host for FIrpic,  the 4ICDPy-based device exhibited best performance of EQE  reaching 22.1%. Moreover, green, red and white PhOLEDs  hosted by 4ICDPy realized EQEs of 27 .0%, 25.3%, and 20.3%,  respectively, and small efficiency roll-off.  

Huang and co-workers synthesized a series of pyridine-carba- zole hybrid hosts (2,6-CzPy, 3,5-CzPy, 2,4-CzPy, 2,3-CzPy, 2,4,6- CzPy, 2,3,5,6-CzPy, 2,3,4,6-CzPy) based on the one-step simple  aromatic nucleophilic substitution reactions.  It was found  that the  E T  was nearly irrelevant to the number of carbazole  units, but slightly affected by D–A interactions. For example,  2,3,5,6-CzPy and 2,3,4,6-CzPy had the same number of carba- zole units, but 2,3,5,6-CzPy showed relatively low  E T  due to  strong donor–acceptor interactions. In PhOLEDs doped with  FIrpic, 2,6-CzPy, and 2,4,6-CzPy showed the highest EQE of  18% among the seven hosts. Later, bulky 9-phenylfluorene was  introduced at peripheral carbazole of 2,6-CzPy to enhance mor- phological stability and inhibit concentration quenching. The  corresponding host 2,6-PFCzPy was synthesized.  However,  2,6-PFCzPy-based device displayed inferior EQE max  compared  to 2,6-CzPy-based device in the TADF OLEDs with DMAC-DPS  as dopant. Additionally, the bulky 9-phenylfluorene was also be  inserted between pyridine unit and p-type units. Four hosts,  PyDFCz, PyDFPPA, PyDFPNA, and PyDFDPA, were prepared  for solution processed OLEDs.  The carbazole embedded hosts  showed higher  E T  than the triphenylamine based hosts, which  might due to its weak ICT. When the PyDFPPA and PyDFCz  were evaluated as host for FIrpic phosphor, the PyDFPPA-based  device exhibited better performance than PyDFCz-based device  on account of balanced hole/electron transport, However, all  devices showed poor efficiency and device lifetime.  

As discussed in PO-based and CN-based bipolar hosts, the  properties of host can be cleverly regulated by adjusting the kind  of p-type group or connection mode. CzDPPy and tCzDPPy  were designed by adapting ortho linkage to twist the molecular  conformation and limit the conjugation.  The  E T  values were  determined to be 2.67 eV for CzDPPy and 2.64 eV for tCzDPPy,  respectively. tCzDPPy exhibited a more balanced bipolar carrier  transporting property than CzDPPy. However, the  E T  values of  the two hosts was not high enough to fully harvest the triplet  excitons of the TADF emitter like 2CzPN by energy transfer,  resulting in low efficiency in the TADF OLEDs. By adapting  meta linkage, DCzPPy, DDMACPy, and DTPAPy with different  electron donors combined with pyridine were developed.   E T levels were calculated to be 2.80 eV for DDMACPy, 2.76 eV for  DCzPPy, and 2.70  eV for DTPAPy, respectively. Because the  E T  of the hosts was low for blue device application, they were  used as the host for a bluish-green TADF emitter 9,9-dimethyl- 9,10dihydroacridine-2,4,6-triphenyl-1,3,5-triazine (DMAC-TRZ),  achieving EQE max  of 21.0% in DDMACPy-based solution-pro- cessed OLED device.  

Except for ortho and meta linkage, adopting unconjugated  spacers or attaching steric moiety in the spacers was also used  to limit molecular conjugation and keep a high  E T . A series of  wide bandgap host materials containing unconjugated core  and pyridine were designed (CzSiPy, CzOPy, CzFPy, DCzSiPy,  DCzOPy, and DCzFPy).  It was observed that thermal sta- bility of host depended on central skeletons and followed the  sequence of diphenylether  <  tetra phenyl si lane  <   9,9-biphe- nylfluorene. The hosts with a carbazole dimer showed lower  E T  than those with single carbazole, but they displayed better  device performance than the corresponding single carbazole- containing hosts. It was attributed to the better hole trans- port property of the carbazole dimer. By using biphenyl and  m -methylbiphenyl as  π -spacers, Tang and co-workers developed  four bipolar transport host materials.  The introduction of  methyl group on biphenyl bridge increased the torsion angle  of the biphenyl bridge, thus effectively reducing the degree of  π -conjugation. The hosts with  m -methylbiphenyl as  π -spacers  (DPMBP-CZ, DPMBP-DMAC) provided higher  E T  than those  with biphenyl as  π -spacers (DPBP-CZ, DPBP-DMAC). Among  the hosts, DPMBP-DMAC can transfer energy to dopant FIrpic  efficiently by FET and DET and achieved the best performance  with EQE max  of 25.12%.  

Other than the approaches utilizing the pyridine as an inde- pendent n-type moiety, a design strategy using pyridine to build  a bipolar unit was also employed to develop bipolar hosts. In  one approach, the benzene rings of diphenylamine can be  replaced by pyridine unit to form a bipolar type dipyridyl amine  moiety. # 2.1.4. Carboline-Based Bipolar Host  

Carboline is a bipolar moiety, which modified the carbazole  unit with an unsaturated nitrogen atom. It shows improved  poor electron transport properties relative to carbazole while  keeping the  E T  of carbazole. Hence, carboline moiety is helpful  to improve the efficiency and stability of blue OLEDs because  of improved carrier transport and similar stability with carba- zole. According to the position of unsaturated nitrogen atom,  the carboline can be divided into  α -carboline,   β -carboline, γ -carboline,  and  δ -carboline.  Moreover, the carboline  can be further modified with an additional nitrogen to offer  1,5-diazacarbazole ( Figure   5 ).  Those carboline and carboline  derived units have been successfully applied to the construction  of bipolar blue host due to their high  E T  ( > 3.1 eV) and electron- transport character. Similar to pyridine-based bipolar host,  those carboline and carboline derived units are often combined  with p-type carbazole as well.  

In 2010, Fukagawa et  al. firstly reported the application of  γ -carboline for deep-blue host. The PhOLED using a carbo- line  derived  2,2-bis(4-pyridoindolyl-9-lyphenyl)adamantane  (Ad-Pd) as host and FIr6 phosphor demonstrated EQE max  of  19%, opening a pathway to design blue hosts using the carbo- line moiety. It was observed that  α -carboline  and  β -carboline showed stronger electron accepting properties  than  γ -carboline because of the stabilization distribution of the  LUMO. The  E T  of CzCb1 with  α -carboline was lower than that  of CzCb2 with  β -carboline and CzCb3 with  γ -carboline, but the  deep-blue PhOLEDs using CzCb1 as host achieved best perfor- mance with EQE max  of 24.3%.  

Among the different types of carboline derived hosts, the  α -carboline-based bipolar hosts have been mostly reported due  to high EQE in the devices. The most representative two hosts  were CzBPCb and CbBPCb which were put forward by Lee and  Lee in 2013.  The blue PhOLED employing CbBPCb as host  and FIrpic as emitter acquired a record EQE max  of 30.1% and  EQE of 28.4% at 1000 cd m − 2 . In the same year, Choi and co- workers prepared a novel bipolar transport host (pBCb2Cz) by  combining  α -carboline with bicarbazole. The optimization of the thickness of  the emitting layer rendered mP2CZCB-based PhOLED to have  EQE max  of 25.8% and half-decay device lifetimes of 870 min at  an initial luminance of 100 cd m − 2 .  

To improve quantum efficiency and further promote the  development of the carboline-based host materials, two  α -carboline derivatives, CzOTCb and CbOTCb, were also pro- posed.  The kinked ortho-terphenyl core limited the exten- sion of conjugation and enabled the host high  E T  of 2.90  eV.  An improved charge balance endowed CbOTCb-based device  with EQE max  of 28.8% and EQE of 26.7% at 1000 cd m − 2  in blue  PhOLEDs.  

Inspired by superior performance of the  α -carboline-based  hosts, they were further functionalized with extra carrier trans- port units to manage the carrier transport properties. Huang  and co-workers designed two  α -carboline/pyridine hybrid  bipolar transport host materials, namely 2,6-CbPy and 2,4,6- CbPy,  derived from 2,6-CzPy and 2,4,6-CzPy.  The replace- ment of carbazole unit with  α -carboline significantly improved  the electron transporting abilities and resulted in comparable  hole-transport property simultaneously. As a result, 2,4,6-CbPy  host exhibited slightly higher maximum current efficiency than  2,4,6-CzPy host in sky-blue PhOLEDs. CN unit was also hired to  adjust the charge transport properties of  α -carboline-carbazole  host. Three host materials (CzCbCN, CzCbPh, and CzCbCz)  with different additional groups were developed.  The strong  electron withdrawing CN unit improved electron transport  property, while 9-phenylcarbazole unit worsened it. As a result,  CzCbCN with CN unit exhibited similar hole and electron  transport, realizing EQE max  of 23.4% in the blue PhOLEDs.  

In addition to serving as host for blue phosphorescent  materials,  α -carboline-based derivatives can also work as host  for blue TADF. Two hosts (ZDN and NDN) were synthesized  by tethering different numbers of carbazole and carboline  units to the 2,8-positions of the DBT unit.  The PL spectra  of those hosts overlapped well with the absorption spec- trum of TADF emitter 2CzPN, and  E T  of the hosts were also  higher than 2CzPN, which ensured efficient energy transfer  from the hosts to emitter. With an increase in the number of  α -carboline moieties in the host, 2CzPN (6 wt%)-doped host  films showed shorter delayed exciton lifetime and higher  reverse intersystem crossing rate constant ( k RISC ). However,  NDN with two  α -carboline units exhibited larger nonradiative  decay rate constant ( k T nr ) of the triplet excitons than ZDN with  one  α -carboline. Consequently, the 2CzPN-doped ZDN films  showed proper  k RISC  and  k T nr , which realized EQE max  of 25.7%  in the blue TADF-OLEDs.  

Although the  α -carboline was popular, the  δ -carboline was  also utilized to construct high-performance bipolar transport  host materials for blue PhOLEDs and blue TADF-OLEDs. For  example, Cz35PyDCb with  E T  of 2.96  eV exhibited EQE max of 22.3% and EQE of 20.1% at 1000  cd m − 2  in sky-blue  PhOLEDs.  Similarly, the CzCbPy and 2CzCbPy with the  δ -carboline unit exhibited EQE max  of 22.9% and 18.8%, respec- tively, in blue TADF OLEDs.  The better device performance  of CzCbPy-based TADF OLEDs was due to the balanced charge  transport, higher PLQY, and higher  k RISC  of DMAC-DPS doped  film.  

Although the carboline unit is a bipolar unit, its electron  transport character was just moderate, which encouraged the  design of the carboline derived diazacarbazole with two sp 2 nitrogen units. The 1,5-diazacarbazole has two electron-defi- cient C  N double bond and shows stronger electron with- drawing ability than the other carboline and carbazole. In  2016, 1,5-diazacarbazole was firstly synthesized and employed  for constructing host materials by Wang and co-workers The reported hosts were NCzBPhCz and NCzBPhNCz with  the same  E T  of 2.85  eV. NCzBPhCz exhibited better bipolar  transporting characteristics than NCzBPhNCz because of  similar holes and electron mobility. NCzBPhCz-based blue  PhOLED achieved EQE max  of 24.0% and it outperformed the  NCzBPhNCz-based device. Furthermore, single layer two- color white phosphorescent device using NCzBPhCz as host  obtained EQE max  of 20.7%. Subsequently, the same group  designed a series of host materials based on 1,5-diazacarba- zole (NCzpPh2Cz, NCzpPy2Cz, and NCzmPy2Cz).  It was  found NCzmPy2Cz with meta-substituted pyridine linker had  higher  E T  and balanced charge transportation. Accordingly,  NCzmPy2Cz acquired superior performance of EQE max  of  20.0% in blue PhOLEDs.  

As described, the  α -carboline-based bipolar hosts showed  superior device efficiency relative to other carboline-based  bipolar hosts, and they confirmed great potential as the hosts of  deep-blue device although only several efficient deep-blue devices  have been reported. Therefore, more exploration of the carboline  derived hosts for deep-blue devices to reach high EQE and long  device lifetime is necessary. # 2.1.5. Triazole-Based Bipolar Host  

Triazole is a five-membered heterocycle which has two unsatu- rated nitrogen atoms (N1 and N2), one electron-rich nitrogen  atom (N4), and two carbon atoms. Due to its temperate elec- tron-deficient character, triazole unit has been used for design  of bipolar host.  According to the molecular structure, the  high triplet energy triazole-based hosts can be divided into two  broad categories. One is the hosts with unsubstituted triazole,  the other is the hosts with aryl substituted triazole. By the inte- gration of unsubstituted triazole and aryl substituted triazole  with carbazole and other p-type moiety, various triazole-based  bipolar hosts were reported.  

The unsubstituted triazole unit was firstly reported as n-type  groups to construct host materials by Liu et  al. in 2016. The  ratio of the n-type unsubstituted triazole unit and p-type car- bazole unit was adjusted to afford STzDCz, DTzDCz, and  DTzSCz.  The meta connection of two neighboring groups  grafted on the central phenylene ring twisted molecular confor- mation, thereby providing high  E T  values of 2.81–2.86  eV for  those hosts. Single charge devices of the hosts indicated that  STzDCz with triazole to carbazole ratio of 1:2 most showed  most balanced carrier-transport properties. Therefore, STzDCz  hosted blue and green devices exhibited the best electrolumi- nescence (EL) performances with EQE max  of 25.0% and 20.3%,  respectively. However, the efficiency roll-off of the devices was  severe at 1000 cd m − 2 .  

The unsubstituted triazole unit shows a little weak electron- deficient characteristic, and its electron injection and trans- portation capability are limited. To further improve electron  injection and transportation properties, two hosts (PyTzSCz  and PyTzDCz) were designed by linking electron-deficient pyri- dine and unsubstituted triazole as a n-type group and tuning  the ratio of n-type unit and p-type unit.  It was demonstrated  that with the increase of the n/p ratio, the LUMO level of  host was pulled down and the electron injection barrier was  reduced. PyTzSCz realized a record low turn-on voltage of 2.6 V  and EQE max  of 26.4% in sky-blue FIrpic PhOLED. However, the  efficiency roll-off was still serious.  

When unsubstituted triazole was fused with benzene, a  novel rigid electron-deficient unit, [1,2,4]-triazolo[1,5-a]pyridine  (TP) was designed.  By modifying mCP with TP, two uni- versal host materials,  o -CzTP and  m -CzTP, were obtained. Since  highly twisted link mode (ortho- or meta- linkage) interrupted  the  π -conjugation, these two mCP derivatives exhibited almost  the same  E T . However,  o -CzTP had a smaller  Δ E ST  and higher  hole and electron mobility than  m -CzTP. # 2.2. Single TADF Type Host for Blue OLEDs  

Although the conventional bipolar transport host materials  have made great achievement in PhOLED and TADF-OLED,  they failed to reach high efficiency when the emitter is tradi- tional fluorescent material, because the triplet excitons of the  conventional bipolar transport host and traditional fluorescent  material are all nonradiative. However, this limitation can be  solved when the host possesses a TADF character because the  nonradiative triplet excitons of the host can be upconverted into  radiative singlet excitons followed by FET to fluorescent dopant.  All excitons of the host can be used for FET, fully harvesting  the singlet excitons of the fluorescence dopant. Therefore, the  device efficiency can be enhanced by full utilization of excitons  for radiative process ( Figure   11 a). This is particularly important  for conventional fluorescent devices rather than PhOLED and  TADF-OLED (Figure 11b,c).  

Aside from the basic requirements of common host, the  ideal TADF host should fulfill the following criteria: i) Small  Δ E ST  between their S 1  and T 1  excited states and high  k RISC  are  necessary to enable the fast upconversion of triplet excitons to a  singlet state and reduces the probability of triplet excitons anni- hilation.  For D–A system, the twisted configuration is con- ducive to separate HOMO and LUMO, resulting in small  Δ E ST and high  k RISC .  ii) High PLQY and large overlap between  absorption spectra of dopant and PL spectra of host, which  is essential to ensure effective FET from host to dopant. To date, D–A or D– π –A backbone structure is mostly used in  the design of TADF. The strong ICT from donor to acceptor  usually causes a decrease of singlet and triplet energy, which  is unfavorable for the realization of blue TADF host. There- fore, for D–A or D– π –A single TADF system, it should select  the acceptor with weak electron withdrawing nature or the  donor with weak electron donating nature to manage the ICT  for TADF emission in deep-blue region. Typically, the donor  moieties are selected from carbazole, 3,6-di-tert-butylcarbazole,  acridine, etc., whereas the acceptor moieties are chosen from  triazine, pyrimidine, benzonitrile, diphenyl sulfone, phosphine  oxide, etc.  

The DMAC-DPS is a well-known TADF emitter with pure  blue emission and it is composed of two acridine units and  one diphenyl sulfone unit.  To increase the emission energy  of the host for efficient energy transfer to conventional fluores- cent 2,5,8,11-tetra-tert-butylperylene (TBPe) dopant, Lee and co- workers replaced one acridine unit of DMAC-DPS with weak  electron donating carbazole, and designed a TADF host of  CzAcSF.  As expected, the singlet energy of CzAcSF (3.11 eV)  was higher than that of DMAC-DPS (3.07 eV), and the  Δ E ST  was  calculated to be 0.07  eV. Transient PL measurements verified  the TADF character of CzAcSF from the delayed fluorescence  (DF) lifetime of 5.6  µ s. When TBPe was doped in CzAcSF, the  PL emission of CzAcSF was not observed, indicating efficient  energy transfer from CzAcSF to TBPe. The blue fluorescent  OLEDs using CzAcSF as host and TBPe as emitter achieved  EQE max  of 15.4%, which was more than three times higher than  that of device using 2-methyl-9,10-bis(naphthalen-2-yl)anthra- cene (MADN) as host. Such a remarkable quantum efficiency  was attributed to the efficient FET from CzAcSF to TBPe, little  charge trapping by TBPe, and little DET from CzAcSF to TBPe.  Meanwhile, white fluorescent OLEDs with an EQE max  of 14.0%  and CIE coordinates of (0.31, 0.37) were developed by additional  doping of 2,8-di[t-butyl]-5,11-di[4-(t-butyl)phenyl]6,12-diphenyl- naphthacene (TBRb) in the CzAcSF:TBPe emitting layer.  

In addition to as host for conventional fluorescent dopant,  TADF materials can also serve as host for blue TADF and phos- phorescent emitter. In 2017, Cheng and co-workers reported  the first example of efficient TADF-based host material for  blue phosphorescence and TADF devices.  To obtain host  with high triplet energy, they also adopted carbazole as donor  and diphenyl sul phone as acceptor. It is  noteworthy that the  Δ E ST s of BT-01 and BT-02 were relatively  large, but they still exhibited DF characteristics. The authors  verified that the origin of the delayed emission was TADF  rather than triplet–triplet annihilation (TTA), and attributed  the TADF properties of BT-01 and BT-02 to ICT process. On the other hand,  BT-01 showed better bipolar character, and its emission spectral  overlap well with absorption spectral of FIrpic. Therefore, small  Δ E ST , high  k RISC , effective energy transfer and bipolar character  rendered BT-01-based device to exhibit high EQE max  of 31.8%

Commonly, the system features three components,  wide-energy-gap host, TADF assistant host (also called assis- tant dopant or sensitizer), emitter ( Figures   13  and  14 ). In this  system, the wide-energy-gap host should have a higher  E S  and  E T  than those of TADF assistant host and emitter. It can be con- ventional fluorescence material or TADF material, but in the  case of the TADF material, it is better to possess a high  k RISC The TADF assistant host should have a higher  E S  and  E T  than  the emitter and a high  k RISC . The emitter can be TADF mate- rial or conventional fluorescence material, and it should have  relatively large radiative decay rate constant ( k r S ) for light emis- sion. In the case of conventional fluorescence emitter material,  then DET needs to be suppressed by the following methods:  i) Reducing fluorescence material doping concentration. ii)  Matching the HOMO and the LUMO of fluorescence material  with that of hosts (wide-energy-gap host and TADF assistant  host). iii) Introducing electronically inert steric substituents  into fluorescence emitter or assistant host. iv) Spatially sepa- rating assistant TADF host layer and fluorescent emitting  layer.  Moreover, a large overlap between PL spectra of hosts  and absorption spectra of emitter and high PLQY of hosts are  also required to ensure effective FET process.  

In 2014, Adachi and co-workers reported the first blue OLED  using TADF as the assistant host.  They selected TADF type  molecule ACRSA as the assistant host, DPEPO as the wide- energy-gap host, and conventional fluorescence TBPe with  large  k r S  (2.1  ×  10 8  s − 1 )  as the blue emitter. When doping con- centrations of the TADF assistant host and emitter were 15%  and 1%, respectively, concentration quenching of the assistant  host and DET from the assistant host to fluorescent emitter  were well controlled, resulting in EQE max  of 13.4%, which was  enhanced by about ten times compared with that of the device  without assistant host. In addition, efficiency roll-off was also  well suppressed. However, incomplete FET from the assistant  host ACRSA to emitter TBPe was observed, which broadened  the EL spectra. The CIE coordinate of the device with assistant  host was only (0.17, 0.30) while that of pure TBPe device was  (0.15, 0.21).  

To enhance FET from the assistant host to TBPe emitter  and improve the quantum efficiency and color purity, ACRSA  was replaced by CzAcSF because CzAcSF exhibited deep-blue  emission and good overlap of the PL emission spectrum of  CzAcSF with absorption spectrum of TBPe.  The EQE of  the OLED device using CzAcSF as the assistant host was  improved to 18.1% when doping concentrations of CzAcSF  and TBPe was 50% and 0.1%, respectively. Also, the color  coordinate was (0.15, 0.22), which was very close to that of  pure TBPe device. To further improve color purity, pyrene- based deep-blue fluorescent material, BPPyA, was also  adopted as the fluorescence emitter.  It was found that high  energy transfer efficiency from the assistant host (DMAC- DPS, DMAC-DMT, and SPAC-DMT) to emitter was exhibited  when the assistant host has high PLQY and small  Δ E ST , and  wide-energy-gap host (DPEPO and DBFPO) has high electron  mobility. As a result, EQE max  of 19.0% with color coordinates  of (0.14, 0.15) was achieved in the device with DBFPO: 40%  DMAC-DMT: 0.7% BPPyA.

# 2.4. Exciplex Type Host  

# 2.4.1. Exciplex Hosts for Sky-Blue OLEDs  

The exciplex host are composed of a p-type and n-type host,  and the hole and electron mobilities in the device can be tuned  by adjusting the ratio of p-type and n-type host. Therefore,  due to balanced charge transport and suppression of the TTA  and triplet–polaron annihilation (TPA) degradation mecha- nisms, the exciplex hosts are helpful to acquire high efficien- cies, low driving voltages, and low efficiency roll-offs in the blue  OLEDs. The exciplex host should have high  E T  over 2.7 eV to  be employed for blue PhOLEDs. As the emission energy of the  exciplex is determined by the HOMO level of the p-type host  and the LUMO level of the n-type host, the HOMO–LUMO  offset between the p-type and n-type host is to be appropriately  managed to obtain high singlet and  E T  for blue device applica- tion.  Generally, this can be accomplished by adopting weak  p-type and n-type hosts, but the selection of the suitable host  materials is important to develop the exciplex hosts because  too weak or strong hosts cannot satisfy the requirements of the  blue exciplex hosts.

POPH was synthesized as the phosphine  oxide derived n-type host and the POPH formed exciplex with  common p-type hosts such as TAPC, TCTA, and mCP. The TPBi is a  benzimidazole based weak n-type host and additional diphe- nylphosphine oxide enhanced the electron deficiency for  improved electron transport properties and n-type character.  The peripheral substitution of diphenyl phosphine oxide moie- ties increased the triplet energy and solubility. As a result, the  TCTA:TPOB based exciplex hosts provided low turn-on voltage  of 2.80 V and EQE max  of 13.8% in the blue PhOLEDs.  

Other diphenyl phosphine oxide derived n-type hosts are 2PO  and 3PO.  The 2PO and 3PO were composed of the same  building block of TPO. Diphenylsulfone is also a weak electron deficient unit with  high triplet energy. The BTPS  derived from a diphenyl- sulfone backbone structure has a moderate LUMO level of  − 2.90 eV, which enabled the formation of exciplex with a strong  hole transport type TAPC host. The TAPC:BTPS exciplex pro- vided the singlet and triplet energies of 2.97 and 2.82  eV,  respectively, and the blue PhOLEDs based on the TAPC:BTPS  exciplex host exhibited low driving voltage of 2.90 V, high power  efficiency of 50 lm W − 1 , and high EQE of 21.7%.  

The imidazole derived hosts could also play a role of the  n-type host for the exciplex host. Although the imidazole-based  electron deficient unit is a weak n-type unit, the combination of  the imidazole derivative with a strong p-type host allowed the  exciplex formation. The well-known imidazole derived TPBi  host was blended with TCTA and doped with FIrpic in emitting  layer. The TCTA:TPBi was an exciplex host  and realized high  current and power efficiency of 36.5  cd A − 1  and 41.7  lm W − 1 ,  respectively. TPBi modification with an electron poor pyridine  unit also provided n-type host for the exciplex. As the TPBi host  is not a strong n-type host for exciplex formation with a conven- tional carbazole based p-type host, it was further modified with  pyridine to strengthen the n-type character. The pyridine modi- fied iTPBIPy was the TPBi derived host and mCP:iTPBIPy per- formed as an exciplex host.  Three n-type hosts based on the  TPBi core were developed and the mCP:iTPBIPy applied blue  PhOLEDs exhibited EQE max  of 19.3% with color coordinates of  (0.15, 0.29), which was better than that of the TPBi device. Only  the iTPBIPy was applied for sky-blue PhOLEDs because of the  low triplet energy of other TPBi derivatives.  

The pyridine modified n-type host for the blue exciplex  includes BP4mPy with four pyridine moieties.  It generated  exciplex with a strong p-type TCTA host and a large HOMO– LUMO gap over 3.0 eV was obtained for exciton confinement.  The TCTA:BP4mPy exciplex based blue device showed EQE max of 15.8%, indicating the effectiveness of the exciplex host.  Another pyridine derived n-type host is CbPyCN designed as a  bipolar n-type host.  The carboline in CbPyCN was adopted  for high triplet energy and good electron transport character.  The large HOMO and LUMO offsets of 0.58 and 0.76 eV with  TAPC allowed the formation of exciplex and the exciplex showed  the peak emission wavelength of 520 nm. In the exciplex host  based FIrpic device, the EQE max  of 13.3% was achieved because  of the low triplet energy of the exciplex.

# 2.4.2. Exciplex Hosts for Deep-Blue OLEDs  

In spite of the superiority of the exciplex host, it is difficult to  apply it for deep-blue PhOLEDs due to the low triplet energy  of exciplex caused by high binding energy between p-type  host and n-type host for exciplex formation. In general, the  triplet energy of the exciplex host needs to be over 2.8  eV to  utilize for deep-blue PhOLEDs, but the conventional approach  cannot satisfy the triplet energy specification. Therefore, a few  design strategies have been proposed to develop the high tri- plet energy exciplexes. One approach is to control the HOMO/ LUMO level of the p-type host and the n-type host to manip- ulate the HOMO–LUMO offset of the exciplex. Either weak  p-type or weak n-type host or bipolar transport host can fulfill  the requirement for the high triplet energy in the exciplex host.  Another approach is to introduce large blocking groups to spa- tially separate the p-type and n-type hosts for weak binding. The

# 2.5. Exciplex Free Type Mixed Host  

# 2.5.1. Conventional Mixed Host for Blue OLEDs  

In the case of exciplex free type mixed host, the p-type and  n-type hosts transport holes and electrons without forming  the exciplex through the small HOMO–LUMO offset between  them. This indicates that they need to be weak p-type or n-type  hosts to avoid the exciplex generation. The exclusion of the  exciplex may allow high singlet and triplet energy in the mixed  host once the p-type and n-type hosts have high singlet and  triplet energy. Similar to the exciplex host, the mixed host can  also achieve charge balance and wide recombination zone,  improving the device efficiency and efficiency roll-off. The  p-type and n-type hosts are generally designed using electron  rich and electron poor units to enhance the carrier injection  and transport properties.  

Several silane-based host materials were noticed in the  development of the mixed host because the silyl unit itself has  weak electron transport properties and can secure high triplet  energy. A mixed host of UGH3 with two p-type hosts, mCP  and TCTA, was reported as the exciplex free mixed host. The UGH3 is a silane-based host with wide HOMO–LUMO  gap, weak n-type character and high triplet energy of 3.50 eV  for blue phosphor. The FIrpic was used as a blue phosphor  and the EQEs were 15.3% in the mCP:UGH3 device and  21.6% in the TCTA:UGH3 device. In addition, the mCP:UGH3  mixed host was applied as the host of the FIr6 phosphor with a  triplet energy of 2.72 eV.  The blue device showed high EQE  of 22.9% with color coordinate of (0.16, 0.29) due to the high  triplet energy of two hosts.

The mCBP and oCBP have been frequently used as the  p-type hosts in the mixed hosts due to chemical stability and  good hole transport properties. The isomeric mCBP and oCBP  were mixed with mCBP-1CN which has CN unit in biphenyl  core for deep LUMO level ( − 2.95 eV) and good electron trans- port properties. Although the mCBP has been widely used as the p-type host,  it can be easily degraded under negative polarons because of  the low BDE of the C  N bond in anionic state. To overcome  the low BDE and instability under negative polarons, the p-type  hosts with bipolar character were developed by substituting a  weak electron withdrawing unit. The oCzBN was synthesized  as the p-type host with a benzonitrile electron accepting unit  for stability under negative polarons.  The oCzBN showed  high triplet energy of 3.02 eV as a host for deep-blue PhOLEDs.  The mSiTrz was used as an n-type host in the mixed host and  the Ir(cb) 3  was chosen as a deep-blue phosphor. The blue  PhOLEDs with the oCzBN:mSiTrz mixed host exhibited high  EQE of 21.6% and elongated device lifetime compared to the  conventional p-type host without the CN substituent. The nega- tive polaron stability of the oCzBN host was much better than  that of the common p-type host such as mCBP, which was cor- related with the device lifetime of the blue PhOLEDs. Similarly,  the bicarbazole derived p-type hosts with the CN substituent  were also developed. The backbone structure of the hosts was  9-phenyl-9 H ,9’ H -3,4’-bicarbazole which has two carbazole  units connected through the 3 and 4 position of them. The  largely twisted backbone structure assisted realizing the triplet  energy and modified with dibenzofuran and benzonitrile as  the negative polaron stabilizing units. Two hosts, DBFBCz and  BNBCz,  with high triplet energy of 2.90 eV were employed  as the p-type hosts for the Ir(CNpi) 3  doped devices. The EQEs  of the DBFBCz:mSiTrz and BNBCz:mSiTrz devices were 18.6%  and 19.1%, respectively with an extended device lifetime com- pared to the mCBP:mSiTrz device.  

The CN group is attractive moiety for n-type host since it can  secure the high triplet energy with less conjugation extension  and good electron transport property. The CN modified carba- zole and benzonitrile were utilized as the building blocks of the  n-type hosts. Four isomeric n-type hosts, 1CNCzBN, 2CNCzBN,  3CNCzBN, and 4CNCzBN, were synthesized by altering the  position of CN group in carbazole.  All four hosts showed  high triplet energy above 2.85 eV and the 3CNCzBN exhibited  the highest triplet energy (3.04 eV) among the series. Based on  the availability, the four hosts were mixed with mCBP and doped  with Ir(CNpi) 3 . The blue PhOLEDs realized high EQE over 20%  with color coordinates of (0.16, 0.32). Additionally, the 3CNCzBN  device achieved stable device lifetime of 20 h at 1000 cd m − 2 .  

The CN substituted dibenzofuran derivatives were developed  as the n-type host with high triplet energy based on the elec- tron stability and transport property of dibenzofuran. Two CN  units and dibenzofuran moieties were merged to the biphenyl  framework by changing the connecting position.  The diben- zofuran was substituted at ortho position of biphenyl to obtain  high triplet energy through large dihedral angle. Four n-type  hosts exhibited quite similar triplet energy close to 2.85  eV  by steric hindrance. The deep LUMO levels of the n-type  hosts close to  − 3.0 eV were adequate to apply for blue TADF  OLEDs. The n-type hosts were mixed with mCBP and 5CzCN  was used as a sky-blue TADF emitter.  The mCN4DBF based  device showed improved EQE of 17 .3% with color coordinate of  (0.17, 0.35) and device lifetime of 19.1 h compared to the mCBP  based device. Another n-type host material with the same func- tional group was 1DBF-2CNCZ, 2DBF-2CNCZ, and 4DBF- 2CNCZ.  The three hosts were comprised of dibenzofuran,  benzonitrile, and 3,6-di cyano car baz ole and fully interconnected  through ortho position for high triplet energy. The 5CzCN  device was fabricated using the mixed host of mCBP and n-type  hosts. The EQEs of the 5CzCN device were 21.1%, 22.0%, and  21.8% in 1DBF-2CNCZ, 2DBF-2CNCZ, and 4DBF-2CNCZ  hosts, respectively, and the device lifetime of three devices was  over 30 h.  

Triazine is common electron acceptor moiety and triazine- dibenzofuran type compounds, DDBFT and TDBFT, were  developed for application in the mixed host with mCBP for  blue TADF OLED.  The dibenzofuran moieties supported  good electron transport property and electron stability while  maintaining high triplet energy. Both n-type hosts showed high  triplet energy of 2.95 eV and were evaluated as the mixed host  with mCBP for the 5CzCN emitter. The mixed host devices  reduced driving voltage, increased EQE, and extended lifetime  compared to the single host devices.  

The benzo[4,5]thieno[3,2-d]pyrimidine (BTP) derivatives  are also weak n-type hosts for the mixed host. The BTP core  is a weaker electron deficient unit than triazine, but has  similar triplet energy, confirming the potential of the BTP  derivatives as the n-type hosts. The BTP based n-type hosts  embedded with carbazole or tetra phenyl sil yl groups were  reported.  The carbazole was introduced to increase the posi- tive polaron stability and the tetra phenyl sil yl group was sub- stituted to restrain intermolecular interaction. The CzBTPmSi,  mSiBTPCz, and 2mSiBTP were the BTP derived n-type hosts.  The three hosts were mixed with mCBP and the Ir(CNpi) 3 doped devices achieved high EQE over 20% and long device  lifetime over 1000 h. Furthermore, the mCBP:CzBTPmSi  mixed host showed high EQE of 19.9% and device lifetime of  1162 h with color coordinate of (0.13, 0.17) in the Ir(cb) 3  doped  device.  

In addition to the vacuum-deposited OLEDs, some host  materials were targeted to the solution-processed OLEDs which  require good solubility in organic solvent and uniform mor- phology without crystallization in film state. The silane and  pyridine based n-type hosts, 2PTPS, 3PTPS, and 4PTPS, were designed for the soluble OLEDs because the tetrahedral  structure of tetra phenyl sil yl backbone structure can provide  good solubility and stable morphology along with interrupted  conjugation for wide bandgap and high triplet energy. Three  hosts showed wide HOMO–LUMO gap over 4.0  eV and high  triplet energy above 2.80  eV for exciton confinement. The  FIrpic doped mixed host device with the three n-type hosts  and TCTA achieved high EQE of 20.9%, 19.6%, and 14.3% for  2PTPS, 3TPTS, and 4PTPS, respectively.

# 2.5.2. Electroplex Hosts for Blue OLEDs  

An electroplex host is an alternative of the exciplex host for  blue PhOLEDs. The electroplex is an intermolecular complex  with a weak binding energy formed only under an electric field.  Therefore, it can easily achieve high emission energy com- pared with the exciplex host, which makes it suitable for blue  device application. In addition, the electroplex host can behave  like an exciplex and take advantage of an energy transfer pro- cess for improved lifetimes in the emission process of dopant  materials. The p-type and n-type hosts with proper HOMO  and LUMO are required to prepare the electroplex host. Exces- sively small or large differences in the HOMO and LUMO  offset between the two hosts can lead to an electroplax free  host. The mCBP:DBFTrz mixed host was developed as the  first electroplex host for blue PhOLEDs.  The mCBP and  DBFTrz were selected as the p-type and n-type hosts of the  electroplex because they have high triplet energy above 2.8 eV  and large HOMO–LUMO offset while preventing the exciplex  formation. The HOMO and LUMO offset of around 0.7  eV  between the two hosts enabled the electroplex generation and  the mCBP:DBFTrz electroplex based blue device doped with  Ir(CNpi) 3  emitter showed high efficiency of 18.0% and more  than four times long device lifetime compared with the single  host based blue device.  

Based on the demonstration of the electroplex host as the  host for long device lifetime, a bipolar type n-host, SiCzTrz, was  synthesized for the electroplex host of deep-blue PhOLEDs. The SiCzTrz host had a diphenyl tria zine core structure modified  with the carbazole and triphenylsilyl groups. The carbazole sub- stituted diphenyl tria zine core structure can control the HOMO/ LUMO level and the triphenylsilyl group surrounding the core  assisted obtaining high emission energy by suppressing inter- molecular packing. The mCBP:SiCzTrz electroplex host doped  with pure blue Ir(cb) 3  phosphor achieved high EQE of 27 .6%  with color coordinate of (0.12, 0.13) in top-emitting device.  Especially, the electroplex host device showed half luminance  device lifetime over 10 000 h at 100 cd m − 2 . In bottom-emitting  device, the exciplex device showed EQE max  of 20.3% and 8460 h  at 100 cd m − 2  with color coordinate of (0.14, 0.19).  

Until now, only a few electroplex hosts have been demon- strated, but they are comparable to the exciplex host in terms  of EQE and device lifetime. In particular, they are advantageous  in the deep-blue OLEDs because high triplet energy can be  easily reached due to small exciton binding energy. Therefore,  they are promising as the host for the deep-blue OLEDs. High EQE was  successfully achieved in the blue OLEDs by harvesting the  triplet excitons of the blue emitters using the various high tri- plet energy hosts. However, the device lifetime of the high effi- ciency blue OLEDs is not long enough although some mixed  hosts demonstrated improved device lifetime. Currently, the  mixed hosts such as exciplex host, electroplex host, and conven- tional mixed host perform better than the single hosts such as  unipolar host, bipolar host, and TADF host. Among the mixed  hosts, the exciplex and electroplex hosts are superior to the con- ventional mixed host in terms of device lifetime. Among the  singlet hosts, the bipolar host and TADF host are better than  the unipolar host in terms of device lifetime. Therefore, an  advanced host development strategy based on the device per- formances of the various hosts is required in order to further  extend the device lifetime of the blue OLEDs.  

First, considering the operation mechanism of the mixed  host and single host, the mixed host is preferred because the  degradation pathways can be reduced in the mixed host. In  particular, the TPA induced degradation can be handled, which  extends the device lifetime. Second, among the mixed hosts,  exciplex or electroplex type hosts are desired because energy  transfer governs the emission mechanism of the device with  less charge trapping. The TPA mechanism can be managed  in those hosts because polaron density would be minimized.  Third, TADF type host embedded exciplex or electroplex host  would upgrade the device lifetime because the TADF type host  would relieve the degradation of the host by reducing the triplet  exciton lifetime of the host. Either p-type or n-type host can be  the TADF type host in the exciplex or electroplex host, but it can  suppress the serious degradation of the host by polaron leakage  (positive polaron leakage from p-type host to n-type host or  negative polaron leakage from n-type host to p-type host) due  to the upconversion process in the TADF type host and polaron  stability. Fourth, the TADF type exciplex host made up of TADF  based p-type or n-type host would be the best host system  judging from the degradation mechanism of the high efficiency  blue OLEDs. This approach can minimize the triplet exciton  lifetime in the host and carrier trapping in the dopants, leading  to the suppression of the triplet exciton or polaron related deg- radation phenomena ( Figure   21 ). At present, only the first and  second approaches were demonstrated because it is very diffi- cult to come up with the high triplet energy and TADF based  p-type or n-type host. However, the third and fourth approaches  can be realized by diversifying the molecular design of the  hosts, which will enable the achievement of the high efficiency  and long lifetime blue OLEDs for practical applications.

Yaxiong Wang  received his Bachelor degree in Chemical Engineering and Technology from  Nanchang University, China in 2018. He is now a Ph.D. candidate working at Wuhan National  Laboratory for Optoelectronics, Huazhong University of Science and Technology under the super- vision of Prof. Lei Wang. His main research topics are the synthesis and the device application of  host and TADF materials for high efficiency and long-lifetime organic light-emitting diodes.  

Ju Hui Yun  received her Ph.D. Degree (2020) from School of Chemical Engineering of  Sungkyunkwan University, Korea. Her main research areas are synthesis of high triplet energy  hosts for high efficiency and long device lifetime in the blue phosphorescent and thermally acti- vated delayed fluorescence organic light-emitting diodes. She also developed highly efficient red  and blue thermally activated delayed fluorescent emitters.  

Lei Wang  received his Ph.D. degree from Department of Chemistry of Wuhan University, China  in 2005. After postdoc at Hong Kong Baptist University (2005–2008) he joined Wuhan National  Laboratory for Optoelectronics as an associate professor and was promoted to the position of  professor in 2015. He devoted his research interests on OLED and QLED materials and devices.  His work on blue material based on anthracene and bipolar transport host for phosphor emitter,  TADF material, PeLED based on perovskite nanocrystals have a major impact on the development  of new LED.  

Jun Yeob Lee  received his Ph.D. degree from Seoul National University, Korea in 1998. After  postdoc at Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute (1998–1999) he joined Samsung SDI and developed  active matrix organic light emitting diode for 6 years. After that, he worked as a professor at  Department of Polymer Science and Engineering at Dankook University, and he is now a professor  at School of Chemical Engineering of Sungkyunkwan University from 2015. His main research  areas are synthesis of organic electronic materials and development of novel device structure for  organic electronic devices.
