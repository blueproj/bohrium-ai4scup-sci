# Synthesis and photovoltaic properties of two-dimension-conjugated D – A copolymers based on benz odi thi oph ene or benzodifuran units  

Yi Zhang,   Lei Gao,   Chang He,   Qingjiang Sun \* a   and Yongfang Li \* b  

A series of two-dimension-conjugated D – p – A copolymers based on benz odi thi oph ene (BDT) or benzodifuran (BDF) as the donor unit, b enzo thi a diaz ole (BT) as the acceptor unit and thiophene as the p -bridge, were designed and synthesized for application as donor materials in polymer solar cells (PSCs). The polymers include PBDTT-BT based on BDT with a thiophene side chain, PBDTF-BT based on BDT with a furan side chain, PBDFT-BT based on BDF with a thiophene side chain and PBDFF-BT based on BDF with a furan side chain. The replacement of some of the thiophene rings in the donor unit with furan rings in ﬂ uences the UV-vis absorption spectra, electronic energy levels and band gaps of the copolymers. The HOMO levels of the copolymers based on BDF are  ca.  0.1 eV up-shifted in comparison with that of the polymers based on BDT. While the furan side chain makes the LUMO level shift downwards a little. Power conversion e ﬃ ciency (PCE) of the PSCs based on the polymers as the donor and [6,6]-phenyl C 71 -butyric acid methyl ester (PC 71 BM) as the acceptor reached 1.85% for PBDTT-BT, 2.88% for PBDTF-BT, 4.42% for PBDFT-BT and 2.60% for PBDFF-BT. The results indicate that the replacement of thiophene rings by furan rings in the 2-D-conjugated BDT unit signi ﬁ cantly in ﬂ uences the photovoltaic properties of the copolymers.  

# Introduction  

Bulk heterojunction (BHJ) polymer solar cells (PSCs) have been intensively studied in recent years, due to their potential applications in large area, lightweight and   exible photovoltaic panels. Great e ﬀ ort has been devoted to the design and synthesis of new conjugated polymer donor materials 1 – 9   and new fullerene derivative acceptor materials 10 – 13   for improving the power conversion e ﬃ ciency (PCE) of PSCs, and over 7% power conversion e ﬃ ciency (PCE) values have been reported by several research groups. – b  

Broad absorption with narrower bandgap, relatively lower HOMO energy levels and higher hole mobility are pursued in the molecular design of high e ﬃ ciency polymer donor materi- als. a   D – A co polymerization has been proven to be an e ﬃ cient way to meet the requirement. Many donor and acceptor building blocks have been used in the design and synthesis of e ﬃ cient D – A copolymer photovoltaic materials. Among various donor units in the D – A copolymers, benzo[1,2- b :4,5- b 0 ]dithio- phene (BDT), with a symmetric and planar conjugated struc- ture, is the most important donor unit in constructing high e ﬃ ciency polymer donor materials. – 17   Benzo[1,2- b :4,5- b 0 ] difuran (BDF), which is similar to BDT with its two thiophene rings replaced by two furan rings, was also introduced to poly- mer donor materials recently.   In addition, the two-dimension- conjugated (2-D-conjugated) BDT unit with thiophene conju- gated side chains is superior to the BDT unit with alkoxy side chains.   For the acceptor units, b enzo thi a diaz ole (BT) has been widely used in high e ﬃ ciency D – A copolymer photovoltaic materials in PSCs. – 21  

In this study, we designed and synthesized four 2-D-conju- gated D – p – A copolymers based on BDT or BDF with thiophene (T) or furan (F) conjugated side-chains as donor units, BT as the acceptor unit and thiophene as the  p -bridge, for investigating the e ﬀ ect of replacing thiophene units with furan units in the BDT donor unit on the photovoltaic properties of the copoly- mers. The four polymers include PBDTT-BT based on BDT with a thiophene side chain, PBDTF-BT based on BDT with a furan side chain, PBDFT-BT based on BDF with a thiophene side chain and PBDFF-BT based on BDF with a furan side chain, as shown in Fig. 1. The PSCs based on the 2-D-conjugated copoly- mers as the donor and [6,6]-phenyl C 71 -butyric acid methyl ester (PC 71 BM) as the acceptor demonstrated the power conversion e ﬃ ciencies of 1.85% for PBDTT-BT, 2.88% for PBDTF-BT, 4.42% for PBDFT-BT and 2.60% for PBDFF-BT, indicating the signi  - cant in  uence of the replacement of thiophene rings by furan rings in the 2-D-conjugated BDT unit.

# Results and discussion  

# Synthesis and characterization of the copolymers  

The synthesis route of the monomers and corresponding poly- mers are outlined in Scheme 1. It is important to note that the alkyl side chain of the polymers plays a critical role in deter- mining the solubility and photovoltaic performance of the polymers. b   In this work, the thiophene -bridge in the main  p chain of the polymers with di ﬀ erent alkyl (hexyl and 2-ethyl- hexyl) chains was used in the synthesis of the D – p – A copoly- mers. It was found that the thiophene  p -bridge with a hexyl chain led to poor solubility of the copolymers. Thus the thio- phene with a 2-ethylhexyl side chain was chosen as the  p - bridges in the copolymers. Polymers PBDTT-BT, PBDTF-BT, PBDFT-BT and PBDFF-BT were all synthesized by palladium- catalyzed Stille-coupling 27   polymerization. Thermal properties of the polymers were determined by thermo grav i metric analysis (TGA) under nitrogen atmosphere at a heating rate of 10    C min  1 . The four polymers have good thermal stability with 5% weight loss temperatures at 331, 334, 386 and 312    C, respec- tively, as shown in Fig. 2. Obviously, the thermal stability of these polymers is adequate for their applications in PSCs.

# Conclusion  

In summary, we designed and synthesized four 2-D-conjugated D – p – A copolymers PBDTT-BT, PBDTF-BT, PBDFT-BT and PBDFF-BT based on BDT or BDF with thiophene (T) or furan (F)  

conjugated side-chain as the donor units, BT as the acceptor unit and thiophene as the  p -bridge. These polymers showed lower energy bandgaps, good solubility in common organic solvents and good thermal stability. The copolymers based on BDF possess a lower bandgap and broader absorption, but a higher HOMO energy level. The PCE of the PSCs based on the polymers as the donor and PC 71 BM as acceptor with a donor – acceptor weight ratio of 1 : 1 reached 1.85% for PBDTT-BT, 2.88% for PBDTF-BT, 4.42% for PBDFT-BT and 2.60% for PBDFF-BT, respectively. Obviously, PBDFT-BT based on the BDF unit with thiophene conjugated side chains shows the best photovoltaic performance, which could be as a bene  t of the broad absorption and higher hole mobility (9.0 s  1 ) of PBDFT-BT. These results indicate that BDFT (BDF with    10  3   cm 2   V  1 thiophene conjugated side chains) is a promising new donor unit in constructing high e ﬃ ciency D – p – A copolymer donor materials for PSCs.
