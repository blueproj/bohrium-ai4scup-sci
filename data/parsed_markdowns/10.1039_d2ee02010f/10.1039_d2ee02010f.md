# Abstract  

Molten salt extraction is a promising means for tritium recovery from the fusion reactor blanket material of liquid lithium. In support of the development of this method, the solubility of lithium salt into liquid lithium was measured in some typical systems.The solubility were obtained as

# 1. Introduction  

Self-cooled liquid lithium blanket system is one of the most promising[1].By combining the cooling and breeding functions,theblanket systembecomes very simple. Because of the higher tritium solubility,how- ever,tritium recovery is recognizedtobe critically importantfortheuseofthissystem.Inordertomeeta design goal of 1 wp pm for the tritium concentration in lithium,a number of tritium recovery methods have been proposed up to the present.These are molten salt extraction[2,3], window permeation[4], gettering [5], and combined gettering and molten salt process [6] Amongthese,molten salt extraction is the most hope ful because it is essentially free from any solid state diffusion processes.  

Thepresent authorshave already studied the ther- mo dynamic and kinetic aspects of tritium extraction [7-9]. It has been confirmed that both the distribution and mass transfer coefficients of tritium are high enough for the further development of the process.  

Following these studies,the solubility of molten salt into liquid lithium has been investigated in the present study,since the presence of dissolved salt in the lithium metal phase is of some concern in connection with blanket neutron ics,corrosion,hazardous transmutation products,etc.Intheexperiment,thesolubilities ofLiF, LiCl,and LiBri n liquid lithium were measured.The results arediscussed for the consequences of having salt dissolved inlithium.  

# 2. Experimental  

All the chemicals were of reagent grade obtained from Nacalai Tesque,Inc.The lithium salts of LiF LiCl, LiBr,and LiF-LiCl (50-50 mol%),were exam- ined for the salt phase. Each salt of 15 cm? was melted with lithium of 50 cm?in a stainless steel container under an inert gas atmosphere.Afterafewhours e qui lib ration at given temperatures,thelithiumphase samples were taken out by using a stainless steel sam- plingtube.The samples were dissolved into a buffer solutionofpH7,and the halide ion concentrations were measured with calibrated ion electrodes,Horiba Ltd.

# 4. Conclusions  

(1) The solubilities of LiF, LiCl and LiBr in liquid lithium were measured, and the observed tempera- turedependences were analyzed to derive some thermodynamic quantities.

 (2)The solubilitiesof themixture salts were compared with those of single salts,and no significant devi a- tionfromtheidealsolutionbehaviorwasobserved for the mixture salts.

 (3)The solubilities of LiF,LiClandLiBr inliquid lithium were evaluated for the reference mixture salt of LiF-LiCl-LiBr at 773 K (500°C), and some impacts on hazardous transmutation products were discussed.
