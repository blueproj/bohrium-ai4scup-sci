The calculation model on the basis of multi-phase-ﬁeld model, coupled with the calculation of phase dia- gram (CALPHAD) approach and classical nucleation theory (CNT) model, was studied for the precipitation behavior of  c 0   precipitates in Ni-Co base superalloy TMW-4M3 (‘‘TMW” is a trademark of National Institute for Materials Science registered in Japan) during the heat treatment process. This alloy is precip- itation strengthened by  c 0 , and thus the control of size and distribution of  c 0   precipitates is very important for practical applications. Precipitation behavior of  c 0   in TMW-4M3 is strongly inﬂuenced by heat treat- ment conditions. Therefore, numerical simulation is useful for ﬁnding the most appropriate manufactur- ing condition prior to experimental trial and error approach. Firstly, we investigated the  c 0   solvus temperature and improved several thermodynamic parameters of the conventional database to obtain reasonable phase boundaries between  c / g (Ni 3 Ti) and  c / c 0 . Based on the new thermodynamic description, the nucleation rates related to temperature histories were calculated by using the CNT with composition balance equation under subsolvus solution treatment. Finally, the multi-phase-ﬁeld method coupled with the CALPHAD approach and CNT model was applied to the microstructure evolution of intragranular secondary  c 0   particles. The experimental and simulated microstructures show good agreement with each other. This method might be useful for microstructure evolution prediction of  c / c 0   dual phase during the heat-treatment.

After the standard disk making process, we can see three types of  c 0   precip- itates, primary  c 0 , secondary  c 0 , and tertiary  c 0 . Primary  c 0   is the pinning particle of  c  grain boundaries. Secondary  c 0   and tertiary c 0   are intragranular precipitates. Precipitation behavior of these two intragranular  c 0   particles are inﬂuenced a lot by heat treat- ment conditions and also very important for precipitation strengthening.  

A lot of heat-resistant superalloys, such as high temperature materials applied to the turbine disk of aircraft jet engines or power generators, have been developed. TMW-4M3 is one devel- oped by the National Institute for Material Science in Japan  [1] . This alloy, a Ni-Co base superalloy, was designed with good phase stability and thermal resistance by adding Co and Ti to Alloy 720Li. The nominal compositions of TMW-4M3 and Alloy 720Li are shown in  Table 1 . Since the Ti composition is higher than that of the alloy 720Li, the amount of  c 0   phase increases, which has a pre- cipitation strengthening effect. The maximum volume fraction of  c 0 phase is about 50% for TMW-4M3 and 45% for Alloy 720Li.  

The turbine disk, for example, is a relatively large forged item whose diameter is about 1 m, so it costs a lot to evaluate the rela- tionship between the manufacturing process and microstructures, which determines mechanical properties, for many conditions in experiments. In this view point, numerical simulation is one of the strongest tools for ﬁnding the most appropriate manufacturing condition. For example, phase-ﬁeld simulation has been used to predict the coarsening, shape evolution, shearing and rafting of  c 0 in Ni-base superalloys  [4–7] . Precipitation during the continuous cooling process from supersolvus temperature was also investi- gated  [3,8] . On the other hand, the solution treatment conditions after the forging process is usually deﬁned as subsolvus tempera- ture of  c 0   precipitates to make maximal use of their pinning effect. So the availability of commercial database for TMW- 4M3 was checked and re-assessed several thermodynamic param- eters. In microstructure prediction, nucleation conditions are also important. However, the nucleation is not introduced in the phase-ﬁeld methodology because it just minimizes the free energy functional of target system. In order to predict the nucleation behavior depending on heat treatment conditions, CNT was applied and the computed nucleation conditions were used as input data for phase-ﬁeld simulation. Finally, the microstructure evolution of intragranular secondary  c 0   particles was simulated by the multi-phase-ﬁeld model coupled with these models and compared with the experimental results.  

# 2. Materials characterization  

# 2.1. Heat treatment conditions and observed microstructures  

In order to study precipitation behavior of  c 0   phase in TMW- 4M3 according to various temperature histories, heat treatment experiments  [2]  shown in  Fig. 1  were conducted. There were four different conditions with two solution temperature and two cool- ing rates. Detailed temperature histories during cooling process are shown in  Fig. 2 . In the numerical simulation, we ignored cool- ing below 400   C because the microstructures’ change is little in this temperature range. Average cooling rates from solution tem- perature to 700   C were about 270   C/min for rapid cooling (RC) and 130   C/min for slow cooling (SC).  

quenched samples were polished with 4000 grit SiC paper and buffed using 1 mm diamond spray. Vibrational polishing for more 10 hours was chosen for the ﬁnal polishing using colloidal silica, which can give a suitable etching effect on polished surface with- out etching pits. The micrographs of the samples were taken by SEM and temperature dependence of  c 0   volume fraction around solvus temperature was quantitatively analyzed by Image J [13,14] . An Arrhenius plot of measured  c 0   fraction is shown in Fig. 5 . The amount of  c 0   phase is linearly reducing with increasing aging temperature. By extrapolating this relationship toward fur- ther higher temperature, the solvus temperatures of  c 0   in these TMW-4M3 and Alloy 720Li were estimated as 1192   C and 1164  C, respectively.  

# 3. Numerical simulation and results  

# 3.1. Thermodynamic database assessment  

The solvus temperature of  c 0   precipitates for TMW-4M3 was calculated by Thermo-Calc software  [15]  and ThermoTech TTNI8 database  [16] . Under the metastable conditions, the computed sol- vus temperature was 1212   C, which is about 20   C higher than the experimental result. This was presumed because TMW-4M3 is lar- ger in the amount of Co and Ti than standard Ni base superalloys. Then, the calculated Ni-Co-Ti ternary phase diagram at 900   C was also compared with recent experiment data  [17]  in order to check whether this database is available for larger Co and Ti composition range (see  Fig. 6 ). Phase boundary between  c / g (Ni 3 Ti) is different from experiments and  g  single phase area looks too broad and sol- ubility of  c  phase is narrow in the calculation. Therefore, we intro- duced some modiﬁed thermodynamic parameters in Ni-Co-Ti systems to TTNI8.  g  and  c 0   phase are described by the compound energy model  [18]  with (Co, Ni, Ti) 3 (Ni, Ti) 1  for  g  phase and (Co, Ni, Ti) 3 (Co, Ni, Ti) 1  for  c 0   phase to reproduce the homogeneity region. In this study, the formation energy of Co 3 Ti in  g  phase, Ni 3 - Ti in  c 0   phase and interaction energy between Co, Ni and Ti in  c phase were modiﬁed. The thermodynamic model of these phases is the same as the TTNI8. Co 3 V structure phase appeared in the experimental phase diagram  [17]  was ignored because thermody- namic parameter of this phase is not found in the original database. # 3.2. Nucleation rate calculation

In this research, we computed nucle- ation conditions on the basis of the CNT  [11,12]  by using the pre- cipitation module of Thermo-Calc. The target process is the intragranular precipitation of  c 0   particles. As the initial condition for nucleation rate calculation, the composition of  c  phase C c  is required. # 5. Summary and conclusions  

In this paper the precipitation behavior of  c 0   precipitates in TMW-4M3 during heat treatment process was numerically simu- lated. Based on the results, the following ﬁndings can be summarized:  

1. The solvus temperatures of  c 0   in TMW-4M3 and Alloy 720Li were estimated 1192   C and 1164   C, respectively. And thermo- dynamic parameters of Co-Ni-Ti system were modiﬁed to reproduce the experimental phase boundaries and the solvus of TMW-4M3 and Alloy 720Li.

3. The morphology and size of secondary  c 0   precipitates can be evaluated by two-dimensional phase-ﬁeld simulation together with nucleation rate prediction. For two-dimensional calcula- tion domain, the nucleation number should be converted from three-dimensional value considering the equivalent diffusion ﬁeld by the same inter-particle distance.
