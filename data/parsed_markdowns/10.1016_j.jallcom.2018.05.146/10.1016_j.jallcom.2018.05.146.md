# 1. Introduction  

The further development of improved gas turbine engines with higher ef ﬁ ciencies and greater thrust is dependent on the discovery and use of improved materials and better design. Although math- ematical modeling can be used to estimate the stresses experienced by rotating parts inside a running turbine engine, accurate exper- imental measurements of strains are urgently needed to con ﬁ rm these estimates. This is particularly important in air cooled gas turbine blades and vanes where high temperatures and very high thermal gradients are present which can vary signi ﬁ cantly with different operating conditions [ 1 e 3 ]. Thin  ﬁ lm sensors fabricated directly on the surface of tested components are ideally suited for in-situ strain monitoring in these applications since they are non- intrusive, have minimal impact on the thermal, strain and vibra- tion patterns due to their negligible mass, and are robust enough to withstand the high  ‘ g ’  loading associated with rotating components [ 4 e 6 ]. PdCr thin  ﬁ lm strain gages are widely used in both dynamic and static strain measurements of high-temperature components  

One of the most effective ways to improve the oxidation resis- tance of the PdCr thin  ﬁ lm strain gage at high temperature is to coat a passivation layer on PdCr thin  ﬁ lm. For example, Lei [ 11 ] over- coated the surface of PdCr strain gage with alumina and alumina with small additions of zirconia using  ﬂ ame spraying and obtained an increased resistance stability. However, the high-velocity  ﬂ ame spraying can cause the damage of PdCr thin  ﬁ lms with the thick- ness of only a few micrometers. In addition, Lei et al. [ 12 ] adopted electron beam evaporation and sputter deposition to fabricate the alumina protective coating onto the surface of thin  ﬁ lm sensors, resulting in their improved oxidation resistance. However, the substrate needs to be heated to 900    C before depositing the alumina protective coating, which is energy-consuming and the dif ﬁ culty in the large-scale application. Besides, Dyer et al. [ 13 ] sputtered Cr thin  ﬁ lm on the surface of PdCr thin  ﬁ lm strain gages and then the thin  ﬁ lm was heat treated in pure oxygen to form native Cr 2 O 3  protective coating. The results showed that the thin ﬁ lm strain gages had a low drift rates of 0.1  U /h. However, the TCR is to relatively þ 789 large ppm/  C, and which unstable, can ranging cause the from inaccurate þ 550 ppm/ strain  C measurements.  

Based on previous studies, a more effective oxidation barrier has to be explored to avoid or alleviate the oxidation of PdCr thin  ﬁ lm strain gages and consequently improve their stability. Recently, it was found that ceramic/metal bilayer coatings were widely inves- tigated to improve the thermal stability, mechanical properties, and the corrosion resistance and oxidation resistance of metals and their alloys [ 14 e 16 ]. For example, Wu et al. [ 15 ] deposited Al 2 O 3 /Al and Al 2 O 3 /Ti duplex coatings on AZ31 magnesium alloy by multi- target magnetron sputtering technique to improve its surface properties, exhibiting that Al 2 O 3 /Ti coated AZ31 magnesium alloy had the better mechanical properties while Al 2 O 3 /Al coated AZ31 magnesium alloy had better corrosion resistance compared to un- coated AZ31 magnesium alloy. In addition, Wang et al. [ 16 ] studied the oxidation resistance properties of NiCoCrAlY coatings with and without an Al 2 O 3 /Al interlayer on an Ti-22Al-26Nb alloy, and the results showed that Al 2 O 3 /Al duplex coating system signi ﬁ cantly improved the oxidation resistance of the alloy substrate under either isothermal or cyclic conditions from 800 to 900    C, with a thin and adherent protective  a -Al 2 O 3  scale forming on the surface. These duplex coating systems used in bulk metal materials provide us the inspiration for the improvement of the oxidation resistance of thin  ﬁ lm strain gages. In this study, one Al interlayer was inserted between Al 2 O 3  overcoat and the PdCr thin  ﬁ lm using the Double Ion Beam Sputtering deposition (DIBSD) and the effect of Al 2 O 3 /Al bilayer coating system on the high-temperature stability of PdCr thin  ﬁ lm strain gages was investigated. It was found that in-situ formed Al 2 O 3  interlayer combined with sputtered Al 2 O 3  overcoat can signi ﬁ cantly enhance the oxidation resistance of PdCr thin  ﬁ lm strain gages and consequently cause the marked improvement in the piezoresistive response and stability.  

# 2. Experimental procedures  

To study the effect of Al 2 O 3 /Al bilayer coating system on the high-temperature stability of thin  ﬁ lm strain gages, the PdCr thin ﬁ lm overcoated with the Al 2 O 3 /Al bilayer coatings (denoted as “ Al 2 O 3 /Al-PdCr ” ) was fabricated on the surface of alumina ceramic substrate by utilizing a micro electromechanical systems (MEMS) fabrication technology. The schematic for the fabrication of Al 2 O 3 / Al-PdCr thin  ﬁ lm on the alumina ceramic substrate is shown in Fig. 1 . The entire fabrication process of Al 2 O 3 /Al-PdCr thin  ﬁ lm strain gages was performed in the clean rooms.  

High purity alumina was used as the substrates for the fabri- cation of PdCr thin  ﬁ lm strain gages, since it is chemically inert, and provides excellent electrical isolation at high temperature. These alumina substrates were laser-cut into the constant strain beams with specially design shape and size for the reliable measurement of the piezoresistive response and stability of thin  ﬁ lm strain gages during high-temperature tests ( Fig. 2 c), and then were annealed in a muf ﬂ e furnace at 1000    C in air for 1 h to eliminate the internal stress generated during laser cutting. The substrates were thor- oughly cleaned in acetone, alcohol and deionized water followed by a nitrogen blow dry and low temperature oven bake prior to  ﬁ lm deposition.  

The substrates were spin-coated with positive photoresist ( Fig. 1 a). Using a UV light source, PdCr thin  ﬁ lm strain gage pattern was contact printed onto the photoresist-coated alumina substrates ( Fig. 1 b). Then, PdCr thin  ﬁ lm with the thickness of 1.2  m m was sputtered with magnetron sputtering system in argon at a pressure

# 3. Results and discussion  

3.1. Microstructure characterization of PdCr thin  ﬁ lm strain gages

It has been reported that different textures can be formed in sputter-deposited thin  ﬁ lms, which can be affected by the sputtering parameters, e.g. sputtering pressure, oxygen  ﬂ ow rate, sputtering power density, substrate temperature, and  ﬁ lm thickness [ 22 , 23 ]. In addition, the peak of Cr disappeared and the peaks of Cr 2 O 3  and PdO generated in the annealed PdCr thin  ﬁ lm, compared to as-deposited PdCr thin  ﬁ lm, which was likely to be caused by the signi ﬁ cant oxidation of PdCr thin  ﬁ lm after annealing at 800    C for 10 h [ 24 ].  

On the other hand, the in-situ formed Al 2 O 3  from Al interlayer after annealing at 800    C for 1 h was too thin to be detected by XRD, hence, XPS was conducted to determine the chemical states of the major elements referenced to the C 1s peak. As shown in  Fig. 3 b, peaks originating from Al, O, and C were observed. The presence of carbon was mainly due to surface contamination from the ambient after deposition. The two insets in  Fig. 3 b are narrow scans of Al 2p and O 1s, respectively. The Al 2p peak at 74.4 eV suggested only one binding state, Al 3 þ , in the in-situ Al 2 O 3  thin  ﬁ lm [ 25 , 26 ]. To analyze the effect of protective coating on the microstructure of PdCr thin  ﬁ lm strain gages, the cross sections of PdCr thin  ﬁ lms with and without protective coatings were fabricated using focus ion beam (FIB) milling.  Fig. 4  shows the FIB cross-section mor- phologies of as-deposited PdCr, Al 2 O 3 -PdCr and Al 2 O 3 /Al-PdCr thin ﬁ lms annealed at 800    C for 10 h. It is obviously observed that PdCr thin  ﬁ lm without protective coating had the oxidation layer of \~500 nm ( Fig. 4 a and b), while PdCr thin  ﬁ lm with Al 2 O 3  overcoat had the oxidation layer of \~200 nm ( Fig. 4 c and d), suggesting that the Al 2 O 3  can effectively suppress the oxidation of PdCr thin  ﬁ lm. In stark contrast, PdCr thin  ﬁ lm with Al 2 O 3 /Al bilayer coatings exhibited only discontinuous oxide particles with the diameter of \~40 nm, indicating that the in-situ formed Al 2 O 3  interlayer com- bined with the Al 2 O 3  overcoat can more signi ﬁ cantly enhance the oxidation resistance of PdCr thin  ﬁ lm compared to a single Al 2 O 3 overcoat. Possible explanations are as follows: for PdCr thin  ﬁ lm without protective coating, Cr in the PdCr alloy is easy to react with oxygen to form the Cr 2 O 3  with loose structure at high temperature. The loose Cr 2 O 3  facilitates the oxygen diffusion along the grain boundary of Cr 2 O 3  toward the PdCr thin  ﬁ lm interior, resulting in the further oxidation of PdCr thin  ﬁ lm with the thickness of oxidation layer up to \~500 nm [ 27 ]. In addition, the formation of Cr 2 O 3  in the high temperature shrinks when cooling due to its higher density and consequently causes the high stress concen- tration in the interface between oxidation layer and PdCr thin  ﬁ lm. Once the interface stress reaches the critical value, interface crack will generate [ 28 ]. So, it is clearly shown that there were large gaps in the interface between oxidation layer and PdCr thin  ﬁ lm ( Fig. 4 a and b). Besides, the formation of Cr 2 O 3  caused the Pd in the PdCr alloy to be replaced, and then the replaced Pd passed through the grain boundaries and diffused to the surface, resulting in the for- mation of many dispersed nodular Pd or PdO particles ( Figs. 3 a and 4a ) [ 29 , 30 ]. For PdCr thin  ﬁ lm with Al 2 O 3  overcoat, the dense Al 2 O 3 thin  ﬁ lm can prevent oxygen from entering the interior of the PdCr thin  ﬁ lm to react with Pd and Cr, so the oxidation resistance of PdCr thin  ﬁ lm was improved to some extent. However, with increasing annealing time, the free oxygen in the interface between Al 2 O 3 overcoat and the PdCr thin  ﬁ lm, as well as the oxygen ionic with extremely high diffusivity through the Al 2 O 3  protective layer, can cause the further oxidation of Cr and result in the thickness of oxidation layer up to 200 nm [ 31 ]. Specially, unlike the straight interface in as-deposited PdCr thin  ﬁ lm, the interface in Al 2 O 3 -PdCr thin  ﬁ lm exhibited a tortuous path, indicative of the reduced oxidation rate due to the increasing dif ﬁ culty in oxygen diffusion. Moreover, the tortuous interface resulted in a good interface adhesion. However, there were some holes in the interface between Al 2 O 3  overcoat and the oxidation layer of Cr 2 O 3 , which was likely attributed that trace impurities in the PdCr alloy diffused along the grain boundary to the interface and reacted with Al 2 O 3 , dissolving Al 2 O 3  and forming pores at the interface [ 11 ]. To further clarify the chemical stability of PdCr thin  ﬁ lm with Al 2 O 3 /Al bilayer protective coatings at high temperature, the FIB cross-section morphologies of Al 2 O 3 /Al-PdCr thin ﬁ lm after annealing at 800    C for 1 h, 3 h, 5 h and 10 h were observed respectively, as show in  Fig. 5 . It is obviously observed that there was a good adhesion in the interface between Al 2 O 3  overcoat and in-situ Al 2 O 3  interlayer formed from the high-temperature oxida- tion of Al thin  ﬁ lm ( Fig. 5 a). After annealing for 3 h, no oxidation layer formed in the PdCr thin  ﬁ lm ( Fig. 5 b). However, after annealing for 5 h, the surface of PdCr thin  ﬁ lm appeared a small amount of discontinuous oxides (\~20 nm in diameter) growing along the grain boundaries towards the PdCr thin  ﬁ lm ( Fig. 5 c). With the annealing time up to 10 h, these oxide particles grew to 40 nm with non-continuous oxidation layer, which further demonstrated that the in-situ formed Al 2 O 3  interlayer combined with sputtered Al 2 O 3  overcoat can effectively keep the structure stability of PdCr thin  ﬁ lm. In addition, there were some precipitates and some nano-pores in the interface between in-situ formed Al 2 O 3 and PdCr thin  ﬁ lm, which was likely due to the segregation of impurity elements in PdCr through the diffusion process at high temperature for long time. Therefore, high-purity PdCr alloy target is very important for the microstructural stability of PdCr thin  ﬁ lm.  

# 3.2. The electrical properties of PdCr thin  ﬁ lm strain gages  

PdCr thin  ﬁ lm strain gages fabricated with and without pro- tective coatings were ramped to 800    C in air and held for an extended period while the electric resistances were monitored. Fig. 6 a shows the variation of the resistances of Al 2 O 3 /Al-PdCr, Al 2 O 3 -PdCr and as-deposited PdCr thin  ﬁ lm strain gages with annealing temperature and time. Based on the Eq.  (1) , over the temperature range from 23    C to 800    C, as-deposited PdCr thin ﬁ lm exhibited a  TCR  of  þ 350 ppm/  C, while Al 2 O 3 -PdCr thin  ﬁ lm displayed a smaller  TCR  of 300 ppm/  C. In contrast, the Al 2 O 3 /Al- PdCr thin  ﬁ lm had the smallest  TCR  of  þ 150 ppm/  C, which was obviously less than that of PdCr thin  ﬁ lm strain gages reported before [ 11 e 13 , 32 ]. In addition, with the increasing time up to 10 h at 800    C, the resistance of as-deposited PdCr thin  ﬁ lm increased by 55  U , while the resistance of Al 2 O 3 -PdCr thin  ﬁ lm increased by 18  U . In stark contrast, Al 2 O 3 /Al-PdCr thin  ﬁ lm had the resistance rise of only 0.5  U , suggesting that Al 2 O 3 /Al bilayer pro- tective coatings can more signi ﬁ cantly improve the resistance sta- bility of PdCr thin  ﬁ lm at high temperature, compared to Al 2 O 3 protective coating. Specially,  Fig. 6 b shows the resistance variation of Al 2 O 3 /Al-PdCr thin  ﬁ lm strain gages with the annealing time during three cycling tests up to 800    C for 10 h. It is clearly observed that the resistance vs. annealing time characteristics were identical for three cycling tests, and the  TCR  were all  þ 150 ppm/  C, further indicating that PdCr thin  ﬁ lm with Al 2 O 3 /Al bilayer protective coatings had the good resistance stability during long-term cycling use at high temperature. The small  TCR  and excellent resistance stability of Al 2 O 3 /Al-PdCr thin  ﬁ lm can be attributed to its structure stability provided by Al 2 O 3 /Al bilayer protective coatings ( Fig. 5 ).

# 4. Conclusions  

In this study, the effect of Al 2 O 3 /Al bilayer protective coatings on the microstructure and properties of PdCr thin  ﬁ lm strain gages was investigated by SEM, and high-temperature strain tests. The main results are as follows:  

(1) After annealing at 800    C for 10 h, PdCr thin  ﬁ lm with Al 2 O 3 / Al bilayer coatings exhibited only discontinuous oxide par- ticles with the diameter of \~40 nm, while as-deposited and Al 2 O 3  overcoated PdCr thin  ﬁ lms had the oxidation layer of \~500 nm and \~200 nm, respectively. These observations suggested that in-situ formed Al 2 O 3  interlayer combined with the Al 2 O 3  overcoat can signi ﬁ cantly enhance the oxidation resistance of PdCr thin  ﬁ lm, compared to as- deposited and Al 2 O 3  overcoated PdCr thin  ﬁ lms.  

(2) The as-deposited PdCr thin  ﬁ lm strain gages exhibited the biggest TCR of  þ 350 ppm/  C, the biggest drift rate of 0.01/h and smallest gage factor of 1.1, while the Al 2 O 3 -PdCr thin  ﬁ lm strain gage displayed the medium TCR of  þ 300 ppm/  C, the medium drift rate of 0.006/h and medium gage factor of 1.4. In stark contrast, the Al 2 O 3 /Al-PdCr thin  ﬁ lm strain gage was extremely stable and responsive with the smallest TCR of  þ 150 ppm/  C, the smallest drift rate less than 0.0004/h and the biggest gage factor of 1.7. This marked improvement in the piezoresistive response and stability of Al 2 O 3 /Al-PdCr thin  ﬁ lm strain gages can be attributed to the excellent structure stability and the oxidation resistance provided by in-situ formed Al 2 O 3  interlayer combined with sputtered Al 2 O 3  overcoat.
