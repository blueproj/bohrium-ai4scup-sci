P(BDT-TT-BO)  showed broad absorption, in the wavelength range of 350 − 700 nm, and low highest occupied molecular orbital (HOMO) energy level ( − 5.31 eV). # INTRODUCTION  

Over the past decades, a great number of polymer semi- conductors have been synthesized and applied in optoelectronic 1    ﬁ devices, such as polymer solar cells (PSCs) and polymer eld- e ﬀ ect transistors (PFETs).   Benzothiadiazole- ( BT- ) based conjugated polymers is one of the most important organic semiconductors, because of their easy preparation, excellent stability and electrooptical characteristics. Many e ﬀ orts have been devoted to the design and synthesis of new conjugated D − A copolymers containing  BT  unit, − 5   in order to get high performance active materials for PSCs and PFETs. For example, power conversion e ﬃ ciencies (PCEs) of the PSCs based on  BT -containing D − A copolymers have reached 7%; 4 hole mobility of  ﬁ eld-e ﬀ ect transistor based on the  BT - containing polymer has been up to 1 cm 2   V − 1   s − 1 ,   after systematic optimization.  

In contrast, there are far less reports on its analogue 6 − 8   benzoxadiazole ( BO )-based conjugated polymers used in PSCs and scarcely reports in PFETs. The main reason is that early researches on the  BO -based polymers had produced disappointing results compared to that of  BT -based polymers. Benzoxadiazole was considered inferior to b enzo thi a diaz ole in the application in organic semiconductors. Recently, several reports have revealed that the PCE of PSCs based on benzoxadiazole moieties had reached 5%.   The inadequate performance of  BO -based polymers in the previous reports could be due to their relatively low molecular weights and poor solubility. Actually, considering the stronger electronegativity of oxygen atom than that of sulfur atom, it is possible to reduce the HOMO level of the polymers by replacing the sulfur atom in b enzo thi a diaz ole unit with an oxygen atom to form benzoxadiazole ring. As a result, air-stable polymers with higher open circuit voltage ( V oc ) of the corresponding bulk heterojunction (BHJ) polymer solar cells can be expected.   The enhancement of open circuit voltage is very important for a PSC device, because the PCE of the device is proportional to the values of this parameter.  

Our previous researches have shown that thieno[3,2- b ]thiophene ( TT ) bridge is propitious to enhance both hole mobility and photovoltaic performance of the D- π -A copolymers.   In this study, we designed and synthesized a new thieno[3,2- b ]thiophene bridged D- π -A copolymer,  P- (BDT-TT-BO) , combining 2,1,3-benzoxadiazole ( BO ) accept- or unit with benzo[1,2- b :4,5- b ′ ]dithiophene ( BDT ) donor unit, as shown in Scheme 1. We introduced two octyloxy chains on the  BO  ring and two long branched 2-octyldodecyloxy groups on  BDT  segment to guarantee good solubility of the polymer for studying the properties of the new  TT  bridged polymer semiconductor, featuring 2,1,3-benzoxadiazole, in the solution- processable optoelectronic devices.

Measurements and Characterization.  The molecular weights of the polymers were measured by gel permeation chromatography (GPC) method. Fabrication of Photovoltaic Devices.  Regular PSCs devices were fabricated with indium tin oxides (ITO) glass as a positive electrode, Ca/Al as a negative electrode, and the photosensitive layer between them. The ITO glass was precleaned and modi ﬁ ed by a thin layer of PEDOT:PSS, which was spin-cast from a PEDOT:PSS aqueous solution (Clevious P VP AI 4083 H. C. Stark, Germany) on the ITO substrate, and the thickness of the PEDOT:PSS layer was about 60 nm. The photosensitive layer was prepared by spin-coating a blend solution of polymer and [6,6]-phenyl C 71 -butyric acid methyl ester (PC 71 BM) in  o -dichlorobenzene (DCB) on the ITO/ PEDOT:PSS electrode. Then, the Ca/Al cathode was deposited on the polymer layer by vacuum evaporation under 3  ×  10 − 4   Pa. The inverted PSC devices were fabricated by the following steps. The poly[(9,9-bis(3 ′ -( N,N -dimethylamino)propyl)-2,7- ﬂ uorene)- alt -2,7- (9,9 − dioctyl ﬂ uorene)] (PFN) interlayer material was deposited by spin-coating PFN solution (concentration of 2 mg mL − 1   in methanol, in the presence of a small amount of acetic acid) on top of the precleaned ITO substrate. The active blend layer, with a thickness of about 100 nm, was prepared by spin-coating a mixed solution of polymer and PC 71 BM in DCB/1,8-diiodoctane (DIO) (100/3, v/v). A 10 nm MoO 3  layer and a 100 nm Al layer were subsequently evaporated through a shadow mask to de ﬁ ne the active area of the devices and form a top anode. The thickness of the photosensitive layer was measured on an Ambios Tech XP-2 pro ﬁ lometer. The e ﬀ ective area of one cell was ca. 4 mm 2 . The current − voltage ( J  versus V ) measurement of the devices was conducted on a computer controlled Keithley 236 source measure unit. A xenon lamp with an AM 1.5  ﬁ lter was used as the white-light source, and the optical power at the sample was 100 mW cm − 2 .  

Fabrication of Field-E ﬀ ect Transistor Devices.  Thin- ﬁ lm PFETs were fabricated on highly doped silicon substrates with thermally grown 300-nm-thick silicon oxide (SiO 2 ) insulating layer, where the substrate served as a common gate electrode. Prior to polymer semiconductor deposition, the substrates were treated with the silylating agent oct yl tri chlo rosi lane (OTS). Thin semiconductor ﬁ lms were then deposited by spin-coating the polymer solutions in chloroform on the substrates. The  ﬁ lm thickness was measured by an XP-2 surface pro ﬁ lometer (Ambios Technology). The samples were then dried and annealed in air. Then, source-drain gold electrodes of transistor were deposited on the polymer layer, by vacuum evaporation under 7  ×  10 − 4   Pa, to form top-contact geometry. The electrical characterization of the transistor devices was performed using a Keithley 4200 semiconductor parameter analyzer.

# RESULTS AND DISCUSSION  

Synthesis and Characterization.  The chemical structure and synthesis route of polymer  P(BDT-TT-BO)  are outlined in Scheme 1 and Scheme 2, respectively.  P(BDT-TT-BO)  was synthesized by palladium-catalyzed Stille-coupling polymer- ization, similar to that of its  BT -containing analogue  P(BDT- TT-BT)  (Scheme 1).  P(BDT-TT-BO)  showed a number- average molecular weight of 21.1 kg mol − 1 , a weight-average molecular weight of 58.9 kg mol − 1 , and a polydispersity index of 2.8. The polymer can be readily dissolved in common organic solvents, such as chloroform, toluene, and chlorobenzene, and processed to form smooth and pinhole-free  ﬁ lms upon spin- coating. The structure of  P(BDT-TT-BO)  was con ﬁ rmed by 1 H NMR spectroscopy and elemental analysis. Thermal property of the polymer was determined by thermo grav i metric analysis (TGA) under nitrogen atmosphere at a heating rate of 10  ° C min − 1 .  P(BDT-TT-BO)  has good thermal stability with onset decomposition temperature corresponding to 5% weight loss at 320  ° C, as shown in Figure S9, Supporting Information. Obviously, the thermal stability of the polymer is adequate for its applications in PSCs, OFETs, and other optoelectronic devices.

With the addition of DIO, the photo- current response of the device was enhanced, especially in the range of 600 − 700 nm. The increase of the photovoltaic performance by adding DIO could be ascribed to the crystallization of the blends.   The increased absorption due to ordered  P(BDT-TT-BO) , particularly in the longer wavelength region beyond 600 nm (vibronic shoulder located at 639 nm, Figure 1), was responsible for the obvious increases in EQE in that region. Additionally, crystalline ordering arrangement of the components in the blends can be conducive to increase the charge carrier mobility in the active layer. Higher mobility can facilitate charge separation and transport to improve the overall level of EQE.  

The inverted PSC devices with the structure of ITO/PFN/ P(BDT-TT-BO) :PC 71 BM/MoO 3 /Al were fabricated at the optimized active layer condition (weight ratio of  P(BDT-TT- BO)  to PC 71 BM of 1:1.2, 3% DIO in DCB). The enhanced J sc  in the inverted PSCs may originate from reduced bimolecular recombination and increased absorption of photons.   As shown in EQE spectra in Figure 4, the inverted structure can harvest more photons from solar spectra than the regular devices. The signi ﬁ cantly increased FF should be attributed to the improved interface between active layer and ITO electrode and decreased work functions of ITO by PFN, which may supply e ﬃ cient electron collection and decrease electron − hole recombination.   Finally, solar cell devices with  P(BDT-TT- BO)  as donor showed power conversion e ﬃ ciencies over 7%, outperforming that of all other benzoxadiazole containing conjugated polymers and most b enzo thi a diaz ole-based photo- voltaic materials.

# CONCLUSION  

We designed and synthesized a benzoxadiazole-containing D- π - A copolymer,  P(BDT-TT-BO) , with thiano[3,2- b ]thiaphene  π - bridges, by palladium catalyzed Stille-coupling reaction. The polymer possesses good solubility and thermal stability. The introduction of stronger accepting benzoxadiazole unit not only enhanced the intramolecular charge transfer in the conjugated chain but also down-shifted the HOMO energy level of the organic semiconductor, compared to its analogue polymer P(BDT-TT-BT)  based on b enzo thi a diaz ole. Benzoxadiazole acceptor unit together with thiano[3,2- b ]thiaphene  π -bridge endow  P(BDT-TT-BO)  with low-lying HOMO energy level, broad absorption as well as high charge carrier mobility, which contributed to the high performance of the polymer in photoelectric devices. The PCE of the PSCs based on P(BDT-TT-BO)  reached 7.05%, which is the highest e ﬃ ciency ever reported for benzoxadiazole-based polymers and equals that of the most e ﬃ cient b enzo thi a diaz ole-based photovoltaic materials. PFETs fabricated from the polymer semiconductor showed sensitive dependence of carrier mobility on the annealing temperature. Hole mobilities of the PFETs based on  P(BDT-TT-BO)  upgraded gradually with the increase of annealing temperature. After annealing at 200  ° C, mobility of P(BDT-TT-BO) -based polymer  ﬁ eld e ﬀ ect transistor increased more than 12 times, reached 0.023 cm 2   V − 1   s − 1 . These results indicate that benzoxadiazole is a very promising building block for high performance polymer semiconductors; using thieno- [3,2- b ]thiophene as  π -bridges is an e ﬀ ective way to improve the photovoltaic performance of the conjugated polymers.
