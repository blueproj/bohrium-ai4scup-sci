Indeed, two novel rat organic anion transporters, each expressed strongly in liver and kidney, were recently identified.Jacqueminetal.(14,15）clonedacDNA called “organic anion-transporting polypeptide" (i.e., oatp)that catalyzes the transport of the classic organic anion dye bro mosul fo phthalein(BSP)(15).Addition- ally,our laboratory showed that a homologous c DNA (called“PGT")encodes a novel prostaglandin trans- porter (17).  

The oatp protein is expressed at the basolateral membrane of he pato cyte s but at the apical membrane of the rat kidneyS 3 segment(3,18).The latter finding is somewhat surprising,sinceBSPis normally secreted negligiblybythekidney(i9).Onlyinseverehepatic disease,where plasma B SP levels are extremelyhigh, is urinary BSP excretion probenecid inhibitable, indicat- ing tubularsecretionbyanorganicanionsecretory pathway(6).Accordingly,we suspected thatoatpmight transport organic anions other than BSP,i.e.,any ofthe manyorganicanionsknowntobesecretedbythe kidney.  

In the present study,and in the two accompanying companion articles(16,23),we have focused on the functional characterization and theregulation of renal oatp.Here,we transiently expressed the full-length oat pc DNA in a mammalian cell expression system and then tested an array of organic anions as candidate substrates by determining their ability to c is-inhibit BSPtransport.Wefindthat o atp does not interact with classicrenal-secretedorganicanionssuchasp-amino- hippurate (PAH)or indigo carmine(25,28).Rather, oatp-mediated B SP transport is inhibited stronglyby steroidhormones,suggesting that this class of com- pounds constitutes themajor substrates.  

The second articlein this series(16)examinesin more detail the hypothesis that steroids,aswellas steroid conjugates, are oatp substrates (16). The third study(23)shows thatoatpmRNA.expression in the kidney, but not the liver, is regulated by testosterone (23). Together, the data present an emerging picture of renal oatp as a transporter of conjugated sex steroid hormones.

Although great strides have been made in functional descriptions of organic anion transport in theliver (1) and kidney (31), the molecular understanding of these transporters has been limited.Although a mono car box- ylatetransporterexpressedinthekidney,amongother tissues,was recently cloned (13), there remains very little understanding of the mechanisms by which or- ganic anions aretransportedbyrenaltubules.Thusthe finding of strong expression of oatp mRNA in the rat kidney prompted us to evaluate this transporter in moredetail.  

Inseparate,unpublished studies,wehavefoundthat antibodies against the deduced COOH terminus of o atp recognize immuno cyto chemically an epitope localized exclusively to the ba so lateral membrane of he pato- cyte s but to the apical membrane of the ratS 3 pro xi- mal tubule (3, 18). Moreover, antisera against the deduced NH2 terminus recognize a protein band of the expectedsize(basedonthecDNA)onimmunoblots from both kidney and liver (A. W. Wolkoff, unpublished data). In addition, the kidney contains numerous oatp mRNA transcripts that might encode diverse proteins (23).Thus,althoughthe renal and hepatic proteins maydiffer,the exact nature of the difference is unclear atpresent.  

Despite these uncertainties,the expression of apical oat pin the kidney tubule is of considerable interest. Studies ono atp expressed by Meier and co-workers(15) inXenopus oo cyte s suggested that bile salts are major endogenous substrate for o atp.We confirmed that o atp expressedinHeLacellstransportstaurocholicacid (Table1).Inthekidney,filteredtaurocholicandcholic acid are actively reabsorbed by Na-dependenttrans- portin theproximal convoluted tubule (2,34,36).Since itisnotclearthatNa-dependentbilesalttransport evenexistsin the S3proximal tubule(2,34,36),it cannotbe concludedthat o atp functions somehow in this process. Moreover,if both the Na-dependent trans- porter andoatpwere apical ly expressed in the same cell,o atp would permit back flux of res or bed bile salts intotheurine.  

Because sucha futile cycle seemed unlikely, we sought to determine whether there are other substrates for renaloatp.Weusedcis-inhibition of B SP transport in the transient expression system as an assay with which to screen a variety of organic compounds as candidate substrates.Some of these have also been evaluated using the same approach in Xenopus oocytes. Like the results in the oocyte (20), we found that PAH did not cis-inhibit BSP transport(Fig.6)and that tracer PAH was not transported (Table 1). Thus oatp is not part of the renal"PAH transport system."Similarly, we confirmed in the mammalian cell expression sys- tem, as was reported in the oocyte, that rifampicin, bumetanide,sulfate,and succ in at edo not c is-inhibit BSP transport (Fig. 5), whereas probenecid is a moder- ate inhibitor(Fig.6) (20).  

On the other hand,some of our present data are at odds with previously reported results from oocyte stud- ies.Unconjugated bilirubin(10 uM) was reported to cis-inhibit B SP transport in the oo cyte,suggestingthat it may represent an oatp substrate (20). In contrast, we found that labeled unconjugated bilirubin, bilirubin monoglucuronide,and bilirubin dig luc ur onide were not transported by oatp,either with or without carrier albumin(Table 1).Because negative results can be somewhat difficult to interpret,we cannot exclude the possibility that o atp is capable of transporting bilirubin under other conditions. On the other hand, recent kinetic data by V ore and Hoffman(33)using hepatic membranes are consistent with the hypothesis that unconjugated bilirubin and bilirubin diglucuronide are transported by a system with properties differentfrom oatp  

The most striking result of the present studies is the strongcis-inhibition of B SP transport by steroids.As shown in Figs.5 and 6,cholate, taurocholate,hydrocor- tisone,corticosterone,corticosterone sulfate,spironolac- tone, and 1,25-di hydroxy vitamin D: all substantially inhibited B SP tracer uptake at concentrations of 10- 100uM.These results thus identify steroids as a general class of potential substrates for o atp  

Since shared transported substrates must, by defini- tion,compete with each other,a competition approach such as ours would be expected to eventually identify allpossibletransportsubstrates.Suchanapproachhas beenusedtoidentifypossiblesubstratesforthePAH system.Congruence between inhibition and transport was apparent in the cases of PAH and sulfate,which wereunabletocis-inhibit B SP transport(Figs.5and6) and which were not transported by oat pin the expr es- sion system (Table 1), and,in the case of taurocholate, which inhibited BSP transport and was itself trans- ported (Table 1; Figs. 5 and 6). On the other hand, corticosterone,which clearly inhibited BSP transport (Figs. 5 and 6), was not transported (Table 1).A similar dichotomy between the strength of an inhibitor and its ability to be transported in the kidney has been re- ported for other organic anions (1o). In this regard, caution should be applied to the interpretation of large series of experiments such as those examining c is- inhibition of PAH transport at the ba so lateral mem- brane of proximal tubules (32), since thelarge number of inhibitors identified using this inhibition system cannot all be considered a priori as transported sub- strates.  

That a variety of steroids inhibit B SP transport by o atp suggests that the steroid structure interacts in an important way with the transporter.Since t aur ocho late is transported, we hypothesized that the best oatp substrates might consist of conjugated steroids with a negative charge on the D ring, e.g., at the 17 or 21 position. The results shown in Fig. 5 are consistent with this hypothesis,inthat21-sulfated corticosterone inhibited BSP transport more potently than the nonsul- fated moiety. This hypothesis is tested more directly in the following companion article(16).  

In summary,oatphas beentransiently expressed in a mammalian cell system.The fundamental properties of B SP transport in this systemare similar tothat previously described in Xen opus oo cyte s.BSPtransport likely occurs via anion-H+cotransport.Cis-inhibition studies suggest that steroids represent a class of com- pounds with ahighlikelihood of representing oatp substrates.
